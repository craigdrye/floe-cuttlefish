# Science Discovery: Year 2
**ID**: `scienceYear2` - **Discipline**: Science - **Year**: 2 (ages 6-7)
**Frameworks**: UK KS1 Science Year 2, US NGSS K-2, ACARA Science Year 2

## Course Aim
Help children deepen their science habits: noticing patterns over time, comparing living things and materials, planning simple fair tests, recording data in drawings and charts, explaining healthy choices, and connecting Earth, sky, habitats, and human actions to everyday life.

## Course Design Notes
Year 2 keeps science close to children's hands and eyes while adding more deliberate enquiry. Learners make predictions, change one thing where possible, record results, compare data, and explain what the evidence suggests. The course works well with mixed-age groups because each chapter begins with shared observation and then offers deeper Year 2 reasoning through habitat links, life cycles, material suitability, data displays, and "what would happen if..." questions.

## Chapter 1: Living, Dead, and Never Alive
**Key concepts**: living, dead, once living, never alive, needs, movement, growth, reproduction, evidence, classification.

**Practical questions**
- What clues show that something is alive?
- Is a wooden chair living, dead, or never alive?
- What evidence should we use before sorting an object?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Sort real objects and picture cards into living, once living, and never alive groups, then defend one tricky choice with a sentence and a drawing.

## Chapter 2: Habitats and Microhabitats
**Key concepts**: habitat, microhabitat, pond, woodland, garden, soil, log, rockpool, shelter, food, water, adaptation, food chain.

**Practical questions**
- How does a habitat meet the needs of a living thing?
- What can live under a log, in soil, near a pond, or in a tree?
- How are a habitat and a microhabitat different?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Map a local microhabitat using sketches, tally marks, and "do not disturb" observation rules; build a simple food chain from a plant to an animal that eats it.

## Chapter 3: Plant Growth and Life Cycles
**Key concepts**: seed, bulb, germination, root, shoot, stem, leaf, flower, fruit, life cycle, light, water, warmth, nutrients.

**Practical questions**
- What does a seed need to begin growing?
- How does a plant change from seed to adult plant?
- What happens when one growing condition changes?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Grow seeds in two safe conditions, such as light and dark or watered and unwatered, record height with cubes or rulers, and make a simple line or bar display.

## Chapter 4: Animals, Offspring, and Health
**Key concepts**: offspring, adult, life cycle, survival needs, food, water, air, exercise, sleep, hygiene, balanced choices, germs.

**Practical questions**
- How do young animals change as they grow?
- Which needs do all animals share?
- How do exercise, food, sleep, and cleanliness help human bodies?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Sequence life-cycle cards for a frog, butterfly, bird, or human, then create a healthy-day plate or timetable with reasons for each choice.

## Chapter 5: Materials for a Purpose
**Key concepts**: material, property, suitability, strong, flexible, absorbent, waterproof, transparent, opaque, natural, human-made, recycle.

**Practical questions**
- Why is a window usually glass and a towel usually fabric?
- Which material is best for a job, and what evidence supports that choice?
- How can reusing and recycling materials reduce waste?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Compare materials for a bridge, bag, shade cover, or shelter using a simple test, then choose the best material and explain the property that mattered most.

## Chapter 6: Changing Materials and Simple Experiments
**Key concepts**: bend, twist, stretch, squash, heat, cool, melt, freeze, change, fair test, variable, prediction, result.

**Practical questions**
- How can some materials change shape?
- Which changes can be reversed, and which cannot easily be reversed?
- How do we make a test fair enough to trust?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Test how paper, foil, fabric, and clay respond to bending, twisting, stretching, and squashing; record each result in a grid with words or symbols.

## Chapter 7: Earth, Sky, and Weather Data
**Key concepts**: Sun, Moon, star, day, night, shadow, weather, season, temperature, rainfall, wind, data, pattern.

**Practical questions**
- What can we observe in the sky during day and night?
- How do shadows and weather change across a day or week?
- What pattern can a chart help us see?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Track daily weather and one shadow at the same time each day for a week, then compare the data using symbols, tallies, and talk.

## Chapter 8: Science in Our Local Place
**Key concepts**: local environment, biodiversity, pollution, litter, water, soil, care, citizen science, question, evidence, action.

**Practical questions**
- What living things share our local place?
- How can people help or harm a habitat?
- What small investigation could help us care for our classroom, garden, street, or park?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Conduct a safe local survey, such as leaf types, bird sightings, litter counts, or puddle changes, and turn the data into a class action idea.

## Capstone: Local Science Field Guide
Learners create a field guide or display with:
- a living/dead/never-alive sorting explanation
- one habitat or microhabitat map
- a plant growth chart
- a life-cycle sequence
- a material test table
- a fair-test plan with one changed variable
- a weather or shadow data display
- a local care action based on evidence

## Assessment Ideas
- Classification explanations using evidence
- Habitat map with needs of living things labelled
- Plant growth diary and data display
- Life-cycle sequencing and oral explanation
- Material suitability challenge with test results
- Fair-test checklist: question, prediction, one changed thing, result
- Weather or shadow data talk
- Local Science Field Guide capstone
