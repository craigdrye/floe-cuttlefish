# Science Discovery: Year 1
**ID**: `scienceYear1` - **Discipline**: Science - **Year**: 1 (ages 5-6)
**Frameworks**: UK KS1 Science, US NGSS K-2, ACARA Science Year 1, India NCERT EVS Class 1

## Course Aim
Help children notice the world like young scientists: looking closely, using their senses safely, asking simple questions, sorting what they find, drawing observations, talking about evidence, and caring for living things and local places.

## Course Design Notes
Year 1 science is concrete, playful, and observation-led. Lessons begin with familiar things: a leaf on the path, a rainy window, a toy car, a lunchbox material, a classroom plant. Children practise "I notice...", "I wonder...", and "I found out..." before formal explanations. Investigations use safe household and classroom materials, short outdoor walks, drawing, sorting hoops, picture cards, simple charts, and talk with partners.

## Chapter 1: Using Our Senses
**Key concepts**: sight, hearing, touch, smell, taste, body parts, safe observing, careful comparison, describing words.

**Practical questions**
- What can my eyes, ears, nose, hands, and tongue help me find out?
- When should I look, listen, or smell instead of touching or tasting?
- How can two objects feel, sound, or look different?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Make a senses table with sealed sound pots, textured fabric, scented jars, magnifiers, and picture labels; draw one careful observation from each sense station.

## Chapter 2: Plants Around Us
**Key concepts**: plant, tree, leaf, flower, root, stem, seed, wild plant, garden plant, deciduous, evergreen, growth.

**Practical questions**
- Which plants can we find near our home, school, or park?
- What parts can we see on a plant?
- How do plants change across days and seasons?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Grow quick seeds in clear cups, keep a picture diary, and compare what changes when plants have light and regular water.

## Chapter 3: Animals and Human Bodies
**Key concepts**: animal groups, human body parts, basic needs, food, water, air, shelter, movement, senses, care.

**Practical questions**
- How are animals the same and different?
- What do animals and people need to stay alive and healthy?
- Which body part helps with each sense or movement?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Sort local animal cards by features such as legs, wings, scales, fur, or where they live, then draw one animal with labels for its important parts.

## Chapter 4: Everyday Materials
**Key concepts**: object, material, wood, plastic, glass, metal, fabric, paper, rock, water, property, hard, soft, rough, smooth, bendy, stiff, waterproof, transparent.

**Practical questions**
- What is this object made from?
- Which words describe this material?
- Why are some materials good for some jobs?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Test small material samples for bending, texture, and waterproofing, then choose a good material for a pretend rain hat or lunch mat.

## Chapter 5: Weather and Seasons
**Key concepts**: weather, season, sun, cloud, rain, wind, snow, temperature, day length, clothing, seasonal change, weather chart.

**Practical questions**
- What is the weather like today?
- How do plants, animals, and people change what they do in different seasons?
- What patterns can we notice after a week of weather watching?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Keep a class weather calendar with symbols, clothing choices, and outdoor observations; compare a sunny day and a rainy day using drawings.

## Chapter 6: Pushes, Pulls, and Movement
**Key concepts**: push, pull, roll, slide, twist, faster, slower, stop, start, ramp, surface, force.

**Practical questions**
- What makes a toy move, stop, or change direction?
- Which surface lets something slide or roll farther?
- How can we make a fair comparison using the same toy?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Roll a toy car down the same ramp onto carpet, tile, paper, and fabric, then mark how far it travels with blocks or tape.

## Chapter 7: Living Things and Their Places
**Key concepts**: living, once living, never alive, habitat, shelter, food, water, pond, garden, tree, soil, minibeast, care.

**Practical questions**
- How can we tell if something is living?
- Where do small animals find food and shelter?
- How can we observe living things without harming them?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Go on a local habitat look-and-listen walk, record signs of life with tally marks or sketches, and return any moved natural objects gently.

## Chapter 8: Looking After Our World
**Key concepts**: environment, litter, reuse, recycle, compost, water saving, plant care, animal care, local action, responsibility.

**Practical questions**
- What helps a classroom plant, garden, or local animal stay healthy?
- Which materials can be reused or recycled?
- What small action can make our shared space better?

**Hands-on investigation**
- Sort clean classroom waste into reuse, recycle, compost, and landfill groups, then design a simple care sign for a plant, habitat, or water-saving spot.

## Capstone: My Science Discovery Journal
Learners create a small science journal or display with:
- a senses observation page
- a labelled plant drawing
- a local animal or minibeast sketch
- a material test result
- a weather chart
- a push or pull investigation drawing
- a caring-for-nature action plan
- one "I used to think... now I think..." reflection

## Assessment Ideas
- Observation drawings with labels and oral explanation
- Sorting tasks for plants, animals, materials, and living/non-living examples
- Teacher listening notes from partner talk and science vocabulary use
- Simple investigation participation: predicting, testing, noticing, and sharing
- Weather chart completion and pattern talk
- Material choice explanation: "I choose this because..."
- Science Discovery Journal capstone
