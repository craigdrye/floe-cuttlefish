# Science Discovery: Early Years
**ID**: `scienceEarlyYears` | **Status**: soon | **Discipline**: Science

## Course Aim
Help young children meet science as careful noticing, safe touching, playful sorting, and wondering aloud. The course builds early confidence with observation, comparison, simple prediction, gentle care for living things, and talk about what children see, hear, smell, feel, and try.

## Course Design Notes
This preschool science course is adult-facilitated and sensory-rich. Activities use familiar materials: water, leaves, seeds, fabric, blocks, magnets, ramps, weather pictures, story props, songs, and outdoor treasures. Children are invited to point, touch, move, sort, draw, tell an adult what they notice, or simply watch. Investigations stay tiny and safe: no tasting unknown materials, no forced touching, and no formal lab reports. Each chapter pairs a question with a short story or song, a hands-on exploration, and a small class artifact that can be revisited.

## Chapter 1: My Scientist Senses
**Circle-time invitations**
- Pass a mystery bag with safe objects such as a pinecone, scarf, spoon, shell, sponge, or smooth stone.
- Ask: "What do your eyes notice?", "What do your hands notice?", and "What words can we use?"
- Sing a senses song and match eyes, ears, nose, hands, and mouth to everyday observing.

**Tiny investigation**
- Sort objects by one property at a time: rough or smooth, heavy or light, loud or quiet, shiny or dull, soft or hard.

**Playful output**
- Make a class "noticing words" board with adult-scribed words and child drawings or photos.

## Chapter 2: Weather Watchers
**Circle-time invitations**
- Look out the window or step outside to notice sky, wind, clouds, warmth, cold, rain, or snow.
- Ask: "What is the weather doing today?" and "What might our bodies need?"
- Match weather cards to clothes, play choices, and safe outdoor routines.

**Tiny investigation**
- Use ribbons, bubbles, pinwheels, or leaves to see wind moving things.

**Playful output**
- Add a daily picture to a weather window chart: sunny, cloudy, rainy, windy, snowy, foggy, or mixed.

## Chapter 3: Water, Ice, and Puddles
**Circle-time invitations**
- Tell a small story about a raindrop, puddle, freezer, cup, and sunny windowsill.
- Ask: "What can water do?" and "Where does water go?"
- Notice wet, dry, drip, splash, float, sink, freeze, melt, and evaporate as simple experience words.

**Tiny investigation**
- Explore water with cups, spoons, sponges, ice cubes, plastic animals, and safe floating objects.

**Playful output**
- Create a "water can..." chart with photos or drawings: pour, drip, freeze, melt, soak, float, and sink.

## Chapter 4: Seeds and Growing Things
**Circle-time invitations**
- Open a fruit, look at seeds, and compare seed shapes, sizes, colors, and textures.
- Ask: "What might this seed need?" and "How can we care for it?"
- Read a seed-to-plant picture story and act out curling up, sprouting, stretching, and blooming.

**Tiny investigation**
- Plant fast-growing seeds or beans and observe them over time with water, light, soil, and patience.

**Playful output**
- Keep a class growth strip with drawings, photos, or adult-scribed child comments.

## Chapter 5: Animals, Homes, and Care
**Circle-time invitations**
- Use toy animals, photos, tracks, feathers, shells, or nest pictures to notice animal features.
- Ask: "Where might this animal feel safe?" and "What might it need?"
- Sort animals by home: pond, tree, burrow, farm, ocean, garden, house, or sky.

**Tiny investigation**
- Build pretend habitats with blocks, fabric, leaves, water trays, sand, tubes, or boxes.

**Playful output**
- Make a class habitat mat with places for food, water, shelter, space, and gentle hands.

## Chapter 6: Push, Pull, Roll, and Build
**Circle-time invitations**
- Watch balls, cars, blocks, scarves, and cardboard tubes move.
- Ask: "What made it move?", "Did it go fast or slow?", and "What changed?"
- Use action words: push, pull, roll, slide, bounce, stack, fall, balance, and stop.

**Tiny investigation**
- Try ramps with different heights and surfaces, noticing what rolls far, rolls slowly, or stops.

**Playful output**
- Add photos or drawings to a "things that move" wall, grouped by push, pull, roll, and slide.

## Chapter 7: Sorting, Patterns, and Materials
**Circle-time invitations**
- Offer buttons, lids, shells, fabric squares, building pieces, or nature finds for child-led sorting.
- Ask: "How did you make your group?" and "Could we sort it a different way?"
- Notice color, size, shape, texture, bendiness, stretchiness, transparency, and sound.

**Tiny investigation**
- Test which classroom materials are waterproof using a dropper or damp sponge, with adult support.

**Playful output**
- Build a class material museum with labels such as soft, hard, bendy, shiny, see-through, and waterproof.

## Chapter 8: Wonder Walks and Caring Choices
**Circle-time invitations**
- Take a slow indoor or outdoor wonder walk with a looking frame, basket, or picture checklist.
- Ask: "What changed?", "What is living?", "What should we leave alone?", and "How can we care for this place?"
- Practice gentle choices around plants, bugs, puddles, stones, soil, shared tools, and classroom pets or photos.

**Tiny investigation**
- Revisit the same small place across days or seasons and notice what is the same, different, growing, resting, or moving.

**Playful output**
- Create a class "we are careful scientists" agreement with pictures for look, ask, gentle hands, return, and wash hands.

## Capstone
Children create a "Wonder Lab and Nature Museum" for the classroom. It may include a senses board, weather chart, water photos, seed growth strip, habitat mat, ramp discoveries, material museum, and care agreement. Children guide a trusted adult or another small group through one favorite investigation, using simple noticing words, gestures, or drawings to show what they found.

## Assessment Ideas
- Adult observation notes on noticing, comparing, sorting, predicting, trying safely, and using science words.
- Photo portfolio of sensory sorts, weather records, water play, seed growth, habitats, ramps, and material tests.
- Adult-scribed child voice records using stems such as "I noticed...", "I think...", "It changed because...", and "I wonder..."
- Small-group talk prompts during play rather than quizzes or formal tests.
- Family connection prompt: invite caregivers to share one safe noticing walk, weather routine, plant care moment, or household material sort.
