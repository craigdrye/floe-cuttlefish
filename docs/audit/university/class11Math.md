## Cross-cluster flags

- **`DEFAULT_FLAW` equivalent present**: `numericWrong()` and `fractionWrong()` helpers in `colGapBuilders.ts:17` and `:25` produce identical generic "why wrong" text across distractors. Affects all NCERT Class 11 & 12 quiz output. **FLAGGED.**
- **Region**: India-specific syllabus (NCERT, "Class 11 (India)" Khan Academy). Syllabus says "ages 18-19, India CBSE/NCERT track" yet `ageGroup` is `'university'` and no region tag exists. **Jurisdiction-specific track; should be tagged `region: 'IN'`** or moved out of US-first launch.
- **Stage mismatch**: catalog calls this "University Freshman" but in India Class 11 is high school (ages 16–17). The age-mapping here is a deliberate adaptation; consider noting "Pre-university bridge (India)" in the subtitle.
- **Math notation**: ASCII, including special chars from prompts like `sin 30°` (uses the actual degree symbol). Renderer is plain text and the degree symbol renders as a normal Unicode glyph — fine.
- **Coverage breadth is the real problem**: 8 chapters, only 3 partial. The templated drills cover ~5 narrow procedures; everything else is uncovered.

## Open actions (priority order)

1. **Rewrite `numericWrong()` and `fractionWrong()` helpers** to require per-distractor explanations as arguments, then update all call sites in `colGapBuilders.ts`. This single change removes the silent `DEFAULT_FLAW` from `class11Math`, `class12Math`, and lower-tier India quizzes. ~6 hours.
2. **Author chapter 2 content**: induction proofs (3 questions), complex number arithmetic (3), Argand plane / modulus / argument (3), quadratic discriminant (2). Highest-value gap — entire chapter blank.
3. **Author chapter 6 content**: distance formula, line forms, circle equations, parabola focus, 3D distance (~8 items).
4. **Author chapter 8 content**: standard deviation for grouped data, P(A union B), sample space (~6 items).
5. **Add `region: 'IN'` tag** and adjust subtitle to flag India-track context in [`src/data/ageCatalog/university.ts`](../../../src/data/ageCatalog/university.ts).
6. **Decide on US-first launch posture**: either keep `class11Math` and accept India-specific content, or move it to a regional bucket and prune from the universal default course list.
