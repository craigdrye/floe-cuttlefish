## Quality of prompts and lessons (subjective read)

Prompts and lessons are largely strong — these were hand-authored from classic interview lore (Heard on the Street, Quant Job Interview Questions, Baruch PMFE). Voice is terse, prompts ask the actual interview question, lessons name the reusable reasoning move at the end. Brain Teasers and Probability chapters in particular feel polished. No prompt is malformed or grammatically broken.

The only systemic prompt-level concern is that some prompts state the entire constraint+question in one block of text, which is fine for a textual exam but reads heavy on a mobile screen. Mild.

## Universal issues

### Issue 1: `mentorHint` is the same for all 124 Core questions

The "Hint" button shows `question.mentorHint`. For all 124 Core questions, this field is auto-generated by [`defaultQuestionScaffold`](../../../src/data/questionCatalog/base.ts) keyed on topic `'Quant Finance'`:

> *"What is the fundamental mathematical or financial principle being tested here?"*

This is technically a hint but it's the same hint every time. The "Hint" button is effectively useless — it nudges no learner toward any specific reasoning move.

**Note:** the third element of each wrong-answer tuple is also a per-distractor hint, *but* that's only shown AFTER a wrong answer is selected, not when the Hint button is pressed.

### Issue 2: "Ask differently" doesn't actually reword

The "Ask differently" button calls [`remixQuestion()`](../../../src/lib/quizRuntime.ts#L551). It has custom remix logic for:
- **Pirates (19001)** — generates a variant with n pirates instead of 5
- **Tigers (in quantAdvanced, not Core)** — variant with n tigers

For every other Core question, it falls through to the default remix at line 859:

```ts
prompt: `In plain language: ${base.prompt}`,
```

— literally prepending "In plain language:" to the prompt. That's not a reword; it's just text-prefixing.

## Recommended remediation order

Three independent workstreams, ranked by leverage:

**A. Fix the 50 length-heuristic flags (mechanical, can do without your input)**

Two approaches per question:
- **Shorten correct** — the worst offenders bury an explanation in the correct answer; e.g., 19030 "89 (the 11th Fibonacci number)" → "89" and move "the 11th Fibonacci number" context to the lesson.
- **Lengthen wrongs** — flesh out the wrong options so they feel like real attempts; e.g., 19407 "5 times" → "5 times — weigh each bag against a reference once".

I'd recommend shortening correct (less invasive, doesn't risk making distractors more plausible).

**B. Author bespoke mentor hints (124 entries, needs your voice)**

For each question, write a 1-line hint that nudges toward the right reasoning move *without giving the answer*. Examples:
- 19001 Screwy Pirates → *"Work backward from 1 pirate. Each step shows who has zero in the fallback state — they're cheapest to buy."*
- 19009 Light Switches → *"You have three switches and only two visual states (on/off). What other physical property persists?"*

If you authorize, I can draft hints in your voice — modeled on the existing wrong-answer `hint` lines (those ARE bespoke and well-written) — and you sign off in batches.

**C. Author custom "Ask differently" remixes (124 entries, big lift)**

The Pirates remix is the model — it generates a variant with different parameters (`n = 5, 7, 9, 11, 15`). For most Core questions, a variant would change:
- Numeric parameters (counterfeit coins from 10 bags → 8 bags; horse race from 25 horses → 30)
- The wording while preserving the same problem
- The narrative wrapper (chess tournament → tennis tournament)

This is the biggest lift. Could be done one cluster at a time, lower priority. Or accept that "Ask differently" is mostly cosmetic for the Core track.
