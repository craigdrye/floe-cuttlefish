# Guess The Thing Regional Asset Guide

This folder contains image assets for the PNG-backed "guess the thing" tracks.

## Current Base Folders

- `animals`
- `birds`
- `body-parts`
- `fish`
- `fossils`
- `historical-ruins`
- `plants`
- `rocks`
- `subject-artifacts`
- `tracks-scat`

## Regional Folder Families

Regional collections currently exist for:

- `animals-regional`
- `birds-regional`
- `fish-regional`
- `plants-regional`
- `rocks-regional`
- `scat-regional`

Each regional family uses the same subfolder scheme:

- `north-america`
- `canada`

## Region Rules

- `north-america`: broad North America when a tighter bucket is not needed.
- `canada`: use when the image set is specifically Canadian rather than general North American.
- `caribbean`: island Caribbean species, habitats, or region-specific forms.
- `central-america`: Mexico is excluded unless we decide otherwise later; use for the Central American land bridge region.
- `south-america`: South American regional material.
- `europe`: Europe-wide or country-specific European material.
- `north-africa`: Mediterranean and Sahara-edge North African material.
- `sub-saharan-africa`: African material south of the Sahara.
- `middle-east`: Levant, Arabian Peninsula, Iraq, Iran-adjacent Middle East usage when not better treated as North Africa or Central Asia.
- `central-asia`: Kazakhstan through surrounding inland Central Asian regions.
- `south-asia`: Indian subcontinent region.
- `east-asia`: China, Korea, Japan, Mongolia-adjacent East Asia when appropriate.
- `southeast-asia`: mainland and island Southeast Asia.
- `oceania`: Australia, New Zealand, Papua New Guinea, and Pacific islands.
- `arctic-antarctic`: polar and near-polar material, including Arctic and Antarctic contexts.

## Naming Rules

- Prefer lowercase kebab-case filenames.
- Prefer stable common names over internal IDs in filenames.
- Use one specimen or subject per image unless the track explicitly depends on comparison.
- Keep transparent-background edits or alternates in the same regional folder unless we later add variant suffix rules.

## Scope Notes

- `rocks-regional` can hold region-linked rock examples, outcrops, or characteristic field specimens.
- `scat-regional` is separate from `tracks-scat` because it is meant for region-specific wildlife sign collections.
- `fossils` and `historical-ruins` do not yet have regional scaffold folders, but we can add them if we decide to regionalize those tracks too.
- `subject-artifacts` stages museum-object expansions by subject lane: philosophy, humanities, politics/civics, exam prep, practical skills, arts/music, geography/history, digital culture, business/finance, maths, and science.
