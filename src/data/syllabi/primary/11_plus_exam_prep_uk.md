# 11+ Exam Prep (UK)
**ID**: `11plusExamPrep` . **Discipline**: Exam Prep . **Year**: 6 (ages 10-11)
**Framework**: UK 11+ Grammar/Independent School Entrance Exams (GL Assessment, CEM, ISEB)

## Course Aim
Prepare upper-primary pupils for selective entrance tests with calm confidence, broad skill, and disciplined exam habits. Students strengthen reading comprehension, vocabulary, grammar, mathematics, verbal reasoning, non-verbal reasoning, spatial reasoning, and timed decision-making while staying rooted in healthy routines and thoughtful reflection. By the end, pupils should be able to approach unfamiliar questions methodically, explain why an answer is correct, recover from mistakes, and use practice papers as learning tools rather than pressure events.

## Course Design Notes
The 11+ varies by area, school, and provider, so this course teaches transferable skills rather than a single paper format. Lessons balance GL Assessment-style verbal, non-verbal, English, and maths practice with CEM-style mixed reasoning, speed, vocabulary breadth, and comprehension stamina. Maths and English draw on upper Key Stage 2 expectations, with selective-school extension through harder multi-step problems, richer texts, tighter timing, and more deliberate error analysis. Every chapter should include accuracy work, timed bursts, untimed strategy discussion, confidence check-ins, and explicit habits for marking, skipping, returning, and learning from mistakes.

## Chapter 1: Diagnostic Strategy and Exam Habits
- **Key concepts**: baseline, accuracy, pace, section timing, instructions, elimination, skip-and-return, answer transfer, reflection.
- **Practice questions**: Complete short mixed sets across English, maths, verbal reasoning, and non-verbal reasoning; compare timed and untimed performance; identify which errors came from knowledge gaps, misreading, rushing, or weak strategy; practise reading instructions before looking at answers.
- **Student output**: A personal exam profile with strengths, priority targets, pacing notes, common error types, and two habits to use in the next timed set.

## Chapter 2: Vocabulary, Word Relationships, and Verbal Reasoning
- **Key concepts**: synonym, antonym, analogy, compound word, homonym, prefix, suffix, root, word class, semantic field.
- **Practice questions**: Match precise synonyms and antonyms; choose the odd word out and justify the link; complete word analogies; build words from prefixes, suffixes, and roots; solve compound-word, hidden-word, and word-pair questions under time limits.
- **Student output**: A vocabulary reasoning notebook page with ten new words, meanings in context, word-family links, two analogy explanations, and one corrected verbal reasoning error.

## Chapter 3: Codes, Sequences, and Logical Deduction
- **Key concepts**: alphabet position, code, pattern, sequence, rule, condition, variable, deduction, contradiction, table.
- **Practice questions**: Crack letter and number codes; continue alphabet and mixed symbol sequences; solve logic-grid and ordering puzzles from clues; identify impossible options; explain the rule behind a sequence rather than only giving the next item.
- **Student output**: A deduction sheet showing one code, one sequence, and one logic puzzle with the working table, eliminated options, final answer, and check.

## Chapter 4: Non-Verbal and Spatial Reasoning
- **Key concepts**: rotation, reflection, symmetry, transformation, matrix, analogy, classification, net, cube, perspective.
- **Practice questions**: Complete shape sequences; identify the odd one out; solve 2 by 2 and 3 by 3 matrices; match shape analogies; visualise rotations, reflections, folds, nets, and cube faces; explain which feature changes and which feature stays fixed.
- **Student output**: A visual reasoning portfolio with labelled examples of sequence, analogy, matrix, odd-one-out, and spatial questions, each annotated with the deciding rule.

## Chapter 5: Number Fluency and Selective Maths Foundations
- **Key concepts**: place value, factor, multiple, prime, square number, fraction, decimal, percentage, ratio, estimate, inverse.
- **Practice questions**: Solve rapid four-operation calculations; simplify and compare fractions; convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages; calculate percentages of quantities; use factors, multiples, primes, squares, and cubes; estimate before calculating and use inverse operations to check.
- **Student output**: A fluency audit with corrected arithmetic, one fraction-decimal-percent conversion set, one factors-and-multiples explanation, and a personal mental-maths routine.

## Chapter 6: Multi-Step Maths, Geometry, and Data
- **Key concepts**: word problem, model, equation, proportion, algebra, area, perimeter, volume, angle, mean, median, range.
- **Practice questions**: Solve multi-step word problems using bar models, tables, or equations; find missing values in sequences and simple algebraic rules; calculate area and perimeter of compound shapes; use angle facts; interpret charts and tables; calculate and compare averages with context.
- **Student output**: A selective maths case file containing three revised problems: one multi-step number problem, one geometry problem, and one data or algebra problem, each with representation, calculation, answer sentence, and reasonableness check.

## Chapter 7: English Comprehension, Grammar, and Writing Precision
- **Key concepts**: inference, evidence, vocabulary in context, summary, viewpoint, tone, authorial choice, grammar, punctuation, syntax.
- **Practice questions**: Read fiction, non-fiction, and older prose extracts; answer retrieval, inference, vocabulary, and evidence questions; identify parts of speech and sentence functions; correct punctuation and spelling errors; explain how language choices shape mood or character.
- **Student output**: A comprehension response set with quoted evidence, two vocabulary-in-context explanations, one grammar correction task, and a short reflection on how the text misled or challenged the reader.

## Chapter 8: Timed Papers, Review Cycles, and Confidence
- **Key concepts**: stamina, pacing, review, error log, resilience, prioritisation, accuracy threshold, confidence, readiness.
- **Practice questions**: Sit mixed timed sections; practise bubbling or answer transfer accurately; rehearse deciding when to move on; complete post-paper reviews; retry missed questions untimed; compare first-attempt, corrected, and later-retry performance.
- **Student output**: A readiness portfolio with timed scores, accuracy-by-topic notes, an error log, three fully corrected questions, and a final exam-day plan for sleep, food, timing, equipment, and self-talk.

## Capstone
Students complete a "Selective Exam Readiness Day" made of four balanced sections: English comprehension and grammar, maths problem solving, verbal reasoning, and non-verbal or spatial reasoning. After the timed work, each student produces a review pack that includes a score profile, three corrected questions with explanations, one pacing decision they would repeat, one strategy they would change, and a realistic two-week revision plan that protects confidence as well as performance.

## Assessment Ideas
- Use short diagnostic sets to separate knowledge gaps from pacing, attention, and instruction-reading issues.
- Track vocabulary growth through word-family notebooks, synonym precision, analogy explanations, and use of context.
- Assess reasoning work for rule identification, elimination of distractors, visual annotation, and checked explanations.
- Score maths for representation choice, calculation accuracy, efficient method, units, answer sentence, and reasonableness.
- Review English responses for evidence, inference, vocabulary-in-context reasoning, grammar accuracy, and concise explanation.
- Include timed and untimed versions of selected tasks so students learn when an error is about skill, speed, or confidence.
- Grade the capstone on balanced preparation, accuracy, pacing, correction quality, reflection, and readiness habits.

- The UK National Curriculum for upper Key Stage 2 supports the English and mathematics foundations expected beneath selective exam extension, including reading, vocabulary, grammar, arithmetic, reasoning, problem solving, geometry, measures, and statistics: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-curriculum-in-england-framework-for-key-stages-1-to-4/the-national-curriculum-in-england-framework-for-key-stages-1-to-4
- Because 11+ formats vary by local authority, consortium, school, and provider, preparation should prioritise transferable reasoning, secure KS2 foundations, calm timed practice, and careful review rather than memorising one fixed paper structure.
