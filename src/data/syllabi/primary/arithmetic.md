# Arithmetic
**ID**: `col-arithmetic` | **Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 2 (ages 6-7)
**Frameworks**: UK National Curriculum primary mathematics, Common Core Number and Operations, Common Core Operations and Algebraic Thinking, NCETM primary mastery guidance

## Course Aim
Build confident, flexible arithmetic through objects, drawings, number talk, and short practice loops. Children learn to see numbers as quantities they can compose, decompose, estimate, compare, and operate on, not just marks to calculate quickly.

## Course Design Notes
Year 2 arithmetic should stay concrete and language-rich. Lessons begin with counters, bead strings, ten frames, base-ten blocks, arrays, number lines, stories, and games before moving to written methods. Fluency is built through calm repetition: a small set of facts, a useful strategy, a quick check, and a chance to explain "how I know."

## Chapter 1: Number Sense and Counting
- **Key concepts**: count on, count back, more, less, equal, odd, even, before, after.
- **Practice activities**: count objects in different arrangements; build numbers with counters; play "one more, one less"; place numbers on a floor number line.
- **Teacher prompts**: How did you count without losing your place? What number comes before? Is there another way to make the same amount?
- **Work product**: a number sense mat showing one chosen number with objects, words, tally marks, and a number line position.

## Chapter 2: Place Value to 100
- **Key concepts**: tens, ones, digit, value, bundle, partition, compare, order.
- **Practice activities**: bundle sticks into tens; build two-digit numbers with base-ten blocks; trade ten ones for one ten; order number cards.
- **Teacher prompts**: What does the 4 mean in 47? How many tens and ones? Which number is greater, and how do you know?
- **Work product**: a place value portrait of three two-digit numbers using blocks, drawings, and expanded form.

## Chapter 3: Addition as Joining and Counting On
- **Key concepts**: add, altogether, sum, count on, make ten, doubles, near doubles.
- **Practice activities**: join two groups of counters; use ten frames to bridge through 10; practise doubles with dominoes; play addition path games.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which number will you start from? Can making 10 help? How can you check the total?
- **Work product**: an addition strategy page showing one problem solved with counters, a drawing, and a number sentence.

## Chapter 4: Subtraction as Taking Away and Difference
- **Key concepts**: subtract, take away, difference, fewer, count back, count up, inverse.
- **Practice activities**: act out subtraction stories; hop back on number lines; count up from the smaller number to find the difference; match subtraction facts to addition checks.
- **Teacher prompts**: Are we taking away or comparing? Could counting up be easier? What addition fact checks this answer?
- **Work product**: a subtraction story strip with objects, jumps on a number line, and an inverse check.

## Chapter 5: Mental Strategies and Fact Families
- **Key concepts**: fact family, inverse, commutative, partition, doubles, halves, near fact.
- **Practice activities**: sort related facts; use known doubles to solve near doubles; split two-digit numbers into tens and ones; play missing-number cards.
- **Teacher prompts**: What fact do you already know? Which two additions and two subtractions belong together? How did you break the number apart?
- **Work product**: a fact family house for three related numbers, with a spoken strategy note.

## Chapter 6: Multiplication and Division Foundations
- **Key concepts**: equal groups, array, repeated addition, share, group, half, double.
- **Practice activities**: build arrays with counters; skip-count in 2s, 5s, and 10s; share objects equally; connect doubles and halves.
- **Teacher prompts**: Are the groups equal? How many rows and columns? What multiplication or division sentence matches the model?
- **Work product**: an equal-groups poster showing repeated addition, an array, and a matching division story.

## Chapter 7: Word Problems and Mathematical Talk
- **Key concepts**: situation, information, question, operation, model, explain, check.
- **Practice activities**: underline the question in a story; choose tools for a problem; retell problems with objects; make up a partner problem.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is happening in the story? What do we need to find out? Does your answer make sense in the story?
- **Work product**: a solved word-problem card with a drawing, number sentence, answer sentence, and check.

## Chapter 8: Estimation, Checking, and Fluency Games
- **Key concepts**: estimate, reasonable, close, exact, quick fact, strategy, revise.
- **Practice activities**: estimate handfuls before counting; choose whether an answer is too high or too low; play low-pressure fact games; practise three-minute mixed strategy loops.
- **Teacher prompts**: What would be a sensible estimate? How do you know the answer is reasonable? Which strategy felt quickest today?
- **Work product**: a fluency journal page with one estimate, one exact answer, and one strategy the child can reuse.

## Capstone
Create a "Number Detective" challenge board. Children design stations for place value, addition, subtraction, equal groups, and a word problem. Each station must include a manipulative model, a number sentence, and a check. Children swap boards with a partner and explain one strategy they used.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe whether children can represent two-digit numbers with objects, drawings, and tens-and-ones language.
- Use short interviews to hear addition and subtraction strategies, including making 10, counting on, counting up, doubles, and inverse checks.
- Review work products for matching models, number sentences, and answer sentences in word problems.
- Use games and low-stakes retrieval to track fluency without making speed the only sign of progress.
- Grade the capstone on clear models, correct operations, reasonable checking, and confident mathematical talk.

## Research Notes
- The UK National Curriculum for primary mathematics emphasizes fluency, reasoning, problem solving, number and place value, and the four operations: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-curriculum-in-england-mathematics-programmes-of-study
- Common Core Grade 2 mathematics includes Operations and Algebraic Thinking and Number and Operations in Base Ten, with attention to fluency, place value, and solving one- and two-step problems: https://www.corestandards.org/Math/Content/2/introduction/
- NCETM primary materials emphasize secure understanding through representations, stem sentences, variation, and connections between facts and strategies: https://www.ncetm.org.uk/classroom-resources/primm-primary-mastery-professional-development/
- Young children benefit from concrete manipulatives, drawings, number lines, arrays, and repeated mathematical talk before moving toward more abstract written methods.
