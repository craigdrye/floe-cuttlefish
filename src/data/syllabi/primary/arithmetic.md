# Arithmetic
**ID**: `col-arithmetic` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 2 (ages 6–7)

## Course Philosophy
Pure arithmetic fluency — speed and accuracy with the four operations. This course complements Class 2 Math by drilling the mechanics. Think of it as the "gym" for mental maths.

## Module 1: Addition Fluency (25%)
- Rapid single-digit addition (all pairs to 9+9)
- Adding a single digit to a two-digit number (34 + 7)
- Adding tens (30 + 50)
- Adding two two-digit numbers mentally (23 + 14)
- Bridging through 10: 8 + 7 → 8 + 2 + 5
- **Questions**: Timed drills, missing addends, "what makes 10?", mental addition chains

## Module 2: Subtraction Fluency (25%)
- Rapid single-digit subtraction
- Subtracting a single digit from a two-digit number (43 − 6)
- Subtracting tens (70 − 30)
- Subtracting two two-digit numbers mentally (45 − 22)
- Counting up as a subtraction strategy: 38 to 45 = 7
- **Questions**: Timed drills, counting-up strategy, difference problems, checking with addition

## Module 3: Multiplication Foundations (25%)
- Repeated addition: 4 + 4 + 4 = 3 × 4
- 2× table: instant recall 2×1 through 2×12
- 5× table: instant recall 5×1 through 5×12
- 10× table: instant recall 10×1 through 10×12
- Doubling numbers up to 50
- **Questions**: Table recall, "how many groups of 5 in 30?", doubling, repeated addition to multiplication

## Module 4: Division Foundations (25%)
- Division as sharing: "Share 15 sweets among 3 children"
- Division as grouping: "How many groups of 2 in 12?"
- Halving numbers up to 100
- Relationship between multiplication and division
- Remainders as a concept: "13 ÷ 2 = 6 remainder 1"
- **Questions**: Sharing word problems, grouping, halving, "which divides exactly?"

## Stretch Zone
- Mixed operations in sequence (5 + 3 × 2 — without BODMAS, just sequenced)
- 3× and 4× tables introduction
- Doubling and halving two-digit numbers
- Estimation: "Is 48 + 33 closer to 70 or 90?"
