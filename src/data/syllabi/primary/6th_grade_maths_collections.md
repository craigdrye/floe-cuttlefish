# 6th Grade Maths Collections
**Covers**: `col-6th-grade-math`, `col-6th-grade-ny-next-gen`, `col-get-ready-for-6th-grade`, `col-eureka-math-foundations-3rd-8th`, `col-eureka-math-3rd-8th`
**Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 6 (ages 10-11)
**Frameworks**: Common Core Grade 6 Mathematics, NY Next Generation Grade 6 Mathematics, UK National Curriculum KS2 to KS3 transition, NCETM primary and secondary mastery guidance

## Course Aim
Prepare students for secondary mathematics by turning strong primary number sense into flexible, symbolic reasoning. Students use ratios, rates, integers, rational numbers, expressions, equations, coordinate graphs, geometry, and statistics to solve problems, explain structure, and check results. By the end, they should move confidently between visual models, tables, graphs, equations, and written arguments.

## Course Design Notes
Grade 6 is a bridge year: concrete models still matter, but students should increasingly select and defend their own representations. Lessons use double number lines, ratio tables, tape diagrams, number lines, algebra tiles, balance models, coordinate grids, nets, area models, dot plots, histograms, and box plots. Fluency routines should revisit fraction, decimal, integer, and equation skills while mixed problems build stamina for secondary-style reasoning.

## Chapter 1: Ratios, Rates, and Percent Reasoning
- **Key concepts**: ratio, part-to-part, part-to-whole, equivalent ratio, rate, unit rate, percent, per 100, proportion.
- **Practice questions**: Describe ratio relationships in words and symbols; build equivalent ratio tables; use double number lines and tape diagrams; solve unit price, speed, recipe, and measurement-conversion problems; find percents of quantities and find the whole from a part and percent.
- **Student output**: A ratio reasoning sheet connecting one context to a tape diagram, double number line, ratio table, unit-rate calculation, and percent explanation.

## Chapter 2: Fraction and Decimal Operations
- **Key concepts**: quotient, reciprocal idea, fraction division, standard algorithm, decimal operation, common factor, common multiple, distributive property.
- **Practice questions**: Divide fractions by fractions using visual models and equations; divide multi-digit whole numbers fluently; add, subtract, multiply, and divide multi-digit decimals; find GCF and LCM; use the distributive property to rewrite sums with common factors.
- **Student output**: A number-system audit with one fraction-division model, one decimal operation set, one GCF or LCM task, and an explanation of how the distributive property reveals structure.

## Chapter 3: Integers, Rational Numbers, and the Coordinate Plane
- **Key concepts**: integer, rational number, opposite, absolute value, number line, quadrant, ordered pair, sign, distance.
- **Practice questions**: Use positive and negative numbers for temperature, elevation, money, and score contexts; order rational numbers; interpret absolute value as distance from zero; graph points in all four quadrants; find horizontal and vertical distances on the coordinate plane.
- **Student output**: An integer and coordinate page with a real-world signed-number context, an ordering problem, an absolute-value explanation, and a four-quadrant graph.

## Chapter 4: Expressions, Exponents, and Equivalent Forms
- **Key concepts**: variable, expression, exponent, term, coefficient, factor, product, sum, equivalent expression, substitution.
- **Practice questions**: Write and evaluate expressions with variables and whole-number exponents; identify parts of expressions; substitute values into formulas; apply properties of operations to create equivalent expressions; use the distributive property to expand and factor simple expressions.
- **Student output**: An expression notebook page with a translated expression, an exponent example, a formula substitution, two equivalent forms, and a short note explaining why they match.

## Chapter 5: Equations, Inequalities, and Relationships
- **Key concepts**: equation, solution, balance, inverse operation, inequality, constraint, independent variable, dependent variable, graph.
- **Practice questions**: Solve one-step equations of the forms x + p = q and px = q with nonnegative rational numbers; write equations from contexts; test whether values make equations or inequalities true; graph simple inequalities on number lines; represent relationships between two variables in tables, graphs, and equations.
- **Student output**: An algebra bridge page with a context equation, a solved equation, an inequality graph, and one relationship shown as a table, graph, and equation.

## Chapter 6: Area, Volume, Nets, and Surface Area
- **Key concepts**: area, triangle, parallelogram, polygon, compose, decompose, fractional edge length, volume, net, surface area.
- **Practice questions**: Find areas of triangles, special quadrilaterals, and polygons by composing and decomposing shapes; find volume of rectangular prisms with fractional edge lengths; draw nets made from rectangles and triangles; calculate surface area from nets; explain units for area and volume.
- **Student output**: A geometry case file with one decomposed area problem, one fractional-volume calculation, one net, one surface-area calculation, and labeled units.

## Chapter 7: Statistics, Distribution, and Data Displays
- **Key concepts**: statistical question, variability, distribution, center, spread, mean, median, interquartile range, mean absolute deviation, histogram, box plot.
- **Practice questions**: Decide whether a question is statistical; collect or inspect numerical data; display data using dot plots, histograms, and box plots; describe shape, center, and spread; calculate mean, median, IQR, and mean absolute deviation when appropriate; connect conclusions to context.
- **Student output**: A statistics mini-report with a statistical question, a data display, measures of center and spread, and a written claim that acknowledges variability.

## Chapter 8: Secondary Maths Readiness Studio
- **Key concepts**: representation choice, multi-step reasoning, mixed review, precision, model, table, graph, equation, justification, revision.
- **Practice questions**: Solve unfamiliar mixed-topic problems; choose among diagrams, tables, graphs, equations, and written reasoning; compare solution strategies; revise incorrect work; write concise explanations using units and mathematical vocabulary; connect ratio, integer, algebra, geometry, and statistics ideas in one task.
- **Student output**: A readiness portfolio with three revised problems: one ratio or percent problem, one algebra or coordinate problem, and one geometry or statistics problem, each with a check and reflection.

## Capstone
Students create a "Secondary Maths Readiness Project" around a school enterprise, sports analytics board, community map, design challenge, or data investigation. The final product must include ratio or rate reasoning, a percent calculation, integer or rational-number reasoning, an expression or equation, a coordinate graph, a geometry calculation using area, volume, or surface area, a statistical display, and a concise explanation of how the work was checked and improved.

## Assessment Ideas
- Use short fluency checks for ratios, unit rates, percent, fraction division, decimal operations, integer order, and one-step equations.
- Review student models for correct use of double number lines, tape diagrams, number lines, coordinate grids, balance models, nets, and statistical displays.
- Score mixed problems for representation choice, calculation accuracy, units, interpretation, and written justification.
- Include error-analysis tasks for percent-of-whole confusion, sign errors, variable meaning, surface-area omissions, and misleading data conclusions.
- Assess readiness through increasingly independent work: students should select tools, explain assumptions, revise after feedback, and check reasonableness.
- Grade the capstone on mathematical accuracy, connection across topics, representation quality, secondary-ready notation, explanation, revision, and independence.

## Research Notes
- Common Core Grade 6 mathematics emphasizes ratios and rates, fraction division, rational numbers, expressions and equations, geometry, and statistics as a foundation for middle-school mathematics: https://www.corestandards.org/Math/Content/6/introduction/
- The UK National Curriculum supports the KS2 to KS3 transition through fluency, reasoning, problem solving, ratio and proportion, algebra, geometry, measures, and statistics: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-curriculum-in-england-mathematics-programmes-of-study
- NCETM mastery guidance emphasizes coherent representations, precise language, variation, connected facts, and transition-ready reasoning across primary and secondary mathematics: https://www.ncetm.org.uk/classroom-resources/primm-primary-mastery-professional-development/
- Students moving into secondary maths benefit from explicit links between concrete models and symbols, distributed fluency practice, worked-example discussion, and mixed review that asks them to choose representations independently.
