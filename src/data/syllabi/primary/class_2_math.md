# Class 2 Math
**ID**: `col-class-2-math` | **Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 2 (ages 6-7)
**Frameworks**: UK KS1 Year 2 National Curriculum, US Common Core Grade 2, ACARA v9 Year 2, India NCERT Class 2

## Course Aim
Extend primary number confidence into flexible two-digit thinking. Children learn to compose and decompose numbers, add and subtract with strategies they can explain, build early multiplication and division through equal groups, measure with standard units, describe shapes, represent data, and solve word problems with drawings and talk.

## Course Design Notes
Class 2 maths remains manipulatives-first while moving children toward more efficient recording. Lessons use base-ten blocks, bead strings, number lines, hundred charts, arrays, counters, rulers, clocks, coins, pattern blocks, tally charts, and quick sketches. Practice is short and frequent: a mental warm-up, a concrete model, a drawing, a written method, a partner explanation, and a check.

## Chapter 1: Place Value to 100 and Beyond
- **Key concepts**: hundreds, tens, ones, digit, value, partition, compare, order, estimate.
- **Practice activities**: build two- and three-digit numbers with base-ten blocks; trade ten ones for one ten; place numbers on a hundred chart; order and compare number cards.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is the value of each digit? How can you partition this number? Which number is greater, and what proves it?
- **Work product**: a place-value portrait showing three numbers with blocks, expanded form, comparison signs, and a number line position.

## Chapter 2: Addition Strategies Within 100
- **Key concepts**: sum, count on, make ten, bridge, partition, regroup, inverse, efficient.
- **Practice activities**: add two-digit numbers with base-ten blocks; use open number lines; make 10 or make the next ten; add three single-digit numbers by grouping helpful pairs.
- **Teacher prompts**: Could you make a ten first? How did you split the number? Which strategy is clearest for this problem?
- **Work product**: an addition strategy page solving one problem in two ways, with a concrete model, a drawing, and a written check.

## Chapter 3: Subtraction Strategies Within 100
- **Key concepts**: subtract, difference, count back, count up, exchange, inverse, compare.
- **Practice activities**: subtract with base-ten blocks; exchange one ten for ten ones; count up to find a difference; use addition to check subtraction.
- **Teacher prompts**: Are we taking away or finding the difference? Do we need to exchange? What addition fact checks the answer?
- **Work product**: a subtraction explanation strip with a model, jumps on a number line, a number sentence, and an inverse check.

## Chapter 4: Equal Groups, Multiplication, and Division
- **Key concepts**: equal group, array, repeated addition, multiply, share, group, divide, odd, even.
- **Practice activities**: make arrays with counters; skip-count in 2s, 3s, 5s, and 10s; share objects equally; sort odd and even numbers; match multiplication and division stories.
- **Teacher prompts**: Are the groups equal? How many rows and columns? What repeated addition sentence matches this array?
- **Work product**: an equal-groups poster showing one array, one repeated addition, one multiplication sentence, and one sharing story.

## Chapter 5: Fractions and Fair Shares
- **Key concepts**: half, quarter, third, three quarters, equal parts, whole, fraction of a shape, fraction of a quantity.
- **Practice activities**: fold paper shapes into equal parts; share counters into halves, thirds, and quarters; shade fractions of grids; compare two quarters with one half.
- **Teacher prompts**: Are the parts equal? What is the whole? How can you show one half of this amount?
- **Work product**: a fair-share mat with a shape fraction, a quantity fraction, and one sentence explaining equal parts.

## Chapter 6: Measurement, Time, and Money
- **Key concepts**: centimetre, metre, gram, kilogram, millilitre, litre, o'clock, half past, quarter past, quarter to, five-minute interval, coin, total, change.
- **Practice activities**: measure classroom objects with rulers; compare mass and capacity; read clocks to five-minute intervals; make amounts with coins; solve simple shop problems.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which unit makes sense? Where should the ruler start? How much time has passed? Do you have enough money?
- **Work product**: a practical measurement record with an estimate, a measured value, a clock time, and a money total.

## Chapter 7: Shape, Pattern, and Data
- **Key concepts**: side, vertex, face, edge, symmetry, pattern, tally, pictogram, bar chart, category.
- **Practice activities**: sort 2D and 3D shapes by properties; build symmetrical designs; continue and describe patterns; collect class data with tally marks; make pictograms and simple bar charts.
- **Teacher prompts**: What property did you sort by? Does the line of symmetry make matching halves? What does the graph tell us?
- **Work product**: a shape-and-data page with one sorted shape group, one symmetrical drawing, and one small chart with a conclusion.

## Chapter 8: Word Problems and Mathematical Reasoning
- **Key concepts**: problem, operation, model, equation, answer sentence, two-step, reasonableness, explain.
- **Practice activities**: act out one- and two-step problems; underline the question; choose tools before calculating; draw bar models or quick pictures; write partner problems.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is happening first? Which operation fits this part? How will you check that the answer makes sense?
- **Work product**: a solved two-step word problem with a drawing, equations, answer sentence, and check.

## Capstone
Create a "Class 2 Maths Fair" challenge booth. Each child or team prepares activities for place value, two-digit addition or subtraction, equal groups, measurement, shape or data, and a word problem. Every activity must include a manipulative model, a drawing or chart, a written number sentence, and a spoken explanation of the strategy used.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe whether children represent and compare two-digit numbers using tens-and-ones language, drawings, and expanded form.
- Use short interviews to hear addition and subtraction strategies, including partitioning, making ten, exchange, counting up, and inverse checks.
- Check early multiplication and division through arrays, equal groups, sharing, and skip-counting rather than isolated fact recall only.
- Review measurement, shape, and data work for accurate unit choice, property language, clear charts, and reasonable conclusions.
- Grade the capstone on mathematical models, strategy explanation, correct recording, checking, and confident participation.
