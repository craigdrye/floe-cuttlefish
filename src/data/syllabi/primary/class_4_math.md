# Class 4 Math
**ID**: `col-class-4-math` | **Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 4 (ages 8-9)
**Frameworks**: UK KS2 Year 4 National Curriculum, US Common Core Grade 4, ACARA v9 Year 4, India NCERT Class 4

## Course Aim
Extend primary maths confidence into larger numbers, more efficient written methods, secure multiplication facts, and richer problem solving. Children use place value to calculate with four-digit numbers, reason about factors and multiples, connect fractions and decimals, measure and convert units, classify shapes, interpret data, and explain multi-step problems with diagrams, equations, estimates, and checks.

## Course Design Notes
Class 4 lessons keep manipulatives active while helping children select efficient written strategies. Base-ten equipment, place-value charts, counters, arrays, multiplication grids, fraction strips, decimal grids, measuring tools, angle testers, geoboards, coordinates, and graphs support every new idea. Number talk is daily: children compare strategies, rehearse precise vocabulary, use sentence stems, and learn that a clear model is often the first step toward a reliable written method.

## Chapter 1: Place Value, Rounding, and Negative Numbers
- **Key concepts**: thousands, hundreds, tens, ones, digit value, partition, compare, order, round, estimate, negative number, Roman numeral.
- **Practice activities**: build and rename four-digit numbers; place numbers on open number lines; round to the nearest 10, 100, and 1000; count in steps of 25, 100, and 1000; use temperature and lifts to cross zero; read Roman numerals to 100.
- **Teacher prompts**: What does this digit represent? Which benchmark is nearest? What happens when the count passes through zero?
- **Work product**: a place-value reasoning page showing one four-digit number in multiple forms, two rounded values, a negative-number sequence, and a Roman numeral match.

## Chapter 2: Multi-Digit Addition and Subtraction
- **Key concepts**: column method, regroup, exchange, estimate, inverse, difference, efficient strategy, multi-step.
- **Practice activities**: add and subtract numbers with up to four digits using place-value counters and column layout; decide when mental methods are more efficient; estimate totals and differences; solve two-step money, distance, and population problems.
- **Teacher prompts**: What needs to be exchanged? Does the estimate fit the final answer? Which part of the story happens first?
- **Work product**: a calculation case file with two four-digit calculations, an estimate for each, an inverse check, and a written explanation of strategy choice.

## Chapter 3: Multiplication Facts, Factors, and Multiples
- **Key concepts**: factor, multiple, product, array, square number, commutative, distributive, related fact, fluent recall.
- **Practice activities**: practise facts to 12 by 12 with arrays and quick recall; identify factor pairs; sort multiples of 6, 7, 8, 9, 11, and 12; use known facts to solve related facts such as 7 x 8, 70 x 8, and 700 x 8.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which factor pair do you see? What related fact helps? How can the array prove two multiplication facts?
- **Work product**: a factor-and-multiple map for one chosen number, including arrays, factor pairs, multiples, related facts, and a short explanation.

## Chapter 4: Multiplication and Division Written Methods
- **Key concepts**: partition, short multiplication, short division, quotient, remainder, scaling, distributive property, check.
- **Practice activities**: multiply two- and three-digit numbers by one-digit numbers with grid and short methods; divide two- and three-digit numbers by one digit using sharing, grouping, and short division; interpret remainders in real contexts; check division with multiplication.
- **Teacher prompts**: How can partitioning make this calculation visible? What does the remainder mean here? Which multiplication fact checks the division?
- **Work product**: an operations comparison page showing one multiplication and one division solved with a model, written method, and check.

## Chapter 5: Fractions, Equivalence, and Mixed Numbers
- **Key concepts**: numerator, denominator, unit fraction, non-unit fraction, equivalent fraction, mixed number, improper fraction, same denominator.
- **Practice activities**: use fraction strips and number lines to compare fractions; make equivalent fractions with diagrams; count in fractions beyond 1; add and subtract fractions with the same denominator; solve fraction-of-quantity problems with counters and bar models.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is the whole? How do the diagrams prove equivalence? Is the answer less than, equal to, or greater than 1?
- **Work product**: a fraction reasoning card with an equivalent pair, a mixed-number number line, a same-denominator calculation, and a fraction-of-quantity problem.

## Chapter 6: Decimals, Money, and Measurement Conversions
- **Key concepts**: tenth, hundredth, decimal point, place value, equivalent decimal, pounds, pence, kilometre, metre, hour, minute.
- **Practice activities**: shade tenths and hundredths grids; match 1/4, 1/2, and 3/4 to decimal equivalents; divide numbers by 10 and 100 with place-value charts; solve money problems with pounds and pence; convert between common metric and time units.
- **Teacher prompts**: What does each digit after the decimal point mean? How can the grid prove the decimal? Which unit makes the comparison fair?
- **Work product**: a decimal-and-measurement mat with a shaded decimal grid, fraction-decimal match, money calculation, and two unit conversions.

## Chapter 7: Geometry, Angles, Area, and Coordinates
- **Key concepts**: triangle, quadrilateral, polygon, acute angle, obtuse angle, right angle, symmetry, perimeter, area, coordinate, first quadrant.
- **Practice activities**: classify triangles and quadrilaterals by properties; compare angles using right-angle testers; find lines of symmetry; calculate perimeter of rectilinear shapes; find area by counting squares; plot and read coordinates in the first quadrant.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which properties prove the shape name? Is the angle smaller or larger than a right angle? How can we count area without missing squares?
- **Work product**: a geometry investigation with a shape sort, angle labels, an area-and-perimeter task, and a coordinate picture.

## Chapter 8: Data, Graphs, and Problem Solving
- **Key concepts**: table, bar chart, pictogram, line graph, scale, interval, compare, multi-step, model, justify.
- **Practice activities**: collect measurement or class survey data; choose an appropriate graph; read scales and intervals carefully; interpret time graphs; solve multi-step problems using bar models, tables, equations, estimates, and written explanations.
- **Teacher prompts**: What does the scale count by? Which graph fits this data? What information do we need before calculating?
- **Work product**: a problem-solving report with one graph, three questions answered from the data, one multi-step calculation, and a justification of the chosen method.

## Capstone
Create a "Class 4 Maths Design Lab" for a small school garden, classroom layout, or mini-event. Teams choose dimensions, costs, quantities, schedules, and visitor data. The final display must include four-digit calculations, multiplication or division with checks, factors or multiples, a fraction or decimal decision, measurement conversions, geometry with area or perimeter, a graph, and a written explanation of one multi-step problem.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe whether children use place value language accurately for four-digit numbers, rounding, negative numbers, and decimal tenths or hundredths.
- Use short interviews to hear why a child chose a mental method, column method, grid method, short multiplication, or short division.
- Check multiplication fluency through facts, arrays, factor pairs, related facts, and application, not only timed recall.
- Review fraction and decimal work for correct whole identification, equivalence, number-line sense, same-denominator operations, and links to money or measures.
- Assess geometry, measurement, and data through property language, tool use, unit conversion, scale reading, coordinate accuracy, and interpretation.
- Grade the capstone on mathematical accuracy, representation quality, efficient strategy choice, checking, communication, and teamwork.
