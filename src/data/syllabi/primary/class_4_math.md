# Class 4 Math
**ID**: `col-class-4-math` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 4 (ages 8–9)
**Frameworks**: UK KS2 Year 4, US Common Core Grade 4, ACARA v9 Year 4, India NCERT Class 4

## Course Philosophy
Year 4 is defined by the completion of foundational arithmetic fluency. In the UK, this culminates in the statutory Multiplication Tables Check (MTC). Across all frameworks, the focus shifts to larger numbers (up to 10,000 or beyond), formal written methods for all four operations, and a significant expansion into fractions and decimals.

## Module 1: Number & Place Value (20%)
- Read, write, order, and compare numbers to at least 10,000 (US: 1,000,000)
- Recognise the place value of each digit in a four-digit number (thousands, hundreds, tens, ones)
- Count in multiples of 6, 7, 9, 25, and 1000
- Round any number to the nearest 10, 100, or 1000
- Count backwards through zero to include negative numbers
- Read Roman numerals to 100 (I to C)
- **Questions**: Place value grids, rounding scenarios, negative number sequences, Roman numeral conversion

## Module 2: The Four Operations (25%)
- Add and subtract numbers with up to 4 digits using formal columnar methods
- Estimate and use inverse operations to check answers
- Multiply 2-digit and 3-digit numbers by a 1-digit number using formal written layout
- Divide 2-digit and 3-digit numbers by a 1-digit number (introducing short division/bus stop method)
- Solve two-step addition and subtraction problems in context
- **Questions**: Column addition/subtraction, short multiplication, short division, word problems

## Module 3: Times Tables Mastery (15%)
- Fluently recall multiplication and division facts up to 12 × 12
- Prepare for the Multiplication Tables Check (MTC format: 6 seconds per question, no division)
- Use place value to multiply and divide mentally: e.g., if 6 × 7 = 42, then 60 × 7 = 420
- **Questions**: Rapid recall (especially 6, 7, 8, 9, 12 tables), related division facts, multiplying by 10/100

## Module 4: Fractions & Decimals (20%)
- Recognise and show equivalent fractions using diagrams
- Add and subtract fractions with the same denominator (e.g., 5/8 + 2/8)
- Recognise fractions greater than 1 (mixed numbers)
- Count up and down in hundredths
- Recognise and write decimal equivalents of any number of tenths or hundredths
- Recognise decimal equivalents to 1/4, 1/2, 3/4
- Divide a one- or two-digit number by 10 and 100 (shifting digits)
- **Questions**: Fraction equivalence, decimal-fraction matching, dividing by 10/100, adding fractions

## Module 5: Measurement, Geometry & Statistics (20%)
- Convert between different units of measure (e.g., kilometres to metres; hours to minutes)
- Calculate the perimeter of a rectilinear figure in cm and m
- Find the area of rectilinear shapes by counting squares
- Compare and classify geometric shapes, including quadrilaterals and triangles (isosceles, equilateral, scalene)
- Identify acute and obtuse angles; compare and order angles by size
- Identify lines of symmetry in 2D shapes
- Describe positions on a 2D grid as coordinates in the first quadrant
- Interpret and present continuous data using appropriate graphical methods, including time graphs
- **Questions**: Area/perimeter calculation, angle classification, coordinate plotting, unit conversions

## Stretch Zone
- Multiply 2-digit by 2-digit numbers
- Add and subtract fractions with different denominators (simple cases like 1/2 + 1/4)
- Calculate area using formula (L × W)
- Interpret line graphs involving temperature or distance over time
