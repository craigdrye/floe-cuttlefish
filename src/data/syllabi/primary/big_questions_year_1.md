# Big Questions: Year 1
**ID**: `philosophyYear1` · **Discipline**: Humanities · **Year**: 1 (ages 5–6)

## Course Philosophy
Building on the Early Years P4C foundation, Year 1 philosophy moves children from personal feelings toward **reasoning about the world around them**. The shift is from "how do I feel?" to "what do I think and why?" Children begin to give reasons, consider alternatives, and respectfully disagree.

## Module 1: Rules & Responsibility (20%)
- Why do we have rules at school and at home?
- What makes a good rule vs an unfair rule?
- Who makes the rules? Can children help make rules?
- Responsibility: looking after your things, your classroom, your pet
- Consequences: what happens when rules are broken?
- **Questions**: Rule judgment scenarios, responsibility matching, "is this rule fair?"

## Module 2: Friendship & Kindness (20%)
- What makes someone a good friend?
- Can you be friends with someone who is very different from you?
- Inclusion: what to do when someone is left out
- The difference between being kind and being nice (kindness can be hard)
- Forgiveness: when a friend makes a mistake
- **Questions**: Scenario judgment, friendship quality identification, inclusion strategies

## Module 3: Truth & Honesty (20%)
- Why is telling the truth important?
- Is it ever hard to tell the truth? Why?
- The difference between a lie, a mistake, and a guess
- "White lies" — telling grandma you love the socks
- Trust: what happens when someone lies a lot?
- **Questions**: Truth vs lie identification, dilemma scenarios, trust reasoning

## Module 4: The Natural World (20%)
- Do animals have feelings?
- Should we look after the environment? Why?
- Is it okay to pick flowers? Catch butterflies? Keep animals in zoos?
- What do we owe to other living things?
- Connecting actions to environmental impact (litter, water waste)
- **Questions**: Ethical reasoning about animals and nature, consequence prediction

## Module 5: Thinking Skills (20%)
- Giving reasons: "I think X because Y"
- Listening to someone else's idea and responding
- Changing your mind when you hear a good reason
- The difference between opinion and fact
- Agreeing and disagreeing politely
- **Questions**: Opinion-with-reason prompts, fact vs opinion sorting, "do you agree? why?"

## Stretch Zone
- Introduction to thought experiments: "What if everyone in the world was the same?"
- Exploring the concept of rights: "Do children have rights? What should they be?"
- Philosophy of ownership: "If you find something, is it yours?"
- Gratitude and perspective: "What would life be like without X?"
