# Big Questions: Year 4
**ID**: `philosophyYear4` · **Discipline**: Humanities · **Year**: 4 (ages 8–9)

## Course Philosophy
Year 4 philosophy deepens students' ability to engage in ethical reasoning and introduces fundamental concepts of epistemology and political philosophy. Students are encouraged to look beyond immediate consequences and consider intentions, systemic fairness, and the nature of truth.

## Module 1: Ethics & Intentions (20%)
- Does the outcome matter more than the intention? (Accidentally breaking something vs. purposefully breaking something)
- "White lies" revisited: Is it ever morally required to lie? (e.g., hiding someone from danger)
- The concept of fairness vs. equality in complex scenarios (e.g., handicaps in sports, progressive taxation concepts simplified)
- Forgiveness: What does it mean to forgive? Do you have to forgive someone who isn't sorry?
- **Questions**: Evaluating intentions vs outcomes, moral dilemmas, fairness scenarios

## Module 2: Truth & Media (20%)
- How do we know what is true on the internet or in the news?
- The difference between facts, opinions, and "fake news"
- Bias: Understanding that everyone has a point of view
- The importance of evidence and reliable sources
- **Questions**: Identifying bias, fact vs opinion, evaluating source reliability

## Module 3: Wealth & Poverty (20%)
- Why are some people rich and others poor?
- Is it fair that wealth is unequally distributed?
- What are our responsibilities to those who have less?
- The difference between charity and justice
- Needs vs. wants on a global scale
- **Questions**: Distributive justice scenarios, charity vs systemic change concepts

## Module 4: Human Nature & Society (20%)
- Are humans naturally good or naturally selfish?
- Why do societies need laws and governments?
- What would happen if there were no rules for a year? (State of nature concepts)
- Freedom vs. Security: When is it okay for rules to limit freedom to keep people safe?
- **Questions**: State of nature thought experiments, evaluating trade-offs between freedom and security

## Module 5: Art, Beauty & Value (20%)
- What makes something "art"?
- Is beauty in the eye of the beholder, or are some things universally beautiful?
- Why do humans create art and music?
- Does something have to be useful to be valuable?
- **Questions**: Defining art, subjective vs objective value, the purpose of creativity

## Stretch Zone
- Introduction to the veil of ignorance (John Rawls) simplified: "How would you design a fair game if you didn't know which player you would be?"
- Epistemology: "How do you know you're not dreaming right now?"
- Animal rights vs. human needs (e.g., medical testing)
