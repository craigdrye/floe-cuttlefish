# NAPLAN Prep (Year 3)
**ID**: `naplanYear3` | **Discipline**: Exam Prep | **Year**: 2 (shown in Year 2 as prep for the Year 3 NAPLAN test)
**Framework**: Australian NAPLAN (National Assessment Program - Literacy and Numeracy)

## Course Aim
Build calm, capable Year 3 NAPLAN readiness through everyday literacy and numeracy habits rather than last-minute test cramming. Students practise reading, writing, language conventions, and numeracy in short, varied tasks that strengthen confidence, explain thinking, notice errors, and use familiar computer-based item types without making the test feel bigger than the learning.

## Course Design Notes
This course is designed for Year 2 learners preparing for the Year 3 NAPLAN assessment in March of Year 3. Lessons should feel steady, practical, and encouraging: short texts, modelled thinking, partner talk, mini whiteboards, number lines, manipulatives, shared editing, and low-stakes digital practice. NAPLAN-style items are used as a format to practise clear thinking, not as pressure. Teachers should name strategies explicitly: read the question twice, underline evidence, estimate first, check one thing at a time, and move on when stuck.

## Chapter 1: Reading for Literal Meaning
**Key concepts**: fiction, non-fiction, title, heading, character, setting, event, detail, sequence, evidence.

**Practice questions**
- What happened first, next, and last in this short text?
- Which sentence tells us where the story or information is set?
- What detail from the text proves your answer?

**Student output**
- A reading evidence card with a short answer, the matching sentence or detail from the text, and one checked multiple-choice item.

## Chapter 2: Reading for Inference and Vocabulary
**Key concepts**: inference, clue, feeling, reason, prediction, vocabulary, context, synonym, phrase, meaning.

**Practice questions**
- How might the character be feeling, and which clue helped you?
- What does this word most likely mean in the sentence?
- Why did the author include this detail?

**Student output**
- An inference-and-vocabulary notebook page showing one feeling inference, one word meaning from context, and one explanation using "I think... because..."

## Chapter 3: Text Features and Visual Information
**Key concepts**: caption, label, diagram, map, table, bold word, list, image, symbol, information.

**Practice questions**
- What extra information does the diagram, picture, or caption give?
- Which heading would help you find this information quickly?
- How do the words and picture work together?

**Student output**
- A labelled mini information page with a heading, caption, diagram or table, and two questions another student can answer from it.

## Chapter 4: Planning and Writing Short Narratives
**Key concepts**: prompt, plan, character, setting, problem, beginning, middle, end, sentence, detail, audience.

**Practice questions**
- Who is in the story, where are they, and what problem happens?
- What detail could make the beginning clearer for a reader?
- Does the ending solve the problem in a simple, satisfying way?

**Student output**
- A one-page narrative plan and short story draft with a clear beginning, middle, and end, plus one revision that adds detail.

## Chapter 5: Giving Opinions and Reasons
**Key concepts**: opinion, reason, example, persuade, because, audience, sentence, paragraph, conclusion.

**Practice questions**
- What is your opinion about the prompt?
- Which reason is strongest, and what example supports it?
- How can you end so the reader remembers your idea?

**Student output**
- A short persuasive paragraph with an opinion, two reasons, one example, and a final sentence, written in a calm and respectful voice.

## Chapter 6: Spelling, Grammar, and Punctuation Checks
**Key concepts**: spelling pattern, phoneme, homophone, noun, verb, adjective, pronoun, capital letter, full stop, question mark, comma, apostrophe.

**Practice questions**
- Which word is spelt correctly in this sentence?
- Which word best completes the sentence?
- Where should the capital letter, full stop, question mark, comma, or apostrophe go?

**Student output**
- An editing checklist applied to five sentences, showing corrected spelling, grammar, and punctuation with one explanation of the change.

## Chapter 7: Number, Operations, and Fractions
**Key concepts**: place value, hundreds, tens, ones, compare, order, addition, subtraction, multiplication, equal groups, half, quarter, third.

**Practice questions**
- What is the value of each digit in this three-digit number?
- Which operation matches the story problem?
- How can a drawing or number line help you check the answer?

**Student output**
- A numeracy strategy sheet with one place-value task, one addition or subtraction problem, one equal-groups problem, and one fraction drawing.

## Chapter 8: Measurement, Geometry, Data, and Mixed Problems
**Key concepts**: centimetre, metre, gram, kilogram, millilitre, litre, time, shape, symmetry, pictogram, bar chart, table, pattern.

**Practice questions**
- Which unit makes sense for this measurement?
- What does this graph, table, or pictogram show?
- What is the next step in the pattern, and how do you know?

**Student output**
- A mixed-problem mini portfolio with a measurement choice, a shape or symmetry explanation, a data question, and a pattern answer with reasoning.

## Capstone
Create a "NAPLAN Confidence Quest" booklet. Each student chooses a short reading text, writes three fair questions about it, drafts either a tiny narrative or persuasive response, edits a sentence set, and designs four numeracy questions using pictures, number lines, shapes, or data. The booklet ends with a personal strategy page: what to do before answering, what to do when unsure, and how to check work kindly.

## Assessment Ideas
- Use short reading conferences to check literal comprehension, inference from clues, vocabulary in context, and evidence-based answers.
- Review writing drafts for clear planning, sentence control, topic focus, audience awareness, and simple revision rather than length alone.
- Assess language conventions through brief editing tasks that require students to explain one spelling, grammar, or punctuation choice.
- Check numeracy work for place-value understanding, sensible operation choice, use of models, unit awareness, and reasonableness checks.
- Include occasional NAPLAN-style computer-based items so students practise the interface calmly, including selecting, typing, dragging, and moving between questions.
- Grade the capstone on accuracy, clarity, variety of item types, evidence of checking, and the student's ability to name helpful strategies.
