# Class 5 Math
**ID**: `col-class-5-math` | **Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 5 (ages 9-10)
**Frameworks**: UK KS2 Year 5 National Curriculum, US Common Core Grade 5, ACARA v9 Year 5, India NCERT Class 5

## Course Aim
Develop upper-primary mathematicians who can work with larger numbers, choose efficient methods, and explain why their answers make sense. Children extend place value to 1,000,000, strengthen written and mental operations, connect fractions with decimals and percentages, measure and convert units, reason about angles and shapes, interpret data, and solve multi-step problems with diagrams, equations, estimates, and checks.

## Course Design Notes
Class 5 maths stays concrete while asking children to become more independent. Lessons use place-value counters, grid paper, bar models, number lines, area models, fraction strips, decimal grids, measuring tools, protractors, coordinate grids, tables, and graphs. Number talk is frequent and calm: learners compare strategies, rehearse vocabulary, explain errors, and practise fluency in short bursts so accuracy and confidence grow without speed panic.

## Chapter 1: Place Value to One Million
- **Key concepts**: million, hundred thousand, ten thousand, digit value, partition, compare, order, round, estimate, negative number.
- **Practice activities**: build and rename six-digit numbers; compare and order numbers using place-value language; round to the nearest 10, 100, 1000, 10,000, and 100,000; count forwards and backwards in powers of 10; place positive and negative numbers on temperature and elevation lines.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is this digit worth? Which benchmark is nearest? How can the number line prove the order? What changes when the count crosses zero?
- **Work product**: a place-value reasoning page showing one large number in multiple forms, rounded values, a comparison, a negative-number sequence, and an estimate check.

## Chapter 2: Addition, Subtraction, and Multi-Step Choices
- **Key concepts**: column method, regroup, exchange, sum, difference, inverse, estimate, efficient strategy, multi-step problem.
- **Practice activities**: add and subtract numbers with more than four digits using place-value counters and column layout; choose mental, written, or calculator-free checking strategies; estimate before calculating; solve money, distance, population, and timetable problems with two or more steps.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which operation happens first? What estimate would catch a mistake? Could a mental strategy be clearer than a column method here? How does the inverse check the answer?
- **Work product**: a calculation decision sheet with two multi-digit calculations, one multi-step context problem, estimates, inverse checks, and a short note explaining method choice.

## Chapter 3: Multiplication, Division, Factors, and Primes
- **Key concepts**: factor, multiple, factor pair, prime, composite, square number, cube number, product, quotient, remainder.
- **Practice activities**: list factor pairs and common factors; sort primes and composites to 100; use known facts for mental multiplication and division; multiply up to four digits by one- and two-digit numbers; divide up to four digits by one digit; interpret remainders by context.
- **Teacher prompts**: How do you know you have found all the factor pairs? What makes this number prime? What does the remainder mean in this situation? Which fact checks the division?
- **Work product**: an operations investigation showing factor pairs, a prime/composite sort, one long multiplication, one short division, and a written explanation of a remainder.

## Chapter 4: Fractions as Numbers and Operators
- **Key concepts**: equivalent fraction, unit fraction, mixed number, improper fraction, common denominator, multiple, fraction of an amount.
- **Practice activities**: use strips and number lines to compare fractions with related denominators; convert between mixed numbers and improper fractions; add and subtract fractions where denominators are the same or multiples; multiply proper fractions and mixed numbers by whole numbers; solve fraction-of-quantity problems with bar models.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is the whole? Which denominator helps both fractions? Is the answer greater than 1? How can the bar model show multiplication by a whole number?
- **Work product**: a fraction proof page with an equivalent pair, a mixed/improper conversion, two related-denominator calculations, a fraction multiplied by a whole number, and one fraction-of-amount problem.

## Chapter 5: Decimals, Percentages, and Powers of Ten
- **Key concepts**: tenth, hundredth, thousandth, decimal place value, decimal fraction, percent, equivalent representation, round.
- **Practice activities**: shade decimal grids for tenths, hundredths, and thousandths; read and write decimals as fractions; order decimals to three places; round decimals to the nearest whole number and one decimal place; multiply and divide whole numbers and decimals by 10, 100, and 1000; match simple fractions, decimals, and percentages.
- **Teacher prompts**: What does each decimal digit mean? How can the grid prove the fraction? Which direction do digits move when multiplying by 100? Why does percent mean out of 100?
- **Work product**: an equivalence mat linking fractions, decimals, and percentages, including a decimal order task, a rounding task, and a multiply/divide by powers of ten explanation.

## Chapter 6: Measurement, Perimeter, Area, and Volume
- **Key concepts**: metric unit, imperial estimate, conversion, perimeter, area, square unit, volume, cubic unit, capacity, elapsed time.
- **Practice activities**: convert between kilometres, metres, centimetres, millimetres, kilograms, grams, litres, and millilitres; estimate common imperial equivalents; calculate perimeter of composite rectilinear shapes; find area of rectangles and estimate irregular areas; build cuboids with cubes to estimate volume; solve time conversion problems.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which unit makes the comparison fair? What operation changes a larger unit to a smaller unit? How can we split the composite shape? What does one cubic unit represent?
- **Work product**: a measurement design page with two unit conversions, an area and perimeter plan, a volume estimate, and one time or capacity problem.

## Chapter 7: Geometry, Angles, and Position
- **Key concepts**: degree, acute, obtuse, reflex, right angle, straight line, full turn, polygon, regular, irregular, translation, reflection.
- **Practice activities**: estimate, measure, and draw angles in degrees; find missing angles around a point, on a straight line, and in rectangles; classify regular and irregular polygons; identify 3D shapes from 2D representations; translate and reflect shapes on coordinate grids.
- **Teacher prompts**: How close was your angle estimate? Which angle facts are useful here? What properties prove the polygon is regular? What changes and what stays the same after a reflection?
- **Work product**: a geometry portfolio page with measured angles, missing-angle reasoning, a polygon sort, a 3D-from-2D sketch, and one translated or reflected shape.

## Chapter 8: Data, Graphs, and Problem Solving
- **Key concepts**: table, timetable, line graph, scale, interval, comparison, sum, difference, model, justify, reasonableness.
- **Practice activities**: read and complete tables and timetables; interpret line graphs; answer comparison, sum, and difference questions from data; draw bar models for multi-step problems; write equations from contexts; check answers using estimates and units.
- **Teacher prompts**: What does the scale count by? Which data point answers the question? What information is hidden in the story? How can you justify the operation you chose?
- **Work product**: a problem-solving report with one table or graph, three data questions, one multi-step calculation, a model, and a written justification.

## Capstone
Create a "Class 5 Maths Expedition Plan" for a museum trip, sports day, school fair, or local investigation. Teams plan costs, distances, groups, schedules, measurements, display layouts, and visitor data. The final display must include large-number calculations, multiplication or division with checks, a fraction-decimal-percentage link, unit conversions, area or perimeter, angle or shape reasoning, a graph, and a clear explanation of one multi-step decision.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe whether children use place-value language accurately for numbers to 1,000,000, rounding, estimates, and negative contexts.
- Use short conferences to hear how children choose between mental methods, written methods, models, and inverse checks.
- Check multiplication and division through factor reasoning, prime/composite language, formal methods, and context-aware remainders.
- Review fraction, decimal, and percentage work for correct whole identification, equivalence, number-line sense, related denominators, and powers-of-ten reasoning.
- Assess measurement, geometry, and data through tool accuracy, unit conversion, angle facts, coordinate movement, graph scales, and interpretation.
- Grade the capstone on mathematical accuracy, visible models, independence, strategy choice, checking, communication, and teamwork.
