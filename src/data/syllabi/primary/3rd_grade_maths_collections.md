**Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 3 (ages 7–8)

## Overview
These are collection-type courses auto-generated from various US maths curricula (Eureka/EngageNY, NY Next Gen, generic 3rd grade). They all target the same **Common Core Grade 3** standards but come from different source question banks. When generating questions for any of these collections, follow the syllabus below.

> **Note**: "Get ready for 3rd grade" is a bridge/review course. Its questions
> should use the same topics but at the lower end of the difficulty range,
> reinforcing Year 2 skills that are prerequisites for Year 3 mastery.

## Domain A: Operations & Algebraic Thinking (30%)
- Interpret products of whole numbers (5 × 7 as "5 groups of 7")
- Interpret quotients (56 ÷ 8 as "how many groups of 8 in 56?")
- Multiply and divide within 100 — fluency goal by end of year
- Solve two-step word problems using all four operations
- Identify arithmetic patterns and explain them (odd + odd = even)
- Use the distributive property: 7 × 8 = 7 × (5+3) = 35 + 21
- **Questions**: Word problems, table fluency, pattern rules, properties

## Domain B: Number & Operations in Base Ten (20%)
- Round whole numbers to the nearest 10 or 100
- Fluently add and subtract within 1000 using strategies and algorithms
- Multiply one-digit × multiples of 10 (6 × 70 = 420)
- Use place value understanding to explain calculation strategies
- **Questions**: Rounding, column addition/subtraction, mental multiplication of multiples of 10

## Domain C: Number & Operations — Fractions (20%)
- Understand fractions as numbers on a number line
- Represent fractions using area models and number lines
- Equivalent fractions (limited: 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6)
- Compare fractions with same numerator or same denominator
- Express whole numbers as fractions (3 = 3/1 = 6/2)
- **Questions**: Number line placement, comparison, equivalence, whole-number-as-fraction

## Domain D: Measurement & Data (20%)
- Tell time to the nearest minute; solve elapsed time problems
- Measure and estimate liquid volumes (liters) and masses (grams, kilograms)
- Represent and interpret data with bar graphs and line plots
- Understand area as square units; relate area to multiplication
- Perimeter: find perimeters of polygons and solve for missing side lengths
- **Questions**: Elapsed time, area calculation, perimeter, bar graph interpretation

## Domain E: Geometry (10%)
- Categorize shapes by attributes: quadrilaterals, rhombuses, rectangles, squares
- Understand that shapes in different categories may share attributes
- Partition shapes into parts with equal areas; express each part as a fraction
- **Questions**: Shape classification, attribute reasoning, partition fractions

## Stretch Zone
- All 12× tables fluent by end of year (US expectation)
- Introduction to multi-digit multiplication (2-digit × 1-digit formal)
- Fractions greater than 1 (improper fractions as a concept)
- Two-step problems mixing multiplication, division, and fractions
