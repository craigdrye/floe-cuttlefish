# 3rd Grade Maths Collections
**Covers**: `col-3rd-grade-math`, `col-3rd-grade`, `col-3rd-grade-ny-next-gen`, `col-get-ready-for-3rd-grade`, `col-eureka-math-foundations-3rd-8th`, `col-eureka-math-3rd-8th`
**Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 3 (ages 7-8)

## Course Aim
Build confident Grade 3 mathematicians who can make numbers visible, choose a strategy, practise with purpose, and explain how they know. Students strengthen place value to 1000, add and subtract fluently, build multiplication and division through equal groups, understand fractions as numbers, measure with care, describe shapes, read data, and solve one- and two-step word problems with models, equations, and checks.

## Course Design Notes
This collection should feel concrete, visual, and confidence-building. Route questions here when they involve Grade 3 place value, operations within 1000, multiplication and division within 100, fractions on shapes or number lines, measurement, time, area, perimeter, geometry, data displays, or mixed word problems. Use manipulatives and pictures before abstract notation: base-ten blocks, number lines, arrays, bar models, fraction strips, clocks, rulers, square tiles, geoboards, tally charts, and scaled bar graphs. "Get ready for 3rd grade" items should use the same topics at the lower end of difficulty, reinforcing Grade 2 prerequisites.

## Chapter 1: Place Value to 1000
- **Key concepts**: Hundreds, tens, ones, digit value, expanded form, composing, decomposing, comparing, ordering, rounding to the nearest 10 and 100, and estimating.
- **Practice questions**: Build and name three-digit numbers; write numbers in standard, word, and expanded form; compare with <, >, and =; place numbers on open number lines; round and explain the nearest benchmark.
- **Student output**: A place-value card for one number showing a model, expanded form, comparison, rounded values, and a number-line position.

## Chapter 2: Addition and Subtraction Within 1000
- **Key concepts**: Sum, difference, regrouping, exchange, column addition, column subtraction, mental strategy, inverse operation, estimate, and reasonableness.
- **Practice questions**: Add and subtract two- and three-digit numbers; choose between mental and written methods; solve missing-number equations; estimate before calculating; check subtraction with addition.
- **Student output**: A calculation strategy page with one addition and one subtraction problem solved by model, written method, estimate, and inverse check.

## Chapter 3: Multiplication as Equal Groups and Arrays
- **Key concepts**: Equal groups, repeated addition, array, row, column, product, factor, multiple, commutative property, distributive property, and scaling.
- **Practice questions**: Interpret products such as 5 x 7 as groups; draw arrays; skip-count by 2s, 3s, 4s, 5s, 6s, 8s, and 10s; use known facts to solve harder facts; multiply one digit by a multiple of 10.
- **Student output**: A multiplication fact web connecting arrays, skip-counting, related facts, and one short scaling story.

## Chapter 4: Division, Sharing, and Remainders
- **Key concepts**: Equal sharing, equal grouping, quotient, divisor, dividend, remainder, inverse relationship, related facts, and efficient recording.
- **Practice questions**: Interpret quotients such as 56 / 8; solve sharing and grouping problems; connect division facts to multiplication facts; decide what a remainder means in a story; practise facts within 100.
- **Student output**: A division explanation sheet with one sharing problem, one grouping problem, one remainder problem, and multiplication checks.

## Chapter 5: Fractions as Equal Parts and Numbers
- **Key concepts**: Whole, equal part, unit fraction, non-unit fraction, numerator, denominator, equivalent fraction, same numerator, same denominator, and number-line fraction.
- **Practice questions**: Partition shapes into equal parts; represent halves, thirds, fourths, sixths, and eighths; place fractions on number lines; compare fractions with the same numerator or denominator; show simple equivalence such as 1/2 = 2/4.
- **Student output**: A fraction mat showing a shape fraction, a quantity fraction, an equivalent pair, a number-line placement, and a comparison sentence.

## Chapter 6: Measurement, Time, Area, and Perimeter
- **Key concepts**: Minute, elapsed time, gram, kilogram, litre, millilitre, length, area, square unit, perimeter, unit choice, estimate, and formula as shortcut.
- **Practice questions**: Tell time to the nearest minute; solve elapsed-time problems; measure and estimate mass and capacity; find area by counting squares and using multiplication; find perimeter of polygons, including missing sides.
- **Student output**: A measurement investigation with estimates, measured values, one elapsed-time problem, one area model, and one perimeter calculation.

## Chapter 7: Geometry and Data Displays
- **Key concepts**: Polygon, quadrilateral, rhombus, rectangle, square, attribute, partition, line plot, bar graph, pictograph, table, scale, and comparison.
- **Practice questions**: Sort shapes by properties; explain why a shape can belong to more than one category; partition shapes into equal areas; read and create scaled bar graphs and pictographs; interpret line plots.
- **Student output**: A geometry-and-data board with a shape sort, a labelled partition, one data display, and two conclusions from the data.

## Chapter 8: Word Problems and Reasoning Studio
- **Key concepts**: Context, known information, unknown, model, equation, one-step problem, two-step problem, operation choice, answer sentence, estimate, and check.
- **Practice questions**: Solve addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, fraction, measurement, geometry, and data word problems; draw bar models and number lines; identify extra or missing information; compare two solution strategies.
- **Student output**: A problem-solving portfolio with three revised problems, including one two-step problem and one written reflection on why the chosen method worked.

## Capstone
Students create a "Maths Makers Market" for a classroom stall, game, or mini-shop. The final display includes prices or quantities, two three-digit calculations, one multiplication or division model, one fraction or measurement task, one shape or area feature, one data display from visitor choices, and a word problem for another student to solve.

## Assessment Ideas
- Use short fluency checks for place value, rounding, addition, subtraction, multiplication facts, and division facts.
- Ask students to explain calculations with models, estimates, written methods, and inverse checks.
- Score fraction work for equal parts, correct whole identification, number-line sense, equivalence, and comparison reasoning.
- Check measurement and geometry tasks for accurate tool use, sensible units, area and perimeter meaning, and property language.
- Use data tasks that require students to read the scale, answer comparison questions, and write a clear conclusion.
- Grade the capstone on mathematical accuracy, visible representations, explanation, checking habits, and steady confidence with challenge.
