# Class 3 Math
**ID**: `col-class-3-math` | **Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 3 (ages 7-8)
**Frameworks**: UK KS2 Year 3 National Curriculum, US Common Core Grade 3, ACARA v9 Year 3, India NCERT Class 3

## Course Aim
Build confident, flexible mathematicians who can see numbers in many ways, choose sensible strategies, and explain their thinking with models, drawings, words, and symbols. Children deepen place value to 1000, secure addition and subtraction, build multiplication and division through arrays and facts, explore fractions as equal parts and numbers, measure with practical tools, describe shapes, read data, and solve word problems with calm persistence.

## Course Design Notes
Class 3 maths is concrete, visual, and talk-rich. Lessons move from "build it" to "draw it" to "record it": base-ten blocks, place-value counters, bead strings, number lines, arrays, fraction strips, clocks, rulers, measuring jugs, scales, geoboards, pattern blocks, tally charts, and bar models stay visible. Every chapter includes number talk, partner explanation, quick fluency, and mistakes-as-clues routines so children learn that maths confidence grows through reasoning, checking, and trying another representation.

## Chapter 1: Place Value to 1000
- **Key concepts**: hundred, ten, one, digit, value, partition, expanded form, compare, order, round, estimate.
- **Practice activities**: build three-digit numbers with base-ten blocks; exchange ten tens for one hundred; place numbers on open number lines; compare numbers with signs and spoken reasoning; round to the nearest 10 and 100.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is each digit worth? How can you partition the number in two different ways? Which hundred is it closest to, and how do you know?
- **Work product**: a place-value gallery card showing one number with concrete materials, expanded form, comparison, rounded values, and a number-line position.

## Chapter 2: Addition and Subtraction With Regrouping
- **Key concepts**: sum, difference, regroup, exchange, column method, mental strategy, inverse, estimate, reasonableness.
- **Practice activities**: add and subtract three-digit numbers with place-value counters; bridge through tens and hundreds on number lines; use column methods with careful alignment; estimate before calculating; check with inverse operations.
- **Teacher prompts**: Do we need to exchange? Is a mental strategy quicker here? What estimate would make this answer feel reasonable?
- **Work product**: an operation strategy sheet solving one addition and one subtraction problem with a model, written method, estimate, and inverse check.

## Chapter 3: Multiplication Facts, Arrays, and Scaling
- **Key concepts**: equal groups, array, row, column, product, factor, multiple, commutative, scale, known fact.
- **Practice activities**: build arrays for 3, 4, and 8 times tables; connect skip-counting to multiplication facts; use known 2, 5, and 10 facts to derive related facts; describe "three times as many" with objects and drawings.
- **Teacher prompts**: How does this array show both facts? Which known fact helps you? What changes when the group is scaled?
- **Work product**: a multiplication fact web linking arrays, skip-counting, related facts, and a short scaling story.

## Chapter 4: Division, Sharing, and Remainders
- **Key concepts**: share, group, quotient, remainder, inverse, equal groups, related fact, efficient recording.
- **Practice activities**: share counters into equal groups; group objects by 3, 4, and 8; draw division bar models; connect division facts to multiplication facts; explore simple remainders with real contexts.
- **Teacher prompts**: Are we sharing or grouping? What multiplication fact checks this division? What does the remainder mean in the story?
- **Work product**: a division explanation page with one sharing problem, one grouping problem, one remainder problem, and multiplication checks.

## Chapter 5: Fractions as Equal Parts and Numbers
- **Key concepts**: whole, equal part, unit fraction, non-unit fraction, numerator, denominator, equivalent, tenths, number line.
- **Practice activities**: fold and cut paper shapes into equal parts; use fraction strips to compare halves, thirds, quarters, sixths, eighths, and tenths; find fractions of quantities with counters; place fractions on number lines; add and subtract fractions with the same denominator within one whole.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is the whole? Are the parts equal? How can two different fractions cover the same amount?
- **Work product**: a fraction mat showing a shape fraction, a quantity fraction, an equivalent pair, a number-line placement, and one same-denominator calculation.

## Chapter 6: Measurement, Time, and Perimeter
- **Key concepts**: millimetre, centimetre, metre, gram, kilogram, millilitre, litre, perimeter, minute, duration, Roman numeral.
- **Practice activities**: measure classroom objects with rulers and tapes; compare mass and capacity with scales and jugs; calculate perimeter by adding side lengths; read clocks to the nearest minute; solve simple duration problems; read Roman numerals I to XII on clock faces.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which unit is sensible? Where does measuring start? How can we count the boundary without missing a side? How much time has passed?
- **Work product**: a measurement investigation recording estimates, measured values, one perimeter calculation, and one time-duration problem.

## Chapter 7: Geometry and Data Displays
- **Key concepts**: right angle, perpendicular, parallel, horizontal, vertical, oblique, polygon, bar chart, pictogram, table, scale.
- **Practice activities**: hunt for right angles in the room; sort lines and shapes by properties; build shapes on geoboards; collect class data; create pictograms and bar charts with sensible scales; answer comparison questions from tables.
- **Teacher prompts**: What property proves your sort? Where do you see parallel or perpendicular lines? What does each symbol or bar stand for?
- **Work product**: a geometry-and-data board with a shape sort, labelled line examples, a class data display, and two written conclusions.

## Chapter 8: Word Problems and Mathematical Reasoning
- **Key concepts**: context, operation, model, equation, two-step, estimate, answer sentence, explain, check.
- **Practice activities**: act out problems with manipulatives; identify what is known and unknown; draw bar models and number-line sketches; choose operations for one- and two-step problems; write partner problems that match given equations.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is the question asking? Which model shows the story clearly? How will you know your answer makes sense?
- **Work product**: a problem-solving portfolio page with one addition or subtraction problem, one multiplication or division problem, one two-step problem, and a reflection on strategy choice.

## Capstone
Create a "Maths Makers Market" in teams. Each team designs a small stall with prices, quantities, measurements, shapes, and data from visitors. The stall must include a place-value display, two written calculations, one multiplication or division model, one fraction or measurement task, one chart of visitor choices, and a word problem for another team to solve.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe whether children build, draw, say, and record three-digit numbers accurately, including flexible partitioning and rounding.
- Use short conferences to hear calculation choices, regrouping language, inverse checks, and estimates for addition and subtraction.
- Assess multiplication and division through arrays, equal groups, related facts, and explanations of remainders in context.
- Review fraction work for equal parts, correct whole identification, number-line sense, equivalence, and same-denominator addition or subtraction.
- Check measurement, geometry, and data tasks for accurate tool use, unit choice, property language, chart scale, and clear conclusions.
- Grade the capstone on mathematical accuracy, visible representations, oral explanation, teamwork, checking habits, and confidence with challenge.
