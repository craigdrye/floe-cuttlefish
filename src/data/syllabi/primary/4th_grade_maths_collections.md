# 4th Grade Maths Collections
**Covers**: `col-4th-grade-math`, `col-4th-grade-ny-next-gen`, `col-get-ready-for-4th-grade`, `col-eureka-math-foundations-3rd-8th`, `col-eureka-math-3rd-8th`
**Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 4 (ages 8-9)

## Course Aim
Extend Grade 4 mathematical confidence into larger numbers, efficient written methods, stronger multiplication facts, fractions and decimals, precise measurement, geometric reasoning, data interpretation, and multi-step problem solving. Students learn to connect models, equations, words, and checks so they can solve unfamiliar problems without rushing to a memorised procedure.

## Course Design Notes
This collection should stay practice-forward, visual, and concrete before abstract. Route questions here when they involve Grade 4 multi-digit operations, factors and multiples, multiplicative comparison, fractions, decimals, measurement conversions, area and perimeter, angles, geometric classification, data displays, or mixed multi-step problems. Use place-value charts, base-ten blocks, arrays, area models, fraction strips, decimal grids, rulers, protractors, geoboards, coordinate grids, line plots, and bar models. "Get ready for 4th grade" items should use the same topics at the lower end of difficulty, especially Grade 3 prerequisites.

## Chapter 1: Place Value, Rounding, and Number Sense
- **Key concepts**: Ones through millions, digit value, expanded notation, compare, order, round to any place, benchmark, estimate, and place-value pattern.
- **Practice questions**: Read and write multi-digit whole numbers; compare and order numbers; round to the nearest 10, 100, 1000, 10,000, or 100,000; explain how each place is ten times the place to its right.
- **Student output**: A place-value reasoning page showing one large number in multiple forms, two comparisons, rounded values, and an estimate check.

## Chapter 2: Multi-Digit Addition and Subtraction
- **Key concepts**: Standard algorithm, regrouping, exchange, alignment, difference, estimate, inverse, multi-step, and reasonableness.
- **Practice questions**: Add and subtract multi-digit numbers; solve money, distance, population, and measurement problems; choose mental or written strategies; check answers with inverse operations and estimates.
- **Student output**: A calculation case file with two multi-digit calculations, an estimate for each, an inverse check, and a short strategy explanation.

## Chapter 3: Multiplication, Factors, and Multiples
- **Key concepts**: Factor, multiple, factor pair, product, prime, composite, square number, array, distributive property, multiplicative comparison, and related fact.
- **Practice questions**: Identify factor pairs from 1 to 100; classify prime and composite numbers; generate number and shape patterns; solve "times as many" problems; use known facts to multiply by multiples of 10, 100, and 1000.
- **Student output**: A factor-and-multiple map for one number, including factor pairs, multiples, prime or composite reasoning, arrays, and a comparison problem.

## Chapter 4: Multi-Digit Multiplication and Division
- **Key concepts**: Area model, partial products, standard algorithm, short division, quotient, remainder, distributive property, place-value reasoning, and check.
- **Practice questions**: Multiply up to four digits by one digit; multiply two two-digit numbers; divide up to four-digit dividends by one-digit divisors; interpret remainders in context; check division with multiplication.
- **Student output**: An operations comparison page showing one multiplication and one division solved with a model, written method, and check.

## Chapter 5: Fraction Equivalence and Operations
- **Key concepts**: Numerator, denominator, equivalent fraction, common denominator, benchmark fraction, mixed number, improper fraction, same-denominator addition, same-denominator subtraction, and fraction of a set.
- **Practice questions**: Build equivalent fractions with area models and number lines; compare fractions with different numerators and denominators; add and subtract fractions and mixed numbers with like denominators; multiply a fraction by a whole number; solve fraction word problems.
- **Student output**: A fraction reasoning card with an equivalent pair, a comparison, one same-denominator calculation, one fraction-times-whole-number model, and a word problem.

## Chapter 6: Decimals, Money, and Measurement Conversions
- **Key concepts**: Tenth, hundredth, decimal notation, decimal comparison, fraction-decimal equivalence, metric conversion, customary conversion, elapsed time, money, and unit rate within a system.
- **Practice questions**: Write tenths and hundredths as fractions and decimals; compare decimals to hundredths; add fractions with denominators 10 and 100; convert km, m, cm, kg, g, l, ml, hr, min, sec, lb, oz, and money units in context.
- **Student output**: A decimals-and-measures mat with a shaded grid, fraction-decimal matches, two unit conversions, and one money or elapsed-time problem.

## Chapter 7: Angles, Geometry, Area, and Symmetry
- **Key concepts**: Point, line, line segment, ray, acute angle, right angle, obtuse angle, straight angle, degree, protractor, parallel, perpendicular, symmetry, rectangle area, and perimeter.
- **Practice questions**: Draw and identify lines, rays, segments, and angles; measure angles in whole-number degrees; find unknown angles by addition and subtraction; classify two-dimensional figures by lines and angles; find area and perimeter of rectangles; identify lines of symmetry.
- **Student output**: A geometry investigation with measured angles, a shape classification, an area-and-perimeter task, and a symmetry example.

## Chapter 8: Data, Line Plots, and Problem Solving Studio
- **Key concepts**: Table, bar graph, line plot, scale, interval, fraction data, comparison, multi-step problem, model, equation, justify, and error analysis.
- **Practice questions**: Make and interpret line plots with fractional measurements; read scaled graphs; solve measurement and data word problems; model multi-step problems with bar models, tables, diagrams, and equations; explain and correct errors.
- **Student output**: A problem-solving report with one data display, three questions answered from it, one multi-step calculation, and a corrected error with explanation.

## Capstone
Students design a "Maths Design Lab" for a small garden, classroom layout, club event, or game tournament. The final product includes multi-digit addition or subtraction, multiplication or division with checks, factors or multiples, a fraction or decimal decision, at least two measurement conversions, an angle or geometry feature, a data display, and a written solution to one multi-step problem.

## Assessment Ideas
- Use frequent short checks for place value, rounding, multiplication facts, factor pairs, and multi-digit operation accuracy.
- Ask students to show why written algorithms work with area models, place-value models, estimates, and inverse checks.
- Review fraction and decimal work for equivalent representations, number-line reasoning, comparison strategies, and word-problem sense.
- Assess measurement and geometry through tool use, unit conversion, angle measurement, property language, area, perimeter, and symmetry.
- Use data tasks that require line plots, fractional measurements, scale reading, written comparisons, and error correction.
- Grade the capstone on mathematical accuracy, representation quality, efficient strategy choice, checking, explanation, and steady problem-solving habits.
