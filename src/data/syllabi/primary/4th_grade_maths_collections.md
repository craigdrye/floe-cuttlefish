# 4th Grade Maths Collections
**Covers**: `col-4th-grade-math`, `col-4th-grade-ny-next-gen`, `col-get-ready-for-4th-grade`, `col-eureka-math-foundations-3rd-8th`, `col-eureka-math-3rd-8th`
**Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 4 (ages 8–9)

## Overview
Combined syllabus for US-aligned 4th grade maths collections. These target **Common Core Grade 4**. 

## Domain A: Operations & Algebraic Thinking (20%)
- Interpret a multiplication equation as a comparison (e.g., 35 = 5 × 7 is "35 is 5 times as many as 7")
- Multiply or divide to solve word problems involving multiplicative comparison
- Solve multi-step word problems posed with whole numbers and having whole-number answers using the four operations
- Interpret remainders in word problems
- Find all factor pairs for a whole number in the range 1–100; recognize prime and composite numbers
- Generate a number or shape pattern that follows a given rule
- **Questions**: Multiplicative comparison, multi-step word problems, factor/prime identification, patterns

## Domain B: Number & Operations in Base Ten (25%)
- Generalize place value understanding for multi-digit whole numbers up to 1,000,000
- Read, write, and compare multi-digit whole numbers
- Round multi-digit whole numbers to any place
- Fluently add and subtract multi-digit whole numbers using the standard algorithm
- Multiply a whole number of up to four digits by a one-digit whole number, and multiply two two-digit numbers
- Find whole-number quotients and remainders with up to four-digit dividends and one-digit divisors
- **Questions**: Place value up to 1M, rounding, column add/sub, multi-digit multiplication, division with remainders

## Domain C: Number & Operations — Fractions (30%)
- Explain why a fraction a/b is equivalent to a fraction (n × a)/(n × b)
- Compare two fractions with different numerators and different denominators
- Understand addition and subtraction of fractions as joining and separating parts referring to the same whole
- Add and subtract mixed numbers with like denominators
- Solve word problems involving addition and subtraction of fractions
- Multiply a fraction by a whole number
- Express a fraction with denominator 10 as an equivalent fraction with denominator 100, and use this to add two fractions with denominators 10 and 100
- Use decimal notation for fractions with denominators 10 or 100
- **Questions**: Fraction equivalence, comparison, mixed number arithmetic, fraction × whole number, decimal notation

## Domain D: Measurement & Data (15%)
- Know relative sizes of measurement units within one system of units (km, m, cm; kg, g; lb, oz.; l, ml; hr, min, sec)
- Use the four operations to solve word problems involving distances, intervals of time, liquid volumes, masses, and money
- Apply the area and perimeter formulas for rectangles in real world and mathematical problems
- Make a line plot to display a data set of measurements in fractions of a unit (1/2, 1/4, 1/8)
- Understand concepts of angle and measure angles in whole-number degrees using a protractor
- **Questions**: Unit conversion word problems, area/perimeter, line plots with fractions, angle measurement

## Domain E: Geometry (10%)
- Draw points, lines, line segments, rays, angles (right, acute, obtuse), and perpendicular and parallel lines
- Classify two-dimensional figures based on the presence or absence of parallel or perpendicular lines, or the presence or absence of angles of a specified size
- Recognize a line of symmetry for a two-dimensional figure
- **Questions**: Geometry term identification, shape classification by lines/angles, symmetry

## Stretch Zone
- Multiply and divide decimals by 10, 100, 1000
- Add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators
- Understand volume as a measurable attribute of solid figures
