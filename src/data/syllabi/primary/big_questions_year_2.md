# Big Questions: Year 2
**ID**: `philosophyYear2` · **Discipline**: Humanities · **Year**: 2 (ages 6–7)

## Course Philosophy
Year 2 philosophy pushes children from personal experience toward **abstract reasoning**. They begin to handle genuine dilemmas where there is no single right answer, learn to weigh competing values, and start constructing simple arguments. This is the capstone of early P4C.

## Module 1: Fairness & Justice (20%)
- Is it fair that some people have more than others?
- Should everyone get the same, or what they need? (Equality vs equity)
- Punishment: what's the point? To teach, to protect, or to get even?
- Second chances: should people always get another try?
- **Questions**: Dilemma scenarios, fairness reasoning, equity vs equality judgment

## Module 2: Courage & Fear (20%)
- What does it mean to be brave? Is it the same as not being scared?
- Can you be brave AND scared at the same time?
- Standing up for others even when it's hard
- The difference between physical courage and moral courage
- When is it wise NOT to be brave? (Safety vs bravery)
- **Questions**: Courage identification, scenario judgment, "was this brave or reckless?"

## Module 3: Knowledge & Belief (20%)
- How do we know things? (Someone told us, we saw it, we read it, we figured it out)
- The difference between knowing and believing
- Can you believe something that turns out to be wrong?
- Evidence: "How could you check if that's true?"
- Changing your mind when new information arrives — that's smart, not weak
- **Questions**: Knowledge source identification, belief vs fact sorting, evidence reasoning

## Module 4: Community & Difference (20%)
- What makes a community? (People who help each other, shared spaces)
- Why do we need each other? (No one can do everything alone)
- Respecting people who think, look, or live differently
- Prejudice: judging someone before you know them — why is it harmful?
- What responsibilities do we have to each other?
- **Questions**: Community role identification, prejudice scenarios, responsibility reasoning

## Module 5: Imagination & Possibility (20%)
- "What if?" thinking as a superpower
- Could the world be different? Should it be? How?
- The value of creativity: new ideas change the world
- Thought experiments: "What if you could fly?", "What if there were no rules for one day?"
- Dreams and ambitions: what do you want to change?
- **Questions**: Creative reasoning, consequence prediction, "what would you change and why?"

## Stretch Zone
- Introduction to logical fallacies at a simple level: "That's not a reason!"
- The trolley problem adapted for children (save one or save many)
- Philosophy of art: "Is a scribble art? Who decides?"
- "Can animals think?" — philosophy of mind for 7-year-olds
- Democracy: "Should children get to vote on classroom rules?"
