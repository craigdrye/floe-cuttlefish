# Big Questions: Year 5
**ID**: `philosophyYear5` · **Discipline**: Humanities · **Year**: 5 (ages 9–10)

## Course Philosophy
In Year 5, students explore questions of identity, the structure of society, and the ethics of technological advancement. The focus is on recognizing that multiple valid perspectives can exist simultaneously and learning to respectfully dissect complex, multi-layered arguments.

## Module 1: Identity & The Self (20%)
- What makes you, "you"? Is it your body, your memories, your personality?
- The Ship of Theseus applied to the human body (if all your cells replace themselves, are you the same person?)
- How much does our environment (where we grow up) shape who we are?
- Is it possible to completely change who you are?
- **Questions**: Thought experiments on identity, nature vs nurture scenarios

## Module 2: Society, Law, & Justice (20%)
- What is the difference between a rule, a law, and a moral duty?
- Can a law be unjust? What should citizens do if a law is unfair? (Introduction to civil disobedience, Rosa Parks)
- Retributive justice (punishment) vs. Restorative justice (repairing the harm)
- The concept of human rights: Are they discovered or invented by humans?
- **Questions**: Evaluating unjust laws, restorative vs retributive justice scenarios

## Module 3: Technology & The Future (20%)
- If an AI or robot becomes very smart, should it have rights?
- Is it ethical to change human DNA? (Designer babies, curing diseases)
- Privacy vs. Security: Is it okay for the government/companies to read our messages to keep us safe?
- How does technology change what it means to be human?
- **Questions**: AI ethics scenarios, evaluating privacy trade-offs

## Module 4: Epistemology (How do we know?) (20%)
- Seeing is believing? Optical illusions and the unreliability of the senses
- How do we know history is true if we weren't there?
- Science vs. Belief: What makes a scientific fact different from an opinion?
- Can we ever be 100% certain of anything?
- **Questions**: Evaluating evidence sources, identifying the limits of sensory perception

## Module 5: Global Ethics (20%)
- Do we have a moral obligation to help people in other countries?
- Peter Singer's drowning child thought experiment (if you'd save a child in front of you, why not one far away?)
- Who owns the Earth's resources? (Water, air, minerals)
- The ethics of space exploration vs. solving problems on Earth
- **Questions**: Global responsibility scenarios, evaluating resource distribution

## Stretch Zone
- Introduction to the Categorical Imperative (Kant) simplified: "What if everyone did that?"
- Existentialism for kids: "Do we have a set purpose, or do we create our own purpose?"
- Determinism vs. Free Will: Do we actually make choices, or is everything predetermined?
