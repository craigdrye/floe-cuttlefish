# Basic Geometry and Measurement
**ID**: `col-basic-geometry-and-measurement` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 2 (ages 6–7)

## Course Philosophy
A dedicated deep-dive into the spatial and measurement domains that often get less time in a generalist maths course. This builds the visual and practical reasoning that underpins geometry at KS2 and beyond.

## Module 1: 2D Shapes (25%)
- Identify and describe: circle, triangle, square, rectangle, pentagon, hexagon, octagon
- Count sides and vertices
- Regular vs irregular shapes (a square is a special rectangle)
- Sort shapes by properties (number of sides, right angles)
- Compose and decompose shapes: two triangles make a square
- Lines of symmetry — find them, draw them
- **Questions**: Property matching, "how many sides?", symmetry identification, shape composition

## Module 2: 3D Shapes (25%)
- Identify: cube, cuboid, sphere, cylinder, cone, pyramid, triangular prism
- Faces, edges, and vertices — count them
- Relate 3D to 2D: "What shape is each face of a cuboid?" (rectangles)
- Real-world 3D shapes: ball = sphere, tin can = cylinder, dice = cube
- Nets as a concept: unfolding a 3D shape into flat 2D
- **Questions**: Face/edge/vertex counting, real-world matching, 2D-face identification

## Module 3: Measurement (25%)
- Length: measure in cm and m using a ruler; estimate before measuring
- Mass: compare and measure in g and kg using scales
- Capacity: compare and measure in ml and l
- Choose appropriate units: "Would you measure a pencil in cm or m?"
- Convert simple cases: 100 cm = 1 m, 1000 g = 1 kg
- Temperature: reading a thermometer (°C), hot vs cold
- **Questions**: Reading scales, unit selection, estimation, simple conversion

## Module 4: Position, Direction & Time (25%)
- Clockwise and anti-clockwise
- Quarter turn, half turn, three-quarter turn, full turn
- Compass directions: North, South, East, West
- Simple coordinates on a grid: "The treasure is at B3"
- Time: o'clock, half past, quarter past, quarter to, 5-minute intervals
- Duration: "The film starts at 3:00 and ends at 4:30. How long is it?"
- **Questions**: Turn identification, compass direction following, grid coordinates, time calculation

## Stretch Zone
- Angles as a concept: right angle, greater than/less than a right angle
- Perimeter as "the distance around" (counting squares)
- Introduction to area as "how many squares fit inside"
- 8-point compass: NE, NW, SE, SW
- Telling time to the nearest minute
