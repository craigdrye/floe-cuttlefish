# Basic Geometry and Measurement
**ID**: `col-basic-geometry-and-measurement` | **Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 2 (ages 6-7)
**Frameworks**: UK National Curriculum primary mathematics, Common Core Geometry Grade 2, Common Core Measurement and Data Grade 2, NCETM primary mastery guidance

## Course Aim
Build confident spatial and measurement thinkers through handling, drawing, comparing, estimating, and explaining. Children learn to describe shapes and positions, notice symmetry, choose sensible units, measure carefully, and use measurement in everyday contexts.

## Course Design Notes
Year 2 geometry and measurement should be practical before it is formal. Lessons use pattern blocks, geoboards, rulers, balance scales, water containers, clocks, coins, classroom maps, body movement, and quick partner talk. Children cycle through estimate, measure, compare, record, and explain so accuracy grows without losing curiosity.

## Chapter 1: 2D Shape Detectives
- **Key concepts**: side, vertex, corner, straight, curved, polygon, regular, irregular.
- **Practice activities**: sort triangles, quadrilaterals, pentagons, hexagons, and circles; count sides and vertices; make shapes with sticks and geoboards.
- **Teacher prompts**: How do you know it is a triangle? What stayed the same when the shape turned? Can a shape look different and keep the same name?
- **Work product**: a shape detective sheet with sorted shapes, labels, and one "prove it" sentence.

## Chapter 2: 3D Shapes in the Real World
- **Key concepts**: face, edge, vertex, cube, cuboid, sphere, cylinder, cone, pyramid.
- **Practice activities**: match solid shapes to classroom objects; print faces in clay or paint; count faces, edges, and vertices; build with blocks.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which faces are flat? Which shapes can roll, stack, or slide? What 2D shape can you see on this face?
- **Work product**: a 3D shape museum card for one object, naming its shape and properties.

## Chapter 3: Position, Direction, and Turns
- **Key concepts**: left, right, above, below, between, clockwise, anticlockwise, quarter turn, half turn.
- **Practice activities**: follow obstacle-course directions; move counters on grids; give partner instructions using position words; turn shape cards through fractions of a turn.
- **Teacher prompts**: Where is it compared with the start? Which way did you turn? Did the shape move, turn, or both?
- **Work product**: a grid route map with arrows, position words, and one turn instruction.

## Chapter 4: Symmetry and Pattern
- **Key concepts**: symmetry, line of symmetry, mirror, match, repeat, pattern, orientation.
- **Practice activities**: fold paper shapes; complete mirror drawings; use pattern blocks to build symmetrical pictures; test whether a line really splits a shape equally.
- **Teacher prompts**: Do both sides match exactly? Where could the mirror line go? What changes if we turn the pattern?
- **Work product**: a symmetrical design with the line of symmetry marked and explained.

## Chapter 5: Length, Height, and Perimeter Foundations
- **Key concepts**: length, height, width, longer, shorter, centimetre, metre, estimate, perimeter.
- **Practice activities**: estimate and measure classroom objects; choose cm or m; compare body measurements; count unit lengths around simple shapes.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which unit makes sense? Where should the ruler start? Is your estimate close to your measure?
- **Work product**: a measurement table with estimate, actual length, unit, and comparison sentence.

## Chapter 6: Mass, Capacity, and Temperature
- **Key concepts**: mass, heavier, lighter, gram, kilogram, capacity, litre, millilitre, temperature.
- **Practice activities**: compare objects on balance scales; read simple scale marks; fill containers and compare capacity; sort items that would be measured in g, kg, ml, or l.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which is heavier? How can we make the scales balance? What unit would you choose for water in a bottle?
- **Work product**: a practical measuring record for one mass comparison and one capacity comparison.

## Chapter 7: Time, Money, and Measurement Contexts
- **Key concepts**: o'clock, half past, quarter past, quarter to, duration, coin, price, total, change.
- **Practice activities**: make times on teaching clocks; sequence daily routines; run a timed classroom task; use coins in a shop game; measure ingredients for a pretend recipe.
- **Teacher prompts**: What time does it start? How long did it take? Do you have enough money? Which measurement helps in this problem?
- **Work product**: a day-plan strip showing times, one duration, and one money or measuring choice.

## Chapter 8: Practical Measuring Projects
- **Key concepts**: plan, tool, unit, record, compare, check, reasonable, explain.
- **Practice activities**: choose tools for a classroom investigation; measure a desk, book, cup, or bag; check a partner's measurement; make a simple bar or tally record.
- **Teacher prompts**: What are you trying to find out? Which tool will give a fair measure? Does the answer seem reasonable?
- **Work product**: a mini investigation page with question, estimate, measurement, drawing, and conclusion.

## Capstone
Create a "Measure and Map Our Classroom" project. Children draw a simple classroom map, label 2D and 3D shapes they can find, add one symmetrical design, measure three objects with suitable units, include one timed or money-based classroom task, and explain their choices to a partner.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe whether children identify shapes by properties rather than by orientation or familiar appearance.
- Use hands-on tasks to check accurate use of rulers, balance scales, containers, clocks, and coins.
- Ask children to choose units for length, mass, capacity, time, and money contexts and explain why the unit fits.
- Review work products for clear labels, estimates, measurements, comparisons, and reasonable checking.
- Grade the capstone on property language, practical measuring accuracy, sensible unit choice, and confident mathematical talk.
