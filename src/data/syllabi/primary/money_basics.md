# Money Basics
**ID**: `col-money-basics` · **Discipline**: Tech & Life · **Year**: 1 (ages 5–6)

## Course Philosophy
Financial literacy starts with understanding that **things cost money, money comes from work, and you can't buy everything at once**. This course uses coins and notes from multiple currencies (£, $, ₹, A$) to stay globally relevant.

## Module 1: Coins & Notes (30%)
- Recognise common coins and notes
- Understand that different coins have different values
- Order coins by value (1p, 2p, 5p, 10p, 20p, 50p, £1, £2 / equivalent)
- The concept of "worth" — a 10p coin is worth more than a 5p coin even though they're similar sizes
- **Questions**: Coin identification, value ordering, "which is worth more?"

## Module 2: Paying for Things (25%)
- Simple totals: "An apple costs 5p and a banana costs 3p. How much altogether?"
- Making amounts using different coin combinations
- Understand that you must have enough money to buy something
- "Can I afford it?" scenarios
- **Questions**: Addition with money, coin combination puzzles, afford/can't afford

## Module 3: Saving & Spending (25%)
- The difference between needs (food, shelter) and wants (toys, treats)
- Saving: putting money aside to buy something later
- Waiting and patience — delayed gratification
- Simple budgeting: "You have 20p. What can you buy?"
- **Questions**: Needs vs wants sorting, saving scenarios, budget choices

## Module 4: Where Money Comes From (20%)
- People work to earn money
- Different jobs earn money in different ways
- Money is exchanged for goods and services
- You can't just print more money (introduction to scarcity)
- Charity: giving money to help others
- **Questions**: Job-to-service matching, "how do people earn money?", giving scenarios

## Stretch Zone
- Making change: "You pay 10p for a 7p item. How much change?"
- Introduction to the concept of a bank (a safe place to keep money)
- Digital money: cards and phones can pay too
- Entrepreneurship for kids: "What could you sell at a bake sale?"
