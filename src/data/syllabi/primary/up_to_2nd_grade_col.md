# Up to 2nd Grade (Collection)
**ID**: `col-up-to-2nd-grade-skills` - **Discipline**: Kids Discovery - **Year**: 1 (ages 5-6)

## Course Aim
Present the preschool-to-Grade-2 collection at a Year 1 level, with questions skewing toward early primary fluency, confidence, and independence.

## Course Design Notes
This course mirrors `preschool/up_to_2nd_grade_skills.md` but should use the upper end of that difficulty range. Route Year 1 questions here when learners need early addition and subtraction, simple sentence reading, concrete science, and classroom/community reasoning.

## Chapter 1: Counting, Place Value, and Number Patterns
**Key concepts**: Counting, comparing, tens and ones, skip counting, number lines, and simple patterns.
**Practice questions**: Count by 2s, 5s, and 10s; compare numbers; build two-digit numbers; continue a pattern.
**Student output**: A number pattern and place-value page.

## Chapter 2: Addition and Subtraction Stories
**Key concepts**: Addition, subtraction, missing addends, making ten, doubles, and word problems.
**Practice questions**: Solve story problems within 20; draw part-whole models; explain a strategy.
**Student output**: A small book of solved number stories.

## Chapter 3: Shape, Time, Measurement, and Fractions
**Key concepts**: 2D shapes, 3D shapes, time to the hour, length, comparison, halves, and quarters.
**Practice questions**: Sort shapes; measure with classroom objects; tell time; split shapes into equal parts.
**Student output**: A measurement and shape hunt.

## Chapter 4: Reading Simple Sentences
**Key concepts**: Decoding, sight words, sentence meaning, punctuation, and fluency.
**Practice questions**: Read and answer short sentences; choose punctuation; match sentence to picture.
**Student output**: A sentence-and-picture page.

## Chapter 5: Comprehension and Vocabulary
**Key concepts**: Character, setting, sequence, main event, synonyms, antonyms, and inference.
**Practice questions**: Retell a short story; answer what happened and why; match similar and opposite words.
**Student output**: A three-part story retell.

## Chapter 6: Materials, Forces, Light, Sound, and Magnets
**Key concepts**: Material properties, push, pull, shadow, sound, magnetism, observation, and prediction.
**Practice questions**: Sort objects; predict magnet attraction; describe shadows; compare sounds.
**Student output**: A science observation sheet.

## Chapter 7: Community, Rules, Needs, and Wants
**Key concepts**: Community helpers, school rules, home rules, needs, wants, maps, and caring for places.
**Practice questions**: Sort needs and wants; identify who can help; explain why a rule matters.
**Student output**: A community helper or rules poster.

## Chapter 8: Feelings, Friendship, and Problem Solving
**Key concepts**: Feelings, sharing, turn-taking, respect, differences, apology, and asking for help.
**Practice questions**: Pick a kind response; name a feeling; solve a classroom problem.
**Student output**: A social problem-solving comic.

## Capstone
Students create a Year 1 readiness folder with one math, reading, science, community, and friendship artifact.

## Assessment Ideas
Use brief oral checks, picture-supported tasks, short independent practice, observation notes, and small portfolio reviews.
