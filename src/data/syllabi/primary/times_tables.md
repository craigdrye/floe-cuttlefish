# Times Tables
**ID**: `col-times-tables` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 2 (ages 6–7)

## Course Philosophy
Dedicated times tables mastery. By the end of Year 2, children should have **instant recall** of the 2×, 5×, and 10× tables, and be building confidence with 3× and beyond. This is the single most important fluency milestone before KS2.

## Module 1: The 2× Table (25%)
- 2× table from 2×1 to 2×12 — instant recall
- Understand as doubling: 2 × 7 = double 7 = 14
- Related division facts: 14 ÷ 2 = 7
- Even numbers as the 2× table outputs
- Skip counting by 2s on a number line
- **Questions**: Rapid recall, "double it", division inverse, "is 17 in the 2× table?"

## Module 2: The 5× Table (25%)
- 5× table from 5×1 to 5×12 — instant recall
- Pattern: all answers end in 0 or 5
- Clock connection: 5-minute intervals on a clock face
- Related division facts: 35 ÷ 5 = 7
- Skip counting by 5s
- **Questions**: Rapid recall, pattern spotting, clock-to-table connection, division inverse

## Module 3: The 10× Table (25%)
- 10× table from 10×1 to 10×12 — instant recall
- Pattern: just add a zero (understanding why, not just the trick)
- Place value connection: 10 × 4 = 4 tens = 40
- Related division facts: 80 ÷ 10 = 8
- Counting in 10s from any starting number
- **Questions**: Rapid recall, place value reasoning, division inverse, counting sequences

## Module 4: Building Toward 3×, 4× and Beyond (25%)
- 3× table: 3×1 to 3×12 (building fluency)
- 4× table: understand as "double the 2× table" (2×6=12, so 4×6=24)
- Mixed tables practice: random questions from 2, 3, 4, 5, 10
- Square numbers: 2×2=4, 3×3=9, 4×4=16, 5×5=25
- Commutativity: 3 × 7 = 7 × 3
- **Questions**: Mixed rapid recall, "which table is this from?", square number identification

## Stretch Zone
- 6× table as "double the 3×"
- 8× table as "double the 4×"
- Missing factor problems: _ × 5 = 45
- Times table patterns on a hundred square (colouring multiples)
- Speed challenge: how many can you answer in 60 seconds?
