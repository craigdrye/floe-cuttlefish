# Times Tables
**ID**: `col-times-tables` | **Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 2 (ages 6-7)
**Frameworks**: UK National Curriculum primary mathematics, Common Core Operations and Algebraic Thinking, Common Core Number and Operations, NCETM primary mastery guidance

## Course Aim
Help children understand, remember, and enjoy multiplication facts by building them from equal groups, arrays, skip-counting, number lines, stories, and games. Recall matters, but it grows from meaning, pattern spotting, and repeated anxiety-safe practice.

## Course Design Notes
Times tables are taught as relationships first and facts second. Children touch and draw groups, say patterns aloud, move along number lines, build arrays, connect multiplication to division, and use short retrieval rounds that feel like practice, not performance. The course prioritizes 2, 5, and 10 facts while opening doors to 3, 4, and derived facts.

## Chapter 1: Equal Groups Everywhere
- **Key concepts**: equal groups, group size, number of groups, total, repeated addition.
- **Practice activities**: make equal groups with counters; sort pictures into equal and unequal groups; tell stories such as "3 bags with 2 apples each."
- **Teacher prompts**: Are all the groups the same size? How many groups are there? How many altogether?
- **Work product**: an equal-groups story page with objects, repeated addition, and a matching multiplication sentence.

## Chapter 2: Arrays, Rows, and Columns
- **Key concepts**: array, row, column, rectangle, commutative, same total.
- **Practice activities**: build arrays with counters or tiles; rotate arrays to see 2 x 5 and 5 x 2; draw arrays on grid paper; match arrays to number sentences.
- **Teacher prompts**: How many rows? How many in each row? What changes when we turn the array?
- **Work product**: an array gallery showing three facts with drawings and paired turn-around facts.

## Chapter 3: Skip-Counting on Number Lines
- **Key concepts**: skip-count, multiple, jump, count forward, count back, pattern.
- **Practice activities**: jump in 2s, 5s, and 10s on a floor number line; chant counts with claps; place missing multiples on a strip.
- **Teacher prompts**: What size are the jumps? What number comes next? How can the number line help if you forget a fact?
- **Work product**: a skip-counting number line for 2s, 5s, or 10s with labelled jumps.

## Chapter 4: The 2 Times Table and Doubling
- **Key concepts**: double, pair, even, twice, half, related division.
- **Practice activities**: pair socks, cubes, or counters; double numbers on ten frames; sort even and odd totals; connect 2 x 7 to 14 divided by 2.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is double this number? Is the total even? What division fact belongs with this multiplication fact?
- **Work product**: a doubles wheel or chart showing 2 times facts and related halves.

## Chapter 5: The 5 Times Table
- **Key concepts**: groups of 5, multiple of 5, ends in 0 or 5, clock minutes, division by 5.
- **Practice activities**: count fingers by 5s; use a clock face for 5-minute jumps; build 5-groups with counters; sort numbers that are and are not in the 5 table.
- **Teacher prompts**: What pattern do you notice in the ones digit? How many groups of 5 make 35? How does the clock help?
- **Work product**: a 5-times pattern poster with a number line, clock link, and division pair.

## Chapter 6: The 10 Times Table and Place Value
- **Key concepts**: ten, tens, place value, multiple of 10, groups of 10, divide by 10.
- **Practice activities**: bundle straws into tens; count ten frames; build 10 x facts with base-ten blocks; compare 6 ones and 6 tens.
- **Teacher prompts**: How many tens are in the total? Why does 10 x 4 make 40? What happens when 70 is shared into groups of 10?
- **Work product**: a place-value table showing 10-times facts as tens and ones.

## Chapter 7: Building 3s and 4s from Known Facts
- **Key concepts**: derived fact, add one group, double, double-double, near fact, square.
- **Practice activities**: build 3s by adding one more group to 2s; build 4s by doubling 2s; use arrays for 3 x 4 and 4 x 3; notice square numbers with tiles.
- **Teacher prompts**: What fact do you already know? Can doubling help? How can the array prove both facts?
- **Work product**: a derived-facts ladder showing how 2s help with 3s and 4s.

## Chapter 8: Retrieval, Division Links, and Calm Practice
- **Key concepts**: recall, retrieve, inverse, missing factor, check, confidence, strategy.
- **Practice activities**: use flashcards with models on the back; play partner games with wait time; sort fact cards into "know now" and "practise next"; solve missing-factor and sharing problems.
- **Teacher prompts**: What picture can you imagine for this fact? What division fact checks it? Which facts are getting stronger?
- **Work product**: a personal practice map with secure facts, growing facts, and one chosen strategy.

## Capstone
Design a "Multiplication Museum" for 2s, 5s, and 10s, with a small exhibit for 3s or 4s. Each exhibit must include equal groups, an array, skip-counting, a multiplication fact, a related division fact, and a game or puzzle for visitors.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe whether children can build and explain equal groups, arrays, and skip-counting before recalling facts.
- Use low-stakes oral checks for 2, 5, and 10 facts, giving wait time and accepting strategy explanations.
- Review work products for correct links between multiplication, repeated addition, arrays, and division.
- Include missing-factor and sharing questions to check inverse understanding.
- Grade the capstone on accurate representations, clear patterns, related division facts, and confidence-building visitor activities.

## Research Notes
- The UK National Curriculum expects Year 2 pupils to recall and use multiplication and division facts for the 2, 5, and 10 multiplication tables, including recognising odd and even numbers: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-curriculum-in-england-mathematics-programmes-of-study
- Common Core Grade 2 Operations and Algebraic Thinking includes work with equal groups, arrays, repeated addition, and foundations for multiplication: https://www.corestandards.org/Math/Content/2/OA/
- NCETM primary mastery materials emphasize using representations, patterns, stem sentences, and connected facts to secure multiplication understanding: https://www.ncetm.org.uk/classroom-resources/primm-primary-mastery-professional-development/
- Anxiety-safe retrieval works best when children can use models, talk through strategies, revisit tricky facts often, and measure progress against their own previous confidence.
