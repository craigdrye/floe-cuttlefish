# Word Builders
**ID**: `col-word-builders` | **Discipline**: English | **Year**: 1 (ages 5-6)
**Frameworks**: UK National Curriculum primary English, UK Phonics Screening Check, Common Core Foundational Skills Grade 1, ACARA English Year 1, EEF literacy guidance

## Course Aim
Build young readers and writers who can hear sounds, read words, spell useful patterns, grow vocabulary, and carry those skills into sentences and short texts. Children practise in short, successful loops: say it, tap it, build it, read it, write it, and use it in a sentence.

## Course Design Notes
Year 1 literacy should be systematic, playful, and full of talk. Lessons use magnetic letters, sound buttons, word cards, mini whiteboards, picture prompts, partner reading, oral rehearsal, and quick games. The course keeps decoding, spelling, vocabulary, and sentence work connected so children see that phonics helps them read and write real messages.

## Chapter 1: Listening for Sounds
- **Key concepts**: phoneme, grapheme, oral blending, segmenting, rhyme, syllable, alliteration.
- **Practice activities**: clap syllables in names; sort objects by first sound; play "I spy" with sounds; orally blend "c-a-t" into "cat."
- **Teacher prompts**: What sound can you hear first? Can you stretch the word? How many sound taps do you need?
- **Work product**: a sound hunt page with drawings for three beginning sounds and one rhyming pair.

## Chapter 2: Secure Single-Letter Sounds
- **Key concepts**: letter name, letter sound, lower-case, upper-case, vowel, consonant.
- **Practice activities**: match letters to pictures; form letters in sand or on whiteboards; build CVC words with magnetic letters; read quick word ladders.
- **Teacher prompts**: What sound does this letter usually show? Which letter changes the word? Can you read it after you build it?
- **Work product**: a CVC word ladder showing three words changed by one letter.

## Chapter 3: Digraphs and Trigraphs
- **Key concepts**: digraph, trigraph, sound button, blend, decode, adjacent consonant.
- **Practice activities**: underline sh, ch, th, ng, ai, ee, igh, oa, and oo; read real and pseudo words; sort words by shared grapheme.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which two or three letters make one sound? Is this a real word or a made-up word? Where is the tricky part?
- **Work product**: a digraph sort with words, drawings, and one sentence using a chosen word.

## Chapter 4: Spelling Patterns and Word Families
- **Key concepts**: word family, short vowel, long vowel, split digraph, double consonant, pattern.
- **Practice activities**: sort cat, cake, cap, cape; build -at, -in, -op, and -ake families; compare hop and hope; play spelling pattern bingo.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is the same in these words? What changed the vowel sound? Which spelling pattern should we try first?
- **Work product**: a word family house with at least six words and two illustrated examples.

## Chapter 5: Common Exception Words and Fluency
- **Key concepts**: high-frequency word, common exception word, tricky part, automatic, reread, expression.
- **Practice activities**: map tricky words such as said, was, one, they, come, and there; read decodable sentence strips; reread short texts with a partner.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which part can we sound out? Which part must we remember? How did rereading change your voice?
- **Work product**: a tricky-word card showing the word, the tricky part, and a sentence the child can read aloud.

## Chapter 6: Vocabulary and Meaning
- **Key concepts**: meaning, category, synonym, antonym, describe, explain, context.
- **Practice activities**: sort words into animal, place, action, and feeling groups; act out verbs; add describing words to pictures; choose a word that fits a sentence.
- **Teacher prompts**: What does this word mean? Can you show it with your body or drawing? Which word gives the best clue?
- **Work product**: a mini word map with a picture, child-friendly meaning, related words, and a sentence.

## Chapter 7: Morphology for Little Word Builders
- **Key concepts**: root word, suffix, plural, tense, -s, -es, -ing, -ed, -er, -est.
- **Practice activities**: add -s and -es to nouns; act out jump, jumping, jumped; compare tall, taller, tallest; sort words by suffix.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is the root word? What did the ending do? Does the word need a spelling change?
- **Work product**: a suffix strip showing one root word changed in three ways with matching drawings.

## Chapter 8: Sentence Play and Reading-Writing Transfer
- **Key concepts**: sentence, capital letter, finger space, full stop, question mark, and, because.
- **Practice activities**: orally rehearse a sentence before writing; rebuild cut-up sentences; join two ideas with and; write from a picture after reading a model sentence.
- **Teacher prompts**: Does it make sense when you read it back? Where does the capital letter go? Which word helped you spell another word?
- **Work product**: a three-sentence mini page that uses taught phonics, one tricky word, and one joining word.

## Capstone
Create a "Word Builder Workshop." Children choose a sound or spelling pattern, build a set of words, sort them, read them to a partner, teach one tricky word, and write a short caption or mini story using their words. The finished workshop includes word cards, one vocabulary map, and a read-aloud check.

## Assessment Ideas
- Listen to children orally blend and segment words, including CVC words and words with taught digraphs.
- Use short reading checks with real and pseudo words to see whether children apply grapheme-phoneme knowledge rather than guessing from pictures.
- Review spelling attempts for plausible sound choices, known spelling patterns, and correct use of common exception words.
- Ask children to explain a new word with a drawing, action, or child-friendly sentence.
- Grade the capstone on accurate sound work, readable word cards, confident talk, and transfer into a sentence or short text.
