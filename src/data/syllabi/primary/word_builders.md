# Word Builders
**ID**: `col-word-builders` · **Discipline**: English · **Year**: 1 (ages 5–6)
**Frameworks**: UK Phonics Screening Check, US Common Core ELA Grade 1, ACARA English Year 1

## Course Philosophy
This is the primary literacy course for Year 1. The emphasis is on **systematic synthetic phonics** — decoding words by blending sounds — and building fluency so children move from "learning to read" toward "reading to learn."

## Module 1: Phonics & Decoding (30%)
- All single letter sounds (a–z) — consolidation
- Consonant digraphs: sh, ch, th, ng, nk
- Vowel digraphs: ai, ee, igh, oa, oo (long and short)
- Split digraphs (magic e): a_e, i_e, o_e, u_e
- Blending CVC, CVCC, CCVC words (e.g. ship, frog, band)
- Adjacent consonants / clusters: bl, cr, st, tr, fl, gr
- **Questions**: "Sound out this word", blend-to-read, digraph identification, real vs nonsense words (aligned with UK Phonics Screening Check)

## Module 2: Sight Words & Fluency (25%)
- 50+ high-frequency / common exception words (the, said, come, were, there, one, do, today)
- Distinguish decodable vs tricky words
- Reading simple sentences with taught phonemes and sight words
- Re-reading for fluency and expression
- **Questions**: Sight word recognition, sentence completion, "which word fits?"

## Module 3: Reading Comprehension (25%)
- Retell a story in sequence (beginning, middle, end)
- Answer "who, what, where, when" questions about a short text
- Make simple predictions: "What do you think will happen next?"
- Identify characters, settings, and main events
- Distinguish fiction from non-fiction
- **Questions**: Comprehension MCQs, sequencing, character identification, prediction

## Module 4: Writing & Grammar Foundations (20%)
- Write simple sentences with a capital letter, finger spaces, and a full stop
- Use question marks and exclamation marks
- Spell words using taught phonics patterns
- Common suffixes: -s, -es, -ing, -ed, -er, -est
- "and" as a joining word (conjunction)
- Days of the week capitalised
- **Questions**: Punctuation correction, suffix matching, sentence building, spelling

## Stretch Zone
- Reading two-syllable words (e.g. sunset, rabbit)
- Inference: "How do you think the character feels? How do you know?"
- Writing 2–3 connected sentences
- Introduction to speech marks ("Hello," said Sam.)
- Alphabetical order
