# Science Discovery: Year 2
**ID**: `scienceYear2` · **Discipline**: Science · **Year**: 2 (ages 6–7)
**Frameworks**: UK KS1 Science Year 2, US NGSS K-2, ACARA Science Year 2

## Module 1: Living Things & Their Habitats (25%)
- Identify things that are living, dead, and have never been alive
- Describe how habitats provide basic needs (food, water, shelter)
- Match animals to their habitats (pond, woodland, ocean, desert, rainforest)
- Micro-habitats: under a log, in a rockpool, in the soil
- Simple food chains: source of food → predator
- Interdependence: "What would happen if all the bees disappeared?"
- **Questions**: Living/dead/never-alive sorting, habitat matching, food chain ordering

## Module 2: Plants — Growth & Survival (25%)
- What plants need to grow: light, water, warmth, nutrients from soil
- Observe and record plant growth over time
- Seeds and bulbs: how new plants begin
- Parts of a plant and their functions (roots absorb water, leaves catch light)
- Why plants are important: oxygen, food, habitats for animals
- **Questions**: "What would happen if a plant got no light?", growth sequencing, function matching

## Module 3: Animals Including Humans (25%)
- Life cycles: birth → growth → reproduction → death
- Offspring: animals produce young; some look like parents, some don't
- Basic needs for survival: water, food, air
- Importance of exercise, healthy eating, and hygiene for humans
- Compare the needs of different animals (a fish vs a dog vs a bird)
- **Questions**: Life cycle sequencing, basic needs identification, healthy choice scenarios

## Module 4: Uses of Everyday Materials (25%)
- Identify and compare suitability of materials for particular uses
- Why a window is made of glass (transparent), not wood (opaque)
- Squashing, bending, twisting, stretching — how materials change shape
- Recycling: some materials can be reused, reducing waste
- Compare natural vs man-made materials (wool vs polyester, wood vs plastic)
- Fair testing: change one variable, observe the result
- **Questions**: "Best material for…?" reasoning, property testing, recycling sorting

## Stretch Zone
- Simple classification keys (yes/no questions to identify an animal)
- Adaptation: "Why does a polar bear have thick fur?"
- Reversible vs irreversible changes (ice melting vs egg cooking)
- Introduction to scientific enquiry types: observing, comparing, testing
- Famous scientists: David Attenborough, Jane Goodall (real people do science!)
