# NAPLAN Prep (Year 5)
**ID**: `naplanYear5` · **Discipline**: Exam Prep · **Year**: 4 (shown in Year 4 as prep for the Year 5 NAPLAN test)
**Framework**: Australian NAPLAN

## Course Philosophy
Preparation for the Year 5 NAPLAN test, which is administered in March of Year 5. This course covers the four NAPLAN domains at a level appropriate for the end of Year 4/start of Year 5, mirroring the CBT format.

## Module 1: Reading (25%)
- Read and comprehend diverse texts (imaginative, informative, persuasive)
- Locate directly stated information in complex texts
- Infer meaning from context, including the main idea and author's purpose
- Identify the effect of literary devices (e.g., similes, metaphors)
- Interpret information in diagrams, maps, and tables within texts
- **Questions**: Complex inference, vocabulary in context, purpose identification, visual text analysis

## Module 2: Writing (25%)
- Narrative writing: develop character, setting, and plot with a clear climax and resolution
- Persuasive writing: structure arguments logically with an introduction, body paragraphs (with evidence), and conclusion
- Use a variety of sentence structures (simple, compound, complex)
- Choose precise and varied vocabulary to engage the reader
- Paragraphing correctly for changes in topic, time, or speaker
- **Questions**: Identifying strong arguments, selecting appropriate transitions, vocabulary enhancement

## Module 3: Language Conventions (25%)
- Spelling: complex sound-letter relationships, unaccented vowels, prefixes/suffixes (e.g., -tion, -sion), and silent letters
- Grammar: agreement (subject-verb, noun-pronoun), proper use of tenses, identifying clauses (main and subordinate)
- Punctuation: quotation marks for direct speech, commas to separate clauses, apostrophes for possession (singular and plural)
- **Questions**: Spelling error identification, grammar rule application, punctuation correction

## Module 4: Numeracy (25%)
- Number & Algebra: operations with numbers to 10,000; decimals to two places; fractions (equivalent, addition/subtraction with same denominator); financial mathematics (purchases and change)
- Measurement & Geometry: use am/pm and 24-hour time; calculate perimeter and area of rectangles; identify 3D objects from 2D representations; angles (acute, right, obtuse)
- Statistics & Probability: interpret data in column graphs, dot plots, and tables; describe probabilities of everyday events
- **Questions**: Multi-step word problems, fraction/decimal conversion, area/perimeter, probability estimation

## Stretch Zone
- Reading: synthesizing information from multiple texts
- Writing: using extended metaphors or rhetorical questions
- Numeracy: problem-solving involving fractions of quantities or elapsed time across days
