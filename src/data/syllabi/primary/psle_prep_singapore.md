# PSLE Prep (Singapore)
**ID**: `pslePrep` · **Discipline**: Exam Prep · **Year**: 6 (ages 11–12)
**Framework**: Singapore Primary School Leaving Examination (PSLE)

## Course Philosophy
The PSLE is the national exam taken by all students in Singapore at the end of Primary 6. It is known globally for its extreme rigor, particularly in Mathematics (the famous "heuristics" problem-solving methods) and Science (application to real-world scenarios). This course focuses on the advanced problem-solving techniques required for PSLE success.

## Module 1: Mathematics - Heuristics & Problem Solving (35%)
- **Model Drawing**: Using the Singapore Bar Model method to solve complex word problems involving fractions, ratios, and percentages
- **Before and After Concepts**: Solving problems where quantities change but a specific variable remains constant (e.g., constant difference, constant total)
- **Guess and Check / Assumption Method**: Solving problems involving two unknown variables (e.g., "There are chickens and cows. There are 20 heads and 56 legs.")
- **Working Backwards**: Solving multi-step problems by starting from the final outcome
- **Patterns**: Finding complex numerical and visual patterns, including finding the *n*th term
- **Geometry & Measurement**: Complex area and perimeter problems involving overlapping shapes and circles; volume of composite solids
- **Questions**: Multi-step word problems requiring bar models, "assumption method" problems, overlapping area geometry

## Module 2: Science - Application & Process Skills (35%)
- **Thematic Application**: Applying concepts from Diversity, Cycles, Systems, Energy, and Interactions to novel, real-world experiments
- **Process Skills**: Formulating hypotheses, identifying independent/dependent/control variables in experimental setups, analyzing data trends
- **Answering Techniques (CER)**: Using the Claim-Evidence-Reasoning structure for open-ended questions
- **Systems Integration**: Understanding how different systems interact (e.g., how the digestive, respiratory, and circulatory systems work together during exercise)
- **Energy Conversion**: Tracing energy pathways in complex setups (e.g., battery → electrical energy → light energy + heat energy)
- **Questions**: Analyzing experimental setups, identifying control variables, complex energy conversion chains, systems interaction

## Module 3: English - Language Use & Comprehension (30%)
- **Grammar Cloze & Editing**: Identifying and correcting nuanced grammatical errors in passages
- **Synthesis & Transformation**: Combining or rewriting sentences without changing their meaning, using specific connectors (e.g., "Despite...", "Not only... but also...", "No sooner had...")
- **Vocabulary Cloze**: Selecting the most precise word from a list of close synonyms to fit a specific context
- **Comprehension (Open-Ended)**: Answering questions that require deep inference, understanding of character motivation, and explaining the meaning of phrases in context
- **Visual Text Comprehension**: Analyzing advertisements, flyers, or web pages for implicit and explicit information
- **Questions**: Sentence synthesis/transformation, vocabulary cloze with nuanced synonyms, deep-inference comprehension

## Stretch Zone
- Math: "Spatial Visualization" problems requiring mental manipulation of 3D objects
- Science: Explaining anomalous results in experimental data
- English: Deducing the tone and attitude of the author towards a specific subject
