# PSLE Prep (Singapore)
**ID**: `pslePrep` . **Discipline**: Exam Prep . **Year**: 6 (ages 11-12)
**Framework**: Singapore Primary School Leaving Examination (PSLE)

## Course Aim
Prepare Primary 6 students for the PSLE with strong subject knowledge, flexible problem solving, clear written explanations, and steady exam routines. Students develop English comprehension and language accuracy, mathematics heuristics, science concept application, reasoning under pressure, and confidence in reviewing their own work. By the end, students should be able to choose suitable strategies, show working clearly, explain answers with evidence, manage paper timing, and use feedback without losing momentum.

## Course Design Notes
This course treats PSLE preparation as disciplined integration, not endless drilling. English, Mathematics, and Science receive sustained attention, with reasoning and exam habits woven through every chapter. Maths lessons emphasise model drawing, heuristics, fractions, ratios, percentages, geometry, and clear working. Science lessons connect concepts to process skills, data, variables, and concise explanations. English lessons develop grammar, vocabulary, synthesis, visual text, comprehension, and evidence-based responses. Practice should include timed sets, untimed strategy clinics, correction routines, oral explanation, and confidence-building reflection.

## Chapter 1: PSLE Readiness, Routines, and Self-Knowledge
- **Key concepts**: baseline, Achievement Level, paper format, target, accuracy, timing, stamina, reflection, correction.
- **Practice questions**: Complete short diagnostic tasks in English, Mathematics, and Science; classify errors by content, carelessness, question interpretation, timing, or incomplete explanation; practise checking answer spaces, units, keywords, and command words.
- **Student output**: A PSLE readiness map with subject strengths, high-priority gaps, pacing targets, correction routines, and one calm habit for before and during papers.

## Chapter 2: English Grammar, Vocabulary, and Language Use
- **Key concepts**: grammar cloze, editing, word class, tense, agreement, connector, vocabulary cloze, collocation, precision.
- **Practice questions**: Complete grammar cloze and editing passages; choose precise vocabulary from close options; identify context clues; revise sentence errors involving tense, subject-verb agreement, pronouns, prepositions, and connectors; explain why a distractor is tempting but wrong.
- **Student output**: A language accuracy log with corrected cloze and editing items, vocabulary-in-context notes, three sentence corrections, and a short explanation of one grammar rule.

## Chapter 3: English Comprehension, Visual Text, and Response Quality
- **Key concepts**: literal meaning, inference, evidence, character motivation, tone, phrase meaning, visual text, purpose, audience.
- **Practice questions**: Answer open-ended comprehension questions using evidence; infer feelings, motives, and cause-effect links; explain phrases in context; identify purpose and audience in visual texts; compare an overly vague answer with a precise PSLE-style response.
- **Student output**: A comprehension response pack with two evidence-based open-ended answers, one visual text analysis, one phrase-in-context explanation, and a revised answer that improves precision.

## Chapter 4: Maths Models, Fractions, Ratios, and Percentages
- **Key concepts**: bar model, unit, part-whole, comparison model, fraction, ratio, percentage, constant total, constant difference.
- **Practice questions**: Use model drawing for fraction, ratio, and percentage word problems; solve before-and-after questions; identify what stays constant; translate between models, equations, and answer sentences; check whether the final quantity matches the question.
- **Student output**: A model-drawing portfolio with three worked problems, each showing labelled bars, unit reasoning, calculations, answer sentence, and a check.

## Chapter 5: Maths Heuristics, Patterns, Geometry, and Data
- **Key concepts**: assumption method, guess and check, working backwards, pattern, n-th term, area, volume, angle, data, average.
- **Practice questions**: Solve assumption and guess-and-check problems; work backwards from final conditions; find numerical and visual patterns; solve area and perimeter problems with overlapping or composite shapes; use volume and angle facts; interpret tables and graphs with multi-step calculations.
- **Student output**: A heuristics case file with one assumption problem, one working-backwards problem, one pattern task, and one geometry or data task, all revised after feedback.

## Chapter 6: Science Concepts Across Themes
- **Key concepts**: diversity, cycles, systems, energy, interactions, adaptation, reproduction, matter, force, electricity.
- **Practice questions**: Apply core concepts to unfamiliar scenarios; compare organisms, materials, and systems; trace energy conversions; explain life cycles, plant and human systems, forces, heat, light, and electrical circuits; identify the concept being tested before writing the answer.
- **Student output**: A science concept map linking the five major themes to examples, common misconceptions, one diagram, and two corrected application questions.

## Chapter 7: Science Process Skills and Open-Ended Explanations
- **Key concepts**: variable, fair test, hypothesis, observation, data trend, evidence, conclusion, Claim-Evidence-Reasoning, precision.
- **Practice questions**: Identify independent, dependent, and controlled variables; interpret tables, graphs, and experimental setups; explain anomalous results cautiously; write open-ended answers with claim, evidence, and scientific reason; improve answers that are true but incomplete.
- **Student output**: A process-skills lab review with one variable table, one data-trend explanation, one improved open-ended answer, and a checklist for avoiding vague science language.

## Chapter 8: Integrated Timed Practice and Exam Confidence
- **Key concepts**: mixed review, pacing, priority, checking, resilience, error log, revision cycle, confidence, exam readiness.
- **Practice questions**: Complete timed English, Maths, and Science sections; practise deciding which questions to leave and return to; mark work with rubrics; redo selected questions untimed; compare first attempt, correction, and later retry performance.
- **Student output**: A final revision portfolio with subject score trends, error patterns, corrected exemplars, pacing reflections, and a realistic weekly plan for review, rest, and confidence.

## Capstone
Students complete a "PSLE Readiness Clinic" with three parts: a timed integrated practice set, a focused correction conference, and a personal revision briefing. The final submission includes one polished English answer, one fully modelled maths problem, one science open-ended response using evidence and reasoning, a subject-by-subject error analysis, and a short exam-day plan covering timing, checking, materials, rest, and mindset.

## Assessment Ideas
- Use diagnostic tasks to identify whether each student needs content reteaching, strategy support, language precision, or timing practice.
- Assess English through grammar accuracy, vocabulary precision, evidence-based comprehension, visual text interpretation, and answer clarity.
- Score maths for model choice, heuristic selection, complete working, calculation accuracy, units, and answer sentence.
- Review science answers for correct concept selection, variable control, evidence use, clear causal explanation, and precise vocabulary.
- Include oral explanation before written work so students can clarify reasoning before tightening exam-style answers.
- Track timed practice alongside correction quality; improvement should include fewer repeated errors, clearer working, and calmer pacing.
- Grade the capstone on balanced subject readiness, correction discipline, explanation quality, independence, and healthy exam habits.

## Research Notes
- SEAB identifies the PSLE as Singapore's national examination at the end of primary education and publishes current PSLE formats, rules, readiness resources, and candidate guidance: https://www.seab.gov.sg/psle
- Singapore MOE explains that the PSLE Score is the sum of Achievement Levels across four subjects, including English, Mathematics, Science, and Mother Tongue where applicable: https://www.moe.gov.sg/microsites/psle-fsbb/psle/psle-scoring-system.html
- MOE PSLE and Full Subject-Based Banding resources emphasise broader posting, scoring, and transition context, supporting preparation that builds readiness rather than narrow rank anxiety: https://www.moe.gov.sg/microsites/psle-fsbb/index.html
- Effective PSLE preparation should combine curriculum mastery, process skills, clear written explanation, timed practice, error analysis, and wellbeing routines so students can demonstrate what they know under exam conditions.
