# Science Discovery: Year 4
**ID**: `scienceYear4` | **Discipline**: Science | **Year**: 4 (ages 8-9)
**Frameworks**: UK KS2 Science Year 4, US NGSS Grade 3-5, ACARA Science Year 4

## Course Aim
Develop practical scientists who can classify living things, investigate materials and energy, read patterns in data, and explain everyday systems with evidence. Children study living things, states of matter, sound, electricity, teeth, digestion, food chains, the water cycle, and data handling through safe investigations, careful diagrams, simple models, and collaborative science talk.

## Course Design Notes
Year 4 science should feel active and increasingly precise. Learners still need concrete models and shared vocabulary, but they can now compare results, spot patterns, improve methods, and explain cause and effect. Chapters pair core content with working scientifically: classification keys, repeated measurements, labelled diagrams, tables, graphs, fair tests, safety routines, and short written conclusions.

## Chapter 1: Classification and Living Things
- **Key concepts**: organism, vertebrate, invertebrate, plant, characteristic, classify, group, key, identify, habitat.
- **Practice activities**: sort living things using visible features; build branching keys for leaves, minibeasts, and familiar animals; compare vertebrate groups; use careful drawings and labels to record organisms.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which feature did you use to make this group? Could another scientist follow your key? What evidence shows where this organism belongs?
- **Work product**: a classification key with at least six organisms, clear yes/no questions, and labelled observation sketches.

## Chapter 2: Habitats, Food Chains, and Environmental Change
- **Key concepts**: habitat, ecosystem, producer, consumer, predator, prey, food chain, adaptation, environmental change.
- **Practice activities**: survey a local habitat; build food chains from cards and drawings; identify producers, predators, and prey; discuss how pollution, building, planting, weather, or conservation can change habitats.
- **Teacher prompts**: Where does the energy in this food chain begin? What might happen if one organism disappears? Which changes help living things, and which create danger?
- **Work product**: a habitat evidence board with one food chain, organism counts, and a claim about environmental change supported by observations.

## Chapter 3: Teeth, Digestion, and Healthy Choices
- **Key concepts**: incisor, canine, molar, enamel, mouth, oesophagus, stomach, intestine, digestion, saliva, nutrition.
- **Practice activities**: compare tooth shapes and jobs; model chewing and stomach mixing with safe classroom materials; trace food through the digestive system; connect food choices to teeth, growth, energy, and health.
- **Teacher prompts**: How does tooth shape match its job? What changes food during digestion? Why does the body need different kinds of food?
- **Work product**: a digestion pathway diagram with tooth functions, major digestive organs, and one healthy habit supported by science.

## Chapter 4: States of Matter and Particle Models
- **Key concepts**: solid, liquid, gas, particle, property, container, flow, compress, melt, freeze, evaporate, condense.
- **Practice activities**: sort materials by state and justify tricky cases; use body or bead models to show particle spacing; observe melting and freezing safely; compare how liquids pour and gases spread.
- **Teacher prompts**: What property proves the state? How does the material change when it is heated or cooled? What does the particle model help us explain?
- **Work product**: a states of matter foldout with properties, particle diagrams, and examples of melting, freezing, evaporation, and condensation.

## Chapter 5: The Water Cycle and Weather Data
- **Key concepts**: evaporation, condensation, precipitation, collection, temperature, rate, cycle, water vapour, data.
- **Practice activities**: model the water cycle in a bag or bowl system; investigate evaporation in warm and cool places; collect rainfall or temperature data; make a simple line graph or bar chart from class measurements.
- **Teacher prompts**: Where is water changing state? How does temperature affect evaporation? What pattern do you see in the weather data?
- **Work product**: a water cycle data page with a labelled cycle diagram, measurements, a graph, and one conclusion about evaporation or weather.

## Chapter 6: Sound, Vibrations, Pitch, and Volume
- **Key concepts**: vibration, sound source, medium, ear, pitch, volume, distance, pattern, absorb, reflect.
- **Practice activities**: feel vibrations from rulers, rubber bands, drums, or tuning forks; compare pitch by changing string length or tension; compare volume with vibration strength and distance; test materials that muffle sound.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is vibrating? How did changing the object change the sound? What happens to sound as distance increases?
- **Work product**: a sound investigation sheet with labelled equipment, a results table, and explanations for pitch, volume, and distance effects.

## Chapter 7: Electricity and Simple Circuits
- **Key concepts**: cell, wire, bulb, buzzer, switch, circuit, complete loop, conductor, insulator, appliance, safety.
- **Practice activities**: build simple series circuits; test whether a bulb lights in open and closed circuits; use switches to control a component; sort conductors and insulators; identify safe uses of electricity at home and school.
- **Teacher prompts**: Is there a complete loop? What does the switch change? Which materials allow electricity to flow, and how do you know?
- **Work product**: a circuit builder page with a labelled circuit diagram, conductor test results, and a safety rule written for younger pupils.

## Chapter 8: Data Detectives and Fair Investigations
- **Key concepts**: fair test, variable, repeat, accuracy, table, bar chart, line graph, pattern, anomaly, conclusion.
- **Practice activities**: improve messy investigations by controlling variables; repeat measurements and compare results; choose tables, bar charts, or line graphs for different data; write conclusions that quote evidence.
- **Teacher prompts**: What will you change, measure, and keep the same? Do repeated results agree? Which graph best shows this pattern?
- **Work product**: a data detective report that revises a test method, shows repeated measurements, identifies a pattern, and explains one possible anomaly.

## Capstone
Design a "Science Systems Expo." Teams choose a system from the year, such as a habitat, digestive system, water cycle, sound maker, or electrical circuit, then create an interactive exhibit. Each exhibit must include a model or demonstration, labelled science diagram, safety note, data display, explanation of cause and effect, and a visitor question that can be answered with evidence.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe whether learners classify using observable characteristics, build usable keys, and revise groups when new evidence appears.
- Review living things work for accurate food chains, habitat vocabulary, producer and consumer roles, and thoughtful discussion of environmental change.
- Check digestion and teeth products for correct organ sequence, tooth functions, and age-appropriate links between nutrition and health.
- Assess matter and water cycle work through particle diagrams, state-change vocabulary, measurement habits, graph choice, and evidence-based conclusions.
- Evaluate sound and electricity investigations for safe equipment use, clear circuit diagrams, repeated trials, pattern recognition, and precise vocabulary.
- Grade the capstone on scientific accuracy, practical demonstration, data display, teamwork, clarity for visitors, and use of evidence in answers.
