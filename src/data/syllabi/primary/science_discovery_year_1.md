# Science Discovery: Year 1
**ID**: `scienceYear1` · **Discipline**: Science · **Year**: 1 (ages 5–6)
**Frameworks**: UK KS1 Science, US NGSS K-2, ACARA Science Year 1, India NCERT EVS Class 1

## Module 1: Everyday Materials (25%)
- Distinguish between an object and the material it's made from
- Identify materials: wood, plastic, glass, metal, fabric, rock, water
- Describe properties: hard/soft, rough/smooth, bendy/stiff, waterproof/not, transparent/opaque
- Group materials by property
- Simple tests: "Which material is best for an umbrella?" (waterproof test)
- **Questions**: Material identification, property matching, "which material would you choose?"

## Module 2: Animals Including Humans (25%)
- Identify and name common animals: mammals, birds, fish, reptiles, amphibians, insects
- Classify by diet: herbivore, carnivore, omnivore
- Name and label basic human body parts (head, neck, arm, elbow, leg, knee, back, face features)
- Senses and the body parts associated with each
- Basic needs of animals and humans: food, water, air, shelter
- **Questions**: Animal classification, body part labelling, diet sorting, sense matching

## Module 3: Plants (25%)
- Identify and name common wild and garden plants (daisy, dandelion, oak, rose)
- Identify and name the parts of a plant: root, stem, leaf, flower, petal
- Identify deciduous vs evergreen trees
- Basic needs of plants: light, water, warmth, soil
- Observe seasonal changes in plants (blossom → leaf → fruit → bare)
- **Questions**: Plant part labelling, deciduous vs evergreen, "what does a plant need?"

## Module 4: Seasonal Changes (25%)
- Observe and describe weather patterns associated with each season
- Day length: longer days in summer, shorter in winter
- How seasons affect animals (hibernation, migration, thick fur)
- How seasons affect humans (clothing, activities, food)
- Record weather over time using simple charts
- **Questions**: Season-weather matching, day length, animal behaviour by season, clothing choices

## Stretch Zone
- Simple food chains: producer → primary consumer → secondary consumer
- Introduction to habitats: "Where do these animals live and why?"
- Floating and sinking as a material property test
- The water cycle in very simple terms
- Introduction to "fair testing": change one thing, keep everything else the same
