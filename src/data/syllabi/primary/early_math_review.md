# Early Math Review
**ID**: `col-early-math-review` | **Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 1 (ages 5-6)
**Frameworks**: UK National Curriculum primary mathematics, Common Core K-2 Mathematics, NCETM primary mastery materials

## Course Aim
Rebuild early maths confidence for children who need a concrete, calm bridge into Year 1 mathematics. Children revisit counting, quantities, operations, shapes, measures, patterns, and word problems through manipulatives, drawings, number talk, games, and short practice loops that make success visible.

## Course Design Notes
This is a review course, so pace and emotional safety matter. Each lesson begins with objects before symbols: counters, cubes, bead strings, fingers, dot cards, dice, ten frames, number tracks, blocks, shape tiles, classroom objects, and drawings. Children practise in compact loops: build it, say it, draw it, write it, check it, play it again. Number talk is valued over speed. Mistakes are treated as information, and fluency grows through repeated low-pressure games rather than long worksheets.

## Chapter 1: Counting That Stays Matched
- **Key concepts**: count, one-to-one match, quantity, last number, forwards, backwards, zero.
- **Practice activities**: count objects by moving each one; count claps and steps; match numeral cards to sets; count forward and backward within 20 from different starting points.
- **Teacher prompts**: How did you keep track? Did each object get one count? What does zero mean here?
- **Work product**: a counting check card showing one set, count marks, a numeral, and a sentence such as "There are 8 shells."

## Chapter 2: Seeing Small Numbers Quickly
- **Key concepts**: subitise, dots, fingers, five-frame, ten-frame, more, fewer, equal.
- **Practice activities**: flash dot cards; show numbers with fingers in different ways; compare two towers; fill five-frames and ten-frames with counters.
- **Teacher prompts**: What did you see without counting? How many more to make 5? Are these groups equal?
- **Work product**: a "ways to see 5" or "ways to see 10" mat using dots, fingers, counters, and drawings.

## Chapter 3: Number Order and the Number Track
- **Key concepts**: before, after, between, one more, one less, first, next, number track.
- **Practice activities**: order numeral cards to 20; jump along a floor number track; hide and replace missing numbers; play one-more and one-less dice games.
- **Teacher prompts**: What number comes before? What changed when we moved one space? How do you know this card belongs here?
- **Work product**: a number track repair strip with missing numbers filled in and one one-more or one-less explanation.

## Chapter 4: Addition as Joining
- **Key concepts**: add, join, altogether, total, count on, number sentence, equals.
- **Practice activities**: join two groups of counters; roll two dice and build the total; use ten frames for small addition facts; act out stories with toys.
- **Teacher prompts**: Which group could you start with? Can you count on? What does the equals sign tell us?
- **Work product**: an addition story card with objects or drawings, a number sentence, and an answer sentence.

## Chapter 5: Subtraction as Taking Away
- **Key concepts**: subtract, take away, left, count back, fewer, difference.
- **Practice activities**: remove counters from a set; cross out pictures; walk backward on a number track; play "shopkeeper takes away" with pretend items.
- **Teacher prompts**: How many did we start with? How many did we take away? How can we check what is left?
- **Work product**: a subtraction drawing showing start, take away, left, and a matching number sentence.

## Chapter 6: Number Bonds, Doubles, and Helpful Facts
- **Key concepts**: part, whole, bond, pair, double, near double, fact, check.
- **Practice activities**: split counters on a part-whole mat; make bonds to 5 and 10 with bead strings; build doubles with mirror mats; play memory games matching facts to pictures.
- **Teacher prompts**: What are the two parts? What whole do they make? How does knowing double 3 help with 3 and 4?
- **Work product**: a part-whole page for one number, including at least three ways to make it.

## Chapter 7: Shapes, Patterns, and Sorting
- **Key concepts**: circle, square, triangle, rectangle, side, corner, sort, pattern, repeat, rule.
- **Practice activities**: sort shape tiles by sides or corners; build shapes with sticks and dough; continue AB, ABB, and ABC patterns; sort classroom objects by child-chosen rules.
- **Teacher prompts**: What is your sorting rule? What repeats in this pattern? Can this shape turn and still be the same shape?
- **Work product**: a pattern-and-sort page with one completed pattern and one labeled sorting rule.

## Chapter 8: Measures, Position, and Word Problems
- **Key concepts**: longer, shorter, taller, heavier, lighter, full, empty, in, on, under, beside, story problem.
- **Practice activities**: compare lengths with cubes; order objects by height or weight; give partner position clues; act out word problems before drawing or writing.
- **Teacher prompts**: What are we comparing? What information matters in the story? Does the answer make sense?
- **Work product**: a solved classroom problem with a manipulative model, quick drawing, number sentence or label, and oral check.

## Capstone
Children create a "Maths Confidence Arcade" with five short stations: count and match, see it fast, one more or one less, join or take away, and shape or measure. Each station uses real objects, a quick drawing, a child-friendly prompt, and a way to check the answer. Children play through the stations with a partner, explaining one strategy they trust.

## Assessment Ideas
- Observe one-to-one counting, stable order, cardinality, and recognition of zero during object tasks.
- Use short number talks with dot cards, fingers, five-frames, and ten-frames to hear subitising and comparison language.
- Review addition and subtraction cards for alignment between objects, drawings, symbols, and oral explanations.
- Check number bonds, doubles, and one-more or one-less facts through games before expecting independent written recall.
- Assess the capstone on confident participation, clear models, correct matching of story to operation, and willingness to explain or check.

## Research Notes
- The UK National Curriculum for primary mathematics emphasizes fluency, reasoning, problem solving, number, measurement, and geometry from Key Stage 1: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/national-curriculum-in-england-mathematics-programmes-of-study
- Common Core K-2 mathematics develops counting and cardinality, operations, base-ten understanding, measurement, data, and geometry through connected representations: https://www.corestandards.org/Math/
- NCETM primary mastery materials emphasize concrete representations, stem sentences, variation, and mathematical discussion before abstract recording: https://www.ncetm.org.uk/classroom-resources/primm-primary-mastery-professional-development/
- Early maths review is strongest when children connect manipulatives, drawings, talk, symbols, games, and short repeated practice in a low-pressure setting.
