# Floe Pre-School Syllabi (Internal)
## Target Age: 3–5 years (Pre-K / Reception / Kindergarten)
## Frameworks Referenced: UK EYFS 2024, US Common Core (K alignment), Australia EYLF v2.0, India NEP 2020 Foundational Stage

> **Purpose**: This document is the internal curriculum backbone for every course
> in the Pre-school age bucket. It drives question generation, lesson sequencing,
> and difficulty curves. Each syllabus has a "Stretch Zone" at the end — content
> that goes slightly beyond standard expectations for this age, designed to
> reward curiosity and gently introduce the next stage of thinking.

## 1. Primary Foundations
**ID**: `primary` · **Status**: playable · **Discipline**: Academics

### Course Philosophy
This is the flagship pre-school course — the generalist "first day of school"
experience. It synthesizes the three pillars that every early-years framework
agrees on: **number sense**, **emergent literacy**, and **logical reasoning**.
The goal is school-readiness without the pressure of formal instruction.

### Module 1: Number Sense & Counting (25%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Count objects from 1–10 with one-to-one correspondence
- Recognise written numerals 0–9
- Understand "more," "fewer," and "the same"
- Compare groups of objects (which has more?)
- Understand cardinality (the last number you say is how many there are)
- Subitise small quantities (instantly recognise 1–4 without counting)

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Counting objects in a picture (1–10)
2. Matching numerals to quantities
3. Ordering numbers on a number line (1–5, then 1–10)
4. "Which group has more?" comparison questions
5. Simple addition stories: "You have 2 apples. You get 1 more. How many?"
6. Missing number in a sequence (1, 2, __, 4)

**Stretch Zone**:
- Count backwards from 10
- Introduce the concept of zero as "nothing" / empty set
- Simple patterns with numbers (2, 4, 6… what comes next?)
- Counting beyond 10 to 20

### Module 2: Emergent Literacy (25%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Recognise uppercase letters A–Z
- Identify initial sounds in words (phonemic awareness)
- Understand that print carries meaning (left-to-right, top-to-bottom)
- Identify rhyming words
- Retell a simple story in correct sequence (beginning, middle, end)
- Recognise own name in print

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Letter identification (show a letter, name it)
2. Initial sound matching: "Which picture starts with the /b/ sound?"
3. Rhyming pairs: "Which word rhymes with 'cat'?"
4. Story sequencing: Put 3 pictures in the right order
5. Vocabulary building: Name common objects (animals, food, body parts)
6. Sight words: Recognise high-frequency words (I, the, a, is, my)

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce lowercase letters and letter-case matching
- Blend CVC words (c-a-t → cat)
- Simple sentence reading: "The cat sat."
- Introduce question marks and full stops as "meaning marks"

### Module 3: Shapes, Patterns & Spatial Reasoning (25%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Identify and name 2D shapes (circle, square, triangle, rectangle)
- Recognise shapes in the real world
- Complete and extend simple AB, ABB, ABC patterns
- Understand spatial vocabulary (above, below, next to, inside, outside, between)
- Sort objects by one attribute (colour, shape, or size)

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Shape identification from pictures
2. "Which shape is different?" (odd one out)
3. Pattern completion: 🔴🔵🔴🔵__
4. "Where is the ball?" spatial reasoning questions
5. Sorting: "Which things are the same colour/shape?"
6. Real-world shapes: "What shape is a clock?" (circle)

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce 3D shapes (sphere, cube, cylinder, cone)
- Two-attribute sorting (big AND red)
- Symmetry: "Which is the mirror image?"
- Tangram-style "build this shape" puzzles

### Module 4: Logic, Reasoning & Problem Solving (25%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Identify what doesn't belong in a group
- Make simple predictions based on patterns
- Understand cause and effect in simple scenarios
- Follow two-step instructions
- Classify and categorise objects by function or type

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Odd one out: "Which doesn't belong? Apple, banana, car, grape"
2. "What comes next?" in visual sequences
3. Cause and effect: "If you drop a glass, what happens?"
4. Simple analogies: "A bird has feathers, a fish has…"
5. "What do you need?" scenarios (It's raining — umbrella, sunglasses, or hat?)
6. Sequential reasoning: "First we put on socks, then…"

**Stretch Zone**:
- Simple mazes and path-finding
- "If… then…" conditional reasoning
- Basic Venn diagram sorting (things that are toys AND things that are red)
- Early deduction: "Sam has a hat. The hat is not blue. What colour could it be?"

## 2. Science Discovery: Early Years
**ID**: `scienceEarlyYears` · **Status**: soon · **Discipline**: Science

### Course Philosophy
This course introduces the **scientific method as a way of being curious**, not
as a formal process. At this age, science is observation, wonder, and play.
Aligned with EYFS "Understanding the World" and EYLF Outcome 4 (confident and
involved learners). The emphasis is on the five senses as scientific instruments
and nature as the primary laboratory.

### Module 1: My Five Senses (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Name the five senses and their associated body parts
- Use senses to describe objects (rough/smooth, loud/quiet, sweet/sour)
- Understand that we learn about the world through our senses
- Make observations and describe what they notice

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Match the sense to the body part (eyes → seeing)
2. "Which sense would you use to…?" questions
3. Sensory descriptions: "Is sandpaper rough or smooth?"
4. Mystery identification: "If something smells like flowers and feels soft, what might it be?"
5. Sensory comparison: "Which is louder — a whisper or a shout?"

**Stretch Zone**:
- Optical illusions for young children (things aren't always what they seem)
- Introduce the idea that some animals have different/better senses than us

### Module 2: Weather & Seasons (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Identify types of weather (sunny, rainy, cloudy, windy, snowy)
- Understand that weather changes daily and seasonally
- Know the four seasons and basic characteristics of each
- Connect weather to daily life (clothing, activities)

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Weather identification from pictures or descriptions
2. "What would you wear on a rainy day?"
3. Season matching: Snowman → winter, flowers → spring
4. Sequencing the seasons in the correct cycle
5. Prediction: "If the sky is full of dark clouds, what might happen?"
6. Temperature concepts: Hot vs. cold (not numerical, contextual)

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce the water cycle as a simple story (rain falls, puddles dry, clouds form)
- Day length differences across seasons
- Weather in different countries (it can be summer in Australia when it's winter in the UK)

### Module 3: Plants & Growing Things (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Understand that plants are living things
- Know what plants need to grow (water, sunlight, soil)
- Name basic parts of a plant (root, stem, leaf, flower)
- Understand the concept of a seed growing into a plant

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. "What does a plant need?" selection questions
2. Label the parts of a plant
3. Sequencing: seed → sprout → plant → flower
4. "Which is a plant?" identification
5. Prediction: "What will happen if we don't water the plant?"
6. Matching fruits/vegetables to their plants

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce the idea that plants make food from sunlight (photosynthesis as a concept, not a term)
- Edible vs. non-edible plants
- Trees as very large plants — parts of a tree

### Module 4: Animals & Habitats (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Identify common animals and categorise them (farm, wild, pet, ocean)
- Understand that different animals live in different places (habitats)
- Know basic needs of animals (food, water, shelter)
- Recognise baby animals and their parent names (puppy→dog, kitten→cat)

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. "Where does this animal live?" habitat matching
2. Animal classification: "Which of these is a farm animal?"
3. Baby animal matching: "A baby sheep is called a…"
4. Animal sounds and features identification
5. "What does a fish need to live?" (water, food)
6. Compare: "How are a cat and a dog similar? How are they different?"

**Stretch Zone**:
- Simple food chains: "The caterpillar eats the leaf. The bird eats the caterpillar."
- Nocturnal vs. diurnal animals
- Migration and hibernation as simple concepts
- Endangered animals: "Some animals need our help"

### Module 5: My Body & Staying Healthy (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Name major body parts (head, arms, legs, eyes, ears, mouth, hands, feet)
- Understand basic hygiene (washing hands, brushing teeth)
- Know that food and sleep help us grow
- Understand that exercise keeps us healthy

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. "Point to the…" body part identification
2. Hygiene sequencing: "What do you do before eating?" (wash hands)
3. Healthy vs. unhealthy food choices
4. "Why do we sleep?" understanding rest
5. Matching activities to body parts: "We kick a ball with our…"
6. Feelings and the body: "When you're scared, your heart beats…"

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce the skeleton as "the bones inside us"
- The heart as a pump — feel your pulse
- Germs as tiny things we can't see
- Teeth: baby teeth vs. grown-up teeth

## 3. Big Questions: Early Years
**ID**: `philosophyEarlyYears` · **Status**: soon · **Discipline**: Humanities

### Course Philosophy
This is our Philosophy for Children (P4C) track adapted for the youngest
learners. At this age, philosophy is about **wondering, asking "why," and
learning that there can be more than one right answer**. Aligned with EYFS
Personal, Social and Emotional Development (PSED) and EYLF Outcomes 1 & 2
(identity and connection). This course builds the foundations of ethical
thinking, empathy, and critical reasoning through stories and scenarios.

### Module 1: Fairness & Sharing (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Understand what "fair" means in everyday situations
- Recognise when something feels unfair
- Think about how to share when there isn't enough for everyone
- Distinguish between "equal" and "fair" (everyone gets what they need)

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Scenario: "Two children want the same toy. What should they do?"
2. "Is this fair?" judgment questions with picture scenarios
3. "What would you do?" open-response choices
4. Sharing strategies: taking turns, splitting equally, compromise
5. "Does fair always mean the same?" (A little child and a big child both need food — do they eat the same amount?)

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce the idea that rules exist to help things be fair
- "Who decides what's fair?" — introducing the concept of authority
- Simple dilemma: "You found a toy. No one is looking. What do you do?"

### Module 2: Feelings & Empathy (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Name and recognise basic emotions (happy, sad, angry, scared, surprised)
- Understand that other people have feelings too
- Begin to understand that the same event can make different people feel different things
- Learn simple strategies for managing big feelings

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Emotion identification from facial expressions
2. "How would you feel if…?" scenario questions
3. "How does [character] feel?" reading emotions in a story context
4. "What could you do if you feel angry?" coping strategy selection
5. Perspective-taking: "Your friend is crying. What might have happened?"
6. "Can two people feel different things about the same thing?" (Yes — one child loves dogs, another is scared of them)

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce "mixed feelings" (excited AND nervous about starting school)
- The idea that feelings change — you won't feel sad forever
- "What makes a good friend?" — qualities beyond "someone who plays with me"

### Module 3: Right & Wrong (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Distinguish between kind and unkind actions
- Understand basic rules and why they exist
- Recognise that actions have consequences
- Begin to understand the difference between "on purpose" and "by accident"

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. "Was this kind or unkind?" action judgment
2. "What might happen next?" consequence prediction
3. "Why do we have rules?" reasoning questions
4. Scenario: "Sam pushed Alex. Sam says it was an accident. Alex is hurt. What should happen?"
5. "Is it ever okay to break a rule?" introducing nuance
6. Apologising: "What makes a good sorry?"

**Stretch Zone**:
- "Is it wrong if nobody sees you do it?" — introducing moral reasoning beyond consequences
- The difference between a mistake and a choice
- "White lies" — is it ever okay to not tell the truth? (e.g., to avoid hurting feelings)

### Module 4: Identity & Belonging (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Understand that everyone is unique and that's valuable
- Recognise things that make them similar to and different from others
- Understand the concept of family and that families come in many forms
- Feel a sense of belonging to groups (family, class, community)

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. "What makes you, YOU?" self-reflection prompts
2. "How are these two children the same? How are they different?"
3. "Who is in your family?" — diverse family structures
4. "What groups do you belong to?" (family, class, neighbourhood)
5. Celebrating differences: "Is it good that we're all different? Why?"
6. Cultural awareness: "People celebrate different things. Can you match the celebration?"

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce the idea of culture and tradition
- "Where do you come from?" — geography of identity
- Gender: "Can boys like pink? Can girls like trucks?" — challenging stereotypes
- Disability awareness: "Some people's bodies work differently. What can we do to include everyone?"

### Module 5: Wondering & Asking Why (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Develop comfort with open-ended questions
- Understand that "I don't know" is a good starting point, not a failure
- Practice giving reasons for opinions ("I think… because…")
- Begin to understand that some questions don't have one right answer

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. "Big wonder" questions: "Why is the sky blue?" — process of wondering matters more than the answer
2. "I think… because…" sentence starters with opinion questions
3. "Can you think of a reason why someone might disagree?"
4. "What's the difference between a question and an answer?"
5. "Is there always one right answer?" — introducing ambiguity
6. Philosophy through stories: After a simple tale, "Was the character right to do that?"

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce the Socratic method at a baby level: "But what if someone said…?"
- "How do you know something is true?" — very early epistemology
- "Is a dream real?" — the nature of reality
- "If animals could talk, what would they say?" — imagination as a philosophical tool

## 4. Pre-K through grade 2 basics (Collection)
**ID**: `col-pre-k-through-grade-2-basics` · **Discipline**: Kids Discovery

### Course Philosophy
This is the broadest generalist collection for the youngest users, covering
the full pre-K to 2nd grade spectrum. It functions as a "sample platter"
that introduces children to the joy of learning across multiple domains. Questions
should be play-oriented, visual, and forgiving.

### Syllabus Overview
Since this is a collection-type course (dynamically generated from the Kids
Discovery group), the syllabus maps to the following domains:

**Domain A: Early Numeracy (30%)**
- Counting 1–20
- Number recognition
- Simple addition and subtraction within 5
- Shape recognition (2D)
- Patterns (AB, ABB, ABC)
- Measurement vocabulary (big/small, tall/short, heavy/light)

**Domain B: Early Literacy (30%)**
- Letter recognition (upper and lower case)
- Letter-sound correspondence (phonics)
- Rhyming
- High-frequency sight words (10–20 words)
- Story comprehension (who, what, where)
- Vocabulary (colours, animals, body parts, food, clothing)

**Domain C: World Knowledge (20%)**
- Community helpers (doctor, teacher, firefighter)
- Seasons and weather
- Maps and where things are (my house, my school, my country)
- Celebrations and holidays from around the world
- Animals and their homes

**Domain D: Social & Emotional Learning (20%)**
- Naming feelings
- Taking turns and sharing
- Being a good friend
- Understanding rules
- Kindness and helpfulness
- Dealing with frustration

**Stretch Zone**:
- Simple coding concepts (sequencing instructions for a robot)
- Introduction to time (morning, afternoon, night; days of the week)
- Money awareness (coins exist, things cost money)

## 5. Up to 2nd grade skills (Collection)
**ID**: `col-up-to-2nd-grade-skills` · **Discipline**: Kids Discovery

### Syllabus Overview
This collection targets the upper end of the pre-school bracket — children
who are at or approaching the 2nd grade level. Content should feel like a
graduation from the "basics" collection above.

**Domain A: Maths Fluency (30%)**
- Addition and subtraction within 10
- Skip counting by 2s, 5s, 10s
- Place value (tens and ones)
- Telling time to the hour
- Simple measurement (length comparison, non-standard units)
- Introduction to fractions (half, quarter — visual/concrete)

**Domain B: Reading & Writing (30%)**
- Decoding CVC and CVCC words
- Reading simple sentences
- Comprehension: "What happened in the story?"
- Writing: Complete a sentence with the correct word
- Punctuation awareness (full stop, question mark)
- Vocabulary expansion (synonyms, antonyms at a basic level)

**Domain C: Science & Discovery (20%)**
- Properties of materials (hard/soft, shiny/dull, rough/smooth)
- Forces: push and pull
- Light and shadow
- Sound: loud/quiet, high/low
- Living vs. non-living things
- Magnets (attract/repel at a conceptual level)

**Domain D: Social Studies & SEL (20%)**
- My neighbourhood: who lives and works here
- Rules at home vs. rules at school
- Needs vs. wants
- Recycling and taking care of the environment
- Respecting differences
- Problem-solving in social situations

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduction to multiplication as "groups of"
- Reading short paragraphs and answering inference questions
- Basic map reading (compass directions, simple keys)
- Scientific method as a 3-step process: Question → Test → What happened?

## 6. Social & emotional learning (Collection)
**ID**: `col-social-emotional-learning` · **Discipline**: Kids Discovery

### Course Philosophy
This is our dedicated SEL (Social-Emotional Learning) track, aligned with
CASEL's five competencies adapted for early childhood: self-awareness,
self-management, social awareness, relationship skills, and responsible
decision-making. In a quiz format, these translate to scenario-based judgment
questions, emotion identification, and perspective-taking exercises.

### Module 1: Self-Awareness (20%)
- Name and recognise emotions in self
- Identify personal strengths ("I am good at…")
- Understand that everyone has things they find hard
- Body awareness: how emotions feel physically (butterflies in tummy, hot cheeks)

### Module 2: Self-Management (20%)
- Calming strategies (deep breathing, counting to 5, squeezing hands)
- Waiting and patience
- Following instructions and routines
- Dealing with disappointment ("I didn't win the game")
- Impulse control: "Stop, think, choose"

### Module 3: Social Awareness (20%)
- Recognising emotions in others (facial expressions, body language)
- Understanding that not everyone feels the same way you do
- Cultural sensitivity: different families, foods, celebrations
- Kindness towards animals and nature
- "How would you feel if someone did that to you?"

### Module 4: Relationship Skills (20%)
- What makes a good friend
- Asking to join a game nicely
- Saying sorry and meaning it
- Helping someone who is struggling
- Working together (taking turns, compromising)
- Dealing with disagreements without hitting or shouting

### Module 5: Responsible Decision-Making (20%)
- Thinking before acting
- Understanding consequences: "If I do X, then Y might happen"
- Asking for help when needed
- Being honest even when it's hard
- Taking care of shared spaces and things
- Standing up for someone who is being treated unfairly

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduction to "growth mindset" — your brain grows when you try hard things
- Gratitude: "What are you thankful for?"
- Leadership: "What does it mean to be a leader? Is it the same as being bossy?"
- Resilience: "What do you do when something goes wrong?"

### Recommendation
All six courses are correctly bucketed. The pre-school stage has good coverage
across the key domains:
- ✅ **Numeracy** (Primary Foundations, Collections)
- ✅ **Literacy** (Primary Foundations, Collections)
- ✅ **Science** (Science Discovery)
- ✅ **Philosophy/Ethics** (Big Questions)
- ✅ **SEL** (Social & emotional learning, Big Questions overlap)

### Gap Analysis
The following areas could be added in a future iteration:
- **Physical Development / Motor Skills** — not easily quizzable but could include
  knowledge questions about health, exercise, and body awareness
- **Creative Arts** — music, art appreciation, colour mixing, instrument
  identification
- **Digital Literacy** — age-appropriate screen safety, basic coding concepts
  (sequencing, instructions)
