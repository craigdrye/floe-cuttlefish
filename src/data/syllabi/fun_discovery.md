# Floe Fun Discovery Syllabi (Internal)

> **Purpose**: Internal curriculum for the 8 Fun Discovery courses. Drives question generation.
> Each has a "Stretch Zone" to push beyond standard expectations.

## 1. Dinosaur World (Pre-school, ages 3–5)
**ID**: `dinosPreschool` · **Status**: playable

### Module 1: Meet the Dinosaurs (25%)
- What is a dinosaur? Ancient reptiles, lived millions of years ago
- Famous dinos: T-Rex, Triceratops, Stegosaurus, Brachiosaurus, Pteranodon
- Size comparison: "As big as a bus" / "as small as a chicken"
- Dino sounds and movements (stomp, roar, fly, swim)
- **Questions**: Name the dino from a picture, match dino to silhouette

### Module 2: What Did They Eat? (25%)
- Herbivore = plant eater (flat teeth, long necks) — Brachiosaurus, Triceratops
- Carnivore = meat eater (sharp teeth, claws) — T-Rex, Velociraptor
- Omnivore = eats both
- Matching teeth shape to diet
- **Questions**: "Is this dino a plant eater or meat eater?", sort by diet

### Module 3: Fossils & Discovery (25%)
- What is a fossil? Bones/footprints turned to rock
- Paleontologists = dinosaur detectives
- Where fossils are found (in rocks, underground)
- Fossil dig concept — brushing away sand
- **Questions**: "What is a fossil?", "Who studies dinosaurs?"

### Module 4: When Dinosaurs Disappeared (25%)
- Dinosaurs are not alive today (extinct)
- Simple explanation: big asteroid, world changed
- Some relatives still here: birds are living dinosaurs!
- **Questions**: "Are dinosaurs alive today?", "What animal is related to dinosaurs?"

**Stretch Zone**: Introduce Triassic/Jurassic/Cretaceous as "chapters"; flying reptiles vs dinosaurs; marine reptiles

## 2. Dinosaur Science (Primary, ages 5–11)
**ID**: `dinosPrimary` · **Status**: playable

### Module 1: The Mesozoic Era (25%)
- Three periods: Triassic (252–201 mya), Jurassic (201–145 mya), Cretaceous (145–66 mya)
- Continental drift — Pangaea breaking apart
- Climate differences across periods
- Key species per period (Coelophysis → Allosaurus → T-Rex)
- **Questions**: Timeline ordering, period-to-dino matching

### Module 2: Anatomy & Adaptation (25%)
- Skeletal structure: bipedal vs quadrupedal
- Armour, horns, plates, feathers — what were they for?
- Warm-blooded vs cold-blooded debate
- Comparing dino anatomy to modern animals
- **Questions**: "What was the Stegosaurus's plates for?", anatomy labelling

### Module 3: Fossils & The Fossil Record (25%)
- How fossils form (sedimentary layers, mineralisation)
- Types: body fossils, trace fossils (footprints, coprolites)
- Famous fossil sites: Badlands, Gobi Desert, Jurassic Coast (UK)
- Mary Anning — pioneering paleontologist
- **Questions**: Fossilisation sequencing, "What can footprints tell us?"

### Module 4: Extinction & Legacy (25%)
- The K-Pg extinction event (asteroid impact, Chicxulub)
- Evidence: iridium layer, impact crater
- Survivors: mammals, birds, crocodilians
- Birds as living theropod dinosaurs (feathered dinos → birds)
- Evolution concept: adaptation over time
- **Questions**: "What evidence supports the asteroid theory?", evolutionary links

**Stretch Zone**: Phylogenetic trees, carbon dating concept, ongoing discoveries (2020s feathered dino finds), dinosaur behaviour inference from trackways

## 3. Space Explorers (Pre-school, ages 3–5)
**ID**: `planetsPreschool` · **Status**: playable

### Module 1: The Sun, Moon & Stars (25%)
- The Sun: a star, very hot, gives us light and warmth
- Day and night: Earth spinning
- The Moon: orbits Earth, changes shape (phases as "the Moon's haircut")
- Stars: far-away suns, only visible at night
- **Questions**: "What gives us light during the day?", Moon phase matching

### Module 2: Our Planet Earth (25%)
- Earth is round (a sphere)
- Land and water — mostly water!
- Sky, clouds, atmosphere as a "blanket"
- Earth is special: air, water, just right temperature
- **Questions**: "What colour is Earth from space?", land vs water identification

### Module 3: The Planets (25%)
- 8 planets orbiting the Sun
- Planet ordering (My Very Excited Mother Just Served Us Nachos)
- Simple facts: Mercury (hot & small), Jupiter (biggest), Saturn (rings), Mars (red)
- Planet vs star: planets orbit, stars make their own light
- **Questions**: Planet ordering, "Which planet has rings?", size comparisons

### Module 4: Astronauts & Space Travel (25%)
- What astronauts do (explore, experiments, float!)
- Rockets: how we get to space
- The International Space Station
- Famous astronauts: Neil Armstrong, Mae Jemison, Tim Peake, Kalpana Chawla
- **Questions**: "Who was the first person on the Moon?", astronaut gear identification

**Stretch Zone**: Gravity as "what keeps us on the ground"; constellations as sky pictures; dwarf planets (Pluto); "Is there life on other planets?"

## 4. Solar System & Beyond (Primary, ages 5–11)
**ID**: `planetsPrimary` · **Status**: playable

### Module 1: The Solar System (25%)
- Sun at the centre, 8 planets, dwarf planets, asteroid belt
- Inner rocky planets vs outer gas giants
- Orbital periods and distances (AU concept)
- Moons: Earth has 1, Jupiter has 95+
- Planetary characteristics: temperature, atmosphere, surface
- **Questions**: Classification (rocky/gas), ordering by distance, fact matching

### Module 2: Earth, Moon & Gravity (25%)
- Earth's tilt → seasons
- Moon phases: new, crescent, quarter, gibbous, full
- Tides caused by Moon's gravity
- Eclipses: solar and lunar (simple diagrams)
- Gravity: what it is, why heavier on Jupiter, lighter on Moon
- **Questions**: Phase identification, season-tilt connection, eclipse types

### Module 3: Stars, Galaxies & The Universe (25%)
- Stars have life cycles (born in nebulae, die as white dwarfs or supernovae)
- Constellations: patterns humans see (Orion, Big Dipper/Plough, Southern Cross)
- The Milky Way: our galaxy, billions of stars
- Light years as a distance measure
- **Questions**: Star lifecycle ordering, constellation matching, galaxy facts

### Module 4: Space Exploration (25%)
- History: Sputnik, Apollo 11, Hubble, ISS, Mars rovers
- Current missions: James Webb Space Telescope, Artemis program
- Space agencies: NASA, ESA, ISRO, JAXA
- The future: Mars colonisation, private spaceflight
- **Questions**: Timeline ordering, mission-to-discovery matching

**Stretch Zone**: Exoplanets and the habitable zone; black holes; the Big Bang (simple version); space debris problem; careers in space science

## 5. Ocean Adventures (Pre-school, ages 3–5)
**ID**: `oceanPreschool` · **Status**: playable

### Module 1: Who Lives in the Sea? (25%)
- Common sea animals: whale, dolphin, shark, turtle, octopus, jellyfish, starfish, clownfish
- Fish vs mammals in the sea (dolphins breathe air!)
- Biggest (blue whale) and smallest sea creatures
- Shells on the beach — who lived inside?
- **Questions**: Animal identification, "Does it breathe underwater?", size sorting

### Module 2: The Beach & Rockpools (25%)
- What you find at the beach: sand, shells, seaweed, crabs
- Rockpools as mini-worlds (anemones, hermit crabs, small fish)
- Tides: the sea comes in and goes out
- Beach safety basics
- **Questions**: Rockpool creature matching, tides concept, beach item identification

### Module 3: Coral Reefs (25%)
- Coral reefs = underwater cities full of colour
- Coral is alive! Tiny animals called polyps
- Clownfish and anemone friendship (symbiosis without the word)
- Why coral reefs are important: home for thousands of species
- **Questions**: "Is coral a plant or animal?", reef creature identification

### Module 4: Taking Care of Our Oceans (25%)
- Pollution: plastic in the ocean hurts animals
- "Reduce, reuse, recycle" connection to ocean health
- Oil spills and their impact (simple)
- What kids can do: pick up litter, use less plastic
- **Questions**: "How does plastic hurt sea animals?", good vs bad choices for oceans

**Stretch Zone**: Bioluminescence ("animals that glow in the dark!"); the deepest place on Earth (Mariana Trench); whale songs; migration patterns

## 6. Ocean Science (Primary, ages 5–11)
**ID**: `oceanPrimary` · **Status**: playable

### Module 1: Ocean Zones (25%)
- Sunlight zone (0–200m): most life, photosynthesis
- Twilight zone (200–1000m): dim, bioluminescent creatures
- Midnight zone (1000m+): pitch black, extreme pressure, hydrothermal vents
- Hadal zone: ocean trenches
- **Questions**: Zone depth matching, "Which zone has no sunlight?", creature-to-zone sorting

### Module 2: Marine Ecosystems & Food Chains (25%)
- Producers: phytoplankton, algae, seagrass
- Primary consumers: krill, zooplankton, small fish
- Apex predators: great white shark, orca
- Food webs vs food chains
- Coral reef, kelp forest, open ocean, deep sea as distinct ecosystems
- **Questions**: Food chain ordering, ecosystem matching, predator-prey relationships

### Module 3: Marine Biology (25%)
- Fish anatomy: gills, fins, scales, swim bladder
- Marine mammals: whales, dolphins, seals — adaptations for ocean life
- Cephalopods: octopus intelligence, camouflage, ink defence
- Migration: grey whales, sea turtles, salmon
- **Questions**: Adaptation matching, "How do dolphins breathe?", migration routes

### Module 4: Ocean Conservation (25%)
- Coral bleaching: causes (warming seas) and consequences
- Overfishing and sustainable fishing
- Plastic pollution: microplastics, the Great Pacific Garbage Patch
- Marine protected areas (MPAs)
- Rising sea levels and coastal impact
- **Questions**: Threat identification, conservation strategy matching, cause-and-effect

**Stretch Zone**: Ocean currents and climate regulation; SONAR and ocean mapping; deep-sea exploration technology (submersibles); marine archaeology; careers in marine science

## 7. Bugs & Minibeasts (Pre-school, ages 3–5)
**ID**: `bugsPreschool` · **Status**: playable

### Module 1: What is a Bug? (25%)
- Insects have 6 legs (ants, beetles, butterflies, bees)
- Not all minibeasts are insects: spiders (8 legs), worms (no legs), woodlice (14 legs!)
- Body parts: head, thorax, abdomen (for insects)
- Where to find them: under rocks, in soil, on flowers, in the air
- **Questions**: "How many legs does an insect have?", insect vs not-insect sorting

### Module 2: Butterflies & Moths (25%)
- Life cycle: egg → caterpillar → chrysalis → butterfly (metamorphosis)
- Butterflies vs moths (day vs night, colourful vs camouflaged)
- What butterflies eat (nectar from flowers)
- Wings: patterns and colours as protection
- **Questions**: Life cycle sequencing, butterfly vs moth, "What does a caterpillar become?"

### Module 3: Bees, Ants & Teamwork (25%)
- Bees: make honey, pollinate flowers, live in hives
- Why bees are important (no bees = no fruit and vegetables)
- Ants: live in colonies, work together, carry heavy loads
- Queen, worker, soldier roles
- **Questions**: "Why are bees important?", role matching, hive vs anthill

### Module 4: Garden Minibeasts (25%)
- Ladybirds: eat aphids, helpful to gardeners
- Snails and slugs: shells vs no shells
- Earthworms: live in soil, help plants grow
- Spiders: webs, catching food, not insects!
- **Questions**: Creature identification, helpful vs harmful (to gardens), habitat matching

**Stretch Zone**: Camouflage and mimicry; bug hotels; counting legs to classify; nocturnal minibeasts; "Are all bugs creepy? Why might someone be scared?"

## 8. Entomology for Kids (Primary, ages 5–11)
**ID**: `bugsPrimary` · **Status**: playable

### Module 1: Classification & Anatomy (25%)
- Arthropod classification: insects, arachnids, myriapods, crustaceans
- Insect anatomy: head (antennae, compound eyes), thorax (6 legs, wings), abdomen
- Exoskeletons: external skeleton, moulting to grow
- Insect orders: Coleoptera (beetles), Lepidoptera (butterflies/moths), Hymenoptera (bees/ants/wasps), Diptera (flies)
- **Questions**: Anatomy labelling, classification sorting, order matching

### Module 2: Life Cycles & Metamorphosis (25%)
- Complete metamorphosis: egg → larva → pupa → adult (butterflies, beetles)
- Incomplete metamorphosis: egg → nymph → adult (grasshoppers, dragonflies)
- Why metamorphosis? Reduces competition between young and adults
- Comparing life cycles across species
- **Questions**: Complete vs incomplete classification, life cycle sequencing, stage identification

### Module 3: Pollination, Colonies & Ecosystems (25%)
- Pollination mechanics: pollen transfer, flower anatomy (stamen, pistil)
- Bee colony structure: queen, workers, drones, waggle dance
- Ant supercolonies and fungus farming
- Termite mounds: natural architecture and ventilation
- Insects in food webs: decomposers, pollinators, prey
- **Questions**: Pollination diagram completion, colony role matching, ecosystem role identification

### Module 4: Adaptation & Conservation (25%)
- Camouflage: leaf insects, stick insects
- Warning colours (aposematism): wasps, poison dart frogs
- Mimicry: hoverflies looking like wasps
- Insect decline: pesticides, habitat loss, light pollution
- Why insects matter: 80% of plants need insect pollination
- What kids can do: plant wildflowers, avoid pesticides, build bug hotels
- **Questions**: Adaptation type matching, threat identification, conservation strategies

**Stretch Zone**: Forensic entomology (bugs help solve mysteries!); insect-inspired engineering (biomimicry); bioluminescent insects (fireflies); citizen science (butterfly counts); the economic value of insect pollination
