# Contemporary Ethical Theory
**ID**: `philSenior` · **Discipline**: Humanities · **Year**: University Senior

## Course Philosophy
This advanced seminar tackles modern, unresolved ethical problems. It moves beyond classical frameworks to explore applied meta-ethics, the ethics of emerging technologies, and systemic global justice issues.

## Module 1: Advanced Meta-Ethics and Moral Epistemology (20%)
- Moral Skepticism and Moral Relativism (cultural vs subjective)
- Evolutionary Debunking Arguments: Does evolution by natural selection undermine the objective truth of moral beliefs? (Sharon Street)
- Moral Epistemology: How do we know what is right? (Reflective Equilibrium, Foundationalism, Coherentism)
- Constructivism (Kantian and Humean)
- **Questions**: Evaluating the evolutionary debunking argument against moral realism, applying Rawls' Reflective Equilibrium to a moral conflict

## Module 2: The Ethics of Emerging Technology (20%)
- Artificial Intelligence: Algorithmic bias, transparency, and the "Alignment Problem" (Nick Bostrom)
- The ethics of autonomous systems (e.g., self-driving cars, lethal autonomous weapons)
- Transhumanism and Human Enhancement: Genetic engineering (CRISPR), cognitive enhancers, and the definition of "human"
- Big Data and Privacy: Surveillance capitalism and the right to mental privacy
- **Questions**: Debating the moral permissibility of germline genetic editing, analyzing the assignment of moral responsibility when an AI causes harm

## Module 3: Global Justice and Effective Altruism (20%)
- Effective Altruism (Peter Singer, William MacAskill): Maximizing the good, earning to give, and cause prioritization
- The Demandingness Objection to utilitarianism
- Global Poverty and duties of assistance: Cosmopolitanism vs Nationalism
- Immigration and open borders (moral arguments for and against)
- **Questions**: Critiquing the Effective Altruism movement, evaluating the moral obligations of citizens in affluent nations toward refugees

## Module 4: Climate Ethics and Intergenerational Justice (20%)
- Anthropogenic climate change: Historic responsibility vs capacity to pay
- The Non-Identity Problem (Derek Parfit): Can we harm people who wouldn't have existed if we had acted differently?
- Discounting the future: Economic vs moral arguments for how we value future lives
- Geoengineering: The moral hazard argument
- **Questions**: Applying the Non-Identity Problem to climate policy, evaluating the ethical implications of spraying aerosols to cool the planet

## Module 5: Bioethics and the Margins of Life (20%)
- Advanced end-of-life issues: Voluntary euthanasia, advance directives, and the definition of brain death
- The ethics of abortion and fetal rights (advanced arguments beyond Thomson and Marquis)
- Animal Ethics: Factory farming, animal testing, and the moral status of non-human animals (Marginal Cases argument)
- Medical allocation: Triage protocols during pandemics and the QALY (Quality-Adjusted Life Year) metric
- **Questions**: Critiquing the use of QALYs in determining healthcare funding, evaluating the Argument from Marginal Cases regarding animal rights

## Stretch Zone
- Population Ethics: The Repugnant Conclusion (Parfit)
- Neuroethics: Brain-computer interfaces and agency
