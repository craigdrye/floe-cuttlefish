# Class 11 Math
**ID**: `class11Math` · **Discipline**: Mathematics · **Stage**: University Freshman (ages 18–19, India CBSE/NCERT track)
**Aligns with**: NCERT Class 11 (also covers ICSE Class 11 substantially), Khan Academy "Class 11 math (India)"

## Course Philosophy
Class 11 is the bridge year between school arithmetic and university mathematics. The decisive shift is from *computation* to *abstraction* — students must move from "find the answer" to "what kind of object is this, and what rules govern it?" Sets, functions, and proof technique are introduced as the universal language; complex numbers, trigonometry, conics, and combinatorics expand the toolkit; and limits/derivatives in the final unit are the trapdoor into calculus. Internally we should treat this course as a launchpad: students who master Class 11 cleanly tend to find Class 12 calculus mechanical; students who fudge it spend two years confused.

## Module 1: Sets, Relations, Functions, and Mathematical Reasoning (15%)
- **Sets**: roster vs set-builder notation, finite/infinite, empty/singleton, equal sets, subsets, power sets, universal set
- Operations: union, intersection, difference, symmetric difference, complement, De Morgan's laws
- Venn diagrams as a proof-and-counting tool; inclusion–exclusion for two and three sets
- **Cartesian products**: ordered pairs, A × B, |A × B| = |A||B|
- **Relations**: domain, codomain, range; representations (roster, arrow, graph)
- **Functions**: definition (every input → exactly one output); types — one-one (injective), onto (surjective), bijective; identity, constant, polynomial, rational, modulus, signum, greatest integer (floor), fractional part
- Domain/range of real-valued functions including those with square roots, denominators, and logs
- Algebra of functions: (f+g), (f·g), (f/g), domain restrictions
- **Mathematical Reasoning**: statements, negation, conjunction, disjunction, conditional, biconditional, converse/contrapositive/inverse
- Validity of arguments; "if and only if"; quantifiers (for all, there exists)
- Proof strategies: direct, contrapositive, contradiction, by cases, counterexample (intro to mathematical induction is sometimes here, sometimes its own short module)
- **Questions**: set-builder ↔ roster conversions, Venn diagram counting, classifying functions as injective/surjective/bijective with reasoning, finding domain of composite/algebraic real functions, identifying contrapositive vs converse, deciding validity of a deductive argument

## Module 2: Algebra — Complex Numbers, Quadratics, Inequalities, P&C, Binomial, Sequences (25%)
- **Complex numbers**: i² = −1, real/imaginary parts, equality, addition, multiplication, division, conjugate, modulus
- Argand plane, polar form r(cos θ + i sin θ), De Moivre's theorem (introductory), square root of a complex number
- Solving quadratic equations with complex roots; relation between roots and coefficients
- **Linear inequalities** in one and two variables; graphical solution of systems; word problems (diet, allocation, mixture)
- **Permutations and combinations**: factorial notation, fundamental principle of counting, nPr and nCr derivations, circular permutations, identical-object permutations, complementary counting
- Pascal's triangle and the **Binomial theorem** for positive integral indices: general term, middle term(s), term independent of x, particular coefficient extraction
- **Sequences and series**: AP (nth term, sum of n terms, AM), GP (nth term, sum of n terms, sum to infinity for |r|<1, GM), AM ≥ GM inequality
- Special series: Σn, Σn², Σn³ formulas and applications
- Arithmetic-geometric series (introductory); harmonic mean
- **Questions**: simplifying (a+bi)/(c+di) and finding modulus/argument, completing-the-square style proofs, solving linear inequality systems graphically, counting arrangements with restrictions, finding the coefficient of x^k in a binomial expansion, identifying AP/GP and computing nth term or sum, AM-GM type optimization

## Module 3: Trigonometry (15%)
- Angles: degrees, radians, conversion (π rad = 180°); arc length s = rθ
- Trig functions on the unit circle; signs in four quadrants; values at standard angles (0, 30, 45, 60, 90, …)
- Domain, range, and graphs of sin, cos, tan, cot, sec, csc; periodicity and symmetry
- **Identities**: Pythagorean, sum/difference (sin(A±B), cos(A±B), tan(A±B)), double-angle, half-angle, triple-angle, product-to-sum and sum-to-product
- General solutions of trigonometric equations: sin x = sin α, cos x = cos α, tan x = tan α
- **Properties of triangles**: sine rule, cosine rule, projection formula, area formulas including ½ab sin C
- Inverse trig functions (introductory: principal values, basic identities — heavier in Class 12)
- **Questions**: converting between rad and deg, evaluating exact values at standard angles, proving identities, expressing sin 3x / cos 3x in terms of sin x / cos x, solving trig equations on a given interval, applying sine/cosine rule to oblique triangles

## Module 4: Coordinate Geometry — Lines, Conics, 3D (15%)
- Distance, section formula, midpoint, slope, parallel/perpendicular conditions
- **Straight lines**: slope-intercept, point-slope, two-point, intercept, normal forms; angle between two lines; distance from a point to a line; family of lines through a point or intersection
- **Circles**: standard and general equations, finding centre/radius, intersection with a line (chord, tangent, secant), tangent at a point on a circle
- **Parabola**: standard forms y² = 4ax (and rotated), focus, directrix, latus rectum, axis, vertex; reflective property
- **Ellipse**: x²/a² + y²/b² = 1, foci, eccentricity, directrix, latus rectum, major/minor axis; sum-of-distances property
- **Hyperbola**: x²/a² − y²/b² = 1, foci, asymptotes, eccentricity, conjugate hyperbola; difference-of-distances property
- **3D geometry intro**: coordinates in space, distance formula in 3D, section formula in 3D, octants
- **Questions**: writing the equation of a line in multiple forms, deriving conic standard equations from focus/directrix, finding the eccentricity of an ellipse/hyperbola, distance and midpoint in 3D, identifying conics from general second-degree equations

## Module 5: Calculus Intro, Statistics, Probability (20%)
- **Limits**: intuitive idea, limit at a point, one-sided limits, algebra of limits, standard limits (lim sin x / x = 1, lim (1+x)^(1/x) = e, lim (a^x − 1)/x = ln a, lim (x^n − a^n)/(x − a) = n a^(n−1))
- Limits of polynomial, rational, trig, log, and exponential functions; indeterminate forms 0/0 and basic factorization/rationalisation tactics
- **Derivatives**: definition as a limit, derivative as instantaneous rate of change and slope of tangent
- Differentiation rules: power, sum, product, quotient; derivatives of polynomials, sin, cos, tan, e^x, ln x (chain rule deferred to Class 12 in some boards but worth previewing)
- **Statistics**: measures of dispersion — range, mean deviation about mean and median, variance, standard deviation; deviation method for grouped data; coefficient of variation
- **Probability**: random experiment, sample space, events (sure, impossible, simple, compound, mutually exclusive, exhaustive); axiomatic definition of probability
- Probability of events: P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A ∩ B); P(not A); equally likely outcomes
- (Conditional probability and independence are formally Class 12, but priming here helps.)
- **Questions**: evaluating limits using factor cancellation or rationalisation, differentiating from first principles, mean deviation and SD for grouped data, basic event probability and P(A ∪ B) computations

## Module 6: Mathematical Induction & Selected Applications (10%)
- **Principle of Mathematical Induction**: base case, inductive step, careful statement of P(n)
- Standard induction proofs: divisibility (e.g., 7^n − 3^n divisible by 4), summation formulas (Σk = n(n+1)/2), inequalities (e.g., 2^n > n for n ≥ 5)
- Common pitfalls: assuming what you're trying to prove, off-by-one base cases
- Selected applications threaded through the year: word problems requiring linked use of P&C with probability, sequences with geometry/trig, motion/optimization with derivatives
- **Questions**: complete inductive proofs, identify the broken step in a flawed induction, multi-tool word problems

## Stretch Zone (push beyond Class 11)
- Strong induction and well-ordering; equivalence with ordinary induction
- Cardinality: countable vs uncountable sets, Hilbert's hotel, Cantor's diagonal argument
- Roots of unity in the Argand plane; ω, ω² and identities; Chebyshev polynomials as a payoff of De Moivre
- Generating functions as a unifying view of sequences and binomial expansion
- ε–δ definition of a limit (preview real analysis); why "intuitive" limits sometimes mislead
- Locus problems: deriving conic equations from focus–directrix definitions and recognizing degenerate conics
- Linear programming as a geometric application of inequalities (formally Class 12, but motivating)
- Why π is irrational (sketch via Niven's proof); why e is irrational
- Combinatorial identities by double counting (e.g., the hockey stick identity, Vandermonde's identity)
- Connection between AM–GM and the Cauchy–Schwarz inequality; Jensen-style intuition
