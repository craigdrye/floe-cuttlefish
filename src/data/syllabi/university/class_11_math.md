# Class 11 Math
**ID**: `class11Math` - **Discipline**: Mathematics - **Stage**: University Freshman (ages 18-19, India CBSE/NCERT track)
**Aligns with**: NCERT Class 11 (also covers ICSE Class 11 substantially), Khan Academy "Class 11 math (India)"

## Course Aim
Build the conceptual and procedural base for senior secondary mathematics: students learn to speak the language of sets, functions, proof, algebra, trigonometry, coordinate geometry, introductory calculus, statistics, and probability with enough precision to succeed in board exams, entrance preparation, and first-year STEM courses. The course treats computation as evidence of understanding, not a substitute for it; every method should connect to a definition, representation, or model.

## Course Design Notes
This is a rigorous pre-university course with steady written practice, cumulative review, and explicit attention to NCERT-style reasoning and exam presentation. Route questions here when students need sets, relations, functions, mathematical reasoning, induction, complex numbers, quadratics, inequalities, permutations and combinations, binomial theorem, sequences, trigonometry, straight lines, conics, 3D coordinates, limits, derivatives, statistics, or introductory probability. Emphasize exact notation, domain restrictions, proof habits, diagram-supported reasoning, clean algebra, and the ability to explain why a method applies before calculating.

## Chapter 1: Sets, Relations, Functions, and Logic
- **Key concepts**: Set notation, subsets, power sets, operations on sets, complements, De Morgan's laws, Venn diagrams, inclusion-exclusion, Cartesian products, relations, domain, codomain, range, functions, injective, surjective, bijective, identity, constant, polynomial, rational, modulus, signum, greatest integer, statements, connectives, negation, converse, contrapositive, and validity of arguments.
- **Practice questions**: Convert between roster and set-builder form; solve a three-set Venn counting problem; find domain and range for a real-valued function; classify a function as one-one or onto with reasoning; identify the contrapositive of a conditional statement.
- **Student output**: A functions and logic notebook containing set proofs or counterexamples, Venn diagrams, domain-range work, function classification, and short validity explanations.

## Chapter 2: Mathematical Induction, Complex Numbers, and Quadratics
- **Key concepts**: Principle of Mathematical Induction, base case, inductive step, summation and divisibility proofs, complex numbers, real and imaginary parts, equality, conjugate, modulus, Argand plane, polar form, basic De Moivre intuition, square roots of complex numbers, quadratic equations, discriminant, completing the square, and relations between roots and coefficients.
- **Practice questions**: Prove a summation formula by induction; find the modulus and argument of a complex number; simplify a quotient of complex numbers; solve a quadratic with complex roots; form a quadratic equation from given roots.
- **Student output**: An induction and complex-number problem set with complete proof structure, Argand-plane sketches, exact algebra, and written checks of root behavior.

## Chapter 3: Linear Inequalities, Counting, and Binomial Theorem
- **Key concepts**: Linear inequalities in one variable, systems of linear inequalities in two variables, graphical solution regions, factorial notation, fundamental principle of counting, permutations, combinations, restricted arrangements, identical objects, complementary counting, Pascal's triangle, binomial theorem for positive integral indices, general term, middle term, and coefficient extraction.
- **Practice questions**: Solve and graph a system of inequalities; count arrangements with repeated letters or restrictions; choose committees under conditions; find the coefficient of a specified term in a binomial expansion; identify a term independent of x.
- **Student output**: A counting and inequalities portfolio with diagrams, region sketches, clearly named counting cases, binomial-term calculations, and error notes on overcounting.

## Chapter 4: Sequences, Series, and Algebraic Structure
- **Key concepts**: Arithmetic progressions, geometric progressions, nth term, finite sums, sum to infinity for a geometric progression with |r| < 1, arithmetic mean, geometric mean, harmonic mean, AM-GM inequality, sigma notation, sums of n, n^2, and n^3, arithmetic-geometric patterns, and sequence modeling.
- **Practice questions**: Identify whether a sequence is arithmetic, geometric, or neither; find the nth term and sum of a progression; use sum formulas to simplify a series; apply AM-GM to a simple optimization problem; model a repeated-growth scenario.
- **Student output**: A sequence-analysis packet connecting formulas, derivations, sigma notation, word-problem setup, and at least one inequality-based argument.

## Chapter 5: Trigonometry and Triangle Applications
- **Key concepts**: Angles in degrees and radians, arc length, unit-circle definitions, signs by quadrant, standard-angle values, graphs and ranges of trigonometric functions, periodicity, symmetry, Pythagorean identities, sum and difference identities, double-angle and half-angle identities, product-to-sum and sum-to-product forms, trigonometric equations, sine rule, cosine rule, projection formula, and triangle area formulas.
- **Practice questions**: Convert between radians and degrees; evaluate exact trigonometric values; prove an identity; solve a trigonometric equation on a given interval; apply the sine rule or cosine rule to an oblique triangle.
- **Student output**: A trigonometry dossier with unit-circle work, identity proofs, equation solutions with general form, and triangle applications that include diagrams and units.

## Chapter 6: Coordinate Geometry, Conics, and 3D Coordinates
- **Key concepts**: Distance formula, section formula, midpoint, slope, parallel and perpendicular lines, forms of a straight line, angle between lines, distance from a point to a line, circle equations, tangent and chord ideas, parabola, ellipse, hyperbola, focus, directrix, eccentricity, latus rectum, axes, asymptotes, 3D coordinates, octants, 3D distance, and 3D section formula.
- **Practice questions**: Write the equation of a line in multiple forms; find the center and radius of a circle; derive a conic equation from focus-directrix data; identify conic features from standard form; compute distance or section points in 3D.
- **Student output**: A coordinate-geometry folio with labeled graphs, equation derivations, conic-feature tables, and exact coordinate calculations.

## Chapter 7: Limits, Derivatives, and Calculus Readiness
- **Key concepts**: Intuitive limits, one-sided limits, algebra of limits, standard limits, indeterminate forms, factor cancellation, rationalisation, limits of polynomial, rational, trigonometric, logarithmic, and exponential expressions, derivative from first principles, derivative as slope and instantaneous rate of change, tangent lines, basic derivative rules, and derivatives of common elementary functions.
- **Practice questions**: Evaluate limits by factorisation or rationalisation; use standard trigonometric limits; find a derivative from first principles; compute a tangent line; interpret a derivative as a rate in context.
- **Student output**: A calculus-readiness packet showing limit methods, first-principles differentiation, tangent-line work, and short explanations of rate-of-change meaning.

## Chapter 8: Statistics, Probability, and Exam Synthesis
- **Key concepts**: Range, mean deviation about mean and median, variance, standard deviation, grouped-data calculation, coefficient of variation, random experiments, sample spaces, events, sure and impossible events, mutually exclusive and exhaustive events, axiomatic probability, complement rule, addition rule, equally likely outcomes, and mixed-question strategy across algebra, geometry, trigonometry, and calculus.
- **Practice questions**: Compute standard deviation for grouped data; compare two datasets using coefficient of variation; build a sample space; calculate P(A union B) from event information; solve a mixed NCERT-style problem requiring more than one topic.
- **Student output**: A statistics and probability report with organized tables, probability notation, interpretation of spread, and a corrected cumulative exam set with error categories.

## Capstone
Students complete a Class 11 mathematics readiness portfolio. It must include one substantial problem from each major strand, corrected solutions from at least two timed assessments, a formula and concept map, and a written reflection explaining which definitions or representations prevent common errors. Strong capstones connect algebraic, graphical, geometric, and verbal reasoning rather than collecting isolated answers.

## Assessment Ideas
- Weekly written problem sets graded for notation, method selection, exactness, and explanation.
- Short cumulative quizzes on functions, identities, conics, counting, limits, and probability.
- Proof and reasoning checks using induction, counterexamples, trigonometric identities, and coordinate-geometry derivations.
- Timed NCERT and entrance-style practice blocks with post-test error logs.
- Capstone rubric covering conceptual coverage, correctness, presentation, diagram use, exam readiness, and correction quality.
