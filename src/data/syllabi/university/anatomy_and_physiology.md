# Anatomy and Physiology
**ID**: `anatomyAndPhysiology` · **Discipline**: Science · **Year**: University Freshman / High School Senior

## Course Philosophy
Anatomy and Physiology explores the structure and function of the human body. This course emphasizes homeostasis and the intricate interdependence of organ systems, serving as the foundational prerequisite for nursing, pre-med, and kinesiology tracks.

## Module 1: Foundations and the Integumentary/Skeletal Systems (20%)
- Anatomical terminology: Body planes (sagittal, coronal, transverse), directional terms, and body cavities
- Histology (Study of Tissues): Epithelial, connective, muscle, and nervous tissues
- The Integumentary System: Layers of the skin (epidermis, dermis, hypodermis), glands, thermoregulation, and tissue repair
- The Skeletal System: Bone tissue (osteoblasts, osteoclasts), ossification, axial vs appendicular skeleton, and joint classifications (synovial joints)
- **Questions**: Identifying the specific epithelial tissue type found in the alveoli based on its function, explaining the process of endochondral ossification

## Module 2: The Muscular and Nervous Systems (25%)
- The Muscular System: Microanatomy of skeletal muscle (sarcomeres, actin/myosin), the sliding filament model, and the neuromuscular junction
- Muscle metabolism and the physiology of a muscle twitch
- The Nervous System: Neuronal anatomy, resting and action potentials, and synaptic transmission
- Central Nervous System (CNS): Brain anatomy, meninges, cerebrospinal fluid (CSF), and the spinal cord
- Peripheral Nervous System (PNS): Cranial nerves, spinal nerves, and the Autonomic Nervous System (Sympathetic vs Parasympathetic divisions)
- **Questions**: Tracing the sequence of events at the neuromuscular junction that leads to muscle contraction, comparing the physiological effects of sympathetic vs parasympathetic activation

## Module 3: The Endocrine and Cardiovascular Systems (20%)
- The Endocrine System: Hormones (lipid-soluble vs water-soluble), the hypothalamus-pituitary axis, negative feedback loops, and major endocrine glands (thyroid, pancreas, adrenal glands)
- Blood: Composition (plasma, erythrocytes, leukocytes, platelets) and hemostasis (blood clotting)
- The Cardiovascular System: Anatomy of the heart, the cardiac cycle, the conduction system (SA node, AV node, Purkinje fibers), and the ECG/EKG
- Blood Vessels and Hemodynamics: Arteries, veins, capillaries, blood pressure regulation, and capillary exchange
- **Questions**: Tracing a drop of blood through the systemic and pulmonary circuits, explaining how the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system regulates blood pressure

## Module 4: The Respiratory, Lymphatic, and Immune Systems (15%)
- The Lymphatic System: Lymph nodes, spleen, thymus, and fluid recovery
- The Immune System: Innate immunity (inflammation, phagocytes) vs Adaptive immunity (B cells, T cells, antibodies, immunological memory)
- The Respiratory System: Anatomy of the respiratory tract, pulmonary ventilation (Boyle's Law), gas exchange at the alveoli, and gas transport in the blood (oxygen-hemoglobin dissociation curve)
- Regulation of breathing (chemoreceptors and blood pH)
- **Questions**: Explaining the role of helper T-cells in the adaptive immune response, analyzing the Bohr effect on hemoglobin's affinity for oxygen

## Module 5: The Digestive, Urinary, and Reproductive Systems (20%)
- The Digestive System: Anatomy of the GI tract, accessory organs (liver, gallbladder, pancreas), mechanical vs chemical digestion, and absorption of nutrients
- Metabolism: Anabolism vs catabolism, cellular respiration, and thermoregulation
- The Urinary System: Gross anatomy of the kidney, the nephron (glomerulus, proximal tubule, loop of Henle, distal tubule), glomerular filtration, tubular reabsorption, and secretion
- Fluid, Electrolyte, and Acid-Base Balance (role of the kidneys and lungs in maintaining blood pH)
- The Reproductive System: Male and female anatomy, spermatogenesis, oogenesis, the ovarian/menstrual cycles, and embryonic development
- **Questions**: Tracing the digestion and absorption of a triglyceride, explaining the mechanism of the countercurrent multiplier in the loop of Henle

## Stretch Zone
- Pathophysiology: Applying A&P concepts to specific disease states (e.g., diabetes mellitus, heart failure)
- Advanced neuroanatomy and functional mapping
