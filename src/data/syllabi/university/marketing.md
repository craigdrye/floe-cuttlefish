# Marketing Principles
**ID**: `marketing` · **Discipline**: Business · **Year**: University Junior

## Course Philosophy
This course covers the strategic and tactical elements of marketing. It moves beyond "advertising" to explore how organizations create, communicate, and deliver value to target audiences through data-driven decision-making and consumer psychology.

## Module 1: The Marketing Concept and Strategy (20%)
- What is Marketing? The creation of value and the marketing exchange
- The Marketing Mix (The 4 Ps): Product, Price, Place, Promotion
- Strategic Planning: Mission statements, SWOT analysis, and the BCG Matrix (Stars, Cash Cows, Question Marks, Dogs)
- The Marketing Environment: Microenvironment (company, suppliers, competitors) and Macroenvironment (PESTLE - Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental)
- **Questions**: Conducting a SWOT analysis for a well-known brand, applying the BCG Matrix to a company's product portfolio

## Module 2: Consumer Behavior and Market Research (20%)
- Consumer Decision-Making Process: Need recognition, information search, evaluation of alternatives, purchase, post-purchase behavior (cognitive dissonance)
- Psychological factors (motivation, perception, learning) and Sociocultural factors (culture, social class, reference groups) influencing behavior
- B2B vs B2C marketing differences
- Marketing Research: Defining the problem, primary vs secondary data, quantitative vs qualitative research methods (surveys, focus groups, observational research)
- **Questions**: Analyzing the cause of post-purchase cognitive dissonance, designing a survey to test a new product concept

## Module 3: Segmentation, Targeting, and Positioning (STP) (20%)
- Market Segmentation: Dividing a market based on geographic, demographic, psychographic, and behavioral variables
- Target Marketing Strategies: Undifferentiated (mass), differentiated, concentrated (niche), and micromarketing
- Buyer Personas: Creating detailed profiles of ideal customers
- Positioning: Designing the company's offering and image to occupy a distinctive place in the mind of the target market
- Value Propositions and Perceptual Mapping
- **Questions**: Creating a buyer persona for a niche fitness app, plotting competitors on a perceptual map to identify market gaps

## Module 4: Product and Pricing Strategies (20%)
- Product Classifications: Convenience, shopping, specialty, and unsought goods
- The Product Life Cycle (PLC): Introduction, Growth, Maturity, Decline, and strategies for each stage
- Branding: Brand equity, brand personality, brand architecture, and packaging
- Pricing Objectives: Profit-oriented, sales-oriented, status quo
- Pricing Strategies: Cost-plus pricing, value-based pricing, penetration pricing vs price skimming, psychological pricing
- **Questions**: Recommending a marketing strategy for a product in the maturity stage, evaluating the decision to use price skimming vs penetration pricing

## Module 5: Place (Distribution) and Promotion (Integrated Marketing Communications) (20%)
- Supply Chain Management and Marketing Channels: Direct vs indirect distribution, intensive vs selective vs exclusive distribution
- Omnichannel retailing
- The Promotion Mix: Advertising, Public Relations, Sales Promotion, Personal Selling, Direct/Digital Marketing
- Integrated Marketing Communications (IMC): Ensuring a consistent message across all channels
- Digital Marketing Metrics: Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Lifetime Value (LTV), Conversion Rate (CVR), Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)
- **Questions**: Designing an IMC campaign for a new beverage, calculating CAC and LTV to determine campaign profitability

## Stretch Zone
- Behavioral economics in pricing (anchor pricing, decoy effect)
- Advanced digital attribution modeling
