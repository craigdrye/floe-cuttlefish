# AP Calculus BC
**ID**: `apCalculusBc` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: University Sophomore (equivalent)
**Aligns with**: College Board AP Calculus BC Course and Exam Description; equivalent to a two-semester single-variable calculus sequence

## Course Aim
Extend the AP Calculus AB toolkit into a fuller single-variable calculus course. Students deepen differentiation and integration fluency, add parametric, polar, and vector-valued functions, and learn to reason carefully about infinite sequences and series.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test any AP Calculus AB topic at BC pace, plus advanced integration, improper integrals, Euler's method, logistic models, parametric or polar calculus, vector-valued motion, series convergence, power series, Taylor polynomials, Taylor series, and BC-style FRQs. Keep the course proof-aware, notation-careful, and exam-practical.

## Chapter 1: AB Core Refresh and Calculus Habits
- **Key concepts**: Limits, continuity, derivative definitions, differentiation rules, implicit differentiation, inverse derivatives, contextual derivative interpretation, and AP mathematical practices.
- **Practice questions**: Diagnose AB prerequisite gaps; justify continuity and differentiability claims; compute mixed derivatives quickly and accurately.
- **Student output**: A BC readiness audit with personal weak spots and a repair plan.

## Chapter 2: Derivative Applications at BC Pace
- **Key concepts**: Motion, related rates, optimization, Mean Value Theorem, concavity, function analysis, L'Hospital's rule, and advanced interpretation from graphs and tables.
- **Practice questions**: Analyze motion and optimization scenarios; justify extrema; use L'Hospital's rule only when conditions are met.
- **Student output**: A derivative applications packet with AP-style written justifications.

## Chapter 3: Integration, Accumulation, and Advanced Techniques
- **Key concepts**: Riemann sums, FTC, u-substitution, integration by parts, partial fractions, improper integrals, comparison of net and total change, and calculator-supported definite integrals.
- **Practice questions**: Choose integration techniques; evaluate improper integrals; interpret accumulation in context.
- **Student output**: An integration technique decision tree with worked examples and common traps.

## Chapter 4: Differential Equations and Numerical Approximation
- **Key concepts**: Slope fields, separable differential equations, exponential models, logistic models, Euler's method, solution verification, and model limitations.
- **Practice questions**: Approximate with Euler's method; solve separable and logistic equations; interpret carrying capacity and initial conditions.
- **Student output**: A differential-equation modeling brief with exact solution, numerical approximation, and contextual interpretation.

## Chapter 5: Applications of Integration
- **Key concepts**: Average value, area between curves, volumes by disks, washers, shells where appropriate, known cross sections, arc length, and applications from velocity or rate functions.
- **Practice questions**: Set up integrals from diagrams and contexts; compute total distance; determine volume and arc-length expressions.
- **Student output**: A setup-first integration portfolio where each problem includes a diagram, integral, and interpretation.

## Chapter 6: Parametric, Polar, and Vector-Valued Functions
- **Key concepts**: Parametric derivatives, second derivatives, parametric arc length, vector-valued position, velocity, acceleration, speed, distance traveled, polar coordinates, polar slope, and polar area.
- **Practice questions**: Analyze motion from vector functions; find tangent slopes for parametric and polar curves; compute polar area.
- **Student output**: A motion-and-curves lab using parametric, polar, and vector representations.

## Chapter 7: Sequences, Series, and Convergence
- **Key concepts**: Sequence limits, series convergence and divergence, nth-term test, geometric series, p-series, integral test, comparison tests, alternating series, ratio test, absolute and conditional convergence, and error bounds.
- **Practice questions**: Select appropriate convergence tests; determine absolute or conditional convergence; estimate remainders.
- **Student output**: A convergence-test map with trigger clues, required conditions, and example series.

## Chapter 8: Power Series, Taylor Series, and BC Exam Studio
- **Key concepts**: Power series, interval and radius of convergence, Taylor and Maclaurin polynomials, familiar series, Taylor series manipulation, Lagrange error bound, FRQ strategy, and multiple-choice pacing.
- **Practice questions**: Find intervals of convergence; construct Taylor polynomials; use known series to build new series; write complete BC FRQ solutions.
- **Student output**: A timed BC FRQ portfolio including one parametric/polar/vector problem and one series problem.

## Capstone
Students build a BC calculus readiness portfolio: AB repair sheet, integration technique map, differential-equation model, parametric/polar/vector lab, convergence-test map, Taylor series packet, and two timed mixed FRQ sets.

## Assessment Ideas
- Short no-calculator checks for algebra, notation, and exact values.
- Calculator-active problems that require interpretation rather than button pushing.
- Convergence-test quizzes where students justify the selected test before applying it.
- FRQ rubrics focused on setup, conditions, notation, units, and conclusion.
- One full mock exam plus a remediation plan by unit and error type.
