# AP Calculus BC
**ID**: `apCalculusBc` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: University Sophomore (equivalent)

## Course Philosophy
AP Calculus BC is a full-year, college-level calculus course covering the same topics as Calculus AB, plus additional topics including parametric/polar/vector functions, advanced integration techniques, and infinite sequences and series.

## Module 1: Limits, Continuity, and Derivatives (20%)
- Limits (graphical, numerical, algebraic), continuity, and the Intermediate Value Theorem
- The derivative as an instantaneous rate of change and as a limit of the difference quotient
- Derivative rules (power, product, quotient, chain) and implicit differentiation
- L'Hôpital's Rule for evaluating indeterminate forms ($\frac{0}{0}$, $\frac{\infty}{\infty}$)
- Applications: Mean Value Theorem, finding extrema, concavity, points of inflection, optimization, related rates
- **Questions**: Evaluating limits using L'Hôpital's Rule, optimizing the surface area of a cylinder, solving related rates involving cones

## Module 2: Integration and its Applications (25%)
- Antiderivatives, Riemann sums, and the definite integral
- The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Parts 1 and 2)
- Integration techniques: U-substitution, Integration by Parts, Partial Fractions
- Improper integrals (limits of integration involving infinity or vertical asymptotes)
- Applications: Area between curves, volume of solids of revolution (disk/washer, shell methods), volume with known cross-sections, arc length
- **Questions**: Using Integration by Parts to evaluate $\int x e^x dx$, calculating the arc length of a curve, evaluating an improper integral

## Module 3: Differential Equations (15%)
- Verifying solutions to differential equations
- Slope fields and Euler's Method for numerical approximation
- Solving separable differential equations
- Exponential growth and decay models
- Logistic growth models ($dP/dt = kP(1 - P/M)$)
- **Questions**: Approximating a value using Euler's Method, solving a logistic differential equation, analyzing the carrying capacity of a population

## Module 4: Parametric, Polar, and Vector Functions (20%)
- Calculus of parametric equations: finding $dy/dx$ and $d^2y/dx^2$, arc length in parametric form
- Vector-valued functions: position, velocity, acceleration vectors, speed, distance traveled
- Polar coordinates: converting between polar and rectangular, graphing polar curves
- Calculus of polar curves: finding the slope of a tangent line, finding the area bounded by polar curves
- **Questions**: Finding the acceleration vector given a position vector, calculating the area of one petal of a rose curve ($r = \sin 3\theta$)

## Module 5: Infinite Sequences and Series (20%)
- Convergence/divergence of sequences and series
- Tests for convergence: $n$th term test, geometric series, p-series, alternating series test, integral test, ratio test, limit comparison test
- Absolute vs conditional convergence
- Power series: finding the interval and radius of convergence
- Taylor and Maclaurin polynomials and series for familiar functions ($e^x$, $\sin x$, $\cos x$, $1/(1-x)$)
- Lagrange error bound (Taylor's remainder theorem)
- **Questions**: Using the ratio test to find the radius of convergence, finding the Taylor polynomial of degree 3 for $\ln(x)$ centered at $x=1$

## Stretch Zone
- Fourier Series (conceptual introduction)
- Double integrals over rectangular regions
