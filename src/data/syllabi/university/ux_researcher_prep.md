# UX Researcher Interview Prep
**ID**: `uxResearch` · **Discipline**: Design · **Year**: University Senior / Career Hopper

## Course Philosophy
This course prepares candidates for User Experience (UX) Research roles in tech. It covers the end-to-end research lifecycle, from generative/exploratory research to evaluative usability testing, and emphasizes stakeholder management and actionable synthesis.

## Module 1: Research Strategy and Method Selection (25%)
- Generative vs Evaluative research
- Qualitative vs Quantitative methods (when to use which)
- The Research Toolkit: User interviews, contextual inquiry, diary studies, card sorting, tree testing, surveys, usability testing (moderated vs unmoderated)
- Scoping a project: Defining research questions, hypotheses, and aligning with business goals
- **Questions**: Choosing the right methodology when a Product Manager asks "Why aren't users adopting this feature?", designing a screener to recruit the right participants

## Module 2: Conducting the Research (Interview Craft) (20%)
- Writing a discussion guide: Open-ended vs leading questions
- Interview techniques: The "Five Whys", active listening, handling difficult/silent participants
- Moderating usability sessions: The "Think Aloud" protocol, minimizing bias, observing non-verbal cues
- Ethics and informed consent
- **Questions**: Rewriting a leading question to be neutral, role-playing a user interview scenario where the participant goes off-topic

## Module 3: Synthesis and Analysis (20%)
- Qualitative coding and thematic analysis (e.g., affinity mapping)
- Identifying actionable insights vs mere observations
- Creating deliverables: Personas, Journey Maps, Service Blueprints, Empathy Maps
- Quantitative analysis: Analyzing survey data, basic descriptive statistics
- **Questions**: Distilling 10 pages of interview transcripts into 3 core themes, building a user journey map based on provided data points

## Module 4: Communication and Stakeholder Influence (20%)
- Storytelling for impact: Presenting research findings to Product, Design, and Engineering
- Defending research methods and sample sizes
- Prioritizing recommendations based on engineering effort vs user impact
- Dealing with pushback (e.g., "But Apple does it this way," or "This was only 5 users")
- **Questions**: Delivering a presentation of negative findings to a stakeholder who is emotionally attached to a feature, addressing skepticism about small qualitative sample sizes

## Module 5: The UXR Portfolio Presentation and Case Studies (15%)
- Structuring the portfolio presentation: Context, Problem, Approach, Findings, Impact
- The "Whiteboard Challenge" or Take-Home Assignment: Thinking on your feet to design a research plan in 30 minutes
- Behavioral Questions: "Tell me about a time your research changed a product decision."
- **Questions**: Structuring a 15-minute portfolio presentation, rapidly outlining a research plan for a hypothetical prompt (e.g., "Design research for an app that helps people find parking")

## Stretch Zone
- Research Operations (ResOps): Managing participant panels, tooling, and data compliance (GDPR)
- Advanced quantitative UXR: Conjoint analysis, MaxDiff scaling
