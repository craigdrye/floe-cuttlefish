# Financial Accounting
**ID**: `financialAccounting` · **Discipline**: Business · **Year**: University Freshman / Sophomore

## Course Philosophy
Financial Accounting is the "language of business." This rigorous introductory course focuses on the preparation, analysis, and interpretation of financial statements. It emphasizes the mechanics of double-entry accounting and the conceptual framework underlying Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP).

## Module 1: The Accounting Framework and the Accounting Equation (20%)
- The purpose of accounting and the users of financial information (investors, creditors, regulators)
- The Conceptual Framework: Relevance, faithful representation, materiality, going concern
- The Fundamental Accounting Equation: $\text{Assets} = \text{Liabilities} + \text{Equity}$
- The Four Financial Statements: 
  - Income Statement (Revenues, Expenses, Net Income)
  - Statement of Retained Earnings
  - Balance Sheet (Snapshot of financial position)
  - Statement of Cash Flows
- **Questions**: Classifying an item as an asset, liability, or equity; demonstrating how a specific transaction impacts the accounting equation

## Module 2: The Recording Process (Double-Entry Accounting) (25%)
- Debits and Credits: The rules of normal balances (DEA LER: Dividends/Expenses/Assets have normal Debit balances; Liabilities/Equity/Revenue have normal Credit balances)
- The Accounting Cycle: Source documents, journalizing transactions, posting to the general ledger (T-accounts)
- Preparing an Unadjusted Trial Balance
- Accrual vs Cash Basis Accounting (the matching principle and revenue recognition principle)
- Adjusting Entries: Deferrals (prepaid expenses, unearned revenues) and Accruals (accrued revenues, accrued expenses)
- Depreciation expense basics
- Preparing the Adjusted Trial Balance and Closing Entries
- **Questions**: Recording the journal entry for the receipt of advance payment from a customer, creating the adjusting entry for one month of rent from a prepaid rent account

## Module 3: Merchandising Operations and Inventory (20%)
- Accounting for merchandising companies: Sales, sales returns and allowances, sales discounts
- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) and Gross Profit
- Perpetual vs Periodic inventory systems
- Inventory Costing Methods: FIFO (First-In, First-Out), LIFO (Last-In, First-Out), and Weighted Average
- The impact of inventory methods on the Income Statement and Balance Sheet during periods of inflation
- Lower of Cost or Market (LCM) rule
- **Questions**: Calculating ending inventory and COGS using the FIFO method, explaining why a company might choose LIFO for tax purposes

## Module 4: Internal Control, Cash, and Receivables (15%)
- The Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) and the importance of internal controls
- Principles of internal control (segregation of duties, physical controls)
- Bank Reconciliations: Adjusting the bank balance (deposits in transit, outstanding checks) vs adjusting the book balance (NSF checks, bank fees)
- Accounts Receivable: Recognizing, valuing, and disposing of receivables
- Allowance for Doubtful Accounts: Estimating uncollectibles (Percent of Sales method vs Aging of Receivables method)
- **Questions**: Preparing a bank reconciliation statement, recording the journal entry to write off an uncollectible account under the allowance method

## Module 5: Long-Term Assets and Liabilities (20%)
- Plant Assets: Determining the cost of property, plant, and equipment (PP&E)
- Depreciation Methods: Straight-line, units-of-activity, and double-declining balance
- Disposal of plant assets (recording gains/losses)
- Intangible Assets: Amortization of patents, copyrights, and goodwill
- Current vs Long-Term Liabilities
- Bonds Payable: Issuing bonds at face value, at a discount, or at a premium; calculating interest expense
- **Questions**: Calculating the annual depreciation expense using the straight-line method, recording the issuance of a bond at a premium

## Stretch Zone
- Financial Statement Analysis: Calculating and interpreting liquidity, solvency, and profitability ratios (e.g., Current Ratio, Debt-to-Equity, Return on Assets)
- The preparation of the Statement of Cash Flows (Indirect Method)
