# Linear Algebra
**ID**: `linearAlgebra` · **Discipline**: Mathematics · **Year**: University Sophomore

## Course Philosophy
Linear algebra is the mathematical foundation for modern physics, computer science, machine learning, and economics. This course emphasizes both computational techniques (solving linear systems) and abstract theoretical concepts (vector spaces, linear transformations).

## Module 1: Systems of Linear Equations & Matrices (20%)
- Systems of linear equations and augmented matrices
- Row reduction and Echelon Forms (RREF)
- Vector equations and the matrix equation $Ax = b$
- Solution sets of linear systems (homogeneous vs non-homogeneous)
- Matrix operations (addition, multiplication, transpose)
- The inverse of a matrix and the Invertible Matrix Theorem
- **Questions**: Solving a $3\times4$ system using Gaussian elimination, proving properties of invertible matrices

## Module 2: Determinants & Vector Spaces (20%)
- Determinants: calculation (cofactor expansion), properties, and Cramer's Rule
- Volume and linear transformations (the geometric meaning of the determinant)
- Vector Spaces and Subspaces: definitions and axioms
- Null spaces, Column spaces, and Linear Transformations
- Linear independence, span, and bases
- Coordinate systems and change of basis
- **Questions**: Finding the basis for a null space, proving a set of vectors is linearly independent, changing basis vectors

## Module 3: Dimension & Rank (20%)
- The dimension of a vector space
- The Rank Theorem ($Rank(A) + Nullity(A) = n$)
- Inner product, length, and orthogonality
- Orthogonal sets and orthogonal projections
- The Gram-Schmidt process
- Least-Squares problems
- **Questions**: Applying the Gram-Schmidt process to find an orthogonal basis, finding the least-squares solution to an inconsistent system

## Module 4: Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors (20%)
- Definition and geometric interpretation of eigenvalues ($\lambda$) and eigenvectors ($v$) ($Av = \lambda v$)
- The characteristic equation ($\det(A - \lambda I) = 0$)
- Diagonalization of matrices ($A = PDP^{-1}$)
- Complex eigenvalues
- Discrete dynamical systems (Markov chains)
- **Questions**: Finding the eigenvalues/eigenvectors of a matrix, diagonalizing a matrix, calculating the steady-state vector of a Markov chain

## Module 5: Symmetric Matrices and Quadratic Forms (20%)
- Diagonalization of symmetric matrices (The Spectral Theorem)
- Quadratic forms and their classification (positive definite, negative definite, indefinite)
- The Singular Value Decomposition (SVD)
- Applications to image processing and Principal Component Analysis (PCA)
- **Questions**: Classifying a quadratic form, performing a basic SVD, conceptualizing how PCA reduces dimensionality

## Stretch Zone
- Abstract vector spaces (spaces of polynomials, spaces of continuous functions)
- Dual spaces and tensors
