# Electrical Engineering
**ID**: `electricalEngineering` - **Discipline**: Engineering - **Year**: University Senior

## Course Aim
Build a rigorous working foundation in circuits, electronics, signals, systems, and digital logic. Students learn to move between physical intuition, mathematical models, simulation, measurement, and design decisions.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they involve DC circuits, KCL/KVL, nodal or mesh analysis, Thevenin/Norton equivalents, op-amps, capacitors, inductors, transients, AC phasors, power, filters, Laplace transforms, semiconductors, transistors, digital logic, or control/signal extensions. Require diagrams and units before algebra.

## Chapter 1: Circuit Variables and DC Analysis
**Key concepts**: Charge, current, voltage, power, energy, passive sign convention, Ohm's law, KCL, KVL, series, parallel, nodal analysis, mesh analysis, and dependent sources.
**Practice questions**: Solve node voltages and mesh currents; calculate power; check sign conventions; compare equivalent reductions with systematic analysis.
**Student output**: A DC circuit analysis notebook with hand solution, simulation, and measurement plan.

## Chapter 2: Circuit Theorems and Operational Amplifiers
**Key concepts**: Superposition, source transformation, Thevenin theorem, Norton theorem, maximum power transfer, ideal op-amp, feedback, inverting amplifier, non-inverting amplifier, summing amplifier, and difference amplifier.
**Practice questions**: Find equivalent circuits; design an op-amp gain stage; calculate load effects; explain why feedback enables useful amplifier behavior.
**Student output**: A design memo for a small signal-conditioning circuit with schematic and predicted output.

## Chapter 3: Capacitors, Inductors, and First-Order Transients
**Key concepts**: Capacitance, inductance, stored energy, continuity, RC circuits, RL circuits, natural response, step response, time constant, initial condition, and final value.
**Practice questions**: Solve switching circuits; sketch capacitor voltage and inductor current; calculate time to reach a threshold; explain transient behavior physically.
**Student output**: A transient lab or simulation comparing measured curves with analytic predictions.

## Chapter 4: RLC Circuits and Dynamic Systems
**Key concepts**: Second-order circuits, damping, natural frequency, overdamped, critically damped, underdamped, resonance, differential equations, characteristic roots, and energy exchange.
**Practice questions**: Determine damping type; solve a series or parallel RLC response; interpret oscillation and decay; connect pole location to time response.
**Student output**: A second-order system analysis with differential equation, response plot, and physical interpretation.

## Chapter 5: AC Steady State and Power
**Key concepts**: Sinusoids, Euler's identity, phasors, impedance, admittance, RMS values, complex power, real power, reactive power, apparent power, power factor, and correction.
**Practice questions**: Analyze AC circuits in phasor form; calculate complex power; design power-factor correction; explain phase relationships among voltage, current, and power.
**Student output**: An AC power analysis for a load, including phasor diagram and correction recommendation.

## Chapter 6: Frequency Response, Filters, and Laplace Methods
**Key concepts**: Transfer function, frequency response, Bode plot, cutoff frequency, low-pass, high-pass, band-pass, band-stop, active filter, Laplace transform, poles, zeros, stability, and initial conditions.
**Practice questions**: Sketch Bode plots; find cutoff frequencies; analyze filters; use s-domain equivalents; determine stability from poles.
**Student output**: A filter design and test report with target specification, transfer function, Bode plot, and simulation.

## Chapter 7: Semiconductors and Analog Electronics
**Key concepts**: Semiconductor, p-n junction, diode, rectifier, clipping, clamping, BJT, MOSFET, operating region, biasing, small-signal model, amplifier, and switching behavior.
**Practice questions**: Analyze diode circuits; design a rectifier; identify transistor operating regions; estimate gain; compare analog amplification and digital switching.
**Student output**: A small electronics project such as a rectifier, sensor interface, or transistor switch.

## Chapter 8: Digital Logic, Signals, and Control Extensions
**Key concepts**: Boolean algebra, logic gates, combinational logic, Karnaugh map, flip-flop, register, counter, sampling, Fourier series, feedback, transfer function, PID intuition, and embedded systems.
**Practice questions**: Simplify Boolean expressions; design a combinational circuit; analyze a counter; identify aliasing risks; explain feedback control qualitatively.
**Student output**: A digital or mixed-signal mini-design with truth table, simplified logic, schematic, and test plan.

## Capstone
Students design, simulate, and document a small electrical system such as an amplifier, filter, sensor interface, power supply, digital controller, or mixed-signal device. The report must include requirements, schematic, analysis, simulation, test evidence, and design tradeoffs.

## Assessment Ideas
Use circuit problem sets, lab notebooks, simulation checks, design reviews, debugging reflections, and a capstone demonstration. Assess mathematical correctness, physical explanation, measurement habits, and documentation.
