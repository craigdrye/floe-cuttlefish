# General Physics 2
**ID**: `generalPhysics2` · **Discipline**: Science · **Year**: University Freshman

## Course Philosophy
Following the mechanics-heavy General Physics 1, this course explores electromagnetism, optics, and modern physics. It relies heavily on vector calculus (if calculus-based) or advanced algebra to model invisible forces and fields.

## Module 1: Electrostatics and Electric Fields (20%)
- Electric Charge: Conductors, insulators, and charging by induction/conduction
- Coulomb's Law ($F = k \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}$) and the superposition principle
- The Electric Field ($E = F/q$) generated by point charges and continuous charge distributions (lines, rings, disks)
- Gauss's Law ($\oint E \cdot dA = \frac{q_{enc}}{\epsilon_0}$) and its application to highly symmetric charge distributions
- Electric Potential ($V$) and Electric Potential Energy ($U$); the relationship between $E$ and $V$ ($E = -\nabla V$)
- Capacitance: Parallel-plate capacitors, dielectrics, and energy storage ($U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2$)
- **Questions**: Using Gauss's Law to find the electric field inside a non-conducting solid sphere, calculating the equivalent capacitance of a complex network

## Module 2: DC Circuits and Magnetism (20%)
- Current, resistance, and Ohm's Law ($V = IR$); electrical power
- DC Circuit Analysis: Kirchhoff's Voltage Law (KVL) and Current Law (KCL)
- RC Circuits: Charging and discharging transients, the time constant ($\tau = RC$)
- Magnetic Fields: The Lorentz Force ($F = qv \times B$) on moving charges (cyclotron motion) and current-carrying wires ($F = IL \times B$)
- Sources of Magnetic Fields: The Biot-Savart Law and Ampere's Law ($\oint B \cdot dl = \mu_0 I_{enc}$)
- Magnetic force between parallel conductors
- **Questions**: Solving a multi-loop circuit using Kirchhoff's rules, using Ampere's Law to find the magnetic field inside a solenoid

## Module 3: Electromagnetic Induction and AC Circuits (20%)
- Faraday's Law of Induction ($\mathcal{E} = - \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt}$) and Motional EMF
- Lenz's Law (conservation of energy in induction)
- Inductance ($L$): Self-inductance, mutual inductance, and energy storage in a magnetic field ($U = \frac{1}{2}LI^2$)
- RL and LC Circuits: Transient analysis and electromagnetic oscillations
- Alternating Current (AC) Circuits: Phasors, reactance ($X_L$, $X_C$), impedance ($Z$), and resonance in series RLC circuits
- Transformers and power transmission
- **Questions**: Calculating the induced EMF in a loop pulled through a magnetic field, determining the resonant frequency of an RLC circuit

## Module 4: Electromagnetic Waves and Geometric Optics (20%)
- Maxwell's Equations (conceptual synthesis) and the derivation of Electromagnetic Waves
- The EM Spectrum, speed of light ($c = 1/\sqrt{\mu_0 \epsilon_0}$), and energy/momentum of EM waves (Poynting vector)
- Geometric Optics: Reflection, refraction, and Snell's Law ($n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2$)
- Total internal reflection and fiber optics
- Mirrors (plane, concave, convex) and the mirror equation ($1/f = 1/d_o + 1/d_i$)
- Lenses (converging, diverging) and ray tracing
- **Questions**: Drawing a ray diagram to locate the image formed by a converging lens, applying Snell's Law to a prism

## Module 5: Physical Optics and Modern Physics (20%)
- Interference: Huygens' principle, Young's Double-Slit experiment, thin-film interference
- Diffraction: Single-slit diffraction, diffraction gratings, and resolving power (Rayleigh criterion)
- Polarization of light
- Special Relativity: Postulates, time dilation, length contraction, relativistic momentum, and mass-energy equivalence ($E=mc^2$)
- Quantum Physics: Blackbody radiation (Planck's hypothesis), the Photoelectric effect, wave-particle duality (de Broglie wavelength), and Heisenberg's Uncertainty Principle
- **Questions**: Calculating the fringe spacing in a double-slit experiment, determining the work function of a metal from photoelectric data, applying the time dilation formula

## Stretch Zone
- The Schrödinger Equation and the quantum particle in a box
- Nuclear physics (radioactive decay series, fission, fusion)
