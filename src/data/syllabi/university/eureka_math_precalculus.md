# Eureka Math Precalculus
**ID**: `eurekaMathPrecalculus` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: University Sophomore (equivalent)

## Course Philosophy
This syllabus adapts the rigorous Eureka Math Precalculus curriculum. It emphasizes deep conceptual understanding of complex numbers, transformations, trigonometry, and matrices, preparing students for university-level calculus.

## Module 1: Complex Numbers and Transformations (25%)
- Understanding complex numbers as points in the complex plane
- Modulus and argument of a complex number
- Arithmetic of complex numbers geometrically and algebraically
- Linear transformations of the complex plane (translations, rotations, dilations)
- Matrices as transformations: representing 2D transformations with $2\times2$ matrices
- Composition of transformations and matrix multiplication
- **Questions**: Describing the geometric effect of multiplying by $i$, finding the matrix that represents a $90^\circ$ rotation

## Module 2: Vectors and Matrices (25%)
- Vectors in 2D and 3D: magnitude, direction, vector addition, scalar multiplication
- The dot product and angle between vectors
- The cross product and its geometric meaning (area of parallelogram, normal vector)
- Matrices: determinants, inverses, and solving systems of linear equations
- Linear transformations in 3D
- **Questions**: Using the cross product to find a normal vector to a plane, solving a $3\times3$ system using an inverse matrix

## Module 3: Trigonometry (25%)
- The Unit Circle and radian measure
- Graphing trigonometric functions ($y = a \sin(bx-c) + d$) and modeling periodic phenomena
- Inverse trigonometric functions and solving trigonometric equations
- Trigonometric identities (Pythagorean, sum/difference, double-angle, half-angle)
- Polar coordinates and graphing polar equations
- **Questions**: Modeling tides using a sinusoidal function, verifying complex trigonometric identities

## Module 4: Advanced Functions and Limits (25%)
- Polynomial functions: Roots, multiplicity, end behavior, Remainder/Factor theorems
- Rational functions: Vertical, horizontal, and oblique asymptotes, holes
- Exponential and logarithmic functions: Properties, solving equations, modeling growth/decay
- Parametric equations: modeling projectile motion
- Introduction to Limits: numerical, graphical, and algebraic evaluation
- Continuity and the concept of a derivative as an instantaneous rate of change
- **Questions**: Finding oblique asymptotes, converting parametric equations to rectangular form, evaluating a limit algebraically

## Stretch Zone
- Conic sections (parabolas, ellipses, hyperbolas) from a geometric and algebraic perspective
- De Moivre's Theorem and roots of complex numbers
