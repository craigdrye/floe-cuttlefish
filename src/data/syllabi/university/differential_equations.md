# Differential Equations
**ID**: `differentialEquations` · **Discipline**: Mathematics · **Year**: University Junior

## Course Philosophy
Differential equations are used to model dynamic systems where the rate of change is known, but the underlying function is not. This course covers analytical, graphical, and numerical techniques for solving Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs).

## Module 1: First-Order Differential Equations (20%)
- Mathematical models: Population dynamics, radioactive decay, Newton's Law of Cooling
- Direction fields (slope fields) and Euler's Method
- Separable equations
- Linear first-order equations and Integrating Factors
- Exact equations and integrating factors for exactness
- Substitution methods: Homogeneous equations and Bernoulli equations
- **Questions**: Sketching a solution curve on a direction field, solving a linear ODE using an integrating factor, solving a mixing tank problem

## Module 2: Second-Order Linear Equations (25%)
- Homogeneous linear equations with constant coefficients
- The Characteristic Equation: real/distinct roots, repeated roots, complex roots
- Linear independence and the Wronskian
- Non-homogeneous equations: Method of Undetermined Coefficients
- Method of Variation of Parameters
- Mechanical and Electrical Vibrations (Mass-spring systems, RLC circuits): Free/forced vibrations, undamped/damped motion, resonance
- **Questions**: Solving a 2nd order homogeneous equation with complex roots, finding a particular solution using Undetermined Coefficients, analyzing resonance in a mass-spring system

## Module 3: Higher-Order Linear Equations & Systems (20%)
- Extending second-order theory to $n$th order linear equations
- Systems of first-order linear equations
- Matrices and linear systems (review of eigenvalues/eigenvectors)
- Solving homogeneous linear systems with constant coefficients ($X' = AX$)
- Phase portraits for $2\times2$ systems (nodes, saddles, spirals, centers)
- **Questions**: Converting a higher-order ODE to a system of first-order ODEs, drawing the phase portrait of a saddle point

## Module 4: The Laplace Transform (20%)
- Definition and properties of the Laplace Transform
- Inverse Laplace Transforms and Partial Fractions
- Solving initial-value problems using Laplace Transforms
- The Unit Step Function (Heaviside function) and piecewise continuous functions
- The Dirac Delta function (impulses)
- Convolution Integrals
- **Questions**: Finding the Laplace transform of a piecewise function, solving an ODE with a discontinuous forcing function using Laplace

## Module 5: Series Solutions and Numerical Methods (15%)
- Review of Power Series
- Series solutions near an ordinary point
- Series solutions near a regular singular point (Method of Frobenius)
- Bessel's Equation and Legendre's Equation (brief introduction)
- Advanced numerical methods: Runge-Kutta methods (RK4)
- **Questions**: Finding the recurrence relation for a series solution, conceptually explaining why RK4 is superior to Euler's method

## Stretch Zone
- Introduction to Partial Differential Equations (PDEs)
- Boundary Value Problems and Fourier Series (The Heat Equation, The Wave Equation)
