# Adulting & Life Skills (University)
**ID**: `lifeSkillsUni` · **Discipline**: Life Skills · **Year**: University (All)

## Course Philosophy
A rigorous, highly practical course designed for university students transitioning into independent adulthood. It expands on high school life skills, focusing on advanced financial planning, career launching, lease negotiation, and independent health management.

## Module 1: Advanced Financial Independence (25%)
- Budgeting on an irregular income (internships, gig work)
- Advanced Credit Management: FICO score components, credit utilization ratios, rewards cards vs interest rates
- Student Loans: Understanding subsidized vs unsubsidized, federal vs private, grace periods, and repayment plans (Income-Driven Repayment, PSLF)
- Investing Basics: Compound interest, the difference between a Roth IRA and a Traditional IRA, 401(k) employer matches, index funds (e.g., S&P 500) vs single stocks
- **Questions**: Calculating the long-term cost of deferring student loans, allocating a starting salary into a 50/30/20 budget including retirement contributions

## Module 2: Housing, Leases, and Independence (20%)
- The Economics of Renting: First/last/security deposits, application fees, co-signers
- Understanding a Lease Agreement: Joint and several liability (roommate risks), subletting clauses, breaking a lease
- Tenant Rights: Habitability laws, handling negligent landlords, the eviction process
- Setting up utilities, Wi-Fi, and managing shared expenses fairly (apps like Splitwise)
- **Questions**: Identifying predatory clauses in a sample lease, creating a roommate agreement for shared expenses

## Module 3: Career Launch & Professionalism (20%)
- The Modern Job Search: Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) optimization for resumes
- Networking: Conducting informational interviews, leveraging LinkedIn, the "hidden job market"
- The Interview: Advanced behavioral interviewing (STAR method), technical interviews, asking strategic questions
- Negotiation: Researching market rates, negotiating salary, equity, and benefits (PTO, relocation)
- Navigating HR: Understanding workplace rights, sexual harassment policies, and performance reviews
- **Questions**: Optimizing a resume for ATS, role-playing a salary negotiation based on market research

## Module 4: Independent Healthcare & Taxes (20%)
- Navigating Health Insurance: Premiums, deductibles, copays, out-of-pocket maximums, HMOs vs PPOs, HSAs (Health Savings Accounts) vs FSAs
- Mental Health: Accessing therapy, understanding in-network vs out-of-network coverage
- Taxes for Adults: W-4s, W-2s, 1099s (freelance tax implications/estimated quarterly taxes)
- Standard vs Itemized deductions (SALT, student loan interest deduction)
- **Questions**: Comparing two health insurance plans to find the lowest total cost for a specific medical scenario, calculating freelance tax liability

## Module 5: Domestic Management & Emotional Intelligence (15%)
- Routine maintenance: Deep cleaning schedules, basic car maintenance (oil changes, tire rotation), basic home repairs
- Nutrition and meal prepping on a tight schedule/budget
- Emotional Intelligence (EQ): Active listening, conflict resolution in professional and personal settings, setting boundaries
- Time Management: Task batching, avoiding burnout, the Pomodoro technique for deep work
- **Questions**: Creating a weekly meal prep plan under $50, drafting a professional email to set a boundary with an overbearing manager

## Stretch Zone
- Purchasing property (understanding mortgages, down payments, and closing costs)
- Estate planning basics (Wills, Power of Attorney)
