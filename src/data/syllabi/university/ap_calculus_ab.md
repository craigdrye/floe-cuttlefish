# AP Calculus AB
**ID**: `apCalculusAB` · **Discipline**: Mathematics · **Stage**: University Freshman (ages 17-19; AP exam taken in HS senior year, recurring as freshman calc-1 prep)
**Aligns with**: College Board AP Calculus AB Course and Exam Description, Units 1-8; equivalent to a one-semester university Calculus I

## Course Aim
Build a clear first calculus toolkit: limits describe approaching behavior, derivatives measure instantaneous change, and integrals measure accumulation. Students should leave able to move among graphs, tables, formulas, words, and units while justifying conclusions in AP-style language.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test AP Calculus AB limits, continuity, differentiation, applications of derivatives, integration, differential equations, applications of integration, FRQ reasoning, calculator interpretation, or notation discipline. The course should be rigorous but not mystical: every rule should connect to a slope, rate, accumulation, approximation, or model.

## Chapter 1: Limits and Continuity
- **Key concepts**: One-sided and two-sided limits, algebraic limit techniques, graphical and tabular limits, limits at infinity, infinite limits, asymptotes, continuity, removable and nonremovable discontinuities, Intermediate Value Theorem.
- **Practice questions**: Evaluate limits from graphs, tables, and formulas; classify discontinuities; use IVT to justify existence.
- **Student output**: A limit-and-continuity reference sheet with examples of each representation and a justification sentence for IVT.

## Chapter 2: Differentiation: Definition and Fundamental Rules
- **Key concepts**: Difference quotient, derivative at a point, derivative as a function, tangent line, instantaneous rate of change, differentiability, power rule, product rule, quotient rule, and basic trigonometric, exponential, and logarithmic derivatives.
- **Practice questions**: Use the derivative definition; match derivative graphs; compute derivatives with mixed rules; interpret derivative values with units.
- **Student output**: A derivative meanings chart connecting formula, graph, table, and context.

## Chapter 3: Differentiation: Composite, Implicit, and Inverse Functions
- **Key concepts**: Chain rule, implicit differentiation, inverse function derivatives, inverse trigonometric derivatives, logarithmic differentiation at an introductory level, and higher-order derivatives.
- **Practice questions**: Differentiate layered functions; find slopes on implicit curves; use table data to compute inverse derivative values.
- **Student output**: A chain-rule and implicit-differentiation problem set with written explanations of each dependency.

## Chapter 4: Contextual Applications of Differentiation
- **Key concepts**: Motion, velocity, acceleration, speed, related rates, local linearity, linear approximation, differentials, L'Hospital's rule, and contextual units.
- **Practice questions**: Solve motion and related-rates problems; approximate values; decide when L'Hospital's rule applies; explain answers in context.
- **Student output**: A contextual derivative notebook page with setup diagrams, equations, units, and final interpretation.

## Chapter 5: Analytical Applications of Differentiation
- **Key concepts**: Mean Value Theorem, Rolle's Theorem, Extreme Value Theorem, critical points, increasing and decreasing intervals, concavity, inflection points, optimization, curve sketching, and derivative graph analysis.
- **Practice questions**: Justify extrema; analyze functions from derivative information; solve optimization problems; write AP-style sign-chart reasoning.
- **Student output**: A full function-analysis report from f, f', or f'' information.

## Chapter 6: Integration and Accumulation of Change
- **Key concepts**: Antiderivatives, Riemann sums, definite integrals, signed area, Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, properties of integrals, u-substitution, average value, and accumulation functions.
- **Practice questions**: Approximate integrals from tables; evaluate definite and indefinite integrals; differentiate accumulation functions; interpret net change.
- **Student output**: An accumulation explainer connecting Riemann sums, definite integrals, and FTC in one example.

## Chapter 7: Differential Equations
- **Key concepts**: Slope fields, verifying solutions, separable differential equations, initial conditions, exponential growth and decay, and model interpretation.
- **Practice questions**: Match slope fields to differential equations; solve separable equations; interpret parameters in growth and decay models.
- **Student output**: A differential-equation model brief with slope field, solution, initial condition, and interpretation.

## Chapter 8: Applications of Integration and FRQ Studio
- **Key concepts**: Net change, total distance, area between curves, volume by disks and washers, volume by known cross sections, average value, setup versus evaluation, calculator-active reasoning, and FRQ communication.
- **Practice questions**: Set up and evaluate area and volume integrals; compute distance from velocity; write complete FRQ solutions with notation and units.
- **Student output**: A timed FRQ portfolio with one derivative, one integral, one differential-equation, and one accumulation/application problem.

## Capstone
Students complete an AP Calculus AB readiness portfolio: one-page unit summaries, a corrected error log, four timed FRQs, one mixed MCQ reflection, and a modeling explanation that uses both derivatives and integrals.

## Assessment Ideas
- Frequent representation-switching checks: graph to formula, table to derivative, context to integral.
- Short written justifications for IVT, MVT, extrema, concavity, and FTC.
- Timed calculator and no-calculator practice blocks.
- FRQ rubrics that reward setup, notation, units, and conclusion, not just final answers.
- Final mock-exam review sorted by concept gap, algebra slip, notation issue, or time-management problem.
