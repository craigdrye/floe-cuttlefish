# Multivariable Calculus
**ID**: `multivariableCalculus` · **Discipline**: Mathematics · **Year**: University Junior

## Course Philosophy
Multivariable Calculus (often Calculus III) extends the concepts of limits, derivatives, and integrals to functions of multiple independent variables. It is the mathematical language of electromagnetism, fluid dynamics, and advanced economics.

## Module 1: Vectors and the Geometry of Space (20%)
- 3D Coordinate Systems (Rectangular, Cylindrical, Spherical)
- Vectors in 3D: Dot product, cross product, lines, and planes
- Quadric surfaces (ellipsoids, paraboloids, hyperboloids)
- Vector-valued functions: Limits, derivatives, integrals
- Space curves: Arc length, curvature, and the TNB frame (Tangent, Normal, Binormal vectors)
- Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion (derived using vector calculus)
- **Questions**: Finding the equation of a plane tangent to a sphere, calculating the curvature of a helix

## Module 2: Partial Derivatives (20%)
- Functions of several variables: Domain, range, level curves, and contour maps
- Limits and Continuity in higher dimensions (proving a limit does not exist using different paths)
- Partial derivatives and Clairaut's Theorem (mixed partials)
- Tangent planes and linear approximations
- The Chain Rule for functions of several variables
- Directional derivatives and the Gradient vector ($\nabla f$)
- **Questions**: Calculating the directional derivative in the direction of steepest ascent, using the chain rule on $z = f(x(t), y(t))$

## Module 3: Extrema and Optimization (15%)
- Maximum and minimum values of $f(x,y)$: Critical points and the Second Derivatives Test (the discriminant $D = f_{xx}f_{yy} - (f_{xy})^2$)
- Absolute extrema on closed, bounded sets
- Lagrange Multipliers (optimizing a function subject to a constraint: $\nabla f = \lambda \nabla g$)
- Applications to economics and physics
- **Questions**: Finding local extrema using the second derivative test, maximizing volume using Lagrange multipliers

## Module 4: Multiple Integrals (25%)
- Double integrals over rectangles and general regions
- Iterated integrals and Fubini's Theorem (changing the order of integration)
- Double integrals in polar coordinates
- Applications: Area, volume, center of mass, moment of inertia
- Triple integrals in rectangular, cylindrical, and spherical coordinates
- Change of Variables in multiple integrals (The Jacobian)
- **Questions**: Changing the order of integration to evaluate a complex integral, finding volume using spherical coordinates, calculating a Jacobian

## Module 5: Vector Calculus (20%)
- Vector fields (conservative vector fields and potential functions)
- Line integrals and the Fundamental Theorem for Line Integrals
- Green's Theorem (relating line integrals to double integrals)
- Curl and Divergence
- Parametric surfaces and their areas
- Surface integrals and flux
- Stokes' Theorem (relating line integrals to surface integrals)
- The Divergence Theorem (Gauss's Theorem - relating surface integrals to triple integrals)
- **Questions**: Evaluating a line integral using Green's Theorem, calculating the flux of a vector field across a surface, applying the Divergence Theorem

## Stretch Zone
- Differential forms and generalized Stokes' Theorem
- Applications to Maxwell's Equations
