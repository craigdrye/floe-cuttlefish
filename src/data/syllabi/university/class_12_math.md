# Class 12 Math
**ID**: `class12Math` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: University Sophomore (equivalent)

## Course Philosophy
Class 12 Math covers advanced calculus, vectors, 3D geometry, and probability, serving as the final preparation for university-level STEM degrees.

## Module 1: Relations, Functions, and Inverse Trigonometry (20%)
- Types of relations: reflexive, symmetric, transitive, and equivalence relations
- One-to-one and onto functions, composite functions, and invertible functions
- Inverse trigonometric functions: domain, range, principal value branches
- Properties of inverse trigonometric functions
- **Questions**: Proving a relation is an equivalence relation, finding the principal value of $\arcsin(-\frac{1}{2})$

## Module 2: Algebra (Matrices and Determinants) (20%)
- Matrices: order, equality, operations (addition, scalar multiplication, matrix multiplication)
- Transpose, symmetric, and skew-symmetric matrices
- Determinants of a square matrix (up to $3\times3$), properties of determinants
- Adjoint and inverse of a square matrix
- Solving system of linear equations in two or three variables using matrix method
- **Questions**: Finding the inverse of a $3\times3$ matrix, solving a system of equations using Cramer's rule

## Module 3: Calculus (Continuity, Differentiability, Applications) (25%)
- Continuity and differentiability, derivative of composite functions, chain rule
- Derivatives of inverse trigonometric, exponential, and logarithmic functions
- Logarithmic differentiation, derivative of functions expressed in parametric forms
- Second-order derivatives
- Applications of derivatives: rate of change, increasing/decreasing functions, tangents and normals, maxima and minima
- **Questions**: Finding the absolute maximum of a function on a closed interval, logarithmic differentiation of $x^x$

## Module 4: Integral Calculus and Differential Equations (20%)
- Integration as inverse process of differentiation
- Integration by substitution, by parts, and by partial fractions
- Definite integrals as a limit of a sum, Fundamental Theorem of Calculus
- Applications of integrals: area under simple curves, area between two curves
- Differential equations: order and degree, general and particular solutions
- Methods of solving first-order, first-degree differential equations (variable separable, homogeneous, linear)
- **Questions**: Finding the area enclosed by a parabola and a line, solving a linear differential equation

## Module 5: Vectors, 3D Geometry, and Probability (15%)
- Vectors and scalars, magnitude and direction, direction cosines/ratios
- Types of vectors, addition, position vector, scalar (dot) product, vector (cross) product
- 3D Geometry: Direction cosines and ratios of a line joining two points
- Cartesian and vector equation of a line, coplanar and skew lines, shortest distance between two lines
- Cartesian and vector equation of a plane, angle between two lines/planes/line and a plane
- Probability: Conditional probability, multiplication theorem, independent events, total probability, Bayes' theorem
- Random variable and its probability distribution, mean and variance
- **Questions**: Finding the shortest distance between two skew lines, applying Bayes' theorem to find posterior probability

## Stretch Zone
- Linear Programming: mathematical formulation of LP problems, graphical method of solution for two variables
