# MCAT Prep
**ID**: `mcat` - **Discipline**: Exam Prep - **Year**: University Senior

## Course Aim
Prepare students for the MCAT by integrating scientific content, passage reasoning, experimental design, data interpretation, timing, and review cycles. The course treats the exam as applied reasoning under pressure, not just memorization.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they involve MCAT chemistry/physics, biology/biochemistry, psychology/sociology, CARS, passage strategy, graph interpretation, experimental design, high-yield review, timing, or missed-question analysis. Keep content review tied to passages and AAMC-style reasoning.

## Chapter 1: MCAT Map, Diagnostic, and Study System
**Key concepts**: Exam structure, section timing, passage style, content outline, diagnostic review, spaced repetition, error log, score goals, stamina, and weekly planning.
**Practice questions**: Diagnose a score report; categorize missed questions; build a review calendar; decide when to review content versus practice passages.
**Student output**: A personal MCAT study plan with diagnostic summary, content priorities, and review loop.

## Chapter 2: Chemical Foundations and Quantitative Reasoning
**Key concepts**: Atomic structure, periodic trends, stoichiometry, thermodynamics, kinetics, equilibrium, acids and bases, electrochemistry, units, estimation, and dimensional analysis.
**Practice questions**: Interpret titration or electrochemistry passages; estimate without a calculator; identify limiting reagents; connect equilibrium shifts to biological systems.
**Student output**: A chemistry passage notebook with equations, units, graph interpretations, and recurring traps.

## Chapter 3: Physics in Biological Systems
**Key concepts**: Kinematics, forces, work, energy, fluids, thermodynamics, electrostatics, circuits, waves, sound, optics, atomic physics, and biomedical applications.
**Practice questions**: Apply Bernoulli's equation to blood flow; analyze circuits; interpret wave and optics data; connect physical laws to physiology.
**Student output**: A physics formula sheet organized by concept, units, assumptions, and biological example.

## Chapter 4: Biology, Physiology, and Cell Systems
**Key concepts**: Cell structure, membranes, organelles, cell cycle, genetics, evolution, viruses, nervous system, endocrine system, cardiovascular system, respiratory system, immune system, digestive system, renal system, reproductive system, and musculoskeletal system.
**Practice questions**: Trace hormone feedback; interpret genetics passages; connect organ-system function to homeostasis; evaluate experimental results in cell biology.
**Student output**: A systems map linking organs, mechanisms, feedback loops, and common passage clues.

## Chapter 5: Biochemistry and Molecular Biology
**Key concepts**: Amino acids, protein structure, enzymes, Michaelis-Menten kinetics, carbohydrates, lipids, metabolism, glycolysis, citric acid cycle, oxidative phosphorylation, DNA replication, transcription, translation, and lab techniques.
**Practice questions**: Interpret enzyme plots; identify inhibitor type; trace metabolic regulation; analyze Western blot, chromatography, or spectroscopy data.
**Student output**: A biochemistry pathway and lab-methods guide built around passage interpretation.

## Chapter 6: Psychology, Sociology, and Behavior
**Key concepts**: Sensation, perception, learning, memory, cognition, emotion, stress, development, personality, psychological disorders, social psychology, culture, institutions, inequality, demographics, health disparities, and biological bases of behavior.
**Practice questions**: Apply theories to scenarios; identify study designs; connect social determinants to health; distinguish similar terms; interpret survey data.
**Student output**: A behavioral-science term map with examples, contrasts, and health-context applications.

## Chapter 7: CARS and Critical Reading
**Key concepts**: Main idea, author's purpose, tone, structure, assumption, inference, evidence, application, humanities passage, social science passage, and answer-choice traps.
**Practice questions**: Summarize dense paragraphs; identify the author's claim; evaluate a new-information question; eliminate extreme or unsupported choices; transfer an idea to a new case.
**Student output**: A CARS review log tracking passage summaries, missed-question types, and revised reading habits.

## Chapter 8: Experimental Design, Full-Lengths, and Test Day
**Key concepts**: Independent variable, dependent variable, control, confounder, validity, reliability, p-value, confidence interval, figure reading, passage mapping, timing, endurance, test-day routine, and review discipline.
**Practice questions**: Identify controls; evaluate conclusions; interpret figures; triage hard questions; review full-length exams; plan breaks and pacing.
**Student output**: A full-length exam review packet with error categories, science gaps, reasoning gaps, and next-step adjustments.

## Capstone
Students complete a final MCAT readiness portfolio: diagnostic history, content maps, CARS log, experiment-design checklist, full-length review, and final test-week plan.

## Assessment Ideas
Use timed passages, content checks, equation fluency, graph interpretation drills, CARS logs, full-length reviews, and oral explanations of missed questions. Assess the quality of review as much as raw score improvement.
