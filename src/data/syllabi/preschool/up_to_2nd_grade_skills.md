# Up to 2nd grade skills (Collection)
**ID**: `col-up-to-2nd-grade-skills` - **Discipline**: Kids Discovery

## Course Aim
Support children who are ready for the upper end of early-years learning, moving toward Grade 2 skills through playful fluency, short texts, concrete science, and everyday social understanding.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when a child is beyond basic preschool foundations but still benefits from short prompts, manipulatives, pictures, movement, and adult read-aloud support. Keep activities warm, concrete, and success-oriented.

## Chapter 1: Number Sense and Counting Patterns
**Key concepts**: Counting, skip counting, number order, more and less, tens and ones, and number lines.
**Practice questions**: Count forward and backward; skip count by 2s, 5s, and 10s; compare two numbers; build numbers with tens and ones.
**Student output**: A number pattern page using objects, drawings, or counters.

## Chapter 2: Addition, Subtraction, and Early Multiplication
**Key concepts**: Addition within 20, subtraction within 20, part-whole thinking, doubles, making ten, and groups of.
**Practice questions**: Solve concrete addition and subtraction stories; find missing parts; show multiplication as equal groups.
**Student output**: A story-problem booklet with drawings and number sentences.

## Chapter 3: Time, Measurement, Shapes, and Fractions
**Key concepts**: Hour time, length, comparison, non-standard units, 2D shapes, 3D shapes, halves, quarters, and spatial language.
**Practice questions**: Tell time to the hour; measure with blocks; sort shapes; split a shape into halves or quarters.
**Student output**: A mini measurement hunt around the classroom or home.

## Chapter 4: Reading Simple Words and Sentences
**Key concepts**: CVC words, blends, simple sentences, punctuation, sight words, and decoding.
**Practice questions**: Read short sentences; match words to pictures; identify full stops and question marks; choose a word that completes a sentence.
**Student output**: A sentence strip collection with drawings.

## Chapter 5: Listening, Comprehension, and Vocabulary
**Key concepts**: Main event, character, setting, sequence, question words, synonyms, antonyms, and inference.
**Practice questions**: Answer who/what/where questions; retell what happened first, next, and last; choose a word with a similar or opposite meaning.
**Student output**: A three-picture retell of a short story.

## Chapter 6: Science Discovery
**Key concepts**: Materials, push, pull, light, shadow, sound, living things, non-living things, magnets, observation, and simple testing.
**Practice questions**: Sort materials by properties; predict whether a magnet will attract an object; describe a shadow; compare loud and quiet sounds.
**Student output**: A science notebook page with question, test, and what happened.

## Chapter 7: Place, Community, and Needs
**Key concepts**: Neighborhood, helpers, maps, rules, needs, wants, recycling, and caring for shared places.
**Practice questions**: Identify community helpers; sort needs and wants; follow a simple map; explain a helpful rule.
**Student output**: A map or poster of a helpful community.

## Chapter 8: Friendship, Feelings, and Problem Solving
**Key concepts**: Feelings, turn-taking, kindness, respect, differences, problem solving, apologies, and asking for help.
**Practice questions**: Name feelings in a story; choose a kind response; explain what to do when two people want the same thing.
**Student output**: A simple social story with a problem and kind solution.

## Capstone
Children create a "Ready for Grade 2" folder with a math page, sentence page, story retell, science observation, community drawing, and friendship scenario.

## Assessment Ideas
Use short oral checks, observation notes, picture sorting, read-aloud responses, counting tasks, simple writing samples, and playful one-on-one conferences.
