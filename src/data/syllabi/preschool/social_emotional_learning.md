# Social & emotional learning (Collection)
**ID**: `col-social-emotional-learning` · **Discipline**: Kids Discovery

## Course Philosophy
This is our dedicated SEL (Social-Emotional Learning) track, aligned with
CASEL's five competencies adapted for early childhood: self-awareness,
self-management, social awareness, relationship skills, and responsible
decision-making. In a quiz format, these translate to scenario-based judgment
questions, emotion identification, and perspective-taking exercises.

## Module 1: Self-Awareness (20%)
- Name and recognise emotions in self
- Identify personal strengths ("I am good at…")
- Understand that everyone has things they find hard
- Body awareness: how emotions feel physically (butterflies in tummy, hot cheeks)

## Module 2: Self-Management (20%)
- Calming strategies (deep breathing, counting to 5, squeezing hands)
- Waiting and patience
- Following instructions and routines
- Dealing with disappointment ("I didn't win the game")
- Impulse control: "Stop, think, choose"

## Module 3: Social Awareness (20%)
- Recognising emotions in others (facial expressions, body language)
- Understanding that not everyone feels the same way you do
- Cultural sensitivity: different families, foods, celebrations
- Kindness towards animals and nature
- "How would you feel if someone did that to you?"

## Module 4: Relationship Skills (20%)
- What makes a good friend
- Asking to join a game nicely
- Saying sorry and meaning it
- Helping someone who is struggling
- Working together (taking turns, compromising)
- Dealing with disagreements without hitting or shouting

## Module 5: Responsible Decision-Making (20%)
- Thinking before acting
- Understanding consequences: "If I do X, then Y might happen"
- Asking for help when needed
- Being honest even when it's hard
- Taking care of shared spaces and things
- Standing up for someone who is being treated unfairly

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduction to "growth mindset" — your brain grows when you try hard things
- Gratitude: "What are you thankful for?"
- Leadership: "What does it mean to be a leader? Is it the same as being bossy?"
- Resilience: "What do you do when something goes wrong?"
