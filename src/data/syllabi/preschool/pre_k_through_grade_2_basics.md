# Pre-K through Grade 2 Basics
**ID**: `col-pre-k-through-grade-2-basics` | **Discipline**: Kids Discovery | **Level**: Pre-K through Grade 2

## Course Aim
Give young learners a warm bridge from early-years play into the first years of school. Children build early literacy, numeracy, science talk, social routines, fine motor confidence, and curiosity through stories, songs, manipulatives, outdoor noticing, pretend play, drawing, talk, and short shared challenges.

## Course Design Notes
This collection is broad by design. It should feel like a friendly learning studio rather than a test-prep course: children can point, build, move, chant, draw, tell, sort, count, compare, and try again. Adults support language, attention, turn-taking, safety, and fine motor access while keeping tasks concrete and joyful. Pre-K children may answer with gestures and objects; Grade 1-2 children can add labels, sentences, simple explanations, and early independence.

## Chapter 1: Classroom Routines and Learning Together
**Story and activity invitations**
- Practise hello routines, name games, tidy-up songs, turn-taking, listening bodies, and "first, next, then" visual routines.
- Use puppets to model asking for help, waiting, joining play, making space, and trying again.
- Explore classroom tools: crayons, glue sticks, scissors, blocks, books, counters, trays, and tablets with adult guidance.

**Adult prompts**
- What do we do first?
- How can we show we are ready to listen or play?
- What can you say if you need help?

**Tiny output**
- Make a personal or class routine card with pictures for one successful learning habit.

## Chapter 2: Letters, Sounds, and Word Play
**Story and activity invitations**
- Sing alphabet songs, match uppercase and lowercase letters, and find letters in names, labels, and book covers.
- Play with rhymes, syllable claps, beginning sounds, alliteration, oral blending, and sound hunts.
- Build letters with dough, sticks, sand trays, body shapes, or magnetic letters.

**Adult prompts**
- What sound do you hear at the start?
- Can you clap the parts of the word?
- Which letter is in your name?

**Tiny output**
- Create a name or sound card with one letter, one picture, and an adult-scribed word.

## Chapter 3: Stories, Talk, and Vocabulary
**Story and activity invitations**
- Read picture books and pause for who, what, where, when, why, and how questions.
- Retell stories with puppets, sequencing cards, small-world play, drawings, or three-part mats.
- Grow vocabulary for feelings, positions, weather, animals, food, body parts, actions, textures, and describing words.

**Adult prompts**
- Who is in the story?
- What happened first, next, and last?
- What word could describe how it feels, looks, moves, or sounds?

**Tiny output**
- Add one drawing, retell card, or adult-scribed sentence to a shared story wall.

## Chapter 4: Counting, Quantity, and Number Stories
**Story and activity invitations**
- Count real objects with one-to-one touching: snacks, shells, buttons, blocks, footsteps, jumps, and toy animals.
- Match numerals to sets, compare more, fewer, and the same, and subitise small groups.
- Act out tiny number stories with joining, taking away, sharing, and "how many altogether?"

**Adult prompts**
- How many are there?
- Did the last number tell us the total?
- What changed when we added one more or took one away?

**Tiny output**
- Make a counting mat showing a numeral, a set of objects, and a child-made mark or drawing.

## Chapter 5: Shapes, Patterns, and Making
**Story and activity invitations**
- Find circles, squares, triangles, rectangles, cubes, spheres, cylinders, and cones in everyday objects.
- Make and extend AB, ABB, AAB, and ABC patterns with beads, blocks, sounds, steps, colors, and claps.
- Build with loose parts, tangrams, pattern blocks, recycled materials, and drawing tools.

**Adult prompts**
- What shape do you see?
- What comes next in the pattern?
- How can we turn, flip, stack, or join these pieces?

**Tiny output**
- Build or draw a shape picture or pattern strip and tell an adult the rule.

## Chapter 6: Science Talk and Curious Questions
**Story and activity invitations**
- Notice weather, shadows, magnets, water, floating and sinking, plant growth, animal homes, sounds, textures, and light.
- Use simple science practices: observe, compare, predict, test, talk, draw, and wonder.
- Keep investigations short and sensory: water trays, seed jars, ramp rolls, nature tables, listening walks, and sorting collections.

**Adult prompts**
- What do you notice?
- What do you think might happen?
- How are these two things the same or different?

**Tiny output**
- Add a drawing, photo, or adult-scribed "I notice..." and "I wonder..." to a science notebook or display.

## Chapter 7: Fine Motor, Drawing, and Early Writing
**Story and activity invitations**
- Strengthen hands with dough, tweezers, pegs, threading, tearing, folding, squeezing, pouring, tracing, and construction play.
- Practise meaningful marks: name marks, labels, maps, menus, signs, captions, lists, and simple sentences.
- Use varied tools: thick crayons, chalk, paintbrushes, pencils, clipboards, whiteboards, scissors, glue, stickers, and stamps.

**Adult prompts**
- What are you making or writing?
- Where should this mark, label, or picture go?
- What tool would help your hand do that job?

**Tiny output**
- Create a purposeful mark-making artifact such as a label, sign, map, list, card, or caption.

## Chapter 8: Community, Feelings, and World Knowledge
**Story and activity invitations**
- Explore families, helpers, places, maps, transport, seasons, celebrations, animals, plants, and caring for shared spaces.
- Use role play to practise kindness, repair, sharing materials, including others, and caring for classroom jobs.
- Compare near and far, home and school, today and tomorrow, morning and night, and different kinds of weather.

**Adult prompts**
- Who helps in this place?
- How can we care for people, things, animals, and plants?
- What is the same and different about these homes, jobs, seasons, or celebrations?

**Tiny output**
- Add a picture, map mark, helper badge, care card, or adult-scribed fact to a class "our world" board.

## Capstone
Create a "Learning Studio Day." Children rotate through friendly stations: a story retell, a counting or shape build, a small science noticing table, a fine motor making task, and a caring-classroom role-play. Each child contributes at least one artifact to a shared portfolio, such as a sound card, counting mat, pattern strip, science drawing, label, map, or helper card.

## Assessment Ideas
- Use observation notes during play, routines, small groups, outdoor time, story time, and making stations.
- Collect portfolio artifacts that show letter awareness, oral language, counting, patterning, observation, fine motor control, and social participation.
- Listen for child explanations through speech, gesture, pointing, drawing, movement, or adult-scribed dictation.
- Use quick, low-pressure checks: count a set, match a letter, retell three events, extend a pattern, predict what will happen, or choose a calming/helping routine.
- Review the capstone for engagement, flexible communication, early academic skills, curiosity, persistence, and kindness in a group setting.
