# Ocean Adventures (Pre-school, ages 3-5)
**ID**: `oceanPreschool` | **Status**: playable

## Course Aim
Invite young children to explore oceans, shores, and sea life through water play, beach treasures, stories, songs, movement, simple models, and gentle care routines. The course builds early science ideas about animals, habitats, floating and sinking, waves, weather, tides, and looking after shared places.

## Course Design Notes
This is a sensory, story-led preschool ocean course. Children learn by splashing, scooping, sorting, listening, pretending, drawing, singing, and talking with an adult. Ocean science stays concrete: shells in a tray, toy animals in water, blue fabric waves, weather pictures, floating objects, sand play, and careful observation of photos or local shore materials. Adults keep activities safe and inclusive: no tasting water or beach finds, no handling unknown sea life, careful hygiene after sand and water play, and respect for children who prefer to watch before joining. Each chapter includes a small ocean idea, a hands-on or pretend-play routine, and a class artifact to revisit.

## Chapter 1: Hello, Ocean
**Circle-time invitations**
- Share ocean photos, shells, pebbles, seaweed pictures, blue fabric, wave sounds, and a short sea story.
- Ask: "What do you notice?", "What might you hear at the sea?", and "What does water do?"
- Move bodies like tiny ripples, rolling waves, splashes, and quiet still water.

**Tiny investigation**
- Explore a water tray with cups, spoons, funnels, sponges, and toy boats, noticing pour, drip, splash, soak, full, empty, fast, and slow.

**Playful output**
- Start an Ocean Wonder Wall with child drawings, photos, adult-scribed noticing words, and questions.

## Chapter 2: Beach Treasures
**Circle-time invitations**
- Sort safe beach-like objects such as shells, smooth stones, driftwood pieces, sea glass pictures, sand, and toy crabs.
- Ask: "Is it rough or smooth?", "Heavy or light?", and "Where might it belong?"
- Talk gently about leaving living things in their homes and asking an adult before touching.

**Tiny investigation**
- Use trays of dry and damp sand to press, scoop, pour, pat, and compare marks.

**Playful output**
- Make a Beach Treasure Museum with labels such as smooth, bumpy, striped, tiny, big, sandy, and shiny.

## Chapter 3: Who Lives in the Sea?
**Circle-time invitations**
- Meet familiar sea animals: fish, crab, sea star, turtle, dolphin, whale, octopus, jellyfish, shark, and seahorse.
- Ask: "How does it move?", "Where might it hide?", and "What body parts do you notice?"
- Keep categories simple: some animals have fins, some have shells, some have arms, and some breathe air.

**Pretend journey**
- Children choose animal cards and move across the room by swimming, gliding, crawling, floating, hiding, or surfacing for air.

**Playful output**
- Build a class Sea Life Parade mural with adult-scribed child comments about movement and body parts.

## Chapter 4: Rockpool Mini-Worlds
**Circle-time invitations**
- Look at rockpool photos or a model tray with stones, blue water, seaweed strips, shells, toy crabs, small fish, and sea stars.
- Ask: "Who might live in this little pool?", "Where is a safe hiding place?", and "What should our hands do?"
- Practice gentle observation: look closely, point, ask, and leave animals where they are.

**Tiny investigation**
- Use magnifiers with shells, stones, and seaweed pictures, noticing patterns, holes, stripes, spirals, and textures.

**Playful output**
- Create a Rockpool Map with hiding spots, seaweed homes, sandy spaces, and "look with care" picture signs.

## Chapter 5: Float, Sink, and Boat
**Circle-time invitations**
- Tell a story about a small boat carrying shells, leaves, blocks, corks, and stones.
- Ask: "What do you think will float?", "What might sink?", and "What changed when we added more?"
- Use everyday words: float, sink, heavy, light, full, empty, tip, balance, wet, and dry.

**Tiny investigation**
- Test classroom objects in a water tray, then build simple foil or recycled-material boats with adult help.

**Playful output**
- Make a Float and Sink picture chart using photos or drawings of what children tried.

## Chapter 6: Waves, Weather, and Tides
**Circle-time invitations**
- Notice ocean weather with pictures of sunny beaches, windy waves, rain, clouds, storms far away, and calm water.
- Ask: "What does the wind do to water?", "What should people wear?", and "When do we need a grown-up?"
- Introduce tides as the sea coming closer and going farther away, using simple before-and-after beach pictures.

**Tiny model**
- Move blue fabric, ribbons, or a shallow tray of water with gentle breaths, fans, or hands to show calm, breezy, and wavy water.

**Playful output**
- Create a Shore Weather Board with picture choices for sunny, windy, rainy, cloudy, calm, wavy, warm, and cold.

## Chapter 7: Ocean Sounds, Songs, and Stories
**Circle-time invitations**
- Listen to shells, wave recordings, rain sticks, drums, shakers, and soft whale-song clips.
- Ask: "Is this sound loud or quiet?", "Fast or slow?", and "What ocean story could it tell?"
- Read or retell a gentle sea journey with repeated phrases children can join.

**Pretend journey**
- Make an ocean orchestra: swishing fabric waves, tapping pebble rhythms, shaker rain, drum thunder, and quiet whale hums.

**Playful output**
- Record a class Ocean Sound Story through drawings, adult-scribed words, or a short audio clip if available.

## Chapter 8: Caring for Beaches and Oceans
**Circle-time invitations**
- Use picture cards to sort beach choices: bin litter, reuse a bottle, watch wildlife, stay with adults, leave shells if asked, and keep plastic away from water.
- Ask: "What helps the beach?", "What might hurt animals?", and "How can small hands help with a grown-up?"
- Emphasize care without fear: children can notice, ask, tidy safely with adults, and make kind choices.

**Tiny investigation**
- Run a pretend clean beach sort with paper, bottle caps, fabric scraps, toy animals, shells, and bins for reuse, recycle, compost where appropriate, and rubbish.

**Playful output**
- Make a Beach Care Promise poster with pictures for gentle hands, clean places, safe watching, and grown-up help.

## Capstone
Children host a "Tiny Ocean Explorer Day" in the classroom. The space may include the Ocean Wonder Wall, Beach Treasure Museum, Sea Life Parade mural, Rockpool Map, Float and Sink chart, Shore Weather Board, Ocean Sound Story, and Beach Care Promise. Children choose roles such as water helper, shell sorter, sea animal mover, boat tester, weather watcher, sound maker, or beach carer, then guide a trusted adult or another small group through one favorite ocean activity.

## Assessment Ideas
- Adult observation notes on sensory language, sorting, animal movement words, safe water and sand routines, floating and sinking predictions, and caring choices.
- Photo portfolio of water play, beach sorting, rockpool maps, animal movement, boat testing, weather boards, sound stories, and care posters.
- Adult-scribed child voice records using stems such as "I noticed...", "It floats because...", "The wave is...", "This animal can...", and "We care by..."
- Small-group conversation during play, story, song, and cleanup rather than quizzes or formal recall checks.
- Family connection prompt: invite caregivers to share a beach memory, notice weather near water, sing a sea song, or look for safe natural textures on a walk.
