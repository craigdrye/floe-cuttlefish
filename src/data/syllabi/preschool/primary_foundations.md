# Primary Foundations
**ID**: `primary` · **Status**: playable · **Discipline**: Academics

## Course Philosophy
This is the flagship pre-school course — the generalist "first day of school"
experience. It synthesizes the three pillars that every early-years framework
agrees on: **number sense**, **emergent literacy**, and **logical reasoning**.
The goal is school-readiness without the pressure of formal instruction.

## Module 1: Number Sense & Counting (25%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Count objects from 1–10 with one-to-one correspondence
- Recognise written numerals 0–9
- Understand "more," "fewer," and "the same"
- Compare groups of objects (which has more?)
- Understand cardinality (the last number you say is how many there are)
- Subitise small quantities (instantly recognise 1–4 without counting)

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Counting objects in a picture (1–10)
2. Matching numerals to quantities
3. Ordering numbers on a number line (1–5, then 1–10)
4. "Which group has more?" comparison questions
5. Simple addition stories: "You have 2 apples. You get 1 more. How many?"
6. Missing number in a sequence (1, 2, __, 4)

**Stretch Zone**:
- Count backwards from 10
- Introduce the concept of zero as "nothing" / empty set
- Simple patterns with numbers (2, 4, 6… what comes next?)
- Counting beyond 10 to 20

## Module 2: Emergent Literacy (25%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Recognise uppercase letters A–Z
- Identify initial sounds in words (phonemic awareness)
- Understand that print carries meaning (left-to-right, top-to-bottom)
- Identify rhyming words
- Retell a simple story in correct sequence (beginning, middle, end)
- Recognise own name in print

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Letter identification (show a letter, name it)
2. Initial sound matching: "Which picture starts with the /b/ sound?"
3. Rhyming pairs: "Which word rhymes with 'cat'?"
4. Story sequencing: Put 3 pictures in the right order
5. Vocabulary building: Name common objects (animals, food, body parts)
6. Sight words: Recognise high-frequency words (I, the, a, is, my)

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce lowercase letters and letter-case matching
- Blend CVC words (c-a-t → cat)
- Simple sentence reading: "The cat sat."
- Introduce question marks and full stops as "meaning marks"

## Module 3: Shapes, Patterns & Spatial Reasoning (25%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Identify and name 2D shapes (circle, square, triangle, rectangle)
- Recognise shapes in the real world
- Complete and extend simple AB, ABB, ABC patterns
- Understand spatial vocabulary (above, below, next to, inside, outside, between)
- Sort objects by one attribute (colour, shape, or size)

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Shape identification from pictures
2. "Which shape is different?" (odd one out)
3. Pattern completion: 🔴🔵🔴🔵__
4. "Where is the ball?" spatial reasoning questions
5. Sorting: "Which things are the same colour/shape?"
6. Real-world shapes: "What shape is a clock?" (circle)

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce 3D shapes (sphere, cube, cylinder, cone)
- Two-attribute sorting (big AND red)
- Symmetry: "Which is the mirror image?"
- Tangram-style "build this shape" puzzles

## Module 4: Logic, Reasoning & Problem Solving (25%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Identify what doesn't belong in a group
- Make simple predictions based on patterns
- Understand cause and effect in simple scenarios
- Follow two-step instructions
- Classify and categorise objects by function or type

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Odd one out: "Which doesn't belong? Apple, banana, car, grape"
2. "What comes next?" in visual sequences
3. Cause and effect: "If you drop a glass, what happens?"
4. Simple analogies: "A bird has feathers, a fish has…"
5. "What do you need?" scenarios (It's raining — umbrella, sunglasses, or hat?)
6. Sequential reasoning: "First we put on socks, then…"

**Stretch Zone**:
- Simple mazes and path-finding
- "If… then…" conditional reasoning
- Basic Venn diagram sorting (things that are toys AND things that are red)
- Early deduction: "Sam has a hat. The hat is not blue. What colour could it be?"
