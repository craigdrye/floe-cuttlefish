# Science Discovery: Early Years
**ID**: `scienceEarlyYears` · **Status**: soon · **Discipline**: Science

## Course Philosophy
This course introduces the **scientific method as a way of being curious**, not
as a formal process. At this age, science is observation, wonder, and play.
Aligned with EYFS "Understanding the World" and EYLF Outcome 4 (confident and
involved learners). The emphasis is on the five senses as scientific instruments
and nature as the primary laboratory.

## Module 1: My Five Senses (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Name the five senses and their associated body parts
- Use senses to describe objects (rough/smooth, loud/quiet, sweet/sour)
- Understand that we learn about the world through our senses
- Make observations and describe what they notice

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Match the sense to the body part (eyes → seeing)
2. "Which sense would you use to…?" questions
3. Sensory descriptions: "Is sandpaper rough or smooth?"
4. Mystery identification: "If something smells like flowers and feels soft, what might it be?"
5. Sensory comparison: "Which is louder — a whisper or a shout?"

**Stretch Zone**:
- Optical illusions for young children (things aren't always what they seem)
- Introduce the idea that some animals have different/better senses than us

## Module 2: Weather & Seasons (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Identify types of weather (sunny, rainy, cloudy, windy, snowy)
- Understand that weather changes daily and seasonally
- Know the four seasons and basic characteristics of each
- Connect weather to daily life (clothing, activities)

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. Weather identification from pictures or descriptions
2. "What would you wear on a rainy day?"
3. Season matching: Snowman → winter, flowers → spring
4. Sequencing the seasons in the correct cycle
5. Prediction: "If the sky is full of dark clouds, what might happen?"
6. Temperature concepts: Hot vs. cold (not numerical, contextual)

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce the water cycle as a simple story (rain falls, puddles dry, clouds form)
- Day length differences across seasons
- Weather in different countries (it can be summer in Australia when it's winter in the UK)

## Module 3: Plants & Growing Things (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Understand that plants are living things
- Know what plants need to grow (water, sunlight, soil)
- Name basic parts of a plant (root, stem, leaf, flower)
- Understand the concept of a seed growing into a plant

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. "What does a plant need?" selection questions
2. Label the parts of a plant
3. Sequencing: seed → sprout → plant → flower
4. "Which is a plant?" identification
5. Prediction: "What will happen if we don't water the plant?"
6. Matching fruits/vegetables to their plants

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce the idea that plants make food from sunlight (photosynthesis as a concept, not a term)
- Edible vs. non-edible plants
- Trees as very large plants — parts of a tree

## Module 4: Animals & Habitats (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Identify common animals and categorise them (farm, wild, pet, ocean)
- Understand that different animals live in different places (habitats)
- Know basic needs of animals (food, water, shelter)
- Recognise baby animals and their parent names (puppy→dog, kitten→cat)

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. "Where does this animal live?" habitat matching
2. Animal classification: "Which of these is a farm animal?"
3. Baby animal matching: "A baby sheep is called a…"
4. Animal sounds and features identification
5. "What does a fish need to live?" (water, food)
6. Compare: "How are a cat and a dog similar? How are they different?"

**Stretch Zone**:
- Simple food chains: "The caterpillar eats the leaf. The bird eats the caterpillar."
- Nocturnal vs. diurnal animals
- Migration and hibernation as simple concepts
- Endangered animals: "Some animals need our help"

## Module 5: My Body & Staying Healthy (20%)
**Learning Objectives**:
- Name major body parts (head, arms, legs, eyes, ears, mouth, hands, feet)
- Understand basic hygiene (washing hands, brushing teeth)
- Know that food and sleep help us grow
- Understand that exercise keeps us healthy

**Topics & Question Types**:
1. "Point to the…" body part identification
2. Hygiene sequencing: "What do you do before eating?" (wash hands)
3. Healthy vs. unhealthy food choices
4. "Why do we sleep?" understanding rest
5. Matching activities to body parts: "We kick a ball with our…"
6. Feelings and the body: "When you're scared, your heart beats…"

**Stretch Zone**:
- Introduce the skeleton as "the bones inside us"
- The heart as a pump — feel your pulse
- Germs as tiny things we can't see
- Teeth: baby teeth vs. grown-up teeth
