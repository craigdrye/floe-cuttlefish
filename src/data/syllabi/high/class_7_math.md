# Class 7 Math
**ID**: `col-class-7-math` | **Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 7 (ages 11-12)
**Frameworks**: UK KS3 Year 7, US Common Core Grade 7, ACARA v9 Year 7, India NCERT Class 7

## Course Aim
Build a calm, well-structured Year 7 mathematics course where students extend arithmetic into rational-number reasoning, proportional thinking, early algebra, geometry, statistics, probability, and practical applications. Students should leave the course able to calculate accurately, choose efficient strategies, explain why methods work, and use diagrams, tables, graphs, equations, and words to make sense of problems.

## Course Design Notes
Use Common Core Grade 7 mathematics, UK KS3 mathematics, NCETM progression materials, and local Year 7 expectations as the spine. Route questions here when they involve rational numbers, fractions, decimals, percentages, ratios, proportions, expressions, one- and two-step equations, angle facts, area, volume, statistics, probability, or multi-step applied problem solving. Keep practice cumulative: students should regularly estimate, check reasonableness, label units, explain operations with signed numbers, and connect numerical work to algebraic structure.

## Chapter 1: Rational Numbers and the Number Line
- **Key concepts**: Integers, positive and negative rational numbers, absolute value as distance from zero, opposites, ordering, comparing, coordinate positions, and interpreting negative quantities in context.
- **Practice questions**: Order mixed rational numbers; compare temperatures, elevations, and account balances; locate fractions and decimals on a number line; explain why one negative value is greater than another.
- **Student output**: A rational-number map with annotated number-line examples, context sentences, and a short explanation of opposites and absolute value.

## Chapter 2: Operations With Fractions, Decimals, and Integers
- **Key concepts**: Addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division with integers, fractions, decimals, and mixed numbers; equivalent forms; order of operations; factors, multiples, and prime factorization as useful tools.
- **Practice questions**: Compute signed-number expressions; convert between fractions and decimals; simplify fractions; use common denominators; apply order of operations with brackets and powers.
- **Student output**: A calculation-strategy page showing three multi-step problems solved with written reasoning, estimation, and a check.

## Chapter 3: Ratio, Proportion, and Unit Rates
- **Key concepts**: Ratio language, equivalent ratios, proportional relationships, unit rates, scale factors, constant of proportionality, ratio tables, double number lines, graphs, and equations of the form y = kx.
- **Practice questions**: Divide a quantity in a ratio; find best buys; scale recipes and drawings; identify proportional relationships from tables and graphs; explain what the constant of proportionality means.
- **Student output**: A proportion investigation comparing two rates or scaled designs with a table, graph, equation, and written recommendation.

## Chapter 4: Percent and Financial Applications
- **Key concepts**: Percent as a rate per 100, fraction-decimal-percent equivalence, percent of a quantity, percent increase and decrease, discounts, tax, markup, simple interest, and error checks using estimation.
- **Practice questions**: Find 15%, 37.5%, or 120% of a quantity; calculate sale prices and final costs; compare percentage changes; decide whether a percent answer is reasonable.
- **Student output**: A short consumer-math brief that models one purchase, discount, tax, or savings scenario and explains each calculation.

## Chapter 5: Expressions, Equations, and Inequalities
- **Key concepts**: Variables, constants, terms, coefficients, equivalent expressions, substitution, collecting like terms, distributive property, one-step and two-step equations, inequalities, and solution checking.
- **Practice questions**: Evaluate expressions; simplify expressions such as 3x + 2x - 4; expand simple brackets; write equations from word problems; solve and graph inequalities on a number line.
- **Student output**: An algebra notebook entry with one expression translation, one equation solution, one inequality graph, and written checks.

## Chapter 6: Geometry, Angles, and Measurement
- **Key concepts**: Angle facts, triangles, quadrilaterals, parallel-line angle relationships, circles, perimeter, area of triangles, parallelograms, trapeziums, and composite shapes, and volume of prisms.
- **Practice questions**: Find missing angles using straight-line, point, vertically opposite, triangle, and parallel-line rules; calculate area and perimeter; find volume of cuboids and triangular prisms; construct accurate diagrams.
- **Student output**: A geometry reasoning sheet with labeled diagrams, angle justifications, and one measurement problem with units.

## Chapter 7: Statistics and Probability
- **Key concepts**: Statistical questions, samples, dot plots, bar charts, line plots, histograms as appropriate, mean, median, mode, range, outliers, probability scale, theoretical probability, and experimental probability.
- **Practice questions**: Choose and calculate measures of center; interpret outliers; build and read data displays; calculate simple event probabilities; compare predicted and observed outcomes.
- **Student output**: A data mini-report using a class or provided dataset, including a graph, summary statistics, and one probability or sampling reflection.

## Chapter 8: Mathematical Applications and Reasoning Studio
- **Key concepts**: Multi-step problem solving, model choice, representation fluency, units, estimation, constraints, proportional reasoning, algebraic setup, geometric modeling, data interpretation, and explaining assumptions.
- **Practice questions**: Solve mixed problems that combine percent, ratio, equations, geometry, and data; identify missing information; critique incorrect solutions; compare two solution methods.
- **Student output**: A reasoning portfolio with three applied tasks, each showing setup, calculation, representation, answer sentence, and reasonableness check.

## Capstone
Students complete a Year 7 applied mathematics portfolio around a practical question such as planning a small event, designing a scaled room layout, comparing subscriptions or prices, analyzing class data, or modeling a simple trip. The final product must include rational-number calculation, proportional reasoning, percent work, one algebraic equation or inequality, one geometric diagram or measurement calculation, one data display, and a short reflection on assumptions and accuracy.

## Assessment Ideas
- Short fluency checks on signed numbers, fraction-decimal-percent conversion, proportional reasoning, percent, equation solving, and angle facts.
- Explanation prompts where students justify a calculation rule, ratio method, missing-angle step, or algebraic move.
- Error-analysis tasks involving common mistakes with negatives, percentages, simplification, units, or probability.
- Mixed representation tasks requiring a table, graph, diagram, equation, and written interpretation.
- Capstone graded on accuracy, representation quality, reasoning clarity, units, and reflection on reasonableness.
