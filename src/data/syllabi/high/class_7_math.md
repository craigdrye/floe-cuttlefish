# Class 7 Math
**ID**: `col-class-7-math` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 7 (ages 11–12)
**Frameworks**: UK KS3 Year 7, US Common Core Grade 7, ACARA v9 Year 7, India NCERT Class 7

## Course Philosophy
Year 7 is the beginning of secondary/high school mathematics. The focus shifts from arithmetic to algebra, deep understanding of rational numbers (fractions, decimals, percentages), and reasoning about geometric properties. Students must move beyond "how" to calculate, and start focusing on "why."

## Module 1: Rational Numbers & Integers (25%)
- Understand the full number line including negative integers and negative rational numbers
- Add, subtract, multiply, and divide integers (e.g., -5 × -3 = 15; -8 - (-4) = -4)
- Perform all four operations with fractions, decimals, and mixed numbers fluidly
- Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages (e.g., 3/8 = 0.375 = 37.5%)
- Use the order of operations (BODMAS/BIDMAS) including powers (indices) and nested brackets
- Find lowest common multiples (LCM) and highest common factors (HCF) using prime factorization
- **Questions**: Integer arithmetic, complex BODMAS, converting F/D/P, LCM/HCF word problems

## Module 2: Proportional Reasoning (20%)
- Express ratios in their simplest form and divide a quantity in a given ratio
- Understand the relationship between ratio and proportion
- Solve complex unit rate problems (e.g., 1/2 mile in 1/4 hour)
- Use proportional reasoning to solve percentage problems (e.g., calculating simple interest, percentage increase/decrease, tax, markup, discount)
- Identify the constant of proportionality in tables, graphs, and equations
- **Questions**: Dividing in a ratio, percentage change, identifying unit rates from graphs, simple interest

## Module 3: Algebra & Equations (25%)
- Use and interpret algebraic notation (e.g., 3y, ab, a/b)
- Simplify algebraic expressions by collecting like terms and expanding brackets (e.g., 3(x + 4) = 3x + 12)
- Substitute numerical values into formulae and expressions
- Form and solve one-step and two-step linear equations (e.g., 2x + 5 = 17)
- Understand and use the concepts of variables and constants
- Represent inequalities on a number line
- **Questions**: Expanding brackets, solving 2-step equations, substitution, writing equations from word problems

## Module 4: Geometry & Measure (15%)
- Calculate the area and perimeter of circles (introducing Pi), composite shapes, and trapeziums
- Calculate the volume and surface area of prisms (including cuboids and cylinders)
- Understand and use basic angle rules: angles on a straight line, around a point, vertically opposite
- Identify properties of parallel lines and angles (alternate, corresponding, co-interior)
- Construct triangles and other shapes using a ruler, protractor, and compasses
- **Questions**: Area of a circle (πr²), volume of a prism, finding missing angles using parallel line rules

## Module 5: Statistics & Probability (15%)
- Calculate and interpret the mean, median, mode, and range of a data set
- Understand how outliers affect the mean and median
- Construct and interpret pie charts, bar charts, and stem-and-leaf diagrams
- Understand that probabilities sum to 1
- Calculate the probability of single events and understand theoretical vs. experimental probability
- **Questions**: Calculating averages from frequency tables, interpreting stem-and-leaf, basic probability fractions

## Stretch Zone
- Solve equations with unknowns on both sides (e.g., 3x + 2 = x + 10)
- Understand the concept of compound interest (introductory)
- Factorise simple algebraic expressions (e.g., 6x + 9 = 3(2x + 3))
