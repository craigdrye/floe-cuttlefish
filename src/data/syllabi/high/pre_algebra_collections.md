# Pre-Algebra Collections
**Covers**: `col-illustrative-mathematics-6th-8th-grade`, `col-pre-algebra`, `col-algebra-basics`
**Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 9 (ages 13–14)

## Overview
These collections bridge the gap between middle school arithmetic and high school Algebra 1. The focus is on mastery of rational numbers, deep understanding of variables, solving equations, and linear relationships.

## Module 1: Rational Numbers & Arithmetic Properties (20%)
- Fluency with positive and negative integers, fractions, and decimals
- Absolute value as distance from zero
- The properties of operations (commutative, associative, distributive)
- Order of operations with integers and exponents
- Evaluating algebraic expressions by substituting specific values
- **Questions**: Complex arithmetic with negatives/fractions, evaluating expressions with nested operations

## Module 2: Expressions & Equations (30%)
- Simplifying algebraic expressions by combining like terms
- Expanding expressions using the distributive property (e.g., -3(2x - 5) = -6x + 15)
- Factoring simple linear expressions
- Solving one-step, two-step, and multi-step linear equations
- Solving equations with variables on both sides
- Identifying equations with one solution, no solution, or infinitely many solutions
- Solving and graphing linear inequalities on a number line
- **Questions**: Equation solving mechanics, identifying solution types (none/infinite), inequality graphing

## Module 3: Ratios, Proportions, & Percents (20%)
- Calculating complex unit rates (e.g., miles per gallon, cost per ounce)
- Setting up and solving proportions using cross-multiplication
- Converting between fractions, decimals, and percentages
- Solving percent problems: percent of a number, finding the whole, finding the percent
- Applications of percents: markups, markdowns, sales tax, tips, and simple interest
- Percent increase and decrease (percent change)
- **Questions**: Proportion solving, percent change calculations, real-world tax/discount word problems

## Module 4: Linear Relationships & Functions (15%)
- Plotting points on the Cartesian coordinate plane (all four quadrants)
- Understanding the concept of a function: each input has exactly one output
- Representing linear relationships with tables, graphs, and equations
- Calculating slope (gradient) from a graph, a table, or two points (rise over run)
- Understanding the y-intercept
- Graphing lines in slope-intercept form (y = mx + b)
- **Questions**: Identifying functions (vertical line test), calculating slope, graphing y=mx+b

## Module 5: Geometry & Data Basics (15%)
- Pythagorean theorem: finding the hypotenuse or a missing leg in right triangles
- Angle relationships: complementary, supplementary, vertical, and angles formed by transversals intersecting parallel lines
- Perimeter and area of 2D shapes; surface area and volume of prisms and cylinders
- Interpreting scatter plots and lines of best fit
- **Questions**: Pythagorean theorem applications, solving for missing angles using algebra, volume formulas

## Stretch Zone
- Introduction to systems of linear equations (graphing method)
- Scientific notation operations
- Exponent rules (multiplication, division, power of a power)
