# NAPLAN Prep (Year 9)
**ID**: `naplanYear9` | **Discipline**: Exam Prep | **Year**: 9 (ages 13-14)
**Framework**: Australian NAPLAN

## Course Aim
Prepare Year 9 students for their final NAPLAN assessment by strengthening mature reading, controlled writing, accurate language conventions, advanced middle-years numeracy, and strategic online test behaviour. Students learn to work from evidence, select efficient methods, write with purpose, and use diagnostic feedback to build confidence rather than last-minute panic.

## Course Design Notes
This course is for Australian lower secondary students approaching upper secondary expectations. Practice should be rigorous but steady: use NAPLAN-style tasks to diagnose patterns, then reteach the underlying reading, writing, conventions, numeracy, and digital-test skills. Align literacy work with the Australian Curriculum English Year 9 emphasis on interpretation, analysis, evaluation, and crafted texts; align numeracy with Australian Curriculum Mathematics Year 9 content in number, algebra, measurement, geometry, statistics, and probability. Include timed rehearsals, modelled thinking, error analysis, and student-owned target setting.

## Chapter 1: Final NAPLAN Readiness and Test Intelligence
- **Key concepts**: test domains, online navigation, adaptive difficulty, command words, time allocation, item triage, estimation, flagging, checking, stamina, and diagnostic error categories.
- **Practice questions**: What skill is hidden inside this question? Which items should be answered first, checked, or flagged? How can a student use an error log to choose the next practice set?
- **Student output**: a final NAPLAN strategy profile with strengths, risk areas, time routines, digital-test habits, and a targeted revision schedule.

## Chapter 2: Reading Inference, Tone, and Authorial Purpose
- **Key concepts**: explicit evidence, inference, implication, tone shift, point of view, audience, purpose, bias, reliability, context clues, and distractor analysis.
- **Practice questions**: Which evidence best supports a subtle inference? How does tone change across the passage? Which answer is attractive but not fully supported?
- **Student output**: a close-reading annotation set with evidence labels, tone notes, and corrected explanations for three challenging items.

## Chapter 3: Reading Complex and Multimodal Texts
- **Key concepts**: satire, irony, extended metaphor, structural choices, visual features, data in texts, source comparison, synthesis, conflicting perspectives, and evaluation.
- **Practice questions**: How does structure shape meaning? What does a graph or image contribute to the argument? How do two sources agree, qualify, or contradict each other?
- **Student output**: a synthesis matrix comparing two or three texts by claim, evidence, tone, reliability, and reader effect.

## Chapter 4: Writing Arguments With Control
- **Key concepts**: prompt analysis, contention, thesis, line of reasoning, paragraph sequence, evidence, elaboration, counterargument, rebuttal, modality, cohesion, and conclusion.
- **Practice questions**: Which thesis gives the clearest direction? Where does the reasoning need stronger evidence? How can a counterargument make the response more convincing?
- **Student output**: a timed persuasive response plan and one polished body paragraph with claim, evidence, elaboration, and rebuttal.

## Chapter 5: Narrative Craft and Writing Precision
- **Key concepts**: narrative perspective, character motivation, setting, tension, pacing, flashback, dialogue control, imagery, precise verbs, sentence rhythm, paragraphing, and revision.
- **Practice questions**: Which opening establishes conflict most efficiently? How does sentence length affect pace? Which revision shows rather than explains a character's emotion?
- **Student output**: a narrative scene rewritten in two drafts, with annotations explaining choices about voice, pacing, imagery, and sentence control.

## Chapter 6: Language Conventions for Mature Editing
- **Key concepts**: complex spelling, etymology, affixes, homophones, agreement in complex sentences, tense control, pronoun clarity, active and passive voice, commas, apostrophes, colons, semicolons, and quotation punctuation.
- **Practice questions**: Which spelling fits both meaning and word family? Which sentence has the clearest grammar? Where does punctuation clarify relationships between ideas?
- **Student output**: a mature editing checklist with corrected NAPLAN-style items, rule explanations, and a short edited paragraph showing convention control.

## Chapter 7: Numeracy - Algebra, Number, Measurement, and Geometry
- **Key concepts**: rational numbers, percentages, rates, scientific notation, index laws, expansion, factorisation, linear equations, simultaneous equations, graphs, Pythagoras, similarity, surface area, volume, trigonometry, angles, and units.
- **Practice questions**: Solve simultaneous equations; interpret a linear graph; calculate a missing length using Pythagoras or trigonometry; choose the correct measurement formula and units.
- **Student output**: a numeracy methods booklet with worked examples, diagrams, algebra steps, unit checks, and one alternative method comparison.

## Chapter 8: Numeracy - Statistics, Probability, and Mixed Reasoning
- **Key concepts**: data displays, scatter plots, association, line of best fit, mean, median, spread, outliers, skew, sample bias, independent events, dependent events, two-way tables, tree diagrams, and multistep modelling.
- **Practice questions**: Interpret a scatter plot; compare distributions; calculate a compound probability; decide whether a sample or conclusion is reliable.
- **Student output**: a data reasoning report with a display interpretation, probability solution, written conclusion, and a note about assumptions or limitations.

## Capstone
Students complete a final NAPLAN readiness portfolio. It includes a complex reading and synthesis set, a persuasive or narrative writing task with revision evidence, a conventions editing log, a mixed numeracy practice set, and a reflection explaining how the student will approach the online test calmly and strategically.

## Assessment Ideas
- Baseline and final diagnostic tasks for each NAPLAN domain, with growth tracked by skill rather than only score.
- Reading assessments marked for inference, evidence, synthesis, tone, structure, and evaluation of source reliability.
- Writing assessments using NAPLAN-style criteria, with separate feedback on ideas, structure, voice, vocabulary, cohesion, paragraphing, sentence control, punctuation, and spelling.
- Numeracy tasks marked for efficient strategy, accurate calculation, representations, units, and explanation of reasoning.
- Timed online rehearsals followed by item review, digital-strategy reflection, and targeted reteaching.
- Capstone graded on independence, accuracy, revision quality, diagnostic insight, and readiness for senior secondary learning.
