# NAPLAN Prep (Year 9)
**ID**: `naplanYear9` · **Discipline**: Exam Prep · **Year**: 9 (ages 13–14)
**Framework**: Australian NAPLAN

## Course Philosophy
Preparation for the Year 9 NAPLAN test, the final NAPLAN assessment in Australia. The focus is on sophisticated synthesis of information, critical analysis of authorial intent, advanced rhetorical writing, and complex algebra/geometry.

## Module 1: Reading (25%)
- Analyzing nuanced, multi-layered texts (satire, historical speeches, dense informational reports, classic literature)
- Evaluating the validity, reliability, and bias of different sources
- Inferring complex character motivations and identifying subtle shifts in tone or narrative perspective
- Analyzing how structural choices (e.g., pacing, extended metaphors, juxtaposition) create meaning
- **Questions**: Synthesizing conflicting sources, analyzing satire/irony, evaluating rhetorical effectiveness

## Module 2: Writing (25%)
- Persuasive: Crafting highly structured, authoritative arguments using sophisticated rhetorical devices (ethos, pathos, logos, rhetorical questions, parallel structure, analogy)
- Narrative: Experimenting with non-linear timelines, unreliable narrators, and atmospheric world-building
- Precision in vocabulary and complete control over syntax (varying sentence length and structure for deliberate effect)
- Demonstrating a clear awareness of audience and purpose throughout the text
- **Questions**: Evaluating rhetorical strategies, structural editing for impact, advanced vocabulary selection

## Module 3: Language Conventions (25%)
- Spelling: Mastery of complex, irregularly spelled words, homophones, and vocabulary derived from varied etymologies
- Grammar: Flawless subject-verb agreement in complex sentences, appropriate use of the subjunctive mood, mastery of active/passive voice for emphasis
- Punctuation: Accurate use of all punctuation marks, including semicolons for linked clauses, colons for exposition, and em-dashes for parenthetical emphasis
- **Questions**: Editing complex texts for subtle grammatical/punctuation errors, vocabulary in context

## Module 4: Numeracy (25%)
- Number & Algebra: Advanced index laws (including zero and negative indices), scientific notation, expanding/factorizing quadratic expressions, solving simultaneous linear equations, graphing linear and non-linear relationships
- Measurement & Geometry: Surface area and volume of complex composite solids (including cylinders/cones/spheres), Pythagoras' theorem in 3D, trigonometry (SOH CAH TOA) in right-angled triangles, congruence and similarity proofs
- Statistics & Probability: Analyzing data distributions (skewness, outliers), interpreting bivariate data (scatter plots, lines of best fit), calculating probabilities of dependent and independent compound events
- **Questions**: Simultaneous equations, 3D Pythagoras/trigonometry, dependent probability, non-linear graphing

## Stretch Zone
- Reading: Deconstructing postmodern literary techniques
- Numeracy: Solving quadratic equations using the quadratic formula, introductory circle theorems
