# Middle School Earth & Space Science
**ID**: `middleSchoolEarthSpace` · **Discipline**: Science · **Year**: 7 (ages 11-12)
**Frameworks**: US NGSS Middle School, UK KS3 Science

## Course Aim
Help students understand Earth as a set of interacting systems across huge scales of time and space. Students learn to use models, maps, data, and evidence to explain space systems, geologic history, water, weather, climate, and human impacts.

## Course Design Notes
This course is the bridge between general science curiosity and later climate, Earth science, and physics courses. Route questions here when they involve seasons, moon phases, gravity, rock layers, plate tectonics, water cycle, weather, climate, resources, or Earth-system interactions.

## Chapter 1: Space Systems and Scale
- **Key concepts**: Solar system objects, gravity, orbits, scale models, galaxies at an introductory level, and how distance changes observation.
- **Practice questions**: Compare planets and smaller solar-system objects; explain gravity's role in orbits; use a scale model to reason about distance.
- **Student output**: A space-scale model with written notes on what the model shows and hides.

## Chapter 2: Earth-Sun-Moon Patterns
- **Key concepts**: Rotation, revolution, seasons, Earth's tilt, moon phases, eclipses, tides at an introductory level, and pattern-based prediction.
- **Practice questions**: Explain seasons without using distance from the Sun; identify moon phases; predict eclipse conditions.
- **Student output**: A diagram sequence explaining one Earth-Sun-Moon pattern to a younger student.

## Chapter 3: Geologic Time and Earth's History
- **Key concepts**: Rock strata, relative dating, fossils as evidence, geologic time scale, major events in Earth history, and how evidence survives.
- **Practice questions**: Order rock layers; interpret a fossil sequence; distinguish relative from absolute dating.
- **Student output**: A geologic-history timeline that marks evidence, uncertainty, and scale.

## Chapter 4: Plate Tectonics and Earth Materials
- **Key concepts**: Earth's layers, plate movement, seafloor spreading, continental drift evidence, earthquakes, volcanoes, mountain building, minerals, and the rock cycle.
- **Practice questions**: Match plate boundaries to features; explain rock-cycle transformations; use map evidence for plate movement.
- **Student output**: A plate-boundary case study with map, hazard, and explanation.

## Chapter 5: Water, Weathering, and Surface Change
- **Key concepts**: Water cycle, groundwater, weathering, erosion, deposition, watersheds, soil formation, and interactions among geosphere, hydrosphere, atmosphere, and biosphere.
- **Practice questions**: Trace water through a system; compare mechanical and chemical weathering; predict how erosion changes a landscape.
- **Student output**: A local watershed or surface-change model with one human or natural influence included.

## Chapter 6: Atmosphere, Weather, and Climate
- **Key concepts**: Uneven heating, air pressure, fronts, wind, ocean currents, weather versus climate, greenhouse effect, and climate data.
- **Practice questions**: Read a weather map; distinguish short-term weather from long-term climate; explain the greenhouse effect with a model.
- **Student output**: A weather-to-climate data brief using a graph and a carefully limited claim.

## Chapter 7: Resources and Human Impacts
- **Key concepts**: Renewable and nonrenewable resources, land use, pollution, water quality, energy resources, climate change evidence, mitigation, adaptation, and sustainability tradeoffs.
- **Practice questions**: Evaluate resource choices; interpret environmental data; compare possible solutions and their tradeoffs.
- **Student output**: A resource or impact decision memo for a school, town, or region.

## Chapter 8: Earth Systems Investigation Studio
- **Key concepts**: System models, feedbacks, maps, field observations, data quality, uncertainty, risk, and communicating Earth-science evidence.
- **Practice questions**: Improve a field-data plan; identify system interactions; revise a model after new evidence.
- **Student output**: A final Earth-system investigation or model with data, map, and explanation.

## Capstone
Students explain one Earth-system change across at least three spheres: geosphere, hydrosphere, atmosphere, biosphere, or human systems.

## Assessment Ideas
- Diagram and map interpretation prompts.
- Short evidence arguments from rock layers, weather maps, or climate graphs.
- Fieldwork or data notebooks with observation quality emphasized.
- A final systems model defense.
