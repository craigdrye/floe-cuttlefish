# Middle School Earth & Space Science
**ID**: `middleSchoolEarthSpace` · **Discipline**: Science · **Year**: 7 (ages 11–12)
**Frameworks**: US NGSS Middle School, UK KS3 Science

## Module 1: Space Systems (20%)
- The Solar System: orbits, gravity, and the scale of the universe
- Earth-Sun-Moon system: phases of the moon, eclipses, and seasons (caused by Earth's tilt)
- Gravity's role in galaxy formation and the orbital motions within the solar system
- Characteristics of planets, moons, asteroids, and comets
- **Questions**: Explaining seasons (tilt vs distance), moon phase identification, gravity's role in orbits

## Module 2: History of Earth (20%)
- Geologic time scale: interpreting the fossil record and rock strata
- Plate tectonics as the unifying theory of geology
- Earth's interior structure (crust, mantle, inner/outer core)
- Continental drift, seafloor spreading, and the fossil evidence supporting Pangaea
- **Questions**: Stratigraphy (which layer is oldest?), fossil record interpretation, plate tectonic evidence

## Module 3: Earth's Systems (Geosphere & Hydrosphere) (20%)
- The rock cycle (igneous, sedimentary, metamorphic) and the processes that drive it (melting, cooling, weathering, pressure)
- The water cycle: evaporation, transpiration, condensation, precipitation, groundwater flow
- Interactions between the geosphere, hydrosphere, atmosphere, and biosphere
- Weathering (mechanical vs chemical) and erosion
- **Questions**: Rock cycle transformations, water cycle energy sources, identifying types of weathering

## Module 4: Weather and Climate (Atmosphere) (20%)
- Unequal heating of the Earth driving weather patterns and ocean currents
- Air masses, high/low pressure systems, and fronts
- The Coriolis effect and global wind patterns
- The difference between weather (short-term) and climate (long-term average)
- The greenhouse effect: how certain gases trap heat in the atmosphere
- **Questions**: Predicting weather from fronts, explaining the greenhouse effect, ocean current drivers

## Module 5: Human Impacts on Earth Systems (20%)
- Natural resources (renewable vs non-renewable) and human dependence on them
- Human activities affecting the environment (deforestation, urbanization, pollution)
- Climate change: evidence, causes (fossil fuels), and potential impacts
- Sustainable practices and mitigating human impact
- **Questions**: Evaluating renewable energy sources, analyzing climate change data graphs, mitigation strategies

## Stretch Zone
- Milankovitch cycles and their role in long-term climate change
- Kepler's laws of planetary motion (conceptual)
- Isotope dating (radiometric dating) concepts
