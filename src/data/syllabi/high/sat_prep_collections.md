# SAT Prep Collections
**Covers**: `col-sat-math`, `col-sat-reading-and-writing`, `col-get-ready-for-sat-prep-math`, `col-get-ready-for-sat-prep-reading-and-writing` · **Discipline**: Exam Prep · **Year**: 12 (ages 16-18)

## Course Aim
Prepare students for the digital SAT by building content mastery, adaptive-test strategy, pacing, official-practice habits, error review, and calmer test-day execution.

## Course Design Notes
The digital SAT has Reading and Writing plus Math sections, delivered in modules through College Board's Bluebook app. This syllabus emphasizes official question logic, short-passage reading, Desmos/calculator fluency, grammar precision, and post-test error analysis.

## Chapter 1: Digital SAT Format and Study Plan
- **Key concepts**: adaptive modules, Bluebook, section scores, official practice, diagnostic test, target score, timing.
- **Practice questions**: What does the diagnostic reveal? Which section is limiting the score? What is the next highest-return drill?
- **Student output**: a study calendar with weekly practice sets, review blocks, and official practice checkpoints.

## Chapter 2: Reading and Writing: Information and Ideas
- **Key concepts**: central ideas, details, command of evidence, inference, data-to-text synthesis.
- **Practice questions**: What claim does the passage support? Which evidence is strongest? What does the graph add?
- **Student output**: a passage-analysis log with evidence and miss-type notes.

## Chapter 3: Reading and Writing: Craft and Structure
- **Key concepts**: words in context, text structure, purpose, function, tone, cross-text connections.
- **Practice questions**: What does the word mean here? Why is this sentence included? How do the two authors differ?
- **Student output**: a vocabulary-in-context and function-question drill set.

## Chapter 4: Reading and Writing: Expression and Conventions
- **Key concepts**: transitions, rhetorical synthesis, boundaries, punctuation, subject-verb agreement, pronouns, modifiers, parallelism.
- **Practice questions**: Which transition fits the logic? Is the sentence complete? What punctuation joins the clauses correctly?
- **Student output**: a grammar and transitions correction notebook.

## Chapter 5: Math: Algebra and Linear Relationships
- **Key concepts**: linear equations, inequalities, systems, slope, intercepts, functions, units, word problems.
- **Practice questions**: What does slope represent? Which equation models the context? What answer is reasonable?
- **Student output**: an algebra drill packet with calculator and non-calculator solution routes.

## Chapter 6: Math: Advanced Math, Geometry, and Trigonometry
- **Key concepts**: quadratics, polynomials, rational expressions, exponents, function transformations, circles, triangles, area, volume, right-triangle trig.
- **Practice questions**: Which form of the quadratic is useful? What theorem applies? Can Desmos reveal the structure?
- **Student output**: a mixed advanced-math and geometry set with strategy notes.

## Chapter 7: Math: Problem Solving, Data, and Desmos Strategy
- **Key concepts**: ratios, rates, percentages, statistics, scatterplots, two-way tables, probability, regression intuition, calculator graphing.
- **Practice questions**: What is being compared? Which statistic answers the question? How can the calculator verify the result?
- **Student output**: a data-analysis set plus a Desmos/Bluebook tool checklist.

## Chapter 8: Full Practice, Review, and Test-Day Execution
- **Key concepts**: pacing, bubbling/digital navigation, guessing, stamina, official practice tests, error taxonomy, retake plan.
- **Practice questions**: Which misses were content, wording, timing, or careless? Which question types deserve one more pass?
- **Student output**: a full-practice-test review with score goals and next drills.

## Capstone
Complete one official-style full practice cycle: diagnostic, targeted drills, full practice test, error log, revised study plan, and test-day checklist.

## Assessment Ideas
- Reading/writing drills graded on evidence and grammar rule identification.
- Math drills graded on setup, efficiency, and verification.
- Capstone graded on error analysis and improvement plan quality.
