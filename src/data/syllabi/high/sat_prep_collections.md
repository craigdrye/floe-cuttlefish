# SAT Prep Collections
**Covers**: `col-sat-math`, `col-sat-reading-and-writing`, `col-get-ready-for-sat-prep-math`, `col-get-ready-for-sat-prep-reading-and-writing`
**Discipline**: Exam Prep · **Year**: 12 (ages 16–18)
**Frameworks**: US College Board Digital SAT

## Course Philosophy
This syllabus is tailored for the Digital SAT. It focuses heavily on time management, strategic guessing, identifying distractor answers, and mastering the specific logic patterns tested by the College Board.

## Module 1: SAT Math - Heart of Algebra & Problem Solving (25%)
- Linear equations, inequalities, and systems of linear equations
- Interpreting linear functions in context (slope as rate of change, y-intercept as initial value)
- Ratios, rates, and proportional relationships
- Percentages (percent increase/decrease, compound interest)
- Data analysis: interpreting scatterplots, two-way tables, and calculating mean/median/mode
- **Questions**: Solving systems of equations quickly, interpreting slope in a word problem, finding median from a frequency table

## Module 2: SAT Math - Passport to Advanced Math & Geometry (25%)
- Equivalent expressions: manipulating polynomials, rational expressions, and exponents
- Non-linear equations: solving quadratics (factoring, quadratic formula, vertex form)
- Functions: function notation, identifying domain/range, and transformations
- Geometry: area/volume of complex shapes, properties of angles/triangles/circles (arc length, sector area)
- Basic trigonometry (SOH CAH TOA) and the relationship between sine and cosine of complementary angles
- **Questions**: Converting standard quadratic to vertex form, using circle theorems, applying $\sin(x) = \cos(90-x)$

## Module 3: SAT Reading & Writing - Information and Ideas (20%)
- Central Idea and Details: identifying the main point and supporting evidence in short passages
- Command of Evidence: Quantitative (interpreting data from charts/graphs to support a claim) and Textual (selecting the quote that best supports an argument)
- Inferences: drawing logical conclusions based on stated facts
- Cross-Text Connections: comparing the viewpoints of two authors on the same topic
- **Questions**: Selecting the best evidence, synthesizing graph data with text, analyzing authorial disagreement

## Module 4: SAT Reading & Writing - Craft and Structure (15%)
- Words in Context: determining the precise meaning of high-utility academic vocabulary based on surrounding clues
- Text Structure and Purpose: analyzing how a paragraph is organized and why the author wrote it
- Point of View: identifying the narrator's perspective or the author's attitude/tone
- **Questions**: Choosing the most precise vocabulary word, explaining the function of a specific sentence within a paragraph

## Module 5: SAT Reading & Writing - Expression of Ideas & Standard English Conventions (15%)
- Transitions: choosing the correct transitional word (e.g., Furthermore, However, Consequently) to link ideas
- Rhetorical Synthesis: using provided notes to achieve a specific writing goal (e.g., introducing a scientist's work)
- Boundaries: fixing run-on sentences, comma splices, and fragments using correct punctuation (commas, semicolons, colons, dashes)
- Form, Structure, and Sense: subject-verb agreement, pronoun-antecedent agreement, verb tense, and parallel structure
- **Questions**: Placing semicolons correctly, identifying subject-verb agreement errors in complex sentences, selecting the best transition word

## Stretch Zone
- Math: Complex numbers ($i^2 = -1$) and circle equations completing the square
- Reading: Analyzing dense, 19th-century historical texts or complex scientific abstracts
