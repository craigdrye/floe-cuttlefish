# Creative Writing Studio
**ID**: `creativeWritingStudio` · **Discipline**: English · **Year**: 10 (ages 14–15)

## Course Philosophy
This course shifts the focus from analytical essay writing to creative expression. Students explore world-building, character development, and narrative structure across fiction, poetry, and screenwriting.

## Module 1: The Elements of Fiction (20%)
- "Show, Don't Tell": Using sensory details to build a scene
- Characterization: creating three-dimensional characters (flaws, desires, and ghosts)
- Setting as a character: world-building and atmosphere
- **Questions**: Rewriting "telling" sentences into "showing" scenes, analyzing character flaws

## Module 2: Plot and Structure (20%)
- The Three-Act Structure and the Hero's Journey (Monomyth)
- Kurt Vonnegut's "Shapes of Stories"
- Managing pacing, tension, and stakes (internal vs external stakes)
- The inciting incident, the midpoint, and the climax
- **Questions**: Identifying story shapes, analyzing stakes in a narrative

## Module 3: Dialogue and Voice (20%)
- Writing naturalistic dialogue vs stylized dialogue
- Using subtext (what characters are *not* saying)
- Developing a distinct authorial voice
- First-person, Second-person, and Third-person (limited vs omniscient) POV
- **Questions**: Identifying subtext in dialogue, rewriting a scene in a different POV

## Module 4: Poetry and Lyrical Prose (20%)
- Rhythm and meter (beyond iambic pentameter)
- Imagery, metaphor, simile, and personification
- Sound devices: alliteration, assonance, consonance, onomatopoeia
- Free verse vs structured forms (e.g., haiku, sonnet, villanelle)
- **Questions**: Analyzing sound devices in poetry, identifying poetic forms

## Module 5: Screenwriting and Playwriting (20%)
- Formatting a screenplay (slug lines, action, dialogue)
- Writing visually: if it can't be seen or heard, it doesn't go in the script
- Scene structure: entering late and leaving early
- **Questions**: Formatting dialogue correctly, translating internal thought into visual action

## Stretch Zone
- Magical realism and surrealism techniques
- Writing non-linear narratives (flashbacks, frame narratives)
