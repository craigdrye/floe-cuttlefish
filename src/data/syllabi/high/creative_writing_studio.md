# Creative Writing Studio
**ID**: `creativeWritingStudio` · **Discipline**: English · **Year**: 10 (ages 14-15)

## Course Aim
Give students a serious but welcoming writing studio where they practice fiction, poetry, dramatic writing, revision, workshop habits, and publication-ready presentation.

## Course Design Notes
This course shifts from analytical essay writing to creative expression while still teaching craft vocabulary and revision discipline. It values experimentation, feedback, and finished pieces, not just inspiration.

## Chapter 1: Writer's Notebook and Creative Practice
- **Key concepts**: observation, freewriting, constraints, sensory detail, image bank, writing routine, creative risk.
- **Practice questions**: What detail makes the moment specific? What happens if the prompt adds a constraint? What should be saved for later?
- **Student output**: a writer's notebook with prompts, observations, fragments, and reflection.

## Chapter 2: Scene, Setting, and Description
- **Key concepts**: scene, summary, sensory detail, atmosphere, setting pressure, concrete nouns, active verbs.
- **Practice questions**: What can the reader see, hear, or infer? How does setting change the character's options?
- **Student output**: a revised scene that turns summary into dramatized action.

## Chapter 3: Character and Point of View
- **Key concepts**: desire, fear, contradiction, voice, first person, third limited, omniscience, unreliable narration.
- **Practice questions**: What does the character want? What do they misunderstand? How does point of view control what readers know?
- **Student output**: a character sketch and the same scene in two points of view.

## Chapter 4: Plot, Structure, and Stakes
- **Key concepts**: inciting incident, conflict, escalation, reversal, midpoint, climax, resolution, internal and external stakes.
- **Practice questions**: What changes? Why now? What happens if the character fails?
- **Student output**: a story map and a short fiction draft.

## Chapter 5: Dialogue, Subtext, and Voice
- **Key concepts**: dialogue tags, beats, subtext, silence, rhythm, idiolect, conflict, compression.
- **Practice questions**: What is the character avoiding saying? Where can exposition be cut? Does each speaker sound distinct?
- **Student output**: a dialogue scene revised for subtext and pacing.

## Chapter 6: Poetry and Lyrical Prose
- **Key concepts**: image, metaphor, line break, sound, rhythm, repetition, form, free verse, prose poem.
- **Practice questions**: What image carries the poem? Where does the line break create surprise? Which sound pattern supports the mood?
- **Student output**: a small poetry portfolio with at least one revision note per poem.

## Chapter 7: Script, Hybrid Forms, and Experiment
- **Key concepts**: screenplay format, stage directions, monologue, visual action, flash fiction, braided essay, non-linear structure.
- **Practice questions**: What can be seen or heard? What does the form make possible? Which experiment clarifies rather than confuses?
- **Student output**: a short script or hybrid piece.

## Chapter 8: Workshop, Revision, and Publication
- **Key concepts**: constructive critique, revision plan, line edit, copyedit, title, submission, reading aloud, artist statement.
- **Practice questions**: What feedback appears more than once? Which revision changes the work most? What is the piece trying to become?
- **Student output**: a polished final portfolio with artist statement.

## Capstone
Create a creative writing portfolio with one short story or scene, three poems, one script or hybrid piece, revision notes, and a reflective artist statement.

## Assessment Ideas
- Drafts graded on craft focus and risk-taking.
- Workshop participation graded on specificity and care.
- Capstone graded on revision, range, voice, and presentation.
