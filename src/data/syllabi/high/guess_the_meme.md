# Know That Meme

## Course Shape
This is a playful meme-literacy course using copyright-safe stylized meme cards. Learners identify what a meme format is doing: preference, denial, escalation, bait, mockery, recognition, private anxiety, group dynamics, or audience judgment.

## Chapters
- Classic templates: preference, temptation, misidentification, and recognition
- Escalation memes: increasingly absurd versions of an idea
- Dilemma memes: bad tradeoffs, pressure, and refusal
- Coping memes: denial, forced calm, and private inner monologue
- Argument memes: bait, debate posture, and punished suggestions
- Tone and context: sarcasm, timing, audience, and when not to post

## Key Skills
- Read the relationship between image, caption, audience, and timing
- Distinguish format recognition from actual communication judgment
- Identify sarcasm, bait, escalation, denial, and social comparison
- Explain why a meme lands in one context and fails in another
