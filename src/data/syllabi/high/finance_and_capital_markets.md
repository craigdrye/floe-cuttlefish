# Finance and Capital Markets
**ID**: `financeAndCapitalMarkets` · **Discipline**: Economics/Business · **Year**: 11 (ages 15–16)

## Course Philosophy
This course provides a practical and theoretical understanding of how money flows through the global economy. Students will learn the mechanics of the stock market, bonds, interest rates, and personal finance strategies.

## Module 1: Time Value of Money & Interest Rates (20%)
- The core concept: A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow (inflation and opportunity cost)
- Simple vs Compound Interest ($A = P(1 + r/n)^{nt}$)
- Present Value (PV) and Future Value (FV) calculations
- Nominal vs Real Interest Rates (the Fisher effect)
- The Rule of 72 (estimating doubling time)
- **Questions**: Calculating compound interest, determining the present value of a future cash flow, applying the Rule of 72

## Module 2: The Bond Market (Fixed Income) (20%)
- What is a bond? (Principal, coupon rate, maturity date)
- Types of bonds: US Treasuries, Municipal Bonds, Corporate Bonds, Junk Bonds
- The inverse relationship between bond prices and interest rates
- Yield to Maturity (YTM)
- Credit ratings (S&P, Moody's) and default risk
- **Questions**: Explaining why bond prices fall when interest rates rise, calculating current yield

## Module 3: The Stock Market (Equities) (20%)
- What is a stock? (Ownership, voting rights, dividends)
- Initial Public Offerings (IPOs) and the secondary market (NYSE, NASDAQ)
- Market Capitalization (Small Cap vs Large Cap)
- Key metrics: Price-to-Earnings (P/E) ratio, Dividend Yield, Earnings Per Share (EPS)
- Market indices: S&P 500, Dow Jones, NASDAQ Composite
- Bull vs Bear markets
- **Questions**: Calculating a P/E ratio, explaining the difference between an IPO and secondary trading

## Module 4: Investment Strategies & Vehicles (20%)
- Risk vs Return tradeoff
- Diversification and modern portfolio theory (conceptually)
- Active vs Passive investing
- Mutual Funds vs Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs)
- Index funds and the argument for passive management
- Retirement accounts (401(k), Traditional IRA, Roth IRA) and the tax advantages of each
- **Questions**: Explaining the benefits of an ETF over individual stocks, contrasting Traditional vs Roth IRAs

## Module 5: Behavioral Finance & Market Crises (20%)
- Common psychological biases in investing: Loss aversion, confirmation bias, herd mentality, anchoring
- The Greater Fool Theory and economic bubbles
- Historical case studies: The Dot-Com Bubble (2000), The Global Financial Crisis (2008) - subprime mortgages and collateralized debt obligations (CDOs)
- **Questions**: Identifying behavioral biases in investment scenarios, explaining the root causes of the 2008 housing crash

## Stretch Zone
- Introduction to derivatives: Options (Calls and Puts) and Futures contracts
- Short selling and margin trading mechanics
