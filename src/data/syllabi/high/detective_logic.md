# Detective Logic
**ID**: `detectiveLogic` · **Discipline**: Humanities/Extracurricular · **Year**: 10 (ages 14–15)

## Course Philosophy
A fun, engaging course that uses the framework of detective fiction to teach inductive and abductive reasoning, lateral thinking, and forensic analysis concepts.

## Module 1: Types of Reasoning (20%)
- Deductive reasoning (top-down certainty, e.g., Sherlock Holmes' classic deductions)
- Inductive reasoning (bottom-up probability)
- Abductive reasoning (inference to the best explanation)
- **Questions**: Classifying reasoning types based on scenarios, applying abductive reasoning to a "crime scene"

## Module 2: The Elements of the Mystery (20%)
- Motive, Means, and Opportunity (MMO)
- The "Locked Room" mystery and impossible crimes
- Identifying Red Herrings (false clues)
- The concept of the "Least Likely Suspect"
- **Questions**: Evaluating suspects based on MMO, identifying red herrings in a narrative

## Module 3: Lateral Thinking Puzzles (20%)
- Thinking outside the box
- Identifying hidden assumptions in problem-solving
- Classic lateral thinking riddles (e.g., "The man in the elevator")
- **Questions**: Solving lateral puzzles by identifying the false assumption

## Module 4: Basic Forensic Concepts (20%)
- Locard's Exchange Principle: "Every contact leaves a trace"
- Types of evidence: Direct vs Circumstantial
- Reliability of eyewitness testimony (the fallibility of memory)
- **Questions**: Applying Locard's principle, distinguishing direct from circumstantial evidence

## Module 5: Cryptography & Ciphers (20%)
- Basic substitution ciphers (Caesar cipher, Atbash)
- Frequency analysis (cracking substitution ciphers based on letter frequency)
- Transposition ciphers (Rail Fence, Columnar)
- Steganography (hiding messages in plain sight)
- **Questions**: Encoding/decoding basic ciphers, applying frequency analysis to a ciphertext

## Stretch Zone
- The Vigenère cipher (polyalphabetic substitution)
- Analyzing complex alibi timelines (logic grid puzzles)
