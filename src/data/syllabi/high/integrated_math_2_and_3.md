# Integrated Math 2 & 3
**Covers**: `col-integrated-math-2`, `col-integrated-math-3`
**Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 11 (ages 15–16)

## Overview
Combined syllabus for Integrated Math 2 and 3. These courses continue the blended approach, introducing complex numbers, advanced geometry (circles and 3D), trigonometry, rational/radical functions, and normal distributions.

## Domain A: Advanced Algebra & Number Systems (25%)
- Understand complex numbers ($i^2 = -1$) and perform operations with them
- Solve quadratic equations with real coefficients that have complex solutions
- Extend the properties of integer exponents to rational exponents
- Rewrite expressions involving radicals and rational exponents
- Understand and solve rational and radical equations, identifying extraneous solutions
- Understand the Remainder Theorem and factor polynomials
- Expand binomials using the Binomial Theorem
- **Questions**: Complex arithmetic, fractional exponents, solving $\sqrt{x-2} = x$, polynomial division

## Domain B: Advanced Functions & Modeling (25%)
- Inverse functions: verify by composition, find inverses algebraically and graphically
- Exponential and Logarithmic functions: understand the inverse relationship, use properties of logarithms to solve equations ($ab^{ct} = d$), model continuous compounding ($A = Pe^{rt}$)
- Graph and interpret piecewise, absolute value, step, and rational functions
- Identify asymptotes of rational functions
- Arithmetic and geometric sequences and series: recursive/explicit formulas, sum of finite geometric series
- **Questions**: Finding $f^{-1}(x)$, solving $\log_2(x) = 3$, graphing piecewise functions, geometric series sum

## Domain C: Advanced Geometry & Trigonometry (30%)
- Circles: prove theorems about chords, secants, tangents, and inscribed/circumscribed angles
- Coordinate Geometry: derive the equation of a circle, complete the square to find center/radius, write equations for parabolas given a focus and directrix
- Volume: use formulas for cylinders, pyramids, cones, and spheres to solve complex 3D problems
- Trigonometry: extend the domain of trigonometric functions using the unit circle, define radian measure
- Graph trigonometric functions (sine, cosine) and identify amplitude, period, midline, and phase shift
- Prove and apply the Pythagorean identity $\sin^2(\theta) + \cos^2(\theta) = 1$
- **Questions**: Finding circle centers via completing the square, converting degrees to radians, finding amplitude/period of $y = 3\sin(2x) + 1$

## Domain D: Advanced Statistics & Probability (20%)
- Normal Distributions: use mean and standard deviation to estimate population percentages (Empirical rule and z-scores)
- Study Design: distinguish between sample surveys, experiments, and observational studies; evaluate the role of randomization
- Inference: use data from a sample survey to estimate a population mean or proportion; develop a margin of error
- Conditional Probability: understand independence, calculate $P(A|B)$, apply the general multiplication rule and the addition rule
- **Questions**: Calculating z-scores, identifying bias in study design, solving conditional probability using two-way tables or tree diagrams

## Stretch Zone
- Law of Sines and Law of Cosines
- Introduction to matrices and vectors
