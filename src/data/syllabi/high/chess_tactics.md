# Chess Tactics
**ID**: `chessTactics` · **Discipline**: Extracurricular · **Year**: 10 (ages 14–15)

## Course Philosophy
This course moves beyond the basic rules of chess into intermediate tactical pattern recognition. It focuses on the fundamental tactical motifs that decide games at the club level, developing calculation skills and geometric vision on the board.

## Module 1: The Basics of Calculation (20%)
- Forcing moves: Checks, Captures, Threats (CCT)
- Evaluating the position: Material, King safety, Piece activity
- The concept of "loose pieces drop off" (LPDO)
- **Questions**: Identifying checks/captures in a given position, evaluating piece safety

## Module 2: Fundamental Tactics (Part 1) (20%)
- The Fork (double attack), usually with knights or pawns
- The Pin (absolute vs relative)
- The Skewer (x-ray attack)
- **Questions**: Finding the fork/pin/skewer in a puzzle position, distinguishing absolute vs relative pins

## Module 3: Fundamental Tactics (Part 2) (20%)
- Discovered attacks and discovered checks
- Double checks (and why they force the king to move)
- Deflection (forcing a defending piece away)
- Decoy (forcing a piece to a bad square)
- **Questions**: Identifying discovered attacks, calculating forced mate after a double check

## Module 4: Checkmating Patterns (20%)
- Back rank mate
- Smothered mate (the power of the knight)
- Anastasia's mate, Boden's mate, and the Arabian mate
- Hook mate and dovetail mate
- **Questions**: Identifying the mating pattern, calculating forced mate in 2 or 3 moves

## Module 5: Endgame Tactics (20%)
- The "Rule of the Square" for pawn promotion
- Opposition (direct and diagonal) in king and pawn endings
- Triangulation (losing a tempo to gain the opposition)
- Zugzwang (when any move is a bad move)
- **Questions**: Calculating pawn races using the rule of the square, identifying zugzwang positions

## Stretch Zone
- Clearance sacrifices
- Interference tactics
- Basic positional concepts: outpost squares, backward pawns
