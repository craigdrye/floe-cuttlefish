# Class 8 Math
**ID**: `col-class-8-math` | **Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 8 (ages 12-13)
**Frameworks**: UK KS3 Year 8, US Common Core Grade 8, ACARA v9 Year 8, India NCERT Class 8

## Course Aim
Build a steady Year 8 mathematics course that prepares students for algebra, functions, geometry, data, and later high-school mathematics. Students strengthen number fluency while learning to solve linear equations, reason with functions and graphs, explore systems, use exponents, analyze transformations, apply the Pythagorean theorem, calculate volume, interpret scatterplots, and communicate methods clearly.

## Course Design Notes
Use Common Core Grade 8 mathematics, UK KS3 mathematics, NCETM progression materials, and local Year 8 expectations as the spine. Route questions here when they involve multi-step linear equations, functions, graphing, systems as simultaneous constraints, exponent rules, scientific notation, transformations, congruence and similarity, Pythagorean theorem, cylinders, cones, spheres, scatterplots, association, or algebra-readiness review. Keep practice representation-rich: students should move among words, tables, graphs, equations, diagrams, and written explanations.

## Chapter 1: Algebraic Fluency and Linear Equations
- **Key concepts**: Expressions, like terms, distributive property, substitution, equations with variables on both sides, equations with brackets, equations with rational coefficients, identities, no-solution cases, and solution checking.
- **Practice questions**: Simplify and expand expressions; solve multi-step linear equations; identify whether an equation has one solution, no solution, or infinitely many solutions; translate contexts into equations.
- **Student output**: An annotated equation-solving portfolio with three equation types, checks, and a short note explaining each algebraic move.

## Chapter 2: Functions, Tables, and Linear Graphs
- **Key concepts**: Function as a rule assigning one output to each input, input-output tables, coordinate graphs, slope as rate of change, intercepts, proportional versus non-proportional linear relationships, and equations of lines.
- **Practice questions**: Decide whether relations are functions; complete tables; graph linear relationships; compare slopes and intercepts; interpret y = mx + b in context.
- **Student output**: A function profile for one real or provided relationship with table, graph, equation, and interpretation of slope and intercept.

## Chapter 3: Systems and Simultaneous Constraints
- **Key concepts**: Systems of linear equations as two conditions at once, graphical solutions, approximate intersection points, substitution as an informal method, solution meaning, and break-even reasoning.
- **Practice questions**: Estimate solutions from graphs; solve simple systems by substitution or inspection; write systems from pricing, distance, or planning contexts; explain what the intersection represents.
- **Student output**: A systems task board showing one graph-based solution and one algebra-supported solution, each with a context sentence.

## Chapter 4: Exponents, Roots, and Scientific Notation
- **Key concepts**: Integer exponents, powers of 10, exponent laws for multiplication and division, zero and negative exponents where appropriate, square roots, cube roots, irrational numbers as non-repeating decimals, and scientific notation.
- **Practice questions**: Simplify exponent expressions; convert between standard and scientific notation; compare very large and very small quantities; estimate square roots; locate irrational numbers on a number line.
- **Student output**: A scale-and-notation page comparing quantities from science, finance, or technology using powers of 10 and written interpretation.

## Chapter 5: Transformations, Congruence, and Similarity
- **Key concepts**: Translations, rotations, reflections, dilations, coordinate rules, congruent figures, similar figures, scale factor, angle preservation, side-length change, and transformation sequences.
- **Practice questions**: Describe transformations on the coordinate plane; draw images after transformations; determine whether figures are congruent or similar; use scale factors to find missing lengths.
- **Student output**: A transformation map with original figure, image, coordinate rule or motion sequence, and explanation of what changed and what stayed the same.

## Chapter 6: Pythagorean Theorem and Coordinate Geometry
- **Key concepts**: Right triangles, squares on side lengths, Pythagorean theorem, converse of the theorem, distance on the coordinate plane, slope connections, and problem solving with diagonal lengths.
- **Practice questions**: Find missing side lengths; decide whether a triangle is right-angled; calculate distance between coordinate points; solve ladder, screen, map, or design problems.
- **Student output**: A Pythagorean reasoning sheet with a diagram, calculation, exact or rounded answer, units, and explanation of why the theorem applies.

## Chapter 7: Volume, Surface Area, and Measurement Models
- **Key concepts**: Area review, volume of prisms, cylinders, cones, and spheres as appropriate, surface area, nets, units, precision, estimation, and choosing formulas from shape structure.
- **Practice questions**: Calculate volume and surface area; compare containers; solve missing-dimension problems; convert units carefully; estimate before calculating.
- **Student output**: A measurement model comparing two containers or designs, including diagrams, formulas, units, and a recommendation.

## Chapter 8: Statistics, Scatterplots, and Algebra Readiness Studio
- **Key concepts**: Bivariate data, scatterplots, positive and negative association, no association, clusters, outliers, lines of best fit, informal prediction, correlation versus causation, cumulative algebra review, and readiness for Algebra 1 or integrated mathematics.
- **Practice questions**: Interpret scatterplots; draw and use an approximate trend line; critique unsupported claims; solve mixed review problems involving equations, functions, exponents, geometry, and data.
- **Student output**: A data-and-algebra readiness report using a small bivariate dataset and a mixed-skill reflection on strengths and next steps.

## Capstone
Students complete a Year 8 modeling portfolio around one practical question such as comparing phone plans, analyzing sports or school data, planning a design with diagonal distances, modeling container volume, or studying a simple linear relationship. The final product must include a linear equation or function, a graph, an introductory system or simultaneous constraint, exponent or scientific-notation reasoning, a transformation or Pythagorean component, a volume or measurement calculation, a scatterplot or data display, and a reflection on model limits.

## Assessment Ideas
- Short fluency checks on equations, slope, function notation, exponent rules, transformations, Pythagorean theorem, volume, and scatterplot interpretation.
- Error-analysis prompts for invalid algebraic moves, misleading graphs, incorrect transformation descriptions, formula misuse, or overclaiming from correlation.
- Mixed representation tasks requiring students to connect equations, tables, graphs, diagrams, and written context.
- Modeling tasks scored on setup, method choice, accuracy, units, interpretation, and reasonableness.
- Capstone graded on mathematical accuracy, representation quality, algebra readiness, communication, and reflection on assumptions.
