# Class 8 Math
**ID**: `col-class-8-math` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 8 (ages 12–13)
**Frameworks**: UK KS3 Year 8, US Common Core Grade 8, ACARA v9 Year 8, India NCERT Class 8

## Course Philosophy
Year 8 is deeply focused on algebraic fluency, linear graphing, and geometric transformations. Students transition from solving basic equations to understanding functions and the relationship between algebraic expressions and graphical representations.

## Module 1: Number & Algebra (25%)
- Apply the four operations to integers, decimals, and fractions, both positive and negative
- Understand and use the laws of indices (powers) for multiplication and division (e.g., a³ × a² = a⁵)
- Expand product of two binomials (e.g., (x+2)(x+3)) and factorise quadratic expressions of the form x² + bx + c
- Solve multi-step linear equations with unknowns on both sides, including those with brackets and fractions
- Formulate and solve simple simultaneous linear equations using elimination and substitution
- Change the subject of a simple formula
- **Questions**: Binomial expansion, factorising quadratics, multi-step equations, changing the subject, index laws

## Module 2: Proportional Reasoning & Rates (20%)
- Solve problems involving direct and inverse proportion, including graphical and algebraic representations
- Use compound units such as speed, unit pricing, and density to solve problems
- Understand and calculate compound interest and repeated percentage change (e.g., depreciation)
- Solve complex ratio problems, including dividing a quantity into a three-part ratio
- **Questions**: Direct/inverse proportion (y = kx, y = k/x), speed/density/pressure, compound interest formulas

## Module 3: Linear Graphs & Functions (20%)
- Plot graphs of linear functions and recognize that equations of the form y = mx + c represent straight lines
- Identify the gradient (slope) and y-intercept of a straight line from its equation or graph
- Find the equation of a straight line given two points, or one point and the gradient
- Model real-world situations using linear graphs (e.g., distance-time graphs, conversion graphs)
- Introduction to the concept of a function: inputs, outputs, and mapping diagrams
- **Questions**: Finding gradient/y-intercept, distance-time graph interpretation, plotting y = mx + c

## Module 4: Geometry & Measures (20%)
- Apply Pythagoras' Theorem to find missing sides in right-angled triangles
- Understand and perform the four geometric transformations: translation, rotation, reflection, and enlargement (including fractional scale factors)
- Calculate the area and circumference of a circle, and the area of a sector and length of an arc
- Calculate the surface area and volume of cylinders and spheres
- Understand and apply the properties of similar shapes to find missing lengths
- **Questions**: Pythagoras in 2D and 3D contexts, describing transformations, arc length/sector area, cylinder volume

## Module 5: Statistics & Probability (15%)
- Construct and interpret scatter graphs of bivariate data; recognize correlation (positive, negative, none)
- Draw an estimated line of best fit and use it to make predictions
- Calculate theoretical probabilities for mutually exclusive and independent events
- Use Venn diagrams and two-way tables to calculate conditional probabilities
- **Questions**: Scatter graph correlation, predicting from lines of best fit, probability from Venn diagrams/two-way tables

## Stretch Zone
- Introduction to basic trigonometry (SOH CAH TOA) in right-angled triangles
- Solving simple quadratic equations by factorisation
- Surds (radicals) simplification
