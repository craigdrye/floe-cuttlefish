# Class 10 Math
**ID**: `col-class-10-math` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 11 (ages 15–16)
**Frameworks**: India NCERT Class 10, UK KS4 Year 10

## Course Philosophy
Class 10 is a pivotal board examination year in many systems (e.g., India's CBSE Class 10 board exams). The curriculum finalizes foundational high school mathematics, integrating complex algebra (quadratics, progressions), trigonometry, and coordinate geometry, preparing students for the rigorous +2 (Years 11/12) science and commerce tracks.

## Module 1: Number Systems & Algebra (25%)
- Real Numbers: Euclid's division lemma, Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, proving irrationality (e.g., $\sqrt{2}$, $\sqrt{3}$)
- Polynomials: Relationship between zeroes and coefficients of quadratic polynomials
- Pair of Linear Equations in Two Variables: Graphical and algebraic methods (substitution, elimination, cross-multiplication)
- Quadratic Equations: Standard form ($ax^2 + bx + c = 0$), solving by factorization and quadratic formula, nature of roots (discriminant)
- Arithmetic Progressions (AP): Deriving the $n$th term and sum of the first $n$ terms
- **Questions**: Proving irrationality, solving simultaneous equations by cross-multiplication, finding roots of quadratics, AP sequence word problems

## Module 2: Trigonometry (25%)
- Introduction to Trigonometry: Trigonometric ratios of an acute angle of a right-angled triangle (sin, cos, tan, cosec, sec, cot)
- Values of trigonometric ratios for 30°, 45°, and 60°
- Trigonometric Identities: Proving and applying $\sin^2 A + \cos^2 A = 1$
- Heights and Distances: Angles of elevation and depression, solving simple 2D problems using trigonometry
- **Questions**: Evaluating trigonometric expressions at standard angles, proving identities, height/distance word problems

## Module 3: Coordinate Geometry & Triangles (20%)
- Coordinate Geometry: Concepts of coordinate geometry, distance formula, section formula (internal division), area of a triangle
- Triangles: Definitions and theorems regarding similar triangles (e.g., if a line is drawn parallel to one side of a triangle to intersect the other two sides, the other two sides are divided in the same ratio)
- Pythagoras Theorem and its converse (formal proofs and applications)
- **Questions**: Using the distance/section formulas, proving triangles are similar, applying theorems to find missing lengths

## Module 4: Circles & Constructions (15%)
- Circles: Tangent to a circle at point of contact
- Theorems: The tangent at any point of a circle is perpendicular to the radius through the point of contact; the lengths of tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal
- Constructions: Division of a line segment in a given ratio, construction of tangents to a circle from an external point
- **Questions**: Applying tangent theorems to find angles/lengths, formal geometric construction steps

## Module 5: Mensuration, Statistics, & Probability (15%)
- Areas Related to Circles: Area of sectors and segments of a circle
- Surface Areas and Volumes: Combinations of solids (cubes, cuboids, spheres, hemispheres, right circular cylinders/cones), frustum of a cone
- Statistics: Mean, median, and mode of grouped data (bimodal situations and step-deviation method avoided in introductory phases but included in stretch)
- Probability: Classical definition of probability, simple problems on single events
- **Questions**: Volume/Surface area of composite solids, calculating grouped mean/median, standard deck of cards probability

## Stretch Zone
- Deriving the quadratic formula by completing the square
- Optional algebraic method: Cross-multiplication method for linear equations in depth
- Complex trigonometric proofs involving sec/tan and cosec/cot identities
