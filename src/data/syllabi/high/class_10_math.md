# Class 10 Math
**ID**: `col-class-10-math` | **Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 11 (ages 15-16)
**Frameworks**: India NCERT Class 10, UK KS4 Year 10

## Course Aim
Prepare students for upper high-school mathematics and formal examinations by consolidating algebra, geometry, trigonometry, statistics, probability, and mathematical modeling. Students learn to select efficient methods, write complete reasoning, interpret graphs and diagrams, and manage mixed exam-style problems with confidence.

## Course Design Notes
Class 10 maths is both a concept-building year and an assessment year in many systems. The course should move from fluency to strategy: students need regular retrieval practice, proof and explanation routines, modeling tasks, and timed mixed sets. Route questions here when students are working toward NCERT Class 10, UK KS4/GCSE foundations and higher preparation, or Common Core high school algebra, functions, geometry, statistics, and probability.

## Chapter 1: Real Numbers, Algebra Review, and Exam Habits
- **Key concepts**: Real numbers, prime factorisation, Euclid's division lemma, irrationality proofs, algebraic identities, rearranging formulas, notation discipline, and checking work under exam conditions.
- **Practice questions**: Find HCF and LCM using prime factors; prove simple irrationality results; simplify algebraic expressions; identify avoidable exam errors.
- **Student output**: A personal exam-readiness notebook with reference examples, common mistakes, and corrected solutions.

## Chapter 2: Quadratic Equations and Polynomials
- **Key concepts**: Quadratic form, factorisation, completing the square, quadratic formula, discriminant, roots and coefficients, graphs of parabolas, and links between algebraic and graphical solutions.
- **Practice questions**: Solve quadratics by different methods; interpret the discriminant; connect zeros to graphs; form equations from area, motion, or number contexts.
- **Student output**: A quadratic methods comparison showing when factorising, graphing, completing the square, or the formula is most efficient.

## Chapter 3: Systems of Equations and Algebraic Modeling
- **Key concepts**: Pairs of linear equations, substitution, elimination, graphical solutions, consistency, dependent and inconsistent systems, and modeling constraints.
- **Practice questions**: Solve systems algebraically and graphically; write equations from word problems; interpret the meaning of intersection points.
- **Student output**: A two-variable modeling task for pricing, mixtures, travel, or resource planning, with equations, graph, solution, and interpretation.

## Chapter 4: Coordinate Geometry and Functions
- **Key concepts**: Distance formula, midpoint, section formula, area of a triangle, gradient, intercepts, linear and quadratic function interpretation, transformations at an introductory level, and coordinate proof.
- **Practice questions**: Calculate lengths and areas; classify shapes using coordinates; use coordinates to justify geometric claims; compare linear and quadratic models.
- **Student output**: A coordinate proof or design problem that combines calculation, graphing, and written justification.

## Chapter 5: Trigonometry and Right-Triangle Modeling
- **Key concepts**: Sine, cosine, tangent, reciprocal ratios where appropriate, exact values for standard angles, core identities, angle of elevation, angle of depression, and units in applied problems.
- **Practice questions**: Evaluate ratios; solve right triangles; prove simple identities; model heights and distances from diagrams or field measurements.
- **Student output**: A trigonometry field report using measured or provided data, a diagram, calculations, and a discussion of measurement error.

## Chapter 6: Triangles, Circles, and Geometric Proof
- **Key concepts**: Similarity, proportional reasoning, Pythagoras theorem and converse, tangent-radius theorem, equal tangents from an external point, circle angle relationships, constructions, and proof structure.
- **Practice questions**: Prove triangles similar; find missing lengths; apply tangent theorems; write construction steps; justify each geometric claim.
- **Student output**: A proof sequence portfolio moving from annotated diagrams to complete paragraph or two-column proofs.

## Chapter 7: Mensuration, Statistics, and Probability
- **Key concepts**: Areas related to circles, sectors and segments, surface area and volume of combined solids, frustums where required, grouped data, mean, median, mode, spread, probability of simple events, and simulation.
- **Practice questions**: Solve composite mensuration problems; calculate grouped statistics; compare data sets; find probabilities for cards, dice, spinners, or survey outcomes.
- **Student output**: A quantitative report that uses measurement or data to make and defend a claim, including limitations.

## Chapter 8: Modeling Studio and Exam Readiness
- **Key concepts**: Choosing representations, assumptions, units, precision, multi-step problem solving, mixed-topic revision, command words, time management, calculator or non-calculator strategy, and checking final answers.
- **Practice questions**: Select a method for an unfamiliar problem; explain why a model fits; complete mixed timed sets; revise from mark schemes or rubrics.
- **Student output**: A final revision dossier with mixed-topic corrections, strategy notes, and one polished modeling solution.

## Capstone
Complete a mathematical modeling and exam-defense project. Students investigate a realistic scenario using at least two of quadratics, systems, trigonometry, coordinate geometry, circles, statistics, or probability, then submit calculations, diagrams or graphs, assumptions, final recommendation, and a short oral defense using exam-style reasoning.

## Assessment Ideas
- Retrieval quizzes mixing algebra, geometry, trigonometry, statistics, and probability.
- Timed exam sets graded for accuracy, method marks, notation, and final-answer communication.
- Proof tasks graded on diagram use, theorem selection, logical sequence, and clarity.
- Modeling tasks graded on assumptions, representation, calculation, interpretation, and limitations.
- Capstone graded on mathematical range, correctness, reasoning, presentation, and response to questions.
