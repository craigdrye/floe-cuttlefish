# Precalculus Collections
**Covers**: `col-get-ready-for-precalculus`, `col-precalculus`, `col-get-ready-for-ap-calculus`
**Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 12 (ages 16–18)

## Overview
This syllabus covers advanced algebra, trigonometry, and analytical geometry necessary to succeed in Calculus. The focus is on function analysis, vectors, matrices, and an introduction to limits.

## Module 1: Complex Numbers and Transformations (15%)
- Perform arithmetic operations with complex numbers
- Represent complex numbers and their operations on the complex plane
- Represent complex numbers in polar form ($z = r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)$)
- Use DeMoivre's Theorem to find powers and roots of complex numbers
- Analyze the effect of transformations ($f(x) \to a f(b(x-h)) + k$) on arbitrary functions
- **Questions**: Graphing complex numbers, converting to polar form, applying DeMoivre's theorem

## Module 2: Advanced Functions (Polynomial, Rational, Exponential, Logarithmic) (25%)
- Graph polynomial functions, identifying zeros, multiplicity, and end behavior
- Graph rational functions, finding vertical, horizontal, and oblique (slant) asymptotes
- Solve complex polynomial and rational inequalities (using sign charts)
- Deep dive into exponential growth/decay models (e.g., Newton's Law of Cooling, logistic growth)
- Use properties of logarithms to solve complex equations
- **Questions**: Finding oblique asymptotes, solving $\frac{x-2}{x+3} \ge 0$, modeling logistic growth

## Module 3: Advanced Trigonometry (25%)
- Mastery of the unit circle and graphing all six trigonometric functions (including transformations)
- Prove complex trigonometric identities
- Solve trigonometric equations over specified intervals and finding general solutions
- Apply the Law of Sines (ambiguous case) and Law of Cosines to solve oblique triangles
- **Questions**: Verifying identities, solving $2\cos^2 x - \cos x - 1 = 0$, analyzing SSA triangle cases

## Module 4: Vectors, Matrices, and Systems (20%)
- Perform operations on vectors in the plane (addition, scalar multiplication, dot product, angle between vectors)
- Perform operations on matrices (addition, scalar multiplication, matrix multiplication)
- Find the determinant and inverse of $2\times2$ and $3\times3$ matrices
- Use matrices to solve systems of linear equations (Cramer's Rule, Inverse Matrices, Gaussian Elimination)
- **Questions**: Calculating vector projections, multiplying $3\times3$ matrices, solving a 3-variable system using matrices

## Module 5: Conic Sections, Sequences, and Limits (15%)
- Derive the equations of ellipses, hyperbolas, and parabolas given foci and directrices
- Graph conic sections and identify key features (vertices, foci, asymptotes)
- Sum infinite geometric series and use sigma notation
- Introduction to limits: estimating limits graphically and numerically
- **Questions**: Completing the square to graph a hyperbola, evaluating $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (\frac{1}{2})^n$, finding $\lim_{x\to 2} \frac{x^2-4}{x-2}$

## Stretch Zone
- Polar coordinates and graphing polar equations (limaçons, rose curves)
- Parametric equations and converting to rectangular form
