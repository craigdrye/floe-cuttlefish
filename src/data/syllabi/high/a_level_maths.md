# A-Level Maths
**Covers**: `col-a-level-maths`
**Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 12 (ages 16–18)
**Frameworks**: UK A-Level Mathematics (Edexcel/AQA/OCR)

## Course Philosophy
A-Level Mathematics is highly regarded globally for its rigorous, proof-oriented approach. It combines Pure Mathematics (algebra, calculus, trigonometry) with Applied Mathematics (Mechanics and Statistics).

## Module 1: Pure Mathematics 1 (Algebra, Graphs, & Trigonometry) (25%)
- Proof: Proof by deduction, exhaustion, and contradiction
- Algebra and Functions: Surds, indices, quadratics, discriminant, simultaneous equations, inequalities, remainder/factor theorem, graphs of functions, transformations
- Coordinate Geometry in the (x, y) plane: Straight lines, circles (completing the square to find center/radius)
- Sequences and Series: Arithmetic and Geometric progressions, binomial expansion $(a+bx)^n$ for positive integer $n$
- Trigonometry: Sine/cosine rules, area of triangle, graphs of trig functions, identities ($\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$), solving trig equations in a given interval
- Exponentials and Logarithms: $y = e^x$, logarithms, laws of logs, solving $a^x = b$, modelling growth/decay
- **Questions**: Proof by contradiction (e.g., $\sqrt{2}$ is irrational), expanding binomials, solving exponential equations using natural logs

## Module 2: Pure Mathematics 2 (Calculus & Vectors) (25%)
- Differentiation: First principles, differentiating $x^n$, $e^{kx}$, $\ln(x)$, $\sin(kx)$, $\cos(kx)$; product, quotient, and chain rules; implicit and parametric differentiation; finding tangents, normals, stationary points, and optimization
- Integration: Fundamental theorem of calculus, evaluating definite/indefinite integrals, integrating polynomials, exponentials, $1/x$, trig functions; integration by substitution, by parts, and using partial fractions; area under/between curves; solving first-order differential equations (separation of variables)
- Numerical Methods: Location of roots, Newton-Raphson method, numerical integration (Trapezium rule)
- Vectors: 2D and 3D vectors, magnitude, direction, position vectors, vector addition/scalar multiplication, solving geometric problems
- **Questions**: Implicit differentiation of circles, integrating using partial fractions, solving rate of change differential equations

## Module 3: Statistics (25%)
- Statistical Sampling: Random, systematic, stratified, quota, and opportunity sampling
- Data Presentation and Interpretation: Histograms, scatter diagrams, regression lines, measures of central tendency and spread (variance, standard deviation)
- Probability: Mutually exclusive, independent events, conditional probability, Venn diagrams, tree diagrams
- Statistical Distributions: Discrete uniform distribution, the Binomial distribution, the Normal distribution (using standard normal tables/calculators, approximating binomial with normal)
- Statistical Hypothesis Testing: Null and alternative hypotheses, significance levels, 1-tail vs 2-tail tests, testing parameters for binomial and normal distributions
- **Questions**: Calculating standard deviation, finding probabilities using the normal distribution, conducting a hypothesis test for a proportion

## Module 4: Mechanics (25%)
- Quantities and Units in Mechanics: Vectors vs scalars
- Kinematics: Displacement-time and velocity-time graphs, constant acceleration formulae (suvat equations), calculus in kinematics (using $v = ds/dt$, $a = dv/dt$)
- Dynamics: Newton's First, Second ($F=ma$), and Third Laws; weight, normal reaction, tension, thrust, friction ($F \le \mu R$); connected particles (pulleys, tow bars)
- Moments: Turning effect of a force, equilibrium of rigid bodies, center of mass
- Projectiles: Motion in 2D under gravity
- **Questions**: Solving complex pulley problems, applying suvat equations to 2D projectiles, calculating moments for a ladder resting against a wall

## Stretch Zone
- Further Mathematics concepts: Complex numbers, matrices, Maclaurin series
- Solving second-order differential equations
