# A-Level Maths
**Covers**: `col-a-level-maths`
**Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 12 (ages 16-18)
**Frameworks**: UK A-Level Mathematics (Edexcel/AQA/OCR)

## Course Aim
Prepare sixth-form students for A-Level Mathematics by building fluent pure mathematics, applied statistics and mechanics, modelling habits, proof, calculator judgement, and exam-ready written reasoning.

## Course Design Notes
Use this syllabus for UK A-Level Mathematics support across Edexcel, AQA, and OCR routes. Keep pure, statistics, and mechanics connected through modelling and interpretation; require clear notation, assumptions, diagrams, technology checks, and post-question review rather than treating exam strategy as separate from mathematics.

## Chapter 1: A-Level Maths Foundations, Proof, and Technology Habits
- **Key concepts**: specification mapping, mathematical argument, proof by deduction, proof by exhaustion, proof by contradiction, notation, calculator modes, graphing technology, statistical functions, exact versus decimal answers, and checking reasonableness.
- **Practice questions**: Which proof method fits the statement? When should an answer be exact? How can a calculator or graph confirm a result without replacing the method?
- **Student output**: a maths toolkit page covering notation, proof templates, calculator settings, and common exam-command expectations.

## Chapter 2: Pure Mathematics - Algebra, Functions, and Coordinate Geometry
- **Key concepts**: indices, surds, quadratics, inequalities, simultaneous equations, polynomials, factor theorem, remainder theorem, algebraic fractions, functions, transformations, modulus, straight lines, circles, and parametric form where required.
- **Practice questions**: Transform a graph and state the new domain; solve an inequality and represent the solution; complete the square to interpret a circle.
- **Student output**: an algebra and graphs problem set with exact working, domain notes, and calculator verification.

## Chapter 3: Pure Mathematics - Sequences, Trigonometry, Exponentials, and Logarithms
- **Key concepts**: arithmetic and geometric sequences, sigma notation, binomial expansion, radians, trigonometric graphs, identities, trigonometric equations, sine and cosine rules, exponentials, logarithms, growth, decay, and model limitations.
- **Practice questions**: Solve a trigonometric equation on a restricted interval; use logs to linearise a model; interpret a growth or decay parameter in context.
- **Student output**: a functions and modelling booklet linking symbolic methods, graphs, and contextual interpretation.

## Chapter 4: Pure Mathematics - Differentiation and Applications
- **Key concepts**: derivative meaning, first principles, standard derivatives, product rule, quotient rule, chain rule, implicit differentiation, parametric differentiation, tangents, normals, increasing and decreasing intervals, stationary points, optimisation, and rates of change.
- **Practice questions**: Choose the correct differentiation rule; classify a stationary point; build an optimisation model from a word problem.
- **Student output**: a differentiation applications portfolio with diagrams, assumptions, algebra, and written conclusions.

## Chapter 5: Pure Mathematics - Integration, Numerical Methods, and Differential Equations
- **Key concepts**: antiderivatives, definite integrals, area, trapezium rule, substitution, integration by parts, partial fractions, differential equations, separation of variables, initial conditions, iteration, Newton-Raphson, and limitations of numerical methods.
- **Practice questions**: Set up an area between curves; solve a separable differential equation; decide whether an iterative method is converging.
- **Student output**: an integration and numerical methods file with exact solutions, approximations, and error checks.

## Chapter 6: Statistics - Data, Probability, Distributions, and Hypothesis Testing
- **Key concepts**: sampling, large data sets, data cleaning, coding, measures of location and spread, histograms, scatter diagrams, correlation, regression, probability rules, binomial distribution, normal distribution, statistical hypothesis testing, significance, and contextual conclusions.
- **Practice questions**: Critique a sampling method; use calculator distribution functions accurately; write a hypothesis-test conclusion in context.
- **Student output**: a statistics investigation using real or exam-board data with technology notes, calculations, and interpretation.

## Chapter 7: Mechanics - Kinematics, Forces, Moments, and Modelling
- **Key concepts**: quantities and units, vectors, displacement, velocity, acceleration, suvat, calculus in kinematics, projectiles, Newton's laws, weight, normal reaction, tension, friction, connected particles, moments, equilibrium, and modelling assumptions.
- **Practice questions**: Draw a force diagram before calculating; decide whether suvat assumptions apply; interpret a negative value in a mechanics context.
- **Student output**: a mechanics modelling pack with diagrams, assumptions, equations, units, and sense checks.

## Chapter 8: A-Level Maths Exam Studio
- **Key concepts**: paper structure, command words, multi-step problem solving, proof presentation, calculator-permitted strategy, exact answers, numerical accuracy, modelling assumptions, statistics wording, mechanics diagrams, time allocation, and error review.
- **Practice questions**: What marks are available for method? Which result needs justification? How should the final sentence answer the question asked?
- **Student output**: a timed mixed-paper review with corrected solutions, mark-scheme notes, and a next-topic revision plan.

## Capstone
Build an A-Level Maths readiness portfolio: one proof task, one pure modelling problem, one calculus application, one statistics investigation, one mechanics model, and one timed mixed-paper correction log showing how errors were diagnosed and fixed.

## Assessment Ideas
- Pure mathematics tests graded for notation, structure, exactness, and interpretation.
- Statistics tasks graded on technology use, assumptions, calculation accuracy, and contextual conclusions.
- Mechanics tasks graded on diagrams, modelling choices, units, and physical sense checks.
- Timed mixed papers followed by mark-scheme self-correction and targeted retrieval practice.
