# High School Physics
**Covers**: `col-high-school-physics`, `col-high-school-physics---ngss`
**Discipline**: Science · **Year**: 11 (ages 15–16)

## Course Philosophy
High School Physics focuses on a mathematically rigorous understanding of kinematics, dynamics, energy, and electromagnetism. Students use algebra and trigonometry to model physical systems and solve multi-step problems.

## Module 1: Kinematics (1D and 2D Motion) (20%)
- Vectors vs Scalars: resolving vectors into x and y components using trigonometry
- 1D Kinematics: interpreting position, velocity, and acceleration graphs; using the four kinematic equations for constant acceleration
- Free fall and acceleration due to gravity ($g = 9.8 m/s^2$)
- 2D Kinematics (Projectile Motion): treating x and y motion independently; solving for time of flight, max height, and range
- **Questions**: Kinematic equation word problems, vector addition/resolution, projectile motion calculations

## Module 2: Dynamics (Forces and Newton's Laws) (20%)
- Drawing Free Body Diagrams (FBDs) for objects on flat and inclined planes
- Applying Newton's Second Law ($\Sigma F = ma$) in 1D and 2D
- Static and kinetic friction ($F_f = \mu F_N$)
- Tension, normal force, and the gravitational force ($F_g = mg$)
- Uniform Circular Motion: centripetal acceleration ($a_c = v^2/r$) and centripetal force
- Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation ($F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$)
- **Questions**: Inclined plane force analysis, solving for coefficient of friction, centripetal force calculations

## Module 3: Work, Energy, and Momentum (20%)
- Work ($W = Fd\cos\theta$) and Power ($P = W/t$)
- Kinetic Energy ($KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$) and Gravitational Potential Energy ($PE = mgh$)
- The Work-Energy Theorem and Conservation of Mechanical Energy
- Spring potential energy ($PE_s = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$) and Hooke's Law ($F = -kx$)
- Momentum ($p = mv$) and Impulse ($J = F\Delta t = \Delta p$)
- Conservation of Momentum in 1D: elastic vs inelastic collisions
- **Questions**: Conservation of energy multi-step problems (e.g., rollercoasters), elastic/inelastic collision calculations

## Module 4: Waves and Sound (20%)
- Properties of waves: transverse vs longitudinal, wave equation ($v = f\lambda$)
- Wave behaviors: reflection, refraction (Snell's Law: $n_1\sin\theta_1 = n_2\sin\theta_2$), diffraction, interference (superposition principle)
- Sound waves: speed of sound, pitch, loudness, standing waves in strings and pipes (harmonics/fundamentals)
- The Doppler Effect (conceptual and basic calculation)
- **Questions**: Snell's Law calculations, determining harmonic frequencies in pipes, wave interference analysis

## Module 5: Electricity and Magnetism (20%)
- Electrostatics: Coulomb's Law ($F = k \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}$), electric fields, and electric potential (voltage)
- DC Circuits: Ohm's Law ($V = IR$), electrical power ($P = IV$)
- Analyzing series, parallel, and complex combination circuits (equivalent resistance, Kirchhoff's laws conceptually)
- Magnetism: magnetic fields created by permanent magnets and current-carrying wires (Right Hand Rule)
- Electromagnetic induction: moving a magnet through a coil generates a current (Faraday's Law conceptually)
- **Questions**: Equivalent resistance in combination circuits, Coulomb's Law calculations, Right Hand Rule applications

## Stretch Zone
- Torque ($\tau = rF\sin\theta$) and rotational statics
- Geometric optics: ray diagrams and the thin lens equation ($1/f = 1/d_o + 1/d_i$)
