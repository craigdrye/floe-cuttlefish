# Guess the Toxic Plant

## Course Shape
This botany-safety course identifies hazardous plants by leaves, stems, flowers, berries, hairs, and avoidance clues. It is for recognition and caution, not tasting, touching, or self-treatment.

## Chapters
- Rash and sting plants: poison ivy and stinging nettle
- Toxic flowers and shrubs: foxglove and oleander
- Dangerous berries and evergreens: deadly nightshade, pokeweed, and yew
- Lookalike risk: poison hemlock and umbrella-shaped flowers

## Key Skills
- Use multiple visual clues before naming a plant
- Treat plant identification as a safety practice
- Notice when lookalikes make expert confirmation important
