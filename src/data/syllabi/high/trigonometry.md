# Trigonometry
**ID**: `trigonometry` · **Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 11 (ages 15–16)

## Course Philosophy
A deep, focused dive into the study of triangles and periodic functions. This course bridges the gap between Geometry and Precalculus, treating trigonometry both analytically (functions and graphs) and geometrically (triangles and vectors).

## Module 1: Right Triangle Trigonometry & The Unit Circle (20%)
- Review of SOH CAH TOA and the Pythagorean Theorem
- Reciprocal functions: secant, cosecant, cotangent
- Angle measurement: degrees vs radians, converting between the two
- Arc length ($s = r\theta$) and sector area ($A = \frac{1}{2}r^2\theta$)
- The Unit Circle: determining exact values of all six trigonometric functions for special angles (multiples of $\pi/6$, $\pi/4$, $\pi/3$, $\pi/2$)
- Reference angles and the ASTC rule (All Students Take Calculus) for determining signs in quadrants
- **Questions**: Evaluating $\csc(5\pi/6)$, arc length calculations, finding all trig ratios given one and the quadrant

## Module 2: Graphing Trigonometric Functions (20%)
- Graphing $y = \sin(x)$ and $y = \cos(x)$
- Amplitude, period, phase shift, and vertical shift transformations ($y = A\sin(B(x-C)) + D$)
- Graphing $y = \tan(x)$ and understanding its asymptotes and period ($\pi/B$)
- Graphing reciprocal functions (csc, sec, cot) and identifying their asymptotes
- Inverse trigonometric functions (arcsin, arccos, arctan): domain restrictions and principal values
- **Questions**: Finding the period and phase shift of a given equation, evaluating $\arcsin(-\frac{1}{2})$

## Module 3: Trigonometric Identities (20%)
- Fundamental identities: Reciprocal, Quotient, and Pythagorean identities (e.g., $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$, $1 + \tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta$)
- Simplifying trigonometric expressions
- Verifying (proving) complex trigonometric identities
- Even/Odd identities and Co-function identities
- **Questions**: Simplifying expressions like $\cos(x)\tan(x)$, step-by-step verification of an identity

## Module 4: Advanced Formulas & Solving Equations (20%)
- Sum and Difference formulas for sine, cosine, and tangent (e.g., $\cos(\alpha - \beta) = \cos\alpha\cos\beta + \sin\alpha\sin\beta$)
- Double-Angle and Half-Angle formulas
- Solving trigonometric equations algebraically over a specified interval (e.g., $[0, 2\pi)$) or for all general solutions
- Solving equations requiring factoring or the use of identities (e.g., $2\sin^2(x) - \sin(x) - 1 = 0$)
- **Questions**: Using sum formulas to find exact values of 15° or 75°, solving trig quadratics

## Module 5: Applications (Oblique Triangles & Vectors) (20%)
- The Law of Sines (including the ambiguous case / SSA)
- The Law of Cosines
- Finding the area of an oblique triangle ($Area = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$ and Heron's Formula)
- Introduction to vectors in the 2D plane: magnitude, direction angle, component form $\langle a,b \rangle$
- Vector operations: addition, scalar multiplication, dot product, and the angle between two vectors
- **Questions**: Solving for a missing side using Law of Cosines, determining the number of solutions in the ambiguous case, calculating the dot product

## Stretch Zone
- Complex numbers in polar (trigonometric) form: $z = r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)$
- De Moivre's Theorem for powers and roots of complex numbers
