# Trigonometry
**ID**: `trigonometry` - **Discipline**: Maths - **Year**: 11 (ages 15-16)

## Course Aim
Build trigonometry as both geometry and function theory: students move from right-triangle ratios to the unit circle, graphs, identities, equations, oblique triangles, vectors, and polar ideas. The course should prepare students for precalculus, physics, engineering pathways, and any context where periodic change or direction matters.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they involve sine, cosine, tangent, reciprocal functions, radians, the unit circle, exact values, graph transformations, inverse trig, identities, trig equations, law of sines, law of cosines, vectors, polar form, or modeling periodic phenomena. Encourage diagrams, quadrant reasoning, domain restrictions, and checking for all possible solutions.

## Chapter 1: Right-Triangle Trigonometry
**Key concepts**: Similar triangles, sine, cosine, tangent, reciprocal ratios, angle of elevation, angle of depression, Pythagorean theorem, inverse trig on calculators, and units.
**Practice questions**: Find missing sides and angles; choose the correct ratio; solve elevation and bearing problems; check whether an answer is plausible from the diagram; explain why trig ratios depend on angle, not triangle size.
**Student output**: A field-measurement report estimating an inaccessible height or distance using a diagram, measurements, trig ratio, and error discussion.

## Chapter 2: Radians, Arc Length, and the Unit Circle
**Key concepts**: Degree measure, radian measure, coterminal angles, arc length, sector area, unit circle, terminal side, reference angle, quadrants, exact values, and signs.
**Practice questions**: Convert between degrees and radians; find arc length and sector area; locate angles on the unit circle; evaluate exact trig values; use reference angles; determine signs by quadrant.
**Student output**: A unit-circle atlas showing key angles, coordinates, exact values, reference angles, and quadrant sign patterns.

## Chapter 3: Trigonometric Functions and Graphs
**Key concepts**: Sine, cosine, tangent, domain, range, intercepts, asymptotes, amplitude, period, frequency, phase shift, vertical shift, and reciprocal-function graphs.
**Practice questions**: Graph sine, cosine, tangent, secant, cosecant, and cotangent; identify transformations; write equations from graphs; compare period and frequency; model a repeating real-world situation.
**Student output**: A periodic-modeling task using data or a scenario such as daylight, tides, sound, circular motion, or seasonal temperature.

## Chapter 4: Inverse Trig and Trig Equations
**Key concepts**: Inverse sine, inverse cosine, inverse tangent, restricted domains, principal values, general solutions, intervals, extraneous solutions, and calculator interpretation.
**Practice questions**: Evaluate inverse trig expressions; solve trig equations on a given interval; write general solutions; use graphs to check solutions; identify when symmetry gives additional answers.
**Student output**: A solution-set guide explaining how to solve one trig equation algebraically, graphically, and with unit-circle reasoning.

## Chapter 5: Identities and Proof Habits
**Key concepts**: Reciprocal identities, quotient identities, Pythagorean identities, even and odd identities, cofunction identities, simplification, verification, and proof strategy.
**Practice questions**: Simplify trig expressions; choose identities strategically; verify identities step by step; avoid illegal cancellation; explain why proving an identity differs from solving an equation.
**Student output**: An identity-proof notebook with annotated decisions, alternate routes, and a reflection on common traps.

## Chapter 6: Sum, Difference, Double-Angle, and Half-Angle Formulas
**Key concepts**: Sum formulas, difference formulas, double-angle formulas, half-angle formulas, exact values, transformations, and trig quadratic equations.
**Practice questions**: Find exact values for compound angles; rewrite expressions using formulas; solve equations that require factoring or identities; compare formula-based and graph-based checks.
**Student output**: A formula derivation and application sheet connecting at least two identities to exact-value or equation-solving tasks.

## Chapter 7: Oblique Triangles and Vectors
**Key concepts**: Law of sines, ambiguous case, law of cosines, area of an oblique triangle, Heron's formula, vector components, magnitude, direction, dot product, and angle between vectors.
**Practice questions**: Solve non-right triangles; determine whether an SSA case has zero, one, or two solutions; find triangle area; add vectors; calculate dot products; connect vectors to navigation and physics.
**Student output**: A navigation or forces problem set with diagrams, triangle solutions, vector components, and interpretation.

## Chapter 8: Polar and Complex Extensions
**Key concepts**: Polar coordinates, rectangular-to-polar conversion, complex numbers, trigonometric form, magnitude, argument, multiplication in polar form, De Moivre's theorem, and roots.
**Practice questions**: Convert points between coordinate systems; graph polar points; multiply complex numbers in polar form; use De Moivre's theorem; connect rotation to multiplication by a complex number.
**Student output**: A short extension project showing how trigonometry describes rotation, waves, complex numbers, or circular motion.

## Capstone
Students complete a trigonometric modeling portfolio with three parts: a triangle measurement problem, a periodic-function model, and an advanced extension using vectors, polar coordinates, or complex numbers. Each part must include diagrams or graphs, equations, solution checks, and a short explanation for a non-specialist.

## Assessment Ideas
Use diagram-first quizzes, exact-value fluency checks, graph transformation tasks, identity-proof notebooks, equation-solving checkpoints, modeling problems, and cumulative mixed reviews. Assess domain and interval reasoning carefully, since many trig errors come from missing possible angles rather than weak algebra.
