# 10th Grade Reading and Vocab
**ID**: `col-10th-grade-reading-and-vocab` · **Discipline**: English · **Year**: 11 (ages 15-16)

## Course Aim
Strengthen 10th-grade reading, vocabulary, rhetoric, and evidence skills so students can handle complex literature, argument, informational texts, and cross-media source synthesis.

## Course Design Notes
This course aligns with advanced grade 9-10 ELA expectations: close reading, literary analysis, rhetoric, argument evaluation, vocabulary nuance, conventions, and synthesis. It is a bridge toward AP English, SAT Reading and Writing, and stronger academic writing generally.

## Chapter 1: Close Reading Habits
- **Key concepts**: annotation, inference, textual evidence, theme, tone, connotation, structure, rereading.
- **Practice questions**: What does the text say directly? What can be inferred? Which word choice shapes tone?
- **Student output**: an annotation portfolio with evidence-based answers.

## Chapter 2: Literary Analysis and Character
- **Key concepts**: character motivation, conflict, setting, plot structure, theme development, symbolism, source transformation.
- **Practice questions**: How does the character change? How does structure create tension? What source material is being transformed?
- **Student output**: a literary analysis paragraph with embedded evidence.

## Chapter 3: Poetry, Figurative Language, and Sound
- **Key concepts**: metaphor, simile, personification, paradox, oxymoron, imagery, rhythm, line break, speaker.
- **Practice questions**: What image carries the meaning? How does the line break affect emphasis? What tension does the paradox create?
- **Student output**: a poem explication with vocabulary and figurative-language notes.

## Chapter 4: Rhetoric and Argument
- **Key concepts**: claim, evidence, reasoning, ethos, pathos, logos, audience, purpose, fallacy, counterclaim.
- **Practice questions**: Is the reasoning valid? What evidence is relevant and sufficient? How does rhetoric advance the purpose?
- **Student output**: an argument map and rhetorical analysis paragraph.

## Chapter 5: Informational Text and Historical Documents
- **Key concepts**: central idea, structure, point of view, seminal documents, context, claims developed across paragraphs.
- **Practice questions**: How does the author build the argument? What context matters? Which sentence refines the claim?
- **Student output**: a historical or informational text analysis with context note.

## Chapter 6: Vocabulary, Roots, and Nuance
- **Key concepts**: context clues, Greek and Latin roots, morphology, denotation, connotation, academic vocabulary, word families.
- **Practice questions**: What clue defines the word? Which synonym is most precise? How does the prefix or root shift meaning?
- **Student output**: a vocabulary notebook with roots, examples, and original sentences.

## Chapter 7: Synthesis Across Sources and Media
- **Key concepts**: source reliability, visual data, charts, multimedia, comparison, synthesis, citation, avoiding patchwriting.
- **Practice questions**: What does the graph add? Which source is more credible? How do two texts answer the same question differently?
- **Student output**: a synthesis paragraph using at least two sources and one visual or quantitative source.

## Chapter 8: Reading Studio and Exam Readiness
- **Key concepts**: stamina, timed reading, question stems, evidence review, vocabulary review, error patterns, discussion.
- **Practice questions**: Which question type causes misses? What evidence was overlooked? What reading habit should change?
- **Student output**: a reading growth portfolio with timed practice and reflection.

## Capstone
Build a 10th-grade reading portfolio with one literary analysis, one rhetoric/argument analysis, one vocabulary notebook sample, one synthesis paragraph, and a timed reading correction log.

## Assessment Ideas
- Reading responses graded on evidence and inference.
- Vocabulary tasks graded on context and precision.
- Capstone graded on analysis, synthesis, and revision.
