# AP Biology
**Covers**: `col-ap-biology`
**Discipline**: Science · **Year**: 12 (ages 16–18)
**Frameworks**: US College Board AP Biology

## Course Philosophy
AP Biology is a college-level course focusing on four Big Ideas: Evolution, Energetics, Information Storage/Transmission, and Systems Interactions. It heavily emphasizes inquiry-based labs, data analysis, and statistical evaluation (Chi-square).

## Module 1: Chemistry of Life & Cell Structure (15%)
- Structure of water and hydrogen bonding; pH
- Macromolecules: synthesis (dehydration) and cleavage (hydrolysis) of proteins, nucleic acids, lipids, and carbohydrates
- Cell structure: organelles and sub-cellular components
- Cell membrane dynamics: fluid mosaic model, water potential ($\Psi = \Psi_p + \Psi_s$), active/passive transport
- **Questions**: Calculating water potential, predicting osmosis, analyzing cell size (surface area to volume ratio)

## Module 2: Cellular Energetics & Cell Communication (20%)
- Enzyme catalysis: structure, function, environmental impacts, and allosteric regulation
- Photosynthesis: detailed mechanisms of light-dependent reactions and Calvin cycle; evolutionary origins (cyanobacteria)
- Cellular Respiration: glycolysis, pyruvate oxidation, Krebs cycle, oxidative phosphorylation (chemiosmosis)
- Cell communication: autocrine, paracrine, endocrine signaling; signal transduction pathways (reception, transduction, response); secondary messengers (cAMP)
- **Questions**: Tracing signal transduction pathways, analyzing enzyme kinetics graphs, comparing electron transport chains

## Module 3: Heredity & Gene Expression (25%)
- Meiosis and genetic diversity (crossing over, independent assortment)
- Complex inheritance: Mendelian and non-Mendelian (linked genes, recombination frequencies, polygenic inheritance)
- DNA and RNA structure; DNA replication mechanisms
- Transcription and Translation: the central dogma, post-transcriptional modifications (introns/exons)
- Regulation of gene expression: operons (lac/trp), transcription factors, epigenetics
- Biotechnology: PCR, gel electrophoresis, bacterial transformation, CRISPR
- **Questions**: Calculating recombination frequencies (mapping genes), predicting the effect of mutations on protein folding, analyzing electrophoresis gels

## Module 4: Evolution (20%)
- Natural selection and artificial selection
- Population genetics: Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium and the mathematical modeling of allele frequencies
- Evidence of evolution: molecular, morphological, and fossil data
- Phylogeny and cladograms: constructing trees based on derived characteristics
- Speciation: allopatric, sympatric, reproductive isolation, punctuated equilibrium vs gradualism
- Origins of life on Earth (Miller-Urey experiment, RNA world hypothesis)
- **Questions**: Performing Hardy-Weinberg calculations, constructing/interpreting cladograms, analyzing speciation scenarios

## Module 5: Ecology (20%)
- Responses to the environment: behavioral and physiological mechanisms (e.g., phototropism, circadian rhythms)
- Energy flow through ecosystems: primary productivity, trophic levels
- Population ecology: density-dependent vs density-independent factors, logistic growth models
- Community ecology: Simpson's Diversity Index, symbiosis, keystone species
- Disruptions to ecosystems: invasive species, human impact, climate change
- **Questions**: Calculating Simpson's Diversity Index, analyzing predator-prey population graphs, evaluating ecosystem resilience

## Stretch Zone
- Advanced statistical analysis: T-tests and Chi-square analysis for genetic crosses
- Detailed immunology (humoral vs cell-mediated responses)
