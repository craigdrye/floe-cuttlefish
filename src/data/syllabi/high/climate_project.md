# Climate Project
**ID**: `climateProject` · **Discipline**: Science/Humanities · **Year**: 10 (ages 14–15)

## Course Philosophy
An interdisciplinary course examining the science, economics, and politics of global climate change. Students will analyze data, evaluate policy proposals, and design local mitigation strategies.

## Module 1: The Science of Climate Change (20%)
- The Greenhouse Effect: the physics of greenhouse gases (CO2, CH4, N2O) and how they trap heat
- The Carbon Cycle: sources (emissions) and sinks (oceans, forests)
- Evidence for climate change: ice cores, global temperature records, sea-level rise, ocean acidification
- Feedback loops: Albedo effect (melting ice), permafrost thawing
- **Questions**: Explaining the greenhouse effect mechanism, interpreting ice core data, identifying positive feedback loops

## Module 2: The Impacts of Climate Change (20%)
- Ecological impacts: shifting habitats, coral bleaching, mass extinction risks
- Human impacts: extreme weather events (hurricanes, droughts), agricultural shifts, water scarcity
- Climate justice: why the poorest populations (who emit the least) suffer the most
- **Questions**: Linking warming to extreme weather, understanding ocean acidification, explaining climate justice

## Module 3: Energy Systems & Mitigation (20%)
- The role of fossil fuels (coal, oil, natural gas) in the modern economy
- Renewable energy technologies: solar (PV vs thermal), wind, hydroelectric, geothermal
- The role of nuclear power (pros and cons)
- Energy efficiency and grid modernization
- **Questions**: Comparing renewable energy sources, evaluating the pros/cons of nuclear, understanding grid challenges

## Module 4: Climate Economics & Policy (20%)
- The tragedy of the commons and negative externalities
- Carbon pricing mechanisms: Carbon Tax vs Cap-and-Trade
- International agreements: The Kyoto Protocol, the Paris Agreement (NDCs)
- The role of subsidies in energy markets
- **Questions**: Explaining carbon taxes, evaluating the Paris Agreement structure

## Module 5: Adaptation and Action (20%)
- Adaptation strategies: seawalls, drought-resistant crops, urban planning (green roofs)
- Individual action vs systemic change
- Designing a local climate action plan (project-based component)
- **Questions**: Distinguishing mitigation from adaptation, designing sustainable urban infrastructure

## Stretch Zone
- Geoengineering proposals (e.g., solar radiation management) and their ethical risks
- Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) of products (e.g., the carbon footprint of an electric vehicle battery)
