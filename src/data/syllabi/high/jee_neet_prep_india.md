# JEE / NEET Prep (India)
**Covers**: `col-jee-main-prep-india`, `col-neet-biology-prep-india`
**Discipline**: Exam Prep | **Year**: 11/12 (ages 15-18)
**Frameworks**: Indian CBSE Class 11/12, NTA Syllabi

## Course Aim
Prepare students for JEE Main and NEET with disciplined concept study, NCERT command, timed objective practice, and calm revision systems. The course treats pressure as a design constraint: students learn to solve accurately, manage negative marking, use mock analysis, and choose the right depth for engineering or medical entrance goals.

## Course Design Notes
This is a senior-secondary entrance-prep syllabus, not a shortcut list of tricks. NCERT Class 11 and 12 remain the base for chemistry and biology, while physics and mathematics require repeated problem solving across standard models. JEE-focused students should deepen mathematics, physics, and chemistry; NEET-focused students should prioritise biology, chemistry, and physics while maintaining enough mathematics for physics calculations and data interpretation. Every chapter should include timed practice, error logging, spaced revision, and exam-language fluency.

## Chapter 1: Exam System, NCERT Base, and Study Architecture
- **Key concepts**: JEE Main structure, NEET structure, NTA information bulletins, negative marking, syllabus mapping, Class 11 and Class 12 balance, NCERT reading, formula sheets, error logs, mock-test cycles, and burnout-aware planning.
- **Practice questions**: Classify questions by topic and difficulty; identify whether an error was conceptual, procedural, careless, or time-management related; build a weekly revision plan from mock data.
- **Student output**: A personalised exam dashboard with target exam, syllabus map, weekly study rhythm, formula and NCERT revision tracker, and error-log categories.

## Chapter 2: Mathematics for JEE Pathways
- **Key concepts**: Sets, relations, functions, quadratic equations, complex numbers, sequences and series, binomial theorem, permutations and combinations, matrices and determinants, limits, continuity, differentiability, applications of derivatives, integration, differential equations, coordinate geometry, vectors, and 3D geometry.
- **Practice questions**: Solve multi-step algebra questions; analyse functions and graphs; evaluate definite integrals; find tangents and normals to conics; use matrices and vectors in objective formats; practise numerical-value questions.
- **Student output**: A JEE mathematics problem notebook with solved examples, alternate methods, formula triggers, and corrected mistakes from timed sets.

## Chapter 3: Physics Mechanics and Measurement
- **Key concepts**: Units and dimensions, errors, vectors, kinematics, laws of motion, friction, circular motion, work, energy, power, centre of mass, momentum, collisions, rotational motion, gravitation, properties of solids and fluids, and mathematical modelling in physics.
- **Practice questions**: Draw force diagrams; choose conservation laws; solve projectile, collision, and rotation problems; check dimensions; estimate whether an answer is physically reasonable.
- **Student output**: A mechanics solution pack with diagrams before equations, key formula derivations, timed objective sets, and a correction log.

## Chapter 4: Physics Electricity, Magnetism, Waves, Optics, and Modern Physics
- **Key concepts**: Thermodynamics, kinetic theory, oscillations, waves, electrostatics, current electricity, capacitors, magnetic effects of current, electromagnetic induction, alternating current, ray optics, wave optics, dual nature of matter, atoms, nuclei, and semiconductors.
- **Practice questions**: Analyse circuits; calculate fields and potentials; solve AC and EMI problems; apply lens and mirror formulae; interpret interference patterns; handle photoelectric and nuclear calculations.
- **Student output**: A physics mixed-topic review file with concept maps, circuit and optics diagrams, formula conditions, and mock-test corrections.

## Chapter 5: Physical Chemistry and Core Inorganic Chemistry
- **Key concepts**: Mole concept, stoichiometry, atomic structure, periodicity, chemical bonding, thermodynamics, equilibrium, redox reactions, solutions, electrochemistry, chemical kinetics, surface chemistry where relevant, coordination compounds, qualitative analysis, and NCERT inorganic detail.
- **Practice questions**: Calculate limiting reagents and yields; solve equilibrium and pH problems; apply the Nernst equation; compare periodic trends; predict hybridisation, geometry, magnetic behaviour, and coordination-compound isomerism.
- **Student output**: A chemistry calculation and NCERT facts notebook with formula conditions, common traps, named examples, and corrected objective questions.

## Chapter 6: Organic Chemistry Strategy
- **Key concepts**: General organic chemistry, resonance, inductive and hyperconjugation effects, acidity and basicity, isomerism, reaction intermediates, hydrocarbons, haloalkanes and haloarenes, alcohols, phenols, ethers, aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids, amines, biomolecules, polymers, chemistry in everyday life where applicable, and practical chemistry.
- **Practice questions**: Predict major products; compare mechanisms; rank stability, acidity, and reactivity; identify functional groups; use named reactions without memorising them blindly.
- **Student output**: An organic reaction map showing reagents, conditions, mechanisms or reasoning cues, common exceptions, and product-prediction corrections.

## Chapter 7: Biology for NEET
- **Key concepts**: Diversity in the living world, structural organisation in plants and animals, cell structure and function, biomolecules, cell cycle, plant physiology, human physiology, reproduction, genetics, molecular basis of inheritance, evolution, health and disease, microbes, biotechnology, ecology, environment, diagrams, and NCERT line-by-line recall.
- **Practice questions**: Interpret NCERT diagrams; distinguish similar biological terms; sequence physiological pathways; solve genetics problems; answer assertion-reason and statement-based questions.
- **Student output**: A NEET biology revision file with NCERT annotations, diagram labels, high-yield tables, genetics practice, and mistake patterns from mock tests.

## Chapter 8: Integrated Mock Tests, Revision, and Exam Temperament
- **Key concepts**: Full-length mocks, sectional mocks, question selection, negative marking strategy, speed versus accuracy, bubbling or response-entry discipline, last-month revision, memory refresh cycles, sleep and recovery, and post-mock analysis.
- **Practice questions**: Complete timed mixed-subject sets; choose skip, solve, or revisit decisions; compare first attempt accuracy with reviewed accuracy; write a correction plan after each mock.
- **Student output**: A final exam portfolio with mock-score trend, subject-wise error analysis, revised formula and NCERT lists, and a realistic last-four-weeks plan.

## Capstone
Students complete an entrance-exam readiness portfolio for either JEE, NEET, or a combined early-prep route. It must include a syllabus map, subject-wise error logs, one mathematics or biology depth file depending on target exam, physics and chemistry correction sets, at least three analysed mock tests, and a final revision plan grounded in evidence rather than anxiety.

## Assessment Ideas
- Weekly timed objective quizzes with error-type tagging.
- NCERT recall checks for chemistry and biology, including diagram and statement-based items.
- Physics and mathematics problem sets graded on representation, method choice, and answer checking.
- Chemistry assessments split across calculation, conceptual reasoning, and NCERT factual precision.
- Capstone graded on accuracy growth, correction quality, subject balance, exam strategy, and sustainability of the revision plan.
