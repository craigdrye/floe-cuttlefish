# JEE / NEET Prep (India)
**Covers**: `col-jee-main-prep-india`, `col-neet-biology-prep-india`
**Discipline**: Exam Prep · **Year**: 11/12 (ages 15–18)
**Frameworks**: Indian CBSE Class 11/12, NTA Syllabi

## Course Philosophy
This syllabus outlines the rigorous foundational preparation required for India's premier engineering (JEE Main) and medical (NEET) entrance exams. The focus is on extreme conceptual clarity, speed-solving techniques, and tackling high-order objective questions.

## Module 1: JEE Main - Mathematics (25%)
- Algebra: Sets, Relations, and Functions; Complex Numbers and Quadratic Equations; Matrices and Determinants; Permutations and Combinations; Binomial Theorem; Sequence and Series
- Calculus: Limits, Continuity, and Differentiability; Applications of Derivatives; Integral Calculus (Indefinite and Definite); Differential Equations
- Coordinate Geometry: Straight Lines, Circles, Conic Sections (Parabola, Ellipse, Hyperbola)
- Vector Algebra & 3D Geometry
- Trigonometry (up to inverse trigonometric functions)
- **Questions**: Evaluating complex definite integrals, finding tangents/normals to conics, matrix properties

## Module 2: JEE/NEET - Physics (25%)
- Mechanics: Kinematics, Laws of Motion, Work/Energy/Power, Rotational Motion (Moment of Inertia), Gravitation, Properties of Solids and Liquids (Fluid Mechanics)
- Thermodynamics & Kinetic Theory of Gases
- Oscillations and Waves (SHM, Doppler Effect)
- Electrodynamics: Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Magnetic Effects of Current, EMI, Alternating Current
- Optics: Ray and Wave Optics (Interference, Diffraction)
- Modern Physics: Dual Nature of Matter, Atoms and Nuclei, Electronic Devices (Semiconductors)
- **Questions**: Calculating Moment of Inertia for complex bodies, solving tricky DC circuits, photoelectric effect calculations

## Module 3: JEE/NEET - Chemistry (Physical & Inorganic) (20%)
- Physical Chemistry: Some Basic Concepts (Mole concept, stoichiometry), Atomic Structure (Quantum numbers), Chemical Bonding (VBT, MOT), Chemical Thermodynamics, Solutions, Equilibrium (Chemical and Ionic), Redox Reactions, Chemical Kinetics, Electrochemistry
- Inorganic Chemistry: Classification of Elements and Periodicity, p-Block, d- and f-Block Elements, Coordination Compounds (Nomenclature, Isomerism, CFT)
- **Questions**: Calculating pH of buffer solutions, applying Nernst equation, predicting hybridization and magnetic properties using MOT

## Module 4: JEE/NEET - Chemistry (Organic) (10%)
- General Organic Chemistry (GOC): Inductive effect, resonance, hyperconjugation, reaction intermediates
- Hydrocarbons (Alkanes, Alkenes, Alkynes, Aromatic)
- Organic Compounds Containing Halogens, Oxygen (Alcohols, Phenols, Ethers, Aldehydes, Ketones, Carboxylic Acids), and Nitrogen (Amines)
- Biomolecules and Polymers
- Principles Related to Practical Chemistry
- **Questions**: Predicting major products of nucleophilic substitution/elimination, distinguishing functional groups via chemical tests

## Module 5: NEET - Biology (20%)
- Diversity in Living World: Taxonomic categories, Biological Classification, Plant/Animal Kingdom
- Structural Organization in Animals and Plants: Morphology and Anatomy
- Cell Structure and Function: Biomolecules, Cell Cycle and Division
- Plant Physiology: Transport, Mineral Nutrition, Photosynthesis, Respiration, Plant Growth
- Human Physiology: Digestion, Breathing, Body Fluids, Excretory products, Locomotion, Neural control, Chemical coordination
- Reproduction: Reproduction in Organisms, Flowering Plants, Human Reproduction, Reproductive Health
- Genetics and Evolution: Principles of Inheritance, Molecular Basis of Inheritance, Evolution
- Biology and Human Welfare: Health and Disease, Microbes
- Biotechnology and Its Applications
- Ecology and Environment
- **Questions**: Identifying specific anatomical features, tracing physiological pathways, molecular genetics mechanisms

## Stretch Zone
- JEE Advanced level problems (multi-concept integration)
- Numerical-value-type questions focusing on precision to two decimal places
