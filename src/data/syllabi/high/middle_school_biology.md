# Middle School Biology
**ID**: `middleSchoolBiology` · **Discipline**: Science · **Year**: 8 (ages 12–13)
**Frameworks**: US NGSS Middle School Life Science, UK KS3 Biology

## Module 1: Structure and Function of Cells (20%)
- Cell theory: all living things are made of cells, cells are the basic unit of life, all cells come from pre-existing cells
- Differentiating between plant and animal cells (cell wall, chloroplasts, large vacuole vs small vacuoles)
- Organelles and their functions: nucleus, mitochondria, cell membrane, cytoplasm, ribosomes
- Unicellular vs. multicellular organisms
- Levels of organization: cells → tissues → organs → organ systems → organism
- **Questions**: Organelle function matching, plant vs animal cell identification, levels of organization hierarchy

## Module 2: Human Body Systems (20%)
- The digestive system: mechanical vs chemical digestion, role of enzymes, nutrient absorption in the small intestine
- The respiratory system: gas exchange (oxygen in, carbon dioxide out) in the alveoli
- The circulatory system: heart, blood vessels (arteries, veins, capillaries), and blood components
- The nervous system: brain, spinal cord, nerves, and reflexes
- How systems interact to maintain homeostasis (e.g., sweating to cool down)
- **Questions**: Tracing the path of food/air/blood, identifying system interactions, homeostasis concepts

## Module 3: Matter and Energy in Organisms and Ecosystems (20%)
- Photosynthesis: inputs (sunlight, CO2, water) and outputs (glucose, oxygen); the role of chloroplasts
- Cellular Respiration: inputs (glucose, oxygen) and outputs (ATP energy, CO2, water); the role of mitochondria
- The carbon cycle: how carbon moves between the atmosphere, biosphere, and geosphere
- Food webs and energy pyramids: producers, primary/secondary/tertiary consumers, decomposers
- The 10% rule: how energy is lost as heat at each trophic level
- **Questions**: Photosynthesis vs respiration equations, analyzing complex food webs, energy pyramid calculations

## Module 4: Genetics and Inheritance (20%)
- DNA structure (double helix) and its role as the instruction manual for proteins
- Chromosomes and genes
- Asexual reproduction (mitosis) vs. Sexual reproduction (meiosis) and their impact on genetic variation
- Mendelian genetics: dominant vs recessive traits, genotype vs phenotype
- Using Punnett squares to predict the probability of traits in offspring
- **Questions**: Punnett square calculations, genotype/phenotype matching, mitosis vs meiosis differences

## Module 5: Natural Selection and Evolution (20%)
- Evidence for evolution: fossil record, comparative anatomy (homologous structures), embryology, DNA similarities
- Charles Darwin and the theory of Natural Selection ("survival of the fittest")
- Variation within a population, overproduction of offspring, competition, and differential survival/reproduction
- Artificial selection (selective breeding) vs Natural selection
- How environmental changes drive adaptation or extinction
- **Questions**: Identifying homologous structures, natural selection scenarios (e.g., peppered moths), artificial vs natural selection

## Stretch Zone
- Introduction to CRISPR and genetic engineering concepts
- Pedigree charts for tracking genetic diseases
- The nitrogen cycle and the role of nitrogen-fixing bacteria
