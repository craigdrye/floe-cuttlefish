# HSC / VCE Maths (Australia)
**Covers**: `col-hsc---vce-maths-australia`
**Discipline**: Maths · **Year**: 12 (ages 16–18)
**Frameworks**: Australian State Curricula (NSW HSC Advanced/Extension 1, VIC VCE Methods/Specialist)

## Course Philosophy
This syllabus aggregates the rigorous final-year mathematics courses from the Australian state systems (specifically targeting the level of HSC Mathematics Extension 1 and VCE Specialist Mathematics). The focus is on calculus, vectors, probability, and mechanics.

## Module 1: Functions, Graphs, & Trigonometry (20%)
- Graphing techniques: Transformations, absolute value graphs, rational functions (finding asymptotes)
- Inverse functions (domain/range restrictions)
- Advanced Trigonometry: Inverse trigonometric functions (graphs and derivatives), compound angles, double angles, $t$-formulae ($t = \tan(\theta/2)$)
- Solving complex trigonometric equations
- **Questions**: Graphing $y = |f(x)|$ vs $y = f(|x|)$, using $t$-formulae to solve equations, evaluating derivatives of inverse trig functions

## Module 2: Calculus (Differentiation & Integration) (25%)
- Differentiation: Product, quotient, chain rules; derivatives of exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions
- Applications: Tangents, normals, rates of change, optimization, curve sketching (first and second derivatives)
- Integration: Indefinite and definite integrals, integration by substitution, integration by parts (Specialist/Ext 2)
- Applications of Integration: Area between curves, volumes of solids of revolution
- Differential Equations: First-order separable differential equations, modeling exponential growth/decay, Newton's Law of Cooling, logistic growth
- **Questions**: Calculating volumes of revolution, solving logistic growth differential equations, related rates problems

## Module 3: Vectors and Complex Numbers (20%)
- Vectors in 2D and 3D: Addition, scalar multiplication, dot product, scalar and vector projections
- Applications of vectors: Geometric proofs, resolving forces
- Complex Numbers: Arithmetic, Cartesian form ($a+ib$), Modulus-Argument (Polar) form, the Argand diagram
- De Moivre's Theorem and its applications (finding roots of complex numbers, multiple angle formulas)
- Loci in the complex plane
- **Questions**: Proving geometric properties using vectors, using De Moivre's theorem to express $\cos(5\theta)$ in terms of $\cos(\theta)$, sketching the locus $|z - 2| = |z + i|$

## Module 4: Probability and Statistics (15%)
- Combinatorics: Permutations and combinations, Pigeonhole Principle
- Discrete random variables: Expected value, variance, standard deviation
- The Binomial Distribution
- Continuous random variables: Probability density functions (PDFs), cumulative distribution functions (CDFs)
- The Normal Distribution and the Central Limit Theorem
- Sample proportions and confidence intervals
- **Questions**: Calculating expected value from a PDF, finding binomial probabilities, constructing a 95% confidence interval for a sample proportion

## Module 5: Mechanics (Specialist / Extension 1) (20%)
- Kinematics: Rectilinear motion, acceleration as a function of velocity ($a = v \frac{dv}{dx}$) or time
- Projectile Motion: Parametric equations for $x$ and $y$, deriving equations for max height, range, and time of flight (including air resistance models in Extension 2)
- Dynamics: Resolving forces, Newton's Laws, motion on an inclined plane
- Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM): $x = A\cos(nt + \alpha)$, $v^2 = n^2(A^2 - x^2)$
- **Questions**: Using $a = v \frac{dv}{dx}$ to find velocity given position, deriving the equation of path for a projectile, solving SHM word problems

## Stretch Zone
- HSC Extension 2 / VCE Specialist: Integration using partial fractions, mechanics in resisting mediums, complex number polynomials
