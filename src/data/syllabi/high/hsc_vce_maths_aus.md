# HSC / VCE Maths (Australia)
**Covers**: `col-hsc---vce-maths-australia`
**Discipline**: Maths | **Year**: 12 (ages 16-18)
**Frameworks**: Australian State Curricula (NSW HSC Advanced/Extension 1, VIC VCE Methods/Specialist)

## Course Aim
Prepare students for senior Australian mathematics pathways by building fluent algebra, careful graph interpretation, calculus control, statistical judgement, and exam-ready communication. The course supports students moving through NSW HSC Mathematics Advanced and Extension pathways, Victorian VCE Mathematical Methods and Specialist Mathematics, or a blended senior maths preparation sequence.

## Course Design Notes
Use this syllabus as a rigorous but sane bridge across state systems. HSC and VCE name topics differently, but the common demands are stable: students must model functions, justify methods, handle multi-step symbolic work, interpret technology output, and write solutions that earn marks under time pressure. Teachers can route students toward Methods/Advanced depth, then add Extension 1/Specialist material through vectors, complex numbers, proof, mechanics, and harder calculus.

## Chapter 1: Pathways, Algebra, and Exam Habits
- **Key concepts**: Course pathways, assumed knowledge, algebraic manipulation, surds, indices, logarithms, exact values, inequalities, interval notation, domain and range, CAS and non-CAS expectations, and mark allocation.
- **Practice questions**: Simplify expressions with restrictions; solve equations and inequalities; identify domain errors; compare exact and decimal answers; annotate a worked solution for lost marks.
- **Student output**: A senior maths readiness sheet with algebra corrections, personal error patterns, and a one-page exam strategy for the student's pathway.

## Chapter 2: Functions, Graphs, and Transformations
- **Key concepts**: Function notation, composite and inverse functions, polynomial, rational, exponential, logarithmic, absolute value, hybrid, and piecewise functions, transformations, asymptotes, intercepts, symmetry, and graphical reasoning.
- **Practice questions**: Sketch transformed functions; find inverse functions with restricted domains; match equations to graphs; solve graph-based inequalities; explain the effect of parameters.
- **Student output**: A graphing portfolio with at least five function families, transformations, key features, and short written interpretations.

## Chapter 3: Trigonometry and Periodic Models
- **Key concepts**: Radian measure, exact trigonometric values, sine and cosine rules, compound-angle identities, double-angle identities, inverse trigonometric functions, trigonometric equations, amplitude, period, phase shift, and periodic modelling.
- **Practice questions**: Solve trigonometric equations on restricted domains; prove or apply identities; model tides, daylight, sound, or circular motion; interpret inverse trigonometric outputs.
- **Student output**: A periodic-model report with graph, equation, parameter interpretation, domain choice, and checking of reasonableness.

## Chapter 4: Differential Calculus
- **Key concepts**: Limits as needed for calculus, first principles as enrichment, derivative rules, chain, product, and quotient rules, implicit differentiation where appropriate, tangents, normals, stationary points, concavity, rates of change, optimisation, and curve sketching.
- **Practice questions**: Differentiate mixed functions; locate and classify stationary points; solve optimisation problems; connect derivative signs to graph features; interpret rates in context.
- **Student output**: A curve-sketching and optimisation set with annotated derivative tables and written conclusions.

## Chapter 5: Integral Calculus and Differential Equations
- **Key concepts**: Antiderivatives, definite integrals, area under and between curves, numerical integration, substitution, integration by parts for higher pathways, volumes of revolution for extension work, separable differential equations, growth, decay, and logistic models.
- **Practice questions**: Evaluate definite integrals; choose between algebraic, numerical, and graphical methods; find areas and volumes; solve separable differential equations; interpret constants in a model.
- **Student output**: A calculus modelling task using integration or a differential equation, with assumptions, solution method, graph, and interpretation.

## Chapter 6: Probability, Statistics, and Data Interpretation
- **Key concepts**: Counting, conditional probability, independence, discrete random variables, expectation and variance, binomial distribution, normal distribution, samples, confidence intervals, hypothesis-test language where required, and technology-assisted statistics.
- **Practice questions**: Build probability trees and tables; calculate binomial and normal probabilities; interpret expected value; critique a confidence interval; read statistical output in context.
- **Student output**: A statistics investigation using a real or simulated data set, including display, calculation, inference, and limitations.

## Chapter 7: Vectors, Complex Numbers, and Proof Extensions
- **Key concepts**: Vector operations in two and three dimensions, dot product, projections, geometric proof, lines and planes for Specialist pathways, complex numbers, Argand diagrams, modulus-argument form, De Moivre's theorem, roots, loci, and proof by induction where required.
- **Practice questions**: Resolve vectors; prove geometric relationships; find angles and projections; convert complex forms; solve root and locus problems; write a short induction proof.
- **Student output**: An extension methods dossier with one vector proof, one complex-number problem, and one proof-style solution revised for clarity.

## Chapter 8: Mechanics and Final Exam Studio
- **Key concepts**: Kinematics, displacement, velocity, acceleration, motion under gravity, projectiles, forces, Newton's laws, inclined planes, connected particles where appropriate, simple harmonic motion for higher pathways, mixed-topic exam strategy, and solution checking.
- **Practice questions**: Derive and use motion equations; resolve forces; analyse projectile motion; solve mechanics problems using calculus; complete timed mixed-topic exam sections.
- **Student output**: A timed exam studio folder with mechanics solutions, mixed-topic corrections, mark-scheme annotations, and a final revision map.

## Capstone
Students complete a senior mathematics exam portfolio for their pathway. It must include one function and graph analysis, one calculus modelling problem, one probability or statistics interpretation, one extension problem from vectors, complex numbers, proof, or mechanics, and a timed mixed-topic paper with correction notes explaining how marks were gained or lost.

## Assessment Ideas
- Short algebra and graph fluency checks with restrictions and exact-answer expectations.
- Topic tests that require written reasoning, not only final answers.
- Technology-aware tasks where students compare hand methods, calculator output, and interpretation.
- Timed exam sets using HSC-style and VCE-style wording.
- Capstone graded on mathematical accuracy, pathway appropriateness, clarity of working, interpretation, and evidence of correction.
