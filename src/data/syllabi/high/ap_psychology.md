# AP Psychology
**ID**: `apPsychology` · **Discipline**: Social Science · **Year**: High School (Years 10-12)

## Course Aim
Prepare students for AP Psychology by teaching the scientific study of behavior and mental processes: research design, biological bases, cognition, development, learning, social and personality psychology, mental health, and evidence-based explanation.

## Course Design Notes
College Board revised AP Psychology for recent exam cycles, with stronger emphasis on science practices, research methods, data interpretation, and application of psychological concepts. This syllabus keeps classic content but organizes it around evidence and scenario reasoning.

## Chapter 1: Psychology as a Science
- **Key concepts**: perspectives, research methods, operational definitions, variables, sampling, ethics, descriptive and inferential statistics.
- **Practice questions**: What is the hypothesis? What variable is confounded? What claim can the data support?
- **Student output**: a research critique identifying design, variables, ethics, and limits.

## Chapter 2: Biological Bases of Behavior
- **Key concepts**: neurons, neurotransmitters, nervous system, endocrine system, brain structures, neuroplasticity, genetics.
- **Practice questions**: Which brain region is involved? How does a neurotransmitter affect behavior? What is biological versus environmental influence?
- **Student output**: a brain-behavior case explanation with evidence.

## Chapter 3: Sensation, Perception, and Consciousness
- **Key concepts**: thresholds, sensory adaptation, vision, hearing, Gestalt principles, attention, sleep, dreams, psychoactive drugs.
- **Practice questions**: Is this sensation or perception? What principle explains the illusion? How does sleep affect cognition?
- **Student output**: a perception demonstration with concept explanation.

## Chapter 4: Learning
- **Key concepts**: classical conditioning, operant conditioning, reinforcement, punishment, schedules, observational learning, cognitive learning.
- **Practice questions**: What is the conditioned stimulus? Is this negative reinforcement or punishment? What behavior is modeled?
- **Student output**: a behavior-change plan using learning principles ethically.

## Chapter 5: Cognitive Psychology
- **Key concepts**: memory, encoding, storage, retrieval, forgetting, thinking, heuristics, biases, language, intelligence, validity and reliability.
- **Practice questions**: Which memory process failed? What bias influenced judgment? Is the test valid for the claim?
- **Student output**: a cognition case analysis connecting memory, thinking, and evidence.

## Chapter 6: Development, Motivation, Emotion, and Personality
- **Key concepts**: developmental stages, attachment, identity, motivation theories, emotion theories, trait theory, psychodynamic and humanistic perspectives.
- **Practice questions**: What developmental pattern appears? Which motivation theory explains the behavior? What personality construct is being measured?
- **Student output**: a lifespan development and personality comparison chart.

## Chapter 7: Social Psychology and Mental Health
- **Key concepts**: attribution, attitudes, conformity, obedience, group processes, psychological disorders, therapy, psychopharmacology, stigma.
- **Practice questions**: What social influence is operating? How should a disorder be described accurately? Which treatment approach fits the scenario?
- **Student output**: a scenario analysis applying social, clinical, and ethical concepts.

## Chapter 8: AP Psychology Exam Studio
- **Key concepts**: concept application, data interpretation, research design, evidence, scenario vocabulary, concise explanation.
- **Practice questions**: Which concept best explains the scenario? What evidence supports the conclusion? How should the FRQ be structured?
- **Student output**: a timed FRQ set with vocabulary precision and scoring reflection.

## Capstone
Create a psychology evidence portfolio: one research critique, one biological case explanation, one learning application, one cognition analysis, one social/clinical scenario, and one timed AP-style correction log.

## Assessment Ideas
- Scenario questions graded on accurate concept application.
- Research tasks graded on design, ethics, and data interpretation.
- Capstone graded on evidence use and concise AP-style explanation.
