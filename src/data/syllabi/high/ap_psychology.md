# AP Psychology
**ID**: `apPsychology` · **Discipline**: Social Science · **Year**: High School (Years 10–12)

## Course Philosophy
AP Psychology introduces students to the systematic and scientific study of human behavior and mental processes. It bridges the biological and social sciences, requiring memorization of anatomy and theory, alongside the application of research methodologies.

## Module 1: Scientific Foundations and Biological Bases (20%)
- History of psychology (Structuralism, Functionalism, Behaviorism, Cognitive revolution)
- Research Methods: Experimental design, correlational studies, independent/dependent variables, confounding variables, operational definitions
- Basic Statistics: Mean, median, mode, standard deviation, normal distribution, and statistical significance
- Neuroanatomy: The neuron, action potentials, neurotransmitters, and the nervous system
- The Brain: Brainstem, limbic system, cerebral cortex lobes, and neuroplasticity
- Endocrine system and behavioral genetics
- **Questions**: Identifying the confounding variable in a flawed study, explaining the function of the sympathetic nervous system during a "fight or flight" response

## Module 2: Sensation, Perception, and Learning (20%)
- Sensation: Absolute vs difference thresholds (Weber's Law), sensory adaptation
- Vision: Anatomy of the eye, feature detectors, trichromatic and opponent-process theories
- Hearing and other senses; Gestalt principles of perception, depth perception
- States of Consciousness: Sleep stages, sleep disorders, and psychoactive drugs
- Classical Conditioning: Pavlov, unconditioned/conditioned stimuli and responses
- Operant Conditioning: Skinner, positive/negative reinforcement and punishment, schedules of reinforcement
- Observational Learning (Bandura)
- **Questions**: Differentiating between negative reinforcement and punishment, applying Gestalt principles to explain visual organization

## Module 3: Cognitive Psychology (20%)
- Memory: Encoding, storage, retrieval (Information Processing Model)
- Types of memory: Sensory, working/short-term, long-term (explicit vs implicit)
- Forgetting: Proactive vs retroactive interference, amnesia, constructed memory (Elizabeth Loftus)
- Thinking and Problem Solving: Algorithms vs heuristics, cognitive biases (confirmation bias, availability heuristic)
- Language: Phonemes, morphemes, syntax, semantics, and language acquisition theories (Chomsky)
- Intelligence: General intelligence ($g$), Gardner's multiple intelligences, Sternberg's triarchic theory, and the standardization of IQ tests (reliability vs validity)
- **Questions**: Explaining how the availability heuristic leads to poor decision making, evaluating the validity of an intelligence test

## Module 4: Developmental and Social Psychology (20%)
- Developmental Psychology: Piaget's cognitive stages, Erikson's psychosocial stages, Kohlberg's moral development
- Attachment theory (Ainsworth, Harlow's monkeys), parenting styles
- Motivation: Drive-reduction, arousal theory, Maslow's hierarchy
- Emotion: James-Lange, Cannon-Bard, Schachter-Singer two-factor theories
- Social Psychology: Attribution theory (Fundamental Attribution Error), attitudes, cognitive dissonance
- Conformity (Asch), Obedience (Milgram), group dynamics (bystander effect, group polarization, social loafing)
- **Questions**: Analyzing a social interaction using the Fundamental Attribution Error, explaining the mechanism of cognitive dissonance

## Module 5: Personality and Clinical Psychology (20%)
- Personality Theories: Psychoanalytic (Freud's id/ego/superego, defense mechanisms), Humanistic (Rogers, Maslow), Trait theory (The Big Five)
- Clinical Psychology: Defining psychological disorders (DSM-5)
- Neurodevelopmental, schizophrenia spectrum, bipolar, depressive, anxiety, obsessive-compulsive, and trauma-related disorders
- Treatment Modalities: Psychoanalysis, Client-centered therapy, Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT), and psychopharmacology
- **Questions**: Identifying the defense mechanism used in a specific scenario, differentiating between an obsession and a compulsion in OCD

## Stretch Zone
- Critical evaluation of the replication crisis in psychology
- Advanced statistical inference (interpreting p-values and effect sizes)
