# US History
**ID**: `usHistory` · **Discipline**: Humanities · **Year**: 10 (ages 14–15)
**Frameworks**: Standard US High School History

## Course Philosophy
A chronological and thematic survey of United States history from pre-Columbian societies to the modern era. The focus is on analyzing primary sources, understanding cause and effect, and evaluating the diverse perspectives that have shaped the American experience.

## Module 1: Early America & The Revolution (1491 - 1783) (20%)
- Pre-Columbian Native American societies and the impact of the Columbian Exchange
- Colonial regions (New England, Middle, Southern) and the development of transatlantic trade (including the trans-Atlantic slave trade)
- The French and Indian War and the end of Salutary Neglect
- The causes of the American Revolution (taxation without representation, Enlightenment ideals)
- The Declaration of Independence and the Revolutionary War
- **Questions**: Causes of the Revolution, differences between colonial regions, impact of the Columbian Exchange

## Module 2: The Constitution & The Early Republic (1783 - 1848) (20%)
- The Articles of Confederation (weaknesses) and the Constitutional Convention (compromises)
- The debate over ratification: Federalists vs Anti-Federalists
- George Washington's presidency and the formation of political parties (Hamilton vs Jefferson)
- The Market Revolution, the cotton gin, and the expansion of slavery
- Andrew Jackson, the Trail of Tears, and Manifest Destiny
- **Questions**: Constitutional compromises, Hamilton's financial plan, causes/effects of Manifest Destiny

## Module 3: Civil War & Reconstruction (1848 - 1877) (20%)
- The debate over slavery in the western territories (Compromise of 1850, Kansas-Nebraska Act, Dred Scott decision)
- The election of Abraham Lincoln and the secession of the South
- The Civil War: key battles, the Emancipation Proclamation, and the Gettysburg Address
- Reconstruction: the 13th, 14th, and 15th Amendments
- The failure of Reconstruction: Jim Crow laws, sharecropping, and the Ku Klux Klan
- **Questions**: Events leading to the Civil War, significance of the Reconstruction Amendments, reasons for Reconstruction's failure

## Module 4: Industrialization, Imperialism & World War I (1877 - 1920) (20%)
- The Gilded Age: the rise of monopolies (Carnegie, Rockefeller), labor unions, and urbanization
- The Progressive Era: political and social reforms (muckrakers, women's suffrage)
- American Imperialism: the Spanish-American War, the annexation of Hawaii, and the Panama Canal
- World War I: causes of US entry, the home front, and Woodrow Wilson's Fourteen Points
- **Questions**: Robber barons vs captains of industry, goals of the Progressive movement, reasons for US entry into WWI

## Module 5: Boom, Bust, WWII & The Cold War (1920 - Present) (20%)
- The Roaring Twenties (consumerism, Harlem Renaissance) and the causes of the Great Depression
- FDR and the New Deal (relief, recovery, reform)
- World War II: Pearl Harbor, the home front (Japanese internment), the atomic bomb
- The Cold War: containment (Truman Doctrine, Marshall Plan), the Korean War, the Vietnam War
- The Civil Rights Movement: MLK Jr., Malcolm X, the Civil Rights Act of 1964
- **Questions**: Causes of the Great Depression, goals of the New Deal, containment policy, key figures in the Civil Rights movement

## Stretch Zone
- Analyze the impact of the Supreme Court cases *Plessy v. Ferguson* and *Brown v. Board of Education*
- Evaluate the long-term impact of the Watergate scandal on American politics
