# NAPLAN Prep (Year 7)
**ID**: `naplanYear7` · **Discipline**: Exam Prep · **Year**: 7 (ages 11–12)
**Framework**: Australian NAPLAN

## Course Philosophy
Preparation for the Year 7 NAPLAN test. This course marks the transition to high school level expectations in Australia, with a significant jump in reading complexity, analytical writing, and algebraic thinking.

## Module 1: Reading (25%)
- Analyze complex literary and informational texts (including historical documents, persuasive essays, and poetry)
- Identify the author's tone, bias, and target audience
- Synthesize information across multiple texts
- Interpret complex figurative language and rhetorical devices
- **Questions**: Evaluating bias, synthesizing cross-text information, rhetorical analysis

## Module 2: Writing (25%)
- Persuasive: Constructing sophisticated arguments with clear thesis statements, logical progression, and anticipating/refuting counter-arguments
- Narrative: Advanced character development, creating tension/atmosphere, manipulating pacing and structure (e.g., flashbacks)
- High-level vocabulary and deliberate syntactic choices for rhetorical effect
- **Questions**: Evaluating argument structure, identifying counter-arguments, analyzing pacing/tension

## Module 3: Language Conventions (25%)
- Complex spelling rules, exceptions, and etymology (Greek/Latin roots)
- Advanced grammar: active vs passive voice, complex and compound-complex sentences, subjunctive mood
- Punctuation: colons, semicolons, em-dashes, and brackets for rhetorical effect
- **Questions**: Correcting passive voice, semicolon usage, complex root word spelling

## Module 4: Numeracy (25%)
- Number & Algebra: Operations with negative integers, rational numbers, index laws (powers), simplifying algebraic expressions, solving two-step linear equations
- Measurement & Geometry: Area of composite shapes, volume of prisms, angle properties of parallel lines and polygons, Cartesian plane (all 4 quadrants)
- Statistics & Probability: Calculating and comparing mean, median, mode; interpreting complex data representations (e.g., back-to-back stem-and-leaf plots); theoretical vs experimental probability
- **Questions**: Two-step equations, composite area, back-to-back stem-and-leaf interpretation, index laws

## Stretch Zone
- Reading: Deconstructing satire and irony
- Numeracy: Solving simple linear inequalities, introduction to Pythagoras' theorem
