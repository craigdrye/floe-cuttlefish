# NAPLAN Prep (Year 7)
**ID**: `naplanYear7` | **Discipline**: Exam Prep | **Year**: 7 (ages 11-12)
**Framework**: Australian NAPLAN

## Course Aim
Prepare Year 7 students for NAPLAN by building calm, flexible test readiness across reading, writing, language conventions, numeracy, and online assessment strategy. Students learn to diagnose what a question is really asking, use evidence and working clearly, recover from errors, and leave the course with stronger confidence for lower secondary literacy and numeracy.

## Course Design Notes
This course suits Australian students moving from primary into secondary expectations. Keep practice diagnostic and confidence-building: short skill checks, explicit error review, model answers, timed rehearsals, and reflection on next steps. Use ACARA NAPLAN-style reading, writing, conventions, and numeracy tasks, while connecting skills to the Australian Curriculum English and Mathematics expectations for Year 7. Digital-test habits should be taught as learning behaviours: reading the screen carefully, using scratch working, flagging uncertain items, checking units, and managing stamina.

## Chapter 1: NAPLAN Map, Mindset, and Digital Strategy
- **Key concepts**: test domains, online format, adaptive pathways, question stems, time awareness, flagging, scratch working, calculator and non-calculator choices, stamina, and error logs.
- **Practice questions**: What domain and skill is this item testing? Which details on the screen matter before answering? When should a student estimate, skip, flag, or check?
- **Student output**: a personal NAPLAN readiness dashboard with domain confidence ratings, digital-test routines, common error types, and a weekly practice plan.

## Chapter 2: Reading for Main Ideas and Evidence
- **Key concepts**: literal meaning, inference, main idea, supporting detail, summarising, evidence selection, context clues, text purpose, and answer elimination.
- **Practice questions**: Which option is directly supported by the text? What is the best summary of the passage? Which detail proves the inference rather than just sounding related?
- **Student output**: an annotated reading passage with a main-idea statement, three evidence notes, and one corrected multiple-choice explanation.

## Chapter 3: Reading Across Text Types
- **Key concepts**: narrative, poem, article, report, review, visual text, audience, purpose, tone, bias, figurative language, layout, and cross-text comparison.
- **Practice questions**: How does the author's tone shape the reader's response? What does the image, heading, or graph add to the written text? How do two texts treat the same issue differently?
- **Student output**: a reading comparison chart covering purpose, audience, language choices, evidence, and one likely NAPLAN-style question for each text.

## Chapter 4: Writing Planning and Text Structure
- **Key concepts**: persuasive purpose, narrative purpose, prompt unpacking, audience, thesis, orientation, complication, paragraphing, cohesion, planning under time, and conclusion.
- **Practice questions**: What does this writing prompt ask the student to create? Which plan has the strongest structure for the audience? Where would a new paragraph improve control?
- **Student output**: two timed writing plans, one persuasive and one narrative, with topic sentences, key evidence or events, and a revision target.

## Chapter 5: Writing Craft, Voice, and Revision
- **Key concepts**: vocabulary precision, sentence variety, cohesion, imagery, characterisation, tension, persuasive devices, modality, counterargument, editing, and proofreading.
- **Practice questions**: Which word choice best suits the audience and purpose? How can a sentence be varied for emphasis? Which revision makes the argument or narrative clearer?
- **Student output**: a revised writing sample showing changes to vocabulary, sentence control, paragraph flow, and one teacher or peer feedback response.

## Chapter 6: Language Conventions in Context
- **Key concepts**: spelling patterns, suffixes, prefixes, homophones, subject-verb agreement, tense consistency, pronoun reference, commas, apostrophes, quotation marks, colons, and semicolons.
- **Practice questions**: Which spelling follows the pattern and meaning? Which sentence is grammatically correct? Where should punctuation be added to clarify the idea?
- **Student output**: a conventions error log with corrected examples, rule reminders in student language, and three original sentences that apply the rules.

## Chapter 7: Numeracy - Number, Algebra, and Measurement
- **Key concepts**: integers, fractions, decimals, percentages, ratios, rates, order of operations, index notation, variables, expressions, simple equations, perimeter, area, volume, angles, and units.
- **Practice questions**: Solve a two-step equation; compare percentage change and ratio; calculate area of a composite shape; check whether a numerical answer is reasonable.
- **Student output**: a mixed numeracy workbook page with clear working, estimates, units, and a short reflection on one corrected mistake.

## Chapter 8: Numeracy - Data, Probability, and Mixed Problem Solving
- **Key concepts**: tables, column graphs, line graphs, stem-and-leaf plots, mean, median, range, outliers, likelihood, theoretical probability, experimental probability, diagrams, and multistep reasoning.
- **Practice questions**: Interpret a data display; compare two data sets; find a probability from a table; decide which information is needed for a multistep problem.
- **Student output**: a data and probability mini-report with one display, one claim supported by numbers, one probability calculation, and a note about uncertainty or limits.

## Capstone
Students complete a Year 7 NAPLAN readiness portfolio. The portfolio includes a reading response set, a persuasive or narrative writing task with revision notes, a language conventions error log, a mixed numeracy set, and a digital-test strategy reflection that names the student's strongest habits and next three targets.

## Assessment Ideas
- Short diagnostic checks across reading, writing, conventions, and numeracy before and after each cycle.
- Reading responses marked for evidence, inference, text-type awareness, and answer elimination reasoning.
- Writing tasks assessed with NAPLAN-style criteria: audience, text structure, ideas, persuasive devices or narrative craft, vocabulary, cohesion, paragraphing, sentence structure, punctuation, and spelling.
- Numeracy tasks marked for method, accuracy, representation, units, and reasonableness checks.
- Digital practice reflections focused on time use, flagging, rereading, scratch working, and recovery from uncertainty.
- Capstone graded on skill growth, correction quality, confidence-building habits, and readiness to explain next steps.
