# National Security Policy Roadmap
**ID**: `nationalSecurityPolicyRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Government - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Train learners to analyze national security problems with disciplined context, clear options, interagency awareness, and concise policy writing. The goal is not to predict every event, but to produce usable judgment under uncertainty.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when learners need a career path through strategic context, threat assessment, interagency process, intelligence-policy boundaries, instruments of power, crisis work, and policy memos. Keep outputs short, evidence-based, and decision-focused.

## Chapter 1: Strategy, Interests, and Threats
- **Key concepts**: national interests, threat, vulnerability, capability, intent, deterrence, escalation, risk appetite.
- **Practice questions**: What interest is at stake? What is known versus assumed? How severe is the risk?
- **Work output**: threat and interest brief with facts, assumptions, uncertainties, and policy relevance.

## Chapter 2: Intelligence and Evidence
- **Key concepts**: source confidence, analytic judgment, collection gap, warning indicator, alternative explanation, politicization risk.
- **Practice questions**: How strong is the evidence? What would change the assessment? What should policymakers not overread?
- **Work output**: intelligence-to-policy note separating evidence, judgment, confidence, and gaps.

## Chapter 3: Instruments of National Power
- **Key concepts**: diplomacy, information, military, economic tools, sanctions, security assistance, alliances, deterrence messaging.
- **Practice questions**: Which tool fits the objective? What second-order effect is likely? What partner or adversary reaction matters?
- **Work output**: options matrix comparing tools, benefits, risks, constraints, and likely reactions.

## Chapter 4: Interagency Process and Decision Making
- **Key concepts**: NSC process, departments and agencies, deputies and principals, clearance, dissent, implementation owner.
- **Practice questions**: Who must be at the table? What decision is actually needed? Which agency owns execution?
- **Work output**: interagency decision memo with stakeholders, decision point, options, and implementation owners.

## Chapter 5: Crisis and Policy Communication
- **Key concepts**: crisis rhythm, situation report, escalation ladder, public line, congressional interest, after-action review.
- **Practice questions**: What changed since the last update? What decision cannot wait? What message reduces confusion?
- **Work output**: crisis update with situation, implications, decisions needed, public line, and next review point.

## Capstone
Produce a national security policy package for a fictional crisis: threat and interest brief, intelligence-to-policy note, options matrix, interagency decision memo, and crisis update.
