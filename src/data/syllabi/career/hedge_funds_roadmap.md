# Hedge Funds Roadmap
**ID**: `hedgeFundsRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Finance - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Teach learners how hedge fund analysts form investment theses, size risk, track catalysts, and explain performance. The course emphasizes variant perception, downside discipline, and clear decision rules.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test long/short research, catalysts, portfolio construction, risk, short ideas, event-driven setups, performance attribution, or pitch writing. Keep the work evidence-led and skeptical.

## Chapter 1: Strategy, Mandate, and Edge
- **Key concepts**: long/short equity, event-driven, macro, credit, quant, gross exposure, net exposure, alpha, beta.
- **Practice questions**: What is the strategy allowed to do? Where could edge come from? What risk is just market exposure?
- **Work output**: strategy brief with mandate, edge source, risk limits, and example trades.

## Chapter 2: Thesis Formation and Variant Perception
- **Key concepts**: consensus, variant view, catalyst, estimate revision, channel checks, positioning, time horizon.
- **Practice questions**: What does the market believe? What evidence says the market is wrong? When should the thesis work?
- **Work output**: investment thesis note with consensus view, variant view, evidence, and catalyst path.

## Chapter 3: Modeling, Valuation, and Scenario Work
- **Key concepts**: driver model, upside case, downside case, probability weighting, valuation gap, expected value.
- **Practice questions**: Which assumption matters most? What is priced in? What is the loss if the thesis is wrong?
- **Work output**: scenario model with expected value, key sensitivities, and decision triggers.

## Chapter 4: Shorts, Crowding, and Risk Control
- **Key concepts**: short borrow, squeeze, crowded trade, stop-loss, liquidity, factor exposure, pair trade.
- **Practice questions**: Can the short be expressed safely? What could force a cover? Is the trade too crowded?
- **Work output**: risk memo covering sizing, borrow, liquidity, factor exposure, and exit rules.

## Chapter 5: Portfolio Review and Performance Attribution
- **Key concepts**: position sizing, drawdown, hit rate, batting average, contribution, attribution, risk review.
- **Practice questions**: What made or lost money? Did the thesis change? Should capital be added, cut, or held?
- **Work output**: portfolio update with P&L drivers, thesis status, risk changes, and action recommendation.

## Capstone
Build a hedge fund pitch packet: strategy fit, thesis note, scenario model, risk memo, catalyst calendar, and portfolio review recommendation.
