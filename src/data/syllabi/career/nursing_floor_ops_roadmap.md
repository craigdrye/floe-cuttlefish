# Nursing Floor Ops Roadmap
**ID**: `nursingFloorOpsRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Healthcare - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Teach learners how nursing units function as real-time operating systems for patient care. Learners practice translating staffing, acuity, admissions, discharges, supplies, safety events, and communication gaps into clear floor-level action.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test unit workflow, charge nurse judgment, staffing pressure, patient flow, handoff quality, escalation, or safety routines. The tone should stay practical and respectful because floor operations depend on fast decisions and trust.

## Chapter 1: Unit Map and Daily Rhythm
- **Key concepts**: census, acuity, assignment, charge nurse, rounds, huddle, handoff, escalation path.
- **Practice questions**: What must happen this shift? Who needs to know? Which patient needs closer attention?
- **Work output**: shift map with census, assignments, risks, and communication checkpoints.

## Chapter 2: Staffing, Acuity, and Workload
- **Key concepts**: nurse-to-patient ratio, acuity, skill mix, float pool, break coverage, sitter need, overtime risk.
- **Practice questions**: Is staffing safe for this acuity? What can be redistributed? What must be escalated?
- **Work output**: staffing adjustment note with acuity rationale and coverage plan.

## Chapter 3: Admissions, Transfers, and Discharges
- **Key concepts**: bed readiness, admission packet, transfer report, discharge barrier, transport, medication reconciliation.
- **Practice questions**: What blocks the bed? What information is missing? Which discharge is time-sensitive?
- **Work output**: patient-flow board with blockers, owners, and expected completion times.

## Chapter 4: Safety and Quality on the Floor
- **Key concepts**: fall risk, pressure injury, infection prevention, medication safety, rapid response, incident reporting.
- **Practice questions**: What risk is rising? What immediate action protects the patient? What documentation is needed?
- **Work output**: floor safety review with priority risks, interventions, and follow-up checks.

## Chapter 5: Handoffs, Families, and Interdisciplinary Coordination
- **Key concepts**: SBAR, provider page, care conference, family update, social work, therapy, case management.
- **Practice questions**: What is the ask? What facts belong in the handoff? Who can remove the barrier?
- **Work output**: SBAR packet for a complex patient with clinical, family, and discharge issues separated.

## Capstone
Run a simulated twelve-hour unit shift. Build the assignment, update the flow board, respond to a staffing change, escalate a safety concern, and prepare an end-of-shift handoff.
