# Physician Practice Roadmap
**ID**: `physicianPracticeRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Healthcare - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners to understand the operating model of outpatient physician practices: access, scheduling, front desk flow, clinical support, revenue cycle, patient experience, staffing, and performance management.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test clinic workflow, appointment access, provider templates, referral management, documentation gaps, patient complaints, billing handoffs, or practice-manager judgment. Keep scenarios close to the day-to-day work of keeping a clinic useful and solvent.

## Chapter 1: Practice Model and Patient Access
- **Key concepts**: specialty, panel, new patient, established patient, template, access, no-show, wait time.
- **Practice questions**: Can the patient get care in time? Which visit type fits? What schedule rule creates friction?
- **Work output**: access dashboard with demand, capacity, no-shows, and next available appointment.

## Chapter 2: Front Desk and Visit Flow
- **Key concepts**: registration, eligibility, referral, copay, rooming, checkout, follow-up, patient portal.
- **Practice questions**: What must be verified before the visit? Where is the handoff failing? What should be fixed today?
- **Work output**: visit-flow checklist from scheduling through checkout.

## Chapter 3: Clinical Support and Provider Productivity
- **Key concepts**: medical assistant, nurse triage, inbox, prior authorization, documentation time, RVU, panel management.
- **Practice questions**: Which work requires a clinician? What can be protocol-driven? What slows the provider?
- **Work output**: clinic workload map with task owner, bottleneck, and improvement idea.

## Chapter 4: Revenue Cycle and Documentation
- **Key concepts**: charge capture, coding handoff, claim, denial, medical necessity, superbill, payer rule.
- **Practice questions**: Is the service documented? What will delay payment? Which denial could be prevented?
- **Work output**: revenue-cycle handoff note with documentation, coding, and follow-up risks.

## Chapter 5: Service, Staffing, and Practice Improvement
- **Key concepts**: patient complaint, service recovery, staff coverage, standard work, metric review, change adoption.
- **Practice questions**: What is the real complaint? Which metric changed? What small process change is testable?
- **Work output**: practice improvement plan with issue, baseline, action, owner, and measure.

## Capstone
Build a physician practice manager packet for a busy clinic month: access review, visit-flow map, workload analysis, revenue-cycle risk list, and service-improvement plan.
