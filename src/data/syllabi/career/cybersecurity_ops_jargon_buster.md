# Cybersecurity Ops Jargon Buster
**ID**: `cybersecOpsJargon` - **Discipline**: Technology - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Teach learners to translate SOC and incident-response language into concrete security actions. The course covers alerts, IOCs, blast radius, containment, privilege escalation, cloud identity, false positives, executive updates, and the difference between a scary phrase and a real incident task.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test cybersecurity operations shorthand, incident triage, alert investigation, containment, evidence gathering, cloud access, executive communication, or escalation. Keep the learner focused on what to check, who to notify, and what action reduces risk.

## Chapter 1: Alerts, Signals, and Triage
- **Key concepts**: alert, event, incident, severity, confidence, IOC, telemetry, false positive, enrichment.
- **Practice questions**: What happened? How confident are we? What evidence raises or lowers severity?
- **Work output**: triage note with signal, scope, confidence, and next step.

## Chapter 2: Identity and Access Language
- **Key concepts**: credential, token, MFA, privilege escalation, service account, impossible travel, session revocation.
- **Practice questions**: Which access path is exposed? What must be disabled, rotated, or reviewed?
- **Work output**: account-compromise response checklist.

## Chapter 3: Endpoint, Network, and Cloud Clues
- **Key concepts**: EDR, C2, lateral movement, persistence, exfiltration, bucket exposure, security group, workload identity.
- **Practice questions**: Is the host compromised? Is data exposed? What scope must be checked?
- **Work output**: investigation map across host, identity, network, and cloud.

## Chapter 4: Containment and Recovery
- **Key concepts**: isolate, block, revoke, rotate, patch, eradicate, restore, monitor, lessons learned.
- **Practice questions**: What action reduces harm fastest? What evidence must be preserved? What could break production?
- **Work output**: containment plan with owner, risk, and rollback path.

## Chapter 5: Communication and Escalation
- **Key concepts**: incident commander, severity bridge, executive update, customer impact, legal notification, post-incident review.
- **Practice questions**: Who needs to know? What is confirmed? What is still unknown?
- **Work output**: executive update with facts, impact, actions, and decisions needed.

## Capstone
Translate a noisy SOC handoff into a prioritized incident plan: evidence to gather, containment steps, owners, communications, and recovery checks.
