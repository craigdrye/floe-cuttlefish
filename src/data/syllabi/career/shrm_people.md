# SHRM: People Ops Maze
**ID**: `shrmPeople` · **Discipline**: Leadership / Human Resources · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners for SHRM-style HR judgment while building genuinely useful people-operations instincts. The course treats HR as a business, legal, ethical, and human system: people are not flowcharts, but the paperwork still matters.

## Course Design Notes
The original syllabus had the right practical tone, but it needed closer alignment to SHRM's Body of Applied Skills and Knowledge: behavioral competencies plus the People, Organization, and Workplace knowledge domains. This rewrite adds scenario judgment, compliance hygiene, analytics, strategy, and exam survival without turning the course into a binder-shaped nap.

## Chapter 1: SHRM Decision Logic and HR's Role
**Key concepts**: SHRM-CP, SHRM-SCP, behavioral competencies, knowledge domains, best answer, business alignment, ethics, evidence-based HR, stakeholder balancing.

**Practical questions**
- What answer best supports the organization while remaining lawful, fair, and humane?
- Is this an operational HR problem, a strategic workforce issue, or a leadership judgment test?
- What facts are missing before HR should act?

**Workplace skill**
- Build a scenario triage note: issue, risk, affected people, policy basis, legal flag, business impact, and next action.

## Chapter 2: People Domain: Talent, Total Rewards, and Employee Relations
**Key concepts**: job analysis, workforce planning, recruiting, selection, onboarding, performance management, discipline, grievances, investigations, retention, compensation, benefits, recognition.

**Practical questions**
- Does the selection process measure actual job requirements?
- Is discipline consistent, documented, proportionate, and policy-aligned?
- What reward or retention lever fits the business problem rather than merely sounding generous?

**Workplace skill**
- Draft a role scorecard and interview plan tied to job analysis, competencies, legal defensibility, and candidate experience.

## Chapter 3: Organization Domain: Strategy, Structure, and Change
**Key concepts**: organizational effectiveness, HR strategy, business acumen, change management, culture, engagement, leadership development, succession, learning transfer, performance metrics.

**Practical questions**
- What people risk could block the strategy?
- Which change barrier is structural, cultural, managerial, or communication-based?
- How will we know training or engagement work actually changed behavior?

**Workplace skill**
- Create a change-readiness brief with stakeholders, risks, communication plan, learning supports, and success measures.

## Chapter 4: Workplace Domain: Compliance, Risk, Safety, and Inclusion
**Key concepts**: employment law, protected classes, discrimination, harassment, retaliation, accommodation, adverse impact, wage and hour, leave, privacy, safety, recordkeeping, global HR, inclusion.

**Practical questions**
- Is the issue a policy matter, legal risk, employee relations risk, or urgent safety concern?
- What documentation is needed before acting?
- Does a practice look neutral but create disparate impact or access barriers?

**Workplace skill**
- Build a compliance checklist for a hiring, leave, accommodation, discipline, or termination scenario.

## Chapter 5: Behavioral Competencies in Practice
**Key concepts**: leadership, ethical practice, business acumen, consultation, communication, relationship management, analytical aptitude, global mindset, diversity/equity/inclusion.

**Practical questions**
- Which response de-escalates while preserving accountability?
- When should HR advise, investigate, coach, escalate, or stop the process?
- What answer shows partnership without becoming management's rubber stamp?

**Workplace skill**
- Write two versions of the same HR response: one technically correct but clumsy, and one that is correct, humane, clear, and business-aware.

## Chapter 6: Data, Metrics, and Evidence-Based HR
**Key concepts**: turnover, retention, time-to-fill, quality of hire, engagement, adverse impact ratio, absenteeism, training evaluation, leading and lagging indicators, HR dashboards.

**Practical questions**
- Which metric actually answers the business question?
- What data would distinguish a manager problem from a compensation problem?
- Where might a metric incentivize bad behavior?

**Workplace skill**
- Build a small HR dashboard with interpretation notes, limitations, and recommended actions.

## Chapter 7: Employee Relations Lab
**Key concepts**: investigations, documentation, coaching, progressive discipline, grievances, conflict resolution, psychological safety, confidentiality, retaliation prevention, just cause.

**Scenario work**
- Harassment complaint with unclear witnesses.
- Manager wants immediate termination with weak documentation.
- Employee requests accommodation after performance warnings.
- Team conflict is being mislabeled as "culture fit."

**Workplace skill**
- Draft an investigation plan and closeout memo that separates facts, findings, policy, credibility, and recommended action.

## Chapter 8: Exam Survival and Scenario Judgment
**Key concepts**: situational judgment items, one-best-answer logic, proportional response, escalation order, stakeholder impact, legal risk, policy alignment, SHRM lens.

**Study habits**
- Read the stem for role, authority, urgency, and missing facts.
- Avoid extremes: ignore it, fire everyone, promise everything, or act without facts.
- Prefer responses that gather relevant information, follow policy, protect people, align to strategy, and escalate when risk is serious.

**Deliverable**
- Personal study map: weak domains, scenario traps, review cadence, practice-question log, and retake prevention plan.

## Capstone: People Ops Maze Simulation
Learners receive a messy HR file involving attrition, manager complaints, a possible accommodation, engagement data, compensation pressure, and a leadership request for "quick action." Final deliverables include:
- issue triage memo
- stakeholder map
- investigation or fact-finding plan
- compliance risk checklist
- business-aligned recommendation
- communication plan for manager and employee audiences

## Assessment Ideas
- Scenario triage notes
- Role scorecard and interview plan
- Change-readiness brief
- Compliance checklist
- HR dashboard interpretation
- Employee relations closeout memo
- Capstone simulation
