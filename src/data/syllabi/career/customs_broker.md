# Customs Broker License
**ID**: `customsBroker` · **Discipline**: Operations · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners for customs-broker reasoning: classification, valuation, origin, entry procedures, broker responsibility, recordkeeping, and exam-style navigation of official references.

## Course Design Notes
CBP's Customs Broker License Exam materials emphasize customs laws, regulations, procedures, accounting, and matters needed to serve importers and exporters. This syllabus follows the CBLE logic while adding practical importer communication, issue spotting, and reference-navigation drills so learners can work from the rules instead of memorizing isolated trivia.

## Chapter 1: Broker Role, Exam Format, and Reference Navigation
- **Key concepts**: broker responsibilities, importer of record, CBP, 19 CFR, HTSUS, open-book strategy, time management.
- **Practice questions**: Which reference controls this question? What term should be searched first? What is the broker's duty here?
- **Work product**: a reference map for 19 CFR, HTSUS, CBP forms, rulings, and exam-day tabs.

## Chapter 2: Classification With the HTSUS
- **Key concepts**: General Rules of Interpretation, section notes, chapter notes, headings, subheadings, essential character.
- **Practice questions**: Which note controls? What competing headings are plausible? What fact would change the classification?
- **Work product**: a classification worksheet with GRI steps and evidence.

## Chapter 3: Valuation
- **Key concepts**: transaction value, assists, packing, commissions, royalties, related parties, dutiable and non-dutiable charges.
- **Practice questions**: What is included in customs value? Is the buyer-seller relationship acceptable? Which invoice detail matters?
- **Work product**: a valuation calculation with cited basis and exclusions.

## Chapter 4: Country of Origin, Marking, and Trade Programs
- **Key concepts**: substantial transformation, marking, preference programs, USMCA basics, rules of origin, documentation.
- **Practice questions**: What is the origin? Does the product qualify for preference? What marking is required?
- **Work product**: an origin memo with facts, rule applied, and documentation needed.

## Chapter 5: Entry, Release, and Entry Summary
- **Key concepts**: entry types, entry summary, bonds, liquidation, deadlines, cargo release, ACE, invoices.
- **Practice questions**: Which entry type applies? What deadline matters? What document is missing?
- **Work product**: an entry checklist for a standard import scenario.

## Chapter 6: Duties, Fees, AD/CVD, and Special Situations
- **Key concepts**: duty calculation, MPF, HMF, antidumping, countervailing duties, quotas, exclusions, drawback, FTZs.
- **Practice questions**: What duties and fees apply? Is AD/CVD in scope? What special program changes the calculation?
- **Work product**: a landed-cost and duty exposure worksheet.

## Chapter 7: Recordkeeping, Corrections, and Compliance
- **Key concepts**: record retention, post-summary correction, prior disclosure, penalties, audits, reasonable care.
- **Practice questions**: What record proves the claim? When should a correction be filed? What creates penalty risk?
- **Work product**: a compliance file checklist and correction decision tree.

## Chapter 8: Practice Exams and Broker Judgment
- **Key concepts**: question triage, reference search, answer elimination, appeal logic, client communication, ethics.
- **Practice questions**: Which questions are fastest? Which are traps? How would you explain the issue to an importer?
- **Work product**: a post-practice-exam error log sorted by reference, concept, and mistake type.

## Capstone
Prepare a customs-entry advisory packet for a fictional importer: classification, valuation, origin, duties/fees, documentation, broker responsibilities, and unresolved questions requiring client or CBP clarification.

## Assessment Ideas
- HTSUS drills graded on citation and reasoning.
- Valuation and duty calculations graded on inclusions, exclusions, and units.
- Capstone graded on reference use, compliance judgment, and importer-facing clarity.
