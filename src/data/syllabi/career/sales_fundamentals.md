# Sales Fundamentals
**ID**: `salesFundamentals` · **Discipline**: Commercial · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Build disciplined, ethical sales ability: finding fit, asking useful questions, understanding buying processes, framing value, handling objections, and managing pipeline without turning the buyer into a spreadsheet entry.

## Course Design Notes
Sales training from HubSpot, Salesforce Trailhead, Coursera, and MEDDIC-style qualification frameworks emphasizes discovery, qualification, value, stakeholder mapping, objection handling, and forecast discipline. This syllabus adds buyer empathy, honest disqualification, and post-loss learning so learners do not mistake pressure for progress.

## Chapter 1: What Selling Is For
- **Key concepts**: buyer problem, fit, trust, consultative selling, commercial ethics, customer outcomes.
- **Practice questions**: What problem does this product solve? Who should not buy it? What would a trustworthy sales motion feel like?
- **Work product**: a buyer-fit statement that names ideal customer, poor-fit customer, pain, value, and proof.

## Chapter 2: Prospecting and First Contact
- **Key concepts**: target accounts, triggers, personalization, referral paths, sequences, relevance, permission.
- **Practice questions**: Why this buyer, why now, why this message? What signal suggests they might care?
- **Work product**: three outreach messages tied to specific buyer triggers.

## Chapter 3: Discovery
- **Key concepts**: open questions, pain, impact, current process, urgency, decision criteria, active listening.
- **Practice questions**: What is the buyer trying to change? What happens if they do nothing? What is still unclear?
- **Work product**: a discovery call map with question bank, expected signals, and follow-up prompts.

## Chapter 4: Qualification and Stakeholder Mapping
- **Key concepts**: budget, authority, need, timeline, champions, blockers, economic buyer, procurement.
- **Practice questions**: Is there a real buying process? Who can say yes? Who can quietly say no?
- **Work product**: a qualified opportunity brief with stakeholders, decision path, risks, and next step.

## Chapter 5: Value, Proof, and ROI
- **Key concepts**: value hypothesis, business case, ROI, social proof, differentiation, risk reduction.
- **Practice questions**: What outcome matters financially or operationally? What evidence makes the claim credible?
- **Work product**: a value narrative that links pain, impact, solution, proof, and expected result.

## Chapter 6: Objections and Negotiation
- **Key concepts**: price concerns, timing, authority, competition, status quo, reframing, tradeable concessions.
- **Practice questions**: Is this a real blocker or a request for confidence? What question should come before the answer?
- **Work product**: an objection playbook with listen, clarify, respond, confirm, and next-step moves.

## Chapter 7: Pipeline and Forecast Hygiene
- **Key concepts**: stages, exit criteria, mutual action plans, next steps, close probability, slippage, post-call notes.
- **Practice questions**: What evidence supports this stage? What must happen before the deal can advance?
- **Work product**: a pipeline review template that separates optimism from evidence.

## Chapter 8: Learning From Wins and Losses
- **Key concepts**: win/loss review, churn signals, expansion, handoff, customer success, continuous improvement.
- **Practice questions**: Why did the buyer choose? What did we learn about fit? What should change in the next cycle?
- **Work product**: a win/loss review with product, buyer, process, and message lessons.

## Capstone
Run a simulated sales cycle from prospecting through discovery, qualification, value narrative, objection handling, and forecast review. Submit call notes, opportunity brief, value story, objection response, and next-step plan.

## Assessment Ideas
- Discovery graded on question quality, listening, and problem diagnosis.
- Opportunity brief graded on qualification evidence and stakeholder clarity.
- Final simulation graded on trust, value framing, commercial judgment, and next-step discipline.
