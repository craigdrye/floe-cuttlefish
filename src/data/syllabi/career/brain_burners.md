# Brain Burners
**ID**: `brainBurners` · **Discipline**: Logic / Puzzles · **Stage**: Career Hopper / Adult Enrichment

## Course Aim
Give adults a puzzle-first reasoning lane: clean, satisfying thinking under constraint, without wrapping every challenge in finance, school exams, or job titles. The course teaches reusable moves: invariants, contradiction, decomposition, information control, counting, and explanation.

## Course Design Notes
Brain Burners should feel playful but not childish. It belongs beside maths, philosophy, and interview prep: rigorous enough to transfer, light enough to make learners want one more problem.

## Chapter 1: Puzzle Posture
- **Key concepts**: constraints, knowns and unknowns, reframing, small cases, assumption checks.
- **Practice questions**: What is fixed? What can vary? What is the smallest version of the puzzle?
- **Work product**: a puzzle-start checklist for turning a wordy setup into a solvable model.

## Chapter 2: Elimination and Contradiction
- **Key concepts**: proof by contradiction, impossibility, exhaustive cases, truth tables, lie/truth constraints.
- **Practice questions**: What would have to be true? Which option contradicts a clue? Have all cases been covered?
- **Work product**: three solved puzzles with elimination grids or contradiction chains.

## Chapter 3: Counting Traps
- **Key concepts**: overcounting, undercounting, symmetry, complements, arrangements, hidden duplicates.
- **Practice questions**: What object is being counted? Is order relevant? Which cases are being counted twice?
- **Work product**: a counting-trap gallery with the mistake and the repair.

## Chapter 4: Invariants and Parity
- **Key concepts**: parity, coloring arguments, conservation, state changes, monovariants, impossibility proofs.
- **Practice questions**: What never changes? What always increases or decreases? Which state can never be reached?
- **Work product**: five invariant arguments explained in one paragraph each.

## Chapter 5: Measurement, Weighing, and Search
- **Key concepts**: balance puzzles, binary search, ternary search, information gain, adaptive strategies.
- **Practice questions**: What does each test reveal? How many states remain after each result? Which first move preserves options?
- **Work product**: a decision tree for a weighing or search puzzle.

## Chapter 6: Movement, Maps, and State Machines
- **Key concepts**: river crossings, graph states, legal moves, reversible moves, bottlenecks, shortest paths.
- **Practice questions**: What is a state? Which moves are legal? What prevents progress?
- **Work product**: a state graph for a transport, crossing, or movement puzzle.

## Chapter 7: Language, Misdirection, and Information Traps
- **Key concepts**: ambiguous wording, hidden assumptions, irrelevant details, conditional statements, common-sense traps.
- **Practice questions**: What did the prompt actually say? Which assumption did you add? What detail is bait?
- **Work product**: a rewrite of three trick prompts into precise mathematical or logical language.

## Chapter 8: Puzzle Studio
- **Key concepts**: puzzle construction, difficulty tuning, hint design, solution clarity, transfer principle.
- **Practice questions**: What is the intended insight? Which wrong path is tempting? What hint preserves the joy of solving?
- **Work product**: an original puzzle with answer, hint ladder, and explanation of the reusable reasoning move.

## Capstone
Build a mini puzzle set of eight problems: two contradiction, two counting, two invariant/parity, one search strategy, and one original hybrid puzzle. Each solution must name the general technique, not just the answer.

## Assessment Ideas
- Solution writeups graded on clarity, generality, and proof quality.
- Puzzle-construction tasks graded on fairness, elegance, and hint design.
- Capstone graded on variety, difficulty progression, and transfer value.
