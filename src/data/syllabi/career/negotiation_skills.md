# Negotiation Skills
**ID**: `negotiation` · **Discipline**: Communication · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Teach negotiation as structured problem-solving under tension. Learners build the ability to prepare, create value, claim value, manage relationships, handle conflict, and close durable agreements in compensation, vendor, client, workplace, and multi-party settings.

## Course Design Notes
The original syllabus had the right building blocks: interests, BATNA, ZOPA, anchoring, conversation control, tradeoffs, and closing. This rewrite adds value creation/claiming, simulation, ethics, difficult tactics, multi-party dynamics, implementation, and a capstone negotiation playbook.

## Chapter 1: Negotiation Fundamentals
**Key concepts**: negotiation, interests, positions, BATNA, reservation point, target, ZOPA, aspiration, leverage, relationship, process.

**Practical questions**
- What happens if no deal is reached?
- What do we want, why do we want it, and what might they want?
- Is there a real zone of possible agreement?

**Workplace skill**
- Build a negotiation prep sheet with goals, BATNA, reservation point, target, interests, counterpart map, and opening plan.

## Chapter 2: Creating Value Before Claiming It
**Key concepts**: integrative negotiation, value creation, issue expansion, logrolling, packages, contingent agreements, objective criteria, information exchange.

**Practical questions**
- What issues besides price might matter?
- Which trade costs me little but matters to them?
- What information can be shared safely to expand the pie?

**Workplace skill**
- Design three multi-issue packages that trade across price, timing, risk, scope, quality, support, or relationship value.

## Chapter 3: Claiming Value and Bargaining Tactics
**Key concepts**: distributive bargaining, anchoring, concessions, framing, first offer, midpoint pull, silence, deadlines, walkaway, counteroffers.

**Practical questions**
- Should we anchor, invite the first offer, or gather more information?
- What concession pattern protects value?
- When is firmness useful, and when is it just theater?

**Workplace skill**
- Run a timed single-issue negotiation and analyze opening, concession pattern, final result, and missed value.

## Chapter 4: Communication, Emotion, and Trust
**Key concepts**: active listening, diagnostic questions, paraphrase, tone, face-saving, emotion, trust, credibility, psychological safety, cultural context.

**Practical questions**
- What question would reveal the constraint behind the position?
- How do we lower heat without surrendering substance?
- What trust signal is credible rather than performative?

**Workplace skill**
- Practice a difficult conversation script with inquiry, summary, reframing, and conditional proposal.

## Chapter 5: Power, Ethics, and Difficult Tactics
**Key concepts**: deception, bluffing, threats, ultimatums, power imbalance, bad faith, confidentiality, fairness, reputation, escalation.

**Practical questions**
- Which tactics are hard bargaining, and which are unethical or relationship-damaging?
- How should we respond to a threat, lie, or take-it-or-leave-it move?
- When should negotiation stop?

**Workplace skill**
- Create a response plan for five difficult tactics: extreme anchor, false deadline, missing authority, personal attack, and hidden constraint.

## Chapter 6: Internal and Multi-Party Negotiation
**Key concepts**: coalition, internal alignment, principal-agent problem, mandate, stakeholder, sequencing, caucus, consensus, implementation risk.

**Practical questions**
- Are we negotiating externally before aligning internally?
- Who can veto implementation after the deal?
- Which coalition changes the bargaining range?

**Workplace skill**
- Build an internal stakeholder map and mandate memo before an external negotiation.

## Chapter 7: Closing, Documentation, and Implementation
**Key concepts**: term sheet, durable agreement, ambiguity, contingency, follow-up, handoff, relationship repair, post-negotiation review.

**Practical questions**
- What exactly did we agree to, and who will do what by when?
- Which ambiguity will become the next conflict?
- How will the relationship function after the signature?

**Workplace skill**
- Draft a plain-language agreement summary with obligations, conditions, dates, owners, open issues, and escalation path.

## Chapter 8: Negotiation Labs
**Simulation set**
- salary negotiation
- vendor renewal
- cross-functional prioritization
- customer discount request
- manager/employee workload conflict
- multi-party partnership

**Reflection discipline**
- What did I prepare?
- What did I learn?
- Where did I claim value?
- Where did I create value?
- What would I do differently?

## Capstone: Negotiation Playbook
Learners assemble:
- prep template
- BATNA/ZOPA analysis
- counterpart map
- issue package plan
- communication script
- difficult tactic response plan
- internal alignment memo
- agreement summary
- post-negotiation retrospective

## Assessment Ideas
- Negotiation prep sheet
- Package design exercise
- Single-issue bargaining simulation
- Difficult conversation roleplay
- Tactic response memo
- Multi-party negotiation simulation
- Agreement summary
- Negotiation Playbook capstone
