# Health Insurance Payers Roadmap
**ID**: `healthInsurancePayersRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Healthcare - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Teach learners how health insurance payers make operational decisions across benefits, networks, claims, utilization management, risk, quality, member service, and regulatory obligations. The goal is to understand the payer system well enough to analyze problems and explain tradeoffs clearly.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test payer operations, claims language, prior authorization, network design, Medicare or Medicaid plan basics, value-based care, risk adjustment, appeals, or member impact. Keep the course concrete: every concept should connect to a decision, workflow, or communication.

## Chapter 1: Payer Ecosystem and Plan Design
- **Key concepts**: commercial plan, Medicare Advantage, Medicaid managed care, self-funded employer, premium, deductible, copay, coinsurance.
- **Practice questions**: Who bears the risk? What does the benefit cover? Which member cost share applies?
- **Work output**: plan-design brief showing population, funding model, benefits, cost sharing, and operational implications.

## Chapter 2: Claims, Payment, and Provider Networks
- **Key concepts**: claim, eligibility, allowed amount, contracted rate, network, credentialing, fee schedule, capitation.
- **Practice questions**: Is the provider in network? Was the claim paid correctly? Which contract term drives the payment?
- **Work output**: claims-payment walkthrough with eligibility, network status, coding, contract logic, and disposition.

## Chapter 3: Utilization Management and Medical Policy
- **Key concepts**: prior authorization, medical necessity, concurrent review, step therapy, clinical criteria, denial, appeal.
- **Practice questions**: Does the request meet policy? What evidence is missing? What member risk comes from delay?
- **Work output**: utilization-review note with criteria, documentation, decision, and appeal-ready rationale.

## Chapter 4: Risk, Quality, and Value-Based Care
- **Key concepts**: risk adjustment, HEDIS, Star Ratings, value-based contract, shared savings, care gap, population health.
- **Practice questions**: Which coding or quality gap matters? What behavior should the contract encourage? How would improvement be measured?
- **Work output**: payer performance dashboard sketch with risk, quality, cost, access, and member experience signals.

## Chapter 5: Member Experience, Compliance, and Operations
- **Key concepts**: grievance, appeal, explanation of benefits, call center, access standard, regulatory filing, audit readiness.
- **Practice questions**: What does the member need now? Which deadline applies? What documentation proves fair handling?
- **Work output**: member-issue escalation summary with timeline, decision rights, owner, and communication plan.

## Capstone
Analyze a fictional payer operations case: plan design, claim payment, utilization review, quality and risk signals, member escalation, and executive summary with tradeoffs.
