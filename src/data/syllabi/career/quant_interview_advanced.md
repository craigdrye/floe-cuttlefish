# Quant Interview Advanced
**ID**: `quantAdvanced` · **Discipline**: Finance · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare advanced quant candidates for desk-facing interviews involving derivatives, fixed income, stochastic models, numerical methods, risk, calibration, and model judgment.

## Course Design Notes
Advanced quant interviews often blend financial engineering coursework, derivatives intuition, stochastic processes, numerical methods, and practical risk conversations. This syllabus draws from MIT finance-math materials, Baruch MFE preparation, CFA derivatives topics, and common quant desk expectations while keeping the emphasis on interview explanation rather than textbook completeness.

## Chapter 1: Advanced Interview Map
- **Key concepts**: quant researcher, quant trader, quant developer, model validation, desk quant, interview loops, whiteboard communication.
- **Practice questions**: Is this a pricing, risk, math, coding, or judgment question? What level of rigor is appropriate in an interview?
- **Work product**: a role-specific preparation map with strengths, gaps, and drill priorities.

## Chapter 2: Derivatives and No-Arbitrage Intuition
- **Key concepts**: forwards, futures, swaps, options, put-call parity, replication, arbitrage bounds, payoff diagrams.
- **Practice questions**: What portfolio replicates the payoff? What arbitrage appears if the price is wrong? Which assumption matters?
- **Work product**: a derivatives payoff and replication notebook.

## Chapter 3: Options, Greeks, and Volatility
- **Key concepts**: delta, gamma, vega, theta, implied volatility, volatility skew, hedging error, local versus stochastic volatility intuition.
- **Practice questions**: What happens to the hedge after a large move? Why does skew exist? Which Greek matters for this risk?
- **Work product**: an options-risk memo explaining a scenario in trader language.

## Chapter 4: Fixed Income and Rates
- **Key concepts**: yield curves, discount factors, duration, convexity, forwards, swaps, curve construction, basis risk.
- **Practice questions**: How does the bond respond to a curve move? What is the par swap rate? Which curve assumption is hidden?
- **Work product**: a rates worksheet with duration, convexity, and swap-rate calculations.

## Chapter 5: Stochastic Processes and Ito Intuition
- **Key concepts**: random walks, Brownian motion, Ito's lemma, martingales, change of measure, geometric Brownian motion.
- **Practice questions**: What process is being assumed? What term appears because of Ito's lemma? What does risk-neutral mean here?
- **Work product**: a derivation sheet for a simple stochastic model with plain-English interpretation.

## Chapter 6: Numerical Methods and Simulation
- **Key concepts**: Monte Carlo, variance reduction, finite differences, discretization error, convergence, stability, calibration.
- **Practice questions**: Is the simulation biased? What controls variance? What numerical instability could corrupt the result?
- **Work product**: a simulation design note with algorithm, assumptions, checks, and failure modes.

## Chapter 7: Risk, Model Risk, and XVA Vocabulary
- **Key concepts**: VaR, expected shortfall, stress testing, scenario analysis, counterparty risk, CVA, FVA, model validation.
- **Practice questions**: What risk measure answers the question? What scenario breaks the model? Which assumption would validation challenge?
- **Work product**: a model-risk review note for a pricing or risk model.

## Chapter 8: Advanced Mock Desk Interview
- **Key concepts**: multi-topic prompts, hint handling, precision versus speed, trader communication, production constraints.
- **Practice questions**: What result matters to the desk? Can the model be implemented reliably? How would you explain the tradeoff to a non-specialist?
- **Work product**: a full mock-interview debrief with technical misses, communication misses, and next drills.

## Capstone
Complete an advanced quant interview simulation: one derivatives replication problem, one rates calculation, one stochastic-process explanation, one numerical-methods design prompt, and one model-risk discussion.

## Assessment Ideas
- Derivatives and rates drills graded on financial intuition and calculation control.
- Stochastic and numerical prompts graded on assumptions and explanation.
- Capstone graded on integrated desk judgment, rigor, and communication.
