# Quant Interview Advanced
**ID**: `quantAdvanced` · **Discipline**: Finance

## Course Philosophy
This syllabus covers the deeper desk-facing material that appears in advanced quant interviews: derivatives, rates, stochastic calculus, numerical thinking, and risk concepts.

## Module 1: Derivatives and Pricing Intuition
- Option payoffs, Greeks, volatility surfaces, and no-arbitrage logic
- Futures, forwards, swaps, and relative pricing arguments
- Hedging tradeoffs and scenario-based reasoning

## Module 2: Rates and Stochastic Models
- Yield curves, duration, convexity, and fixed-income mechanics
- Brownian motion, Ito intuition, martingales, and measure changes
- How model assumptions drive behavior, calibration, and failure modes

## Module 3: Risk and Quant Desk Judgment
- VaR intuition, scenario analysis, XVA vocabulary, and model risk
- Numerical approximation, Monte Carlo reasoning, and stability checks
- Explaining advanced finance ideas clearly in interview settings

## Stretch Zone
- HJM, local-vol vs stochastic-vol tradeoffs, and implementation pitfalls in production systems
