# Certified General Appraiser
**ID**: `certifiedGeneralAppraiser` - **Discipline**: Real Estate

## Course Aim
Certified General Appraiser work is where real estate judgment, market evidence, math, and professional standards all have to sit at the same table. This course prepares learners to think through commercial valuation problems: define the assignment, identify the property rights, choose the right approaches, support the assumptions, and write a report another professional can actually follow.

The aim is practical credibility. A learner should be able to explain why a value conclusion is supported, what could change it, and where the analysis is limited.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test USPAP-style professional judgment, assignment conditions, property rights, highest and best use, commercial market analysis, sales comparison, income approach, cost approach, cap rates, DCF, depreciation, adjustments, assumptions, reconciliation, or appraisal reporting. Keep scenarios grounded in commercial property, not generic house-price guessing.

## Chapter 1: Assignment Setup and Professional Standards
**Core questions:** What exactly is being valued, for whom, for what use, as of what date, and under what conditions?

**Key concepts**
- Intended use, intended users, effective date, scope of work, report type, and assignment conditions
- Competency, independence, ethics, record keeping, and credible assignment performance
- Extraordinary assumptions, hypothetical conditions, jurisdictional exception, and limiting conditions

**Practice deliverables**
- Assignment-definition checklist
- Scenario drill distinguishing extraordinary assumptions from hypothetical conditions
- Competency response memo for an unfamiliar property type

## Chapter 2: Property Rights and Market Analysis
**Core questions:** Which bundle of rights is being appraised, and what market participants would actually care about?

**Key concepts**
- Fee simple, leased fee, leasehold, easements, restrictions, and partial interests
- Market area, neighborhood, supply and demand, vacancy, absorption, exposure time, and marketing period
- Highest and best use: legally permissible, physically possible, financially feasible, maximally productive

**Practice deliverables**
- Property-rights comparison table
- Highest-and-best-use screen for a vacant site and improved property
- Market snapshot with vacancy, rent, absorption, and competing supply

## Chapter 3: Sales Comparison Approach
**Core questions:** Which sales are comparable, what needs adjustment, and which adjusted indications deserve the most weight?

**Key concepts**
- Comparable selection, verification, conditions of sale, financing terms, market conditions, location, size, quality, and utility
- Adjustment direction, paired-sales analysis, qualitative ranking, bracketing, and reconciliation
- Data reliability and the limits of unverified online information

**Practice deliverables**
- Sales grid with at least five adjustment categories
- Paired-sales adjustment mini-lab
- Reconciliation paragraph explaining why one sale is weighted more heavily

## Chapter 4: Income Approach
**Core questions:** What income belongs to the property interest, what expenses are normal, and how does income become value?

**Key concepts**
- Potential gross income, vacancy and collection loss, effective gross income, operating expenses, NOI
- Direct capitalization, overall cap rate extraction, yield capitalization, DCF, reversion, terminal cap rate
- Contract rent versus market rent, lease rollover, tenant risk, reimbursements, concessions, and expense normalization

**Practice deliverables**
- NOI worksheet from a rent roll and operating statement
- Cap-rate extraction drill
- DCF sensitivity table for rent growth, vacancy, discount rate, and terminal cap rate

## Chapter 5: Cost Approach and Depreciation
**Core questions:** When does cost help, and how do physical, functional, and external losses affect value?

**Key concepts**
- Replacement cost new versus reproduction cost new
- Physical deterioration, functional obsolescence, external obsolescence, and entrepreneurial incentive
- Land value, depreciated improvement value, cost-to-cure, and age-life methods

**Practice deliverables**
- Cost-approach calculation packet
- Obsolescence classification drill
- Short note explaining when the cost approach is strong or weak evidence

## Chapter 6: Reconciliation and Reporting
**Core questions:** How does the appraiser turn evidence into a defensible final opinion?

**Key concepts**
- Approach relevance, data quality, consistency checks, uncertainty, sensitivity, and final reconciliation
- Report organization, reviewer trail, certifications, assumptions, limiting conditions, and exhibits
- Common review findings: unsupported adjustments, unclear property rights, weak market support, and inconsistent conclusions

**Practice deliverables**
- Final reconciliation memo using three value indications
- Reviewer checklist for a commercial appraisal report
- Error log sorted by standards, property rights, math, market evidence, and reporting support
