# Regulatory Affairs Roadmap
**ID**: `regulatoryAffairsRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Healthcare - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners for entry-level regulatory affairs work in life sciences: product classification, pathway selection, evidence planning, submission support, labeling review, change control, and post-market obligations. The course emphasizes clear regulatory reasoning over memorizing acronyms.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test regulatory pathway judgment, agency interactions, submission planning, evidence gaps, labeling claims, lifecycle maintenance, or post-market reporting. Learners should practice writing short decision records that explain what is known, what is uncertain, and what should happen next.

## Chapter 1: Regulatory Role and Product Profile
- **Key concepts**: intended use, indication, claim, product type, risk class, lifecycle stage, regulatory strategy.
- **Practice questions**: What product is being regulated? What claim creates regulatory risk? Which lifecycle stage drives the next task?
- **Work output**: product profile with intended use, market, claims, risk, and evidence needs.

## Chapter 2: Pathways and Agency Questions
- **Key concepts**: IND, NDA, BLA, 510(k), De Novo, PMA, pre-submission, meeting package, precedent.
- **Practice questions**: Which pathway fits? What precedent supports it? What question should be taken to the agency?
- **Work output**: pathway memo comparing options, assumptions, risks, and recommendation.

## Chapter 3: Evidence and Submission Readiness
- **Key concepts**: nonclinical evidence, clinical evidence, GCP, GMP, GLP, data integrity, traceability, reviewer usability.
- **Practice questions**: Does evidence support the claim? Which gap blocks submission? Can a reviewer find the answer?
- **Work output**: evidence-gap table with document source, owner, risk, and remediation.

## Chapter 4: Labeling, Claims, and Promotional Review
- **Key concepts**: labeling, indication, instructions for use, contraindication, warning, substantiation, off-label risk.
- **Practice questions**: Is the wording supported? Does it expand intended use? What evidence should be cited?
- **Work output**: claims review table with proposed language, support, risk level, and decision.

## Chapter 5: Change Control and Post-Market Duties
- **Key concepts**: design change, manufacturing change, complaint, adverse event, field action, periodic report, regulatory impact.
- **Practice questions**: Does the change require submission? Is the complaint reportable? What record would an inspector expect?
- **Work output**: lifecycle assessment with change impact, reporting decision, document updates, and rationale.

## Capstone
Create a regulatory roadmap for a fictional drug, biologic, or device: product profile, pathway memo, evidence-gap table, claims review, change-control assessment, and post-market triage note.
