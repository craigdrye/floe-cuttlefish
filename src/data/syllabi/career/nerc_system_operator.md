# NERC System Operator
**ID**: `nercSystemOperator` - **Discipline**: Energy - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners for bulk electric system operations work: reliability principles, situational awareness, clear communications, emergency operations, restoration coordination, and exam-style reasoning under time pressure.

## Course Design Notes
NERC system operator certification materials emphasize reliability standards, balancing and transmission operations, emergency procedures, communications, and restoration. This syllabus follows those pillars while adding control-room judgment, alarm triage, shift turnover habits, and scenario drills that connect rule knowledge to real operating decisions.

## Chapter 1: Bulk Electric System and Operator Role
- **Key concepts**: BES, reliability coordinator, balancing authority, transmission operator, generation operator, NERC, regional entities.
- **Practice questions**: Which operator function owns the decision? What reliability objective is at risk? Which handoff matters?
- **Work product**: an operator-role map showing responsibilities, interfaces, and escalation paths.

## Chapter 2: Reliability Standards and Operating Limits
- **Key concepts**: reliability standards, SOL, IROL, voltage limits, thermal limits, frequency, contingency planning.
- **Practice questions**: What limit is being approached? What is the required response time? Which condition creates the highest reliability risk?
- **Work product**: a reliability-limit worksheet with condition, limit, consequence, required action, and citation.

## Chapter 3: Situational Awareness and Control-Room Monitoring
- **Key concepts**: EMS, SCADA, alarms, state estimator, telemetry, one-line diagrams, outage awareness, weather and load trends.
- **Practice questions**: Which alarm is actionable? What information is missing? What trend changes the operating plan?
- **Work product**: a situational-awareness board for a shift scenario with risks, assumptions, and next checks.

## Chapter 4: Transmission Operations and Contingency Response
- **Key concepts**: power flow, congestion, voltage support, reactive power, switching, outages, N-1 contingency, remedial actions.
- **Practice questions**: What contingency must be studied? Which facility is constrained? What action relieves the overload safely?
- **Work product**: a transmission response memo with observed condition, options, coordination needs, and selected action.

## Chapter 5: Balancing, Frequency, and Interchange
- **Key concepts**: load-generation balance, ACE, frequency response, reserves, interchange schedules, ramping, time error basics.
- **Practice questions**: Is the area over-generating or under-generating? What reserve action is appropriate? Which schedule mismatch matters?
- **Work product**: a balancing log that tracks load, generation, interchange, ACE trend, and corrective actions.

## Chapter 6: Communications and Shift Discipline
- **Key concepts**: three-part communication, directives, logs, shift turnover, neighboring entities, field switching, documentation.
- **Practice questions**: Is this a reliability directive? Was the instruction repeated correctly? What must be logged before turnover?
- **Work product**: a communications script set for directives, confirmations, clarifications, and shift handoff.

## Chapter 7: Emergency Operations and Load Shed
- **Key concepts**: emergency declarations, capacity deficiency, voltage emergency, manual load shed, firm load, operating plans, public safety.
- **Practice questions**: Which emergency level applies? What action is mandatory? Who must be notified and when?
- **Work product**: an emergency action checklist with triggers, operator actions, communications, and records.

## Chapter 8: Restoration and Exam Readiness
- **Key concepts**: blackstart, cranking paths, islanding, synchronization, restoration priorities, lessons learned, timed question strategy.
- **Practice questions**: What must be energized first? Is the island stable? Which answer is most consistent with reliability priority?
- **Work product**: a restoration sequence plan and a practice-exam error log sorted by topic and reasoning miss.

## Capstone
Run a simulated control-room event from normal operations through disturbance, emergency response, and restoration. Submit a shift log, situational-awareness board, communications record, emergency checklist, restoration sequence, and post-event reliability review.

## Assessment Ideas
- Standards and limit drills graded on citation, response time, and reliability consequence.
- Communications simulations graded on clarity, confirmation, escalation, and documentation.
- Capstone graded on situational awareness, operating judgment, emergency discipline, and restoration logic.
