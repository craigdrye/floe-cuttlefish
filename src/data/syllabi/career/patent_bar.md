# Patent Bar
**ID**: `patentBar` | **Discipline**: Law | **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners for patent-bar reasoning and entry-level patent prosecution work: patentability, claim analysis, prior art, obviousness, MPEP navigation, USPTO procedure, and professional responsibility.

## Course Design Notes
USPTO registration-exam materials, the MPEP, patent statutes, and prosecution practice emphasize rule navigation, fact-sensitive analysis, deadlines, claim scope, and ethical duties before the Office. This syllabus follows those pillars while adding practical office-action response habits, reference-search discipline, and clear explanation of prosecution choices.

## Chapter 1: Patent System, Exam Format, and MPEP Navigation
- **Key concepts**: USPTO role, patent types, registration exam format, MPEP structure, statutes, rules, official guidance.
- **Practice questions**: Which authority controls this issue? What MPEP chapter should be searched first? What fact changes the answer?
- **Work product**: a reference map for MPEP chapters, statutes, rules, forms, and common exam topics.

## Chapter 2: Patentability and Subject Matter Eligibility
- **Key concepts**: utility, novelty, nonobviousness, written description, enablement, definiteness, subject matter eligibility.
- **Practice questions**: Is the invention eligible and useful? Which patentability requirement is at issue? What disclosure gap matters?
- **Work product**: a patentability issue-spotting memo with facts, controlling rule, and missing information.

## Chapter 3: Claims and Specification Support
- **Key concepts**: independent claims, dependent claims, claim construction, antecedent basis, specification support, drawings, amendments.
- **Practice questions**: What does the claim cover? Is every limitation supported? Does an amendment introduce new matter?
- **Work product**: a claim chart linking limitations to specification support and possible prior-art risk.

## Chapter 4: Prior Art and Novelty
- **Key concepts**: prior art categories, effective filing date, public disclosure, patents, publications, on-sale activity, exceptions.
- **Practice questions**: Is the reference prior art? Does it disclose every limitation? Which date controls the analysis?
- **Work product**: a prior-art timeline with references, disclosure dates, filing dates, and novelty conclusions.

## Chapter 5: Obviousness and Rejection Analysis
- **Key concepts**: Graham factors, motivation to combine, reasonable expectation of success, secondary considerations, teaching away.
- **Practice questions**: Why would a skilled artisan combine the references? Which claim limitation is missing? What evidence rebuts obviousness?
- **Work product**: an obviousness response outline with claim limitations, reference teachings, arguments, and evidence.

## Chapter 6: Prosecution Procedure and Office Actions
- **Key concepts**: application filing, restriction, election, non-final rejection, final rejection, amendments, RCE, appeal, abandonment.
- **Practice questions**: What is the applicant's next procedural option? What deadline applies? Which response keeps prosecution alive?
- **Work product**: an office-action response plan with deadlines, claim strategy, argument strategy, and procedural path.

## Chapter 7: MPEP Drills and Procedural Edge Cases
- **Key concepts**: priority claims, PCT basics, reissue, reexamination, certificates of correction, information disclosure statements, revival.
- **Practice questions**: What petition or filing is available? What must be submitted? Which procedural defect can be corrected?
- **Work product**: a procedural decision tree for priority, correction, disclosure, and post-allowance scenarios.

## Chapter 8: USPTO Ethics and Practitioner Judgment
- **Key concepts**: duty of candor, conflicts, client communication, signatures, unauthorized practice, inequitable conduct, fee handling.
- **Practice questions**: What duty does the practitioner owe? What must be disclosed? When should representation be declined or limited?
- **Work product**: an ethics triage note with facts, applicable duty, risk, and recommended next step.

## Capstone
Complete a patent prosecution packet for a fictional invention: patentability memo, claim chart, prior-art timeline, office-action response plan, procedural decision tree, and ethics triage note. Include a short exam-strategy reflection on how the MPEP was used.

## Assessment Ideas
- MPEP drills graded on speed, authority selection, and rule application.
- Claim and prior-art exercises graded on limitation-by-limitation reasoning.
- Capstone graded on prosecution judgment, procedural accuracy, and ethical awareness.
