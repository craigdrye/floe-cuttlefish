# IB Jargon Buster
**ID**: `ibJargon` - **Discipline**: Finance - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Help learners decode investment-banking deal language into concrete execution tasks. The course covers live-deal comments about comps, QoE, buyer process, diligence, markup turns, management presentations, closing conditions, and the many polite phrases that mean "fix this before it becomes expensive."

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test banking shorthand, sell-side process language, M&A diligence, valuation workstreams, client-service urgency, or deal execution judgment. The learner should practice hearing the phrase and identifying the actual deliverable.

## Chapter 1: Valuation and Market Read
- **Key concepts**: trading comps, transaction comps, precedent deals, EBITDA adjustments, multiple range, bridge, football field.
- **Practice questions**: Which comp is relevant? What adjustment changes value? What does the client need to see?
- **Work output**: short valuation bridge explaining source, adjustment, and implication.

## Chapter 2: Quality of Earnings and Diligence
- **Key concepts**: QoE, normalized EBITDA, add-backs, working capital, revenue recognition, customer concentration.
- **Practice questions**: Is the add-back supportable? What does the buyer need verified? Which issue changes price?
- **Work output**: diligence tracker with issue, owner, evidence, risk, and next step.

## Chapter 3: Buyer Process and Deal Tension
- **Key concepts**: teaser, CIM, IOI, LOI, process letter, data room, confirmatory diligence, bid deadline.
- **Practice questions**: Is buyer interest real? What makes a process competitive? What should be escalated?
- **Work output**: buyer-status summary with seriousness, open items, and next action.

## Chapter 4: Materials, Markups, and Client Readiness
- **Key concepts**: management presentation, board book, comment turn, redline, version control, talking points.
- **Practice questions**: What changed? What must be reconciled? Who needs the next draft?
- **Work output**: clean comment-response log and final deck readiness checklist.

## Chapter 5: Signing, Closing, and Chaos Control
- **Key concepts**: SPA, reps and warranties, closing conditions, funds flow, disclosure schedules, closing checklist.
- **Practice questions**: What blocks signing? What blocks closing? Which party owns the open item?
- **Work output**: closing issue list with owner, deadline, dependency, and risk level.

## Capstone
Take a simulated Friday-night deal email chain and produce the real task list: valuation update, diligence asks, buyer follow-up, client response, and closing risk.
