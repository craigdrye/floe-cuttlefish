# Clinical Research Roadmap
**ID**: `clinicalResearchRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Healthcare - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners to enter clinical research operations with safe habits around participant protection, protocol execution, source documentation, safety reporting, monitoring, and inspection readiness.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test clinical trial roles, consent, eligibility, visit windows, source-to-CRF data, safety events, deviations, sponsor communication, or regulatory files. The course should connect every acronym to a concrete site task.

## Chapter 1: Trial Ecosystem and Site Roles
- **Key concepts**: sponsor, CRO, investigator, coordinator, monitor, IRB, protocol, delegation log.
- **Practice questions**: Who is responsible? What must be delegated? What needs investigator oversight?
- **Work output**: clinical trial role map with handoffs and escalation paths.

## Chapter 2: Consent, Eligibility, and Visit Planning
- **Key concepts**: informed consent, screening, inclusion criteria, exclusion criteria, visit window, schedule of assessments.
- **Practice questions**: Was consent complete before procedures? Is the participant eligible? Which visit tasks are due?
- **Work output**: visit-prep checklist with consent, eligibility, assessments, and open risks.

## Chapter 3: Source Documentation and Data Quality
- **Key concepts**: source document, CRF, eCRF, ALCOA, query, audit trail, correction, missing data.
- **Practice questions**: Can the data be verified? What needs clarification? Is the correction traceable?
- **Work output**: source-to-CRF reconciliation note with query responses.

## Chapter 4: Safety, Deviations, and Escalation
- **Key concepts**: AE, SAE, causality, relatedness, reporting timeline, protocol deviation, CAPA.
- **Practice questions**: Is this reportable? What facts are missing? What prevents recurrence?
- **Work output**: safety or deviation summary with impact, notification path, and corrective action.

## Chapter 5: Monitoring, Closeout, and Inspection Readiness
- **Key concepts**: monitoring visit, action item, essential documents, IP accountability, regulatory binder, closeout.
- **Practice questions**: What will the monitor ask for? What document is missing? Can the site reconstruct the study?
- **Work output**: inspection-readiness checklist with open items, owners, and deadlines.

## Capstone
Run a simulated study month. Prepare a visit, check consent and eligibility, reconcile source and CRF data, escalate a safety issue, update the regulatory file, and write a monitor-ready status email.
