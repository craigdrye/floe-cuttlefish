# Pharma Drug Safety Roadmap
**ID**: `pharmaDrugSafetyRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Healthcare - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners for pharmacovigilance and drug-safety work: case intake, seriousness assessment, expedited reporting, signal detection, safety documents, vendor handoffs, and inspection-ready records. The course keeps the focus on patient risk, timelines, and defensible decisions.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test adverse-event language, safety operations, case processing, aggregate reporting, signal review, medical review handoffs, or pharmacovigilance compliance. Learners should practice turning incomplete safety information into a clear next action.

## Chapter 1: Drug Safety Role and Safety Language
- **Key concepts**: pharmacovigilance, adverse event, product complaint, special situation, seriousness, expectedness, causality.
- **Practice questions**: Is this a safety case? What facts are missing? Which detail changes urgency?
- **Work output**: safety-intake triage note with event, product, patient, reporter, and follow-up needs.

## Chapter 2: Case Intake, Follow-Up, and Data Quality
- **Key concepts**: identifiable patient, identifiable reporter, suspect product, adverse event, narrative, follow-up, duplicate search.
- **Practice questions**: Does the case meet minimum criteria? What follow-up question matters most? Can another reviewer reconstruct the case?
- **Work output**: case-processing checklist with minimum criteria, missing fields, and follow-up plan.

## Chapter 3: Reporting Rules and Timeline Discipline
- **Key concepts**: expedited report, ICSR, serious unexpected event, regulatory clock, local affiliate, submission gateway.
- **Practice questions**: Is this reportable? When did the clock start? Which market or partner needs notification?
- **Work output**: reporting-decision log with rationale, timeline, recipient, and evidence.

## Chapter 4: Medical Review and Signal Detection
- **Key concepts**: medical assessment, listedness, disproportionality, trend, signal, risk factor, confounder, benefit-risk.
- **Practice questions**: Is the pattern new? What alternative explanation exists? What evidence supports escalation?
- **Work output**: signal review note with case pattern, evidence, uncertainty, and recommendation.

## Chapter 5: Aggregate Safety and Inspection Readiness
- **Key concepts**: PBRER, PSUR, DSUR, risk management plan, SOP, reconciliation, quality control, audit trail.
- **Practice questions**: Are cases reconciled? Do safety documents tell one story? What would an inspector ask for?
- **Work output**: inspection-readiness tracker for cases, submissions, reconciliations, deviations, and open CAPAs.

## Capstone
Process a simulated safety issue from intake through case validity, follow-up, reporting decision, medical review, signal note, and inspection-ready action tracker.
