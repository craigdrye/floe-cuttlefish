# Bar Exam: Law Fog Machine
**ID**: `barExam` · **Discipline**: Legal

## Course Aim
The bar exam is not a personality test, a referendum on your worth, or a cursed binder that learned to breathe. It is a timed licensing exam that rewards repeatable legal moves: read the call, find the issues, state usable rules, apply facts with discipline, and stop writing when the answer is cooked.

This syllabus is built around the current U.S. Uniform Bar Examination frame: MBE multiple choice, MEE essays, and MPT performance tasks. It trains black-letter recall, issue spotting, lawyerly writing, fact selection, document execution, and exam-day stamina. Jurisdiction-specific rules still matter, so learners should layer in their state board's requirements, local component, deadlines, accommodations process, and any transition rules for the NextGen UBE.

## Course Design Notes
Keep this course practical, humane, and jurisdiction-aware. Route questions here when they test UBE or NextGen-style legal reasoning, MBE doctrine, MEE issue spotting, MPT document execution, rule memorization, distractor analysis, or bar-study planning. Because the bar is in transition during 2026-2028, prompts should name whether they target legacy UBE, NextGen UBE, or a jurisdiction-specific add-on when that matters.

## Exam Map and Study Rhythm
The legacy UBE is a two-day exam: MEE and MPT on Tuesday, MBE on Wednesday. UBE scoring weights are commonly 50% MBE, 30% MEE, and 20% MPT, so the course treats doctrine, writing, and performance work as one system rather than three separate weather events.

| Component | What It Tests | Practice Posture |
|---|---|---|
| MBE | 200 multiple-choice questions across Civil Procedure, Constitutional Law, Contracts/Sales, Criminal Law and Procedure, Evidence, Real Property, and Torts | Daily mixed questions, rule extraction, wrong-answer autopsies |
| MEE | Six 30-minute essays across MBE subjects plus tested essay subjects | Fast issue spotting, compact rule statements, organized IRAC/CRAC |
| MPT | Two 90-minute lawyering tasks using a File and Library | Task memo control, fact sorting, rule synthesis, finished work product |

Suggested cadence: 8-10 weeks full time or 14-18 weeks part time. Every week should include memorization, MBE sets, at least one timed essay, one performance-task drill, and an error log. The error log is the tiny courthouse where your mistakes become precedent.

## Chapter 1: The Bar Exam Operating System
**Core questions:** What is the exam asking? What can be safely ignored? How do you turn panic into procedure?

**Key concepts**
- Call-of-the-question discipline, facts-to-elements matching, and answer hierarchy
- IRAC, CRAC, CREAC, mini-IRAC, and when a short conclusion beats a dramatic essay arc
- Time budgets: 1.8 minutes per MBE question, 30 minutes per MEE, 90 minutes per MPT
- Error-log taxonomy: unknown rule, misread fact, wrong issue, distractor trap, timing failure, and avoidable wobble

**Issue-spotting skills**
- Identify the governing subject before chasing facts
- Convert each fact into a possible legal consequence
- Separate high-yield issues from decorative clutter
- Spot "because," "unless," "however," and date/order facts that change the answer

**Deliverables**
- Personal exam map with component weights, jurisdiction deadlines, and score target
- 50-question diagnostic with written postmortem
- Two-page attack plan for reading, outlining, writing, and moving on

## Chapter 2: MBE Core I - Civil Procedure, Constitutional Law, and Evidence
**Core questions:** Who can sue where, what can government do, and what proof gets through the courtroom door?

**Key concepts**
- Civil Procedure: personal jurisdiction, subject matter jurisdiction, venue, Erie, pleadings, joinder, discovery, summary judgment, trial motions, verdicts, preclusion, appealability
- Constitutional Law: judicial review, federalism, separation of powers, individual rights, due process, equal protection, First Amendment, state action, takings
- Evidence: relevance, character, impeachment, hearsay and exclusions, hearsay exceptions, privileges, authentication, best evidence, opinion testimony, expert testimony

**Issue-spotting skills**
- Distinguish jurisdiction, venue, transfer, and choice-of-law questions without mushy overlap
- Ask whether government action exists before launching a rights analysis
- Run hearsay in order: statement, offered for truth, exclusion, exception, confrontation problem

**Deliverables**
- Jurisdiction and venue flowchart for federal civil actions
- Constitutional scrutiny matrix with triggering facts and likely outcomes
- Evidence objection sheet with one-sentence rule statements and sample objections
- Three 33-question mixed MBE sets with source-of-error notes

## Chapter 3: MBE Core II - Contracts, Torts, Criminal Law, and Real Property
**Core questions:** What duties were created, breached, excused, transferred, invaded, or criminally crossed?

**Key concepts**
- Contracts and Sales: formation, consideration, defenses, Statute of Frauds, parol evidence, interpretation, conditions, breach, excuses, remedies, UCC Article 2, third-party rights
- Torts: intentional torts, negligence, causation, damages, strict liability, products liability, defamation, privacy, nuisance, vicarious liability, immunities
- Criminal Law and Procedure: homicide, property crimes, inchoate crimes, parties, defenses, search and seizure, confessions, identification, right to counsel, guilty pleas, double jeopardy
- Real Property: estates, future interests, landlord-tenant, servitudes, conveyancing, recording, mortgages, zoning, takings-adjacent land use issues

**Issue-spotting skills**
- In Contracts, decide common law or UCC before writing the rule
- In Torts, separate duty, breach, causation, damages, and defenses so negligence does not become soup
- In Criminal Procedure, place the police conduct on a timeline before choosing the amendment
- In Property, draw the ownership chain when words start wearing little hats

**Deliverables**
- Remedies grid for contract, tort, and property fact patterns
- Criminal procedure timeline drill covering stop, arrest, search, interrogation, lineup, charge, plea, trial, and appeal
- Future interests and recording-act mini-lab with diagrams
- Four timed 25-question MBE sets, each followed by rule-card creation

## Chapter 4: Essay Subjects and MEE Rule Muscles
**Core questions:** Can you produce a legally organized answer when the facts are loud and the clock is rude?

**Key concepts**
- Business Associations: agency, partnership, corporations, LLCs, fiduciary duties, authority, liability, governance
- Family Law: marriage, divorce, property division, support, custody, jurisdictional overlays
- Trusts and Estates: wills, intestacy, trusts, fiduciary duties, future interests, probate problems
- Secured Transactions: attachment, perfection, priority, default, proceeds, buyers and lien creditors
- Conflict of Laws where applicable, especially as embedded in other subjects
- Transition awareness: NCBE materials indicate that beginning with the July 2026 bar exam, Conflict of Laws, Family Law, Trusts and Estates, and Secured Transactions leave the MEE tested-subject list, while Family Law and Trusts and Estates are expected to appear regularly through the MPT from July 2026 through February 2028

**Issue-spotting skills**
- Read the call first and write only for that audience
- Outline before drafting: issues, rules, facts, conclusion, then type
- Use compact rule statements that can survive imperfect memory
- Make factual application do the scoring work; rules alone are ornamental furniture

**Deliverables**
- One-page attack outline for each essay subject tested in the learner's administration
- 18 timed essays: at least 12 MEE essays and 6 weak-area rewrites
- Rule-statement bank with "perfect," "good enough," and "emergency fog-light" versions
- Self-grading packet comparing answers to point sheets or released analyses

## Chapter 5: MPT - Lawyering in a 90-Minute Box
**Core questions:** What did the supervising attorney ask for, what law controls, which facts matter, and what finished document would a beginning lawyer actually file, send, or hand over?

**Key concepts**
- File, Library, task memo, format memo, cases, statutes, client documents, transcripts, correspondence, irrelevant facts
- Objective memos, persuasive briefs, demand letters, client letters, bench memos, closing arguments, negotiation plans, and wildcard office documents
- Rule synthesis from provided authority; no outside law hunting
- Professional tone, headings, fact selection, citation use, and ethical issue recognition

**Issue-spotting skills**
- Translate the task memo into required sections before reading deeply
- Extract rules from Library authorities and pair them with File facts
- Distinguish relevant facts from background confetti
- Preserve document format while still doing legal analysis

**Deliverables**
- MPT format bank with templates for objective, persuasive, and client-facing tasks
- Six 90-minute MPTs, including at least one memo, one brief, one letter, and one unfamiliar task type
- 15-minute Library rule-synthesis drills
- Post-task audit: task compliance, organization, rule use, factual application, tone, and completion

## Chapter 6: Memorization, Retrieval, and Attack Outlines
**Core questions:** How do you make law retrievable under pressure without trying to swallow a treatise whole?

**Key concepts**
- Spaced repetition, active recall, interleaving, cumulative mixed practice, and closed-book writing
- Attack outlines versus full outlines versus flashcards versus flowcharts
- Rule compression: element lists, tests, burdens, standards of review, and exceptions
- Personal weak-area loops based on evidence from practice, not vibes in a wig

**Issue-spotting skills**
- Convert missed questions into rules and triggering facts
- Notice when two doctrines look similar but answer different legal questions
- Use short mnemonics only when they aid retrieval rather than becoming a second syllabus

**Deliverables**
- Master attack outline capped at 60 pages
- One-page final-review sheet per subject
- 300-rule flashcard deck or equivalent retrieval system
- Weekly closed-book essay sprint using only memory and a timer

## Chapter 7: MBE Strategy and Distractor Surgery
**Core questions:** Why is the right answer right, why are the wrong answers wrong, and why did that one answer look so annoyingly handsome?

**Key concepts**
- Fact-pattern triage, answer-choice prediction, elimination, and exception hunting
- Common distractors: true but irrelevant, wrong legal standard, reversed burden, overbroad rule, missing element, premature conclusion
- Mixed-subject stamina and score tracking
- Reading discipline for "best," "most likely," "least likely," "proper," and "constitutional" prompts

**Issue-spotting skills**
- Identify the tested rule before reading answer choices when possible
- Keep elements in order and refuse answer choices that skip one
- Mark uncertainty type: rule gap, fact ambiguity, or confidence problem

**Deliverables**
- 1,500-2,000 MBE practice questions, scaled to timeline and need
- Two 100-question simulated half-days and one 200-question full simulation
- Wrong-answer notebook organized by subject, subtopic, rule, and trap type
- Personal "do not fall for this again" list for recurring distractors

## Chapter 8: Simulation, Final Review, and Exam-Day Execution
**Core questions:** Can you perform the routine when tired, bored, nervous, and surrounded by laptop chargers?

**Key concepts**
- Full-day simulation, lunch strategy, hydration, sleep, permitted items, software check, and jurisdiction instructions
- Score interpretation: raw practice trends, scaled-score humility, and component balancing
- Final two-week triage: protect strengths, repair high-yield weaknesses, stop opening new rabbit holes
- Mental management: reset rituals, breathing, moving on, and refusing to litigate old questions during the break

**Issue-spotting skills**
- Decide when to abandon a rabbit trail and bank points elsewhere
- Use checklists under fatigue without sounding robotic
- Finish every component with something coherent on the page

**Deliverables**
- One full simulated UBE sequence: MPT/MEE day plus MBE day
- Final 14-day calendar with specific subjects, drills, and rest blocks
- Exam-day checklist for logistics, timing, food, software, IDs, and permitted materials
- Post-simulation memo identifying score risks and final repairs

## Capstone: The Passing-Score Portfolio
By the end, learners assemble a working portfolio rather than a pile of good intentions:

- Jurisdiction-specific exam checklist and score target
- Subject attack outlines and final-review sheets
- MBE wrong-answer notebook with trend analysis
- 18+ essays with self-grades and rewrites
- 6+ MPTs with format notes and timing reflections
- Full simulated exam results and final two-week triage plan

## Assessment Ideas
- Daily mixed MBE sets with wrong-answer autopsies by rule, fact, and distractor type.
- Timed MEE essays with compact rule statements and self-grading against released analyses.
- MPT drills that score task compliance, organization, rule synthesis, fact use, and completion.
- Closed-book attack-outline sprints to build retrieval under pressure.
- Full simulated exam sequence plus a final triage memo.
