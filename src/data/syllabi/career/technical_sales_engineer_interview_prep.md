# Technical Sales Engineer Interview Prep
**ID**: `technicalSales` · **Discipline**: Commercial · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare candidates for sales engineering and solutions consulting interviews by combining discovery, technical explanation, demo strategy, architecture tradeoffs, objection handling, and cross-functional judgment.

## Course Design Notes
Technical sales roles sit between commercial sales training, solution architecture, product knowledge, and customer trust. Comparable resources from sales training programs, PreSales communities, cloud architecture frameworks, and security questionnaires point toward the same core abilities: diagnose the customer problem, explain technology in plain language, design credible solutions, and manage proof without overpromising.

## Chapter 1: The Sales Engineer Role
- **Key concepts**: AE-SE partnership, discovery, demo ownership, technical validation, customer trust, internal handoff.
- **Practice questions**: What does the account executive need from you? What does the buyer need from you? Where can an SE create risk?
- **Interview output**: a concise role narrative explaining how sales engineering supports buyer confidence and deal quality.

## Chapter 2: Technical Discovery
- **Key concepts**: business pain, technical requirements, current stack, integration points, security constraints, success criteria.
- **Practice questions**: What must be true for this solution to work? Which requirement is blocking versus preferred? Who owns the technical decision?
- **Interview output**: a discovery script with business, technical, security, integration, and success questions.

## Chapter 3: Demo Strategy
- **Key concepts**: demo agenda, persona alignment, value moments, proof points, pacing, live-demo risk, fallback plans.
- **Practice questions**: What should the buyer see first? Which feature proves the value story? What should be skipped?
- **Interview output**: a 15-minute demo plan with opening, workflow, proof moment, objection pause, and close.

## Chapter 4: Architecture and Solution Design
- **Key concepts**: systems thinking, APIs, data flow, authentication, scalability, reliability, implementation constraints.
- **Practice questions**: What systems connect? Where does data move? What is the simplest credible architecture?
- **Interview output**: a whiteboard architecture with components, data flow, risks, and implementation assumptions.

## Chapter 5: Security, Privacy, and Compliance Objections
- **Key concepts**: SOC 2, SSO, encryption, data retention, audit logs, privacy, shared responsibility, security questionnaires.
- **Practice questions**: What is the buyer really worried about? What evidence can you provide? When should you bring in security or legal?
- **Interview output**: a security objection response sheet with evidence, boundary, and escalation language.

## Chapter 6: Pilots, Proofs of Concept, and Mutual Action Plans
- **Key concepts**: pilot scope, success criteria, timeline, owners, evaluation plan, procurement risk, implementation handoff.
- **Practice questions**: What should the POC prove? How will success be measured? What happens after the pilot?
- **Interview output**: a mutual action plan for a technical evaluation.

## Chapter 7: Interview Scenarios and Role Plays
- **Key concepts**: mock discovery, demo critique, whiteboard prompt, objection role play, cross-functional judgment.
- **Practice questions**: How do you recover if the demo fails? How do you explain a tradeoff to a nontechnical buyer?
- **Interview output**: a practice packet with STAR stories for ambiguity, conflict, technical learning, and customer trust.

## Chapter 8: Working With Product, Engineering, and Customer Success
- **Key concepts**: feedback loops, feature requests, roadmap honesty, implementation risks, success handoff, internal credibility.
- **Practice questions**: When is a request a one-off? What should be documented after a call? How do you avoid promising roadmap fiction?
- **Interview output**: a post-call recap and internal handoff note.

## Capstone
Complete a mock technical sales interview: discovery call, tailored demo plan, architecture whiteboard, security objection response, and POC mutual action plan. Finish with a debrief on what evidence would make the buyer comfortable moving forward.

## Assessment Ideas
- Discovery graded on business and technical signal capture.
- Demo plan graded on buyer relevance, proof, and risk control.
- Architecture and objection responses graded on clarity, credibility, and escalation judgment.
