# Supply Chain Analyst
**ID**: `supplyChain` · **Discipline**: Operations

## Course Philosophy
This syllabus trains analysts to think in flows, constraints, forecasts, and service-level tradeoffs instead of isolated spreadsheet outputs.

## Module 1: Demand, Forecasts, and Inventory
- Forecast inputs, error, seasonality, and judgment overrides
- Safety stock, reorder points, lead times, and stockout risk
- Service levels, working capital, and inventory tradeoffs

## Module 2: Network Flow and Operations
- Bottlenecks, throughput, utilization, and queueing intuition
- Supplier performance, transportation tradeoffs, and failure points
- Mapping where delays or variability actually enter the system

## Module 3: Analysis and Decision Support
- KPI design, root-cause analysis, and exception management
- Scenario modeling for cost, service, and resilience tradeoffs
- Communicating recommendations to planners, ops, and leadership

## Stretch Zone
- S&OP, multi-echelon inventory, and disruption planning under uncertainty
