# Investment Banking Roadmap
**ID**: `investmentBankingRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Finance - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners for the analyst work of investment banking: company research, accounting flow, valuation, transaction materials, process management, and client-ready communication. The goal is accurate judgment under deadline pressure.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test banking analyst workflow, valuation, M&A, capital raising, pitch books, diligence requests, model checks, or managing comments. Keep answers concrete and output-focused.

## Chapter 1: Banking Workflow and Client Context
- **Key concepts**: coverage, product groups, pitch, live deal, CIM, data room, diligence, signing, closing.
- **Practice questions**: What is the client trying to decide? Is this a pitch or live process? What workstream owns the answer?
- **Work output**: deal-process map with deliverables, owners, deadlines, and dependencies.

## Chapter 2: Accounting and Financial Statement Links
- **Key concepts**: revenue, EBITDA, working capital, capex, debt, cash flow, nonrecurring items, adjustments.
- **Practice questions**: Do the statements connect? What adjustment changes valuation? Which metric will buyers care about?
- **Work output**: company financial summary with key adjustments and quality notes.

## Chapter 3: Valuation Core
- **Key concepts**: trading comps, transaction comps, DCF, WACC, terminal value, multiples, sensitivity table.
- **Practice questions**: Which peer set is defensible? What drives the valuation range? Where is the model fragile?
- **Work output**: valuation football field with assumptions and explanation of range.

## Chapter 4: M&A and Capital Markets Materials
- **Key concepts**: strategic rationale, accretion/dilution, sources and uses, debt capacity, equity story, investor questions.
- **Practice questions**: Why does the deal make sense? How is it funded? What objection must the materials answer?
- **Work output**: transaction page with rationale, valuation, financing, and key risks.

## Chapter 5: Execution, Comments, and Quality Control
- **Key concepts**: turn, markup, version control, tie-out, footnotes, diligence tracker, management presentation.
- **Practice questions**: Does every number tie? Which comment changes analysis? What is the next bottleneck?
- **Work output**: clean workplan with comment log, tie-out checks, and next deliverables.

## Capstone
Create an analyst packet for a fictional sell-side process: company profile, financial summary, valuation range, buyer rationale page, diligence tracker, and client-ready recommendation.
