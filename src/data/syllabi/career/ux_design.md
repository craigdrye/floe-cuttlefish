# UX Design
**ID**: `uxDesign` · **Discipline**: Design · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Develop practical UX design judgment from research and problem framing through flows, prototypes, accessibility, usability testing, and portfolio storytelling. The course is built for learners who need to make useful product decisions, not just pretty screens.

## Course Design Notes
Comparable UX programs such as Google's UX certificate, Nielsen Norman Group training, CareerFoundry, and W3C accessibility resources emphasize user research, wireframes, prototypes, testing, and inclusive design. This syllabus keeps those pillars while adding stronger product constraints, design critique habits, and handoff quality.

## Chapter 1: UX Mindset and Problem Framing
- **Key concepts**: user goals, business goals, constraints, problem statements, assumptions, success metrics.
- **Practice questions**: Who is the user? What job are they trying to do? What would make the current experience fail?
- **Design output**: a problem brief with user, context, pain, constraint, and measurable outcome.

## Chapter 2: Research That Changes the Design
- **Key concepts**: interview planning, observation, survey limits, personas, jobs-to-be-done, bias, synthesis.
- **Practice questions**: What do we need to learn before designing? Which finding is strongest? Which assumption remains untested?
- **Design output**: a lightweight research plan plus an affinity map of findings and opportunities.

## Chapter 3: Information Architecture and User Flows
- **Key concepts**: navigation, task flows, content grouping, hierarchy, friction, edge cases, empty states.
- **Practice questions**: What path does the user expect? Where could they get stuck? Which content must be visible before action?
- **Design output**: a site map or app map and two annotated user flows.

## Chapter 4: Wireframes and Interaction Structure
- **Key concepts**: layout, affordance, visual hierarchy, states, feedback, error prevention, progressive disclosure.
- **Practice questions**: What is the primary action? What feedback does the user need? What happens when input is invalid?
- **Design output**: low- and mid-fidelity wireframes for a core workflow, including loading, error, and empty states.

## Chapter 5: Prototyping and Usability Testing
- **Key concepts**: clickable prototypes, task scripts, usability metrics, think-aloud testing, severity, iteration.
- **Practice questions**: Can users complete the task? Where do they hesitate? Which issue has the highest cost?
- **Design output**: a prototype test plan, five usability findings, and a prioritized revision list.

## Chapter 6: Accessibility and Inclusive Design
- **Key concepts**: WCAG basics, color contrast, keyboard navigation, semantic structure, readable copy, cognitive load.
- **Practice questions**: Can the design work without a mouse? Does the interface communicate state clearly? Which user is currently excluded?
- **Design output**: an accessibility review checklist applied to the current prototype.

## Chapter 7: Product Collaboration and Handoff
- **Key concepts**: design systems, tokens, components, specs, engineering tradeoffs, product critique, decision records.
- **Practice questions**: Which details must be specified? What can be handled by an existing component? Which tradeoff needs product agreement?
- **Design output**: an annotated handoff package with component states, responsive behavior, and open questions.

## Chapter 8: Portfolio Case Study
- **Key concepts**: story arc, constraints, decisions, evidence, impact, honest reflection, visual clarity.
- **Practice questions**: What changed because of your work? Which decisions show judgment? What would you improve next?
- **Design output**: a portfolio-ready case study with problem, process, artifacts, result, and reflection.

## Capstone
Redesign a flawed product workflow from research through prototype and usability test. Submit the research brief, flow, wireframes, prototype notes, accessibility review, final screens, and portfolio case study.

## Assessment Ideas
- Research synthesis graded on evidence quality and design relevance.
- Prototype graded on task completion, states, accessibility, and clarity.
- Portfolio case study graded on decision quality, narrative, and honest tradeoff explanation.
