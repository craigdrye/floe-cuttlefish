# Cybersecurity Operations Roadmap
**ID**: `cybersecurityOperationsRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Technology - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners to understand the work language of a security operations team: alerts, IOCs, triage, blast radius, privilege escalation, containment, recovery, and incident updates. The goal is to translate security signals into calm next actions.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test SOC workflows, alert triage, incident response, identity compromise, cloud exposure, containment, evidence preservation, or executive communication. Keep it practical and operational.

## Chapter 1: SOC Triage and Alert Quality
- **Key concepts**: event, alert, incident, severity, confidence, enrichment, false positive.
- **Practice questions**: What signal matters? What evidence changes severity? What should be checked next?
- **Work output**: alert triage note.

## Chapter 2: Identity, Access, and Privilege
- **Key concepts**: credentials, tokens, MFA, session revocation, privilege escalation, service accounts.
- **Practice questions**: Which account is exposed? What should be disabled, rotated, or reviewed?
- **Work output**: compromised-account response checklist.

## Chapter 3: Endpoint, Network, and Cloud Scope
- **Key concepts**: EDR, lateral movement, C2, persistence, exfiltration, buckets, security groups.
- **Practice questions**: What is affected? Has the attacker moved? Is data exposed?
- **Work output**: scope map across host, identity, network, and cloud.

## Chapter 4: Containment and Recovery
- **Key concepts**: isolate, block, revoke, rotate, patch, restore, monitor.
- **Practice questions**: Which action reduces risk fastest? What could break production?
- **Work output**: containment plan with owner, risk, and rollback.

## Chapter 5: Incident Communication
- **Key concepts**: incident commander, status update, confirmed facts, unknowns, customer impact, post-incident review.
- **Practice questions**: Who needs to know? What is confirmed? What decision is needed?
- **Work output**: executive incident update.

## Capstone
Turn a noisy SOC handoff into a prioritized incident plan with triage findings, containment actions, communications, and recovery checks.
