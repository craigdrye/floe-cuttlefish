# CPA Exam: Ledger Quest
**ID**: `cpaExam` · **Discipline**: Finance

## Course Aim
The CPA Exam is a four-part professional gauntlet: three Core sections (AUD, FAR, REG) plus one Discipline section (BAR, ISC, or TCP). This syllabus treats the exam as a career rehearsal, not a trivia parade. You will practice reading facts like an auditor, measuring transactions like a controller, classifying tax consequences like a preparer, and choosing your Discipline like a tiny but consequential fork in the road.

The tone is serious; the ledger may occasionally wear a little hat.

## Course Design Notes
Keep this course practical, humane, and blueprint-aware. Route questions here when they test CPA Core sections, Discipline choice, MCQ reasoning, task-based simulations, audit evidence, financial reporting, tax and business law, systems and controls, business analysis, or candidate pacing. The course should make the exam feel conquerable without hiding its professional seriousness.

## What You Will Build
By the end, you will have:
- A section-by-section exam map tied to the AICPA blueprint skill levels: remembering and understanding, application, analysis, and AUD-only evaluation.
- A working notebook of accounting, audit, tax, systems, and business-analysis procedures.
- A personal Discipline decision memo for BAR, ISC, or TCP.
- Timed MCQ sets, task-based simulation drills, and post-practice error logs.
- A final CPA battle plan: section order, study calendar, pacing rules, weak-area triage, and exam-day routines.

## Chapter 1: CPA Exam Architecture and Study Operating System
**Goal:** Understand the exam you are actually taking, then build a repeatable study machine.

**Key concepts**
- Core + Discipline model: AUD, FAR, REG, plus one of BAR, ISC, or TCP.
- Four-hour sections, five testlets, MCQs, and task-based simulations.
- Score weighting: most sections split MCQ/TBS 50/50; ISC weights MCQs more heavily.
- Blueprint reading: content areas, groups, topics, representative tasks, and skill levels.
- Rolling credit window, Notice to Schedule logistics, Prometric routines, and state-board checks.

**Applied skills**
- Convert a blueprint area into a study checklist.
- Translate missed questions into content gaps, skill gaps, or pacing gaps.
- Build a weekly loop: learn, drill, simulate, debrief, review.

**Practice deliverables**
- One-page exam dashboard with section counts, target dates, and current confidence.
- Personal study calendar with rest days, cumulative review blocks, and simulated exams.
- Error log template with columns for rule, clue missed, trap type, and next action.

## Chapter 2: FAR Core - Financial Accounting and Reporting
**Goal:** Become fluent in recognition, measurement, presentation, and disclosure across common reporting frameworks.

**Key concepts**
- Financial reporting foundations for for-profit, not-for-profit, and governmental contexts.
- Financial statements, note disclosures, adjusting entries, and statement of cash flows.
- Cash, receivables, inventory, property, plant and equipment, intangibles, payables, debt, equity, contingencies, and subsequent events.
- Revenue recognition, leases, income taxes, fair value, foreign currency, accounting changes, and error corrections.
- Ratios, performance metrics, and the accounting story behind the numbers.

**Applied skills**
- Record journal entries from messy fact patterns.
- Decide recognition timing and measurement basis.
- Tie account balances to disclosures and assertions.
- Analyze ratios without treating them like decorative math.
- Reconcile book activity to financial statement presentation.

**Practice deliverables**
- FAR transaction matrix: trigger, entry, measurement, presentation, disclosure.
- Cash flow classification drill set with explanations.
- TBS packet: adjusting entries, account rollforwards, financial statement excerpts, and disclosure corrections.
- Mini close binder for one reporting cycle.

## Chapter 3: AUD Core - Auditing and Attestation
**Goal:** Think in evidence, risk, professional responsibility, and reporting consequences.

**Key concepts**
- Ethics, independence, professional skepticism, due care, and engagement acceptance.
- Audit risk, materiality, fraud risk, internal control, IT general controls, and entity-level controls.
- Assertions, evidence quality, sampling, confirmations, estimates, substantive procedures, and analytical procedures.
- Audit documentation, subsequent events, going concern, misstatements, and representation letters.
- Audit, review, compilation, attestation, and SOC-related reporting basics.

**Applied skills**
- Match procedures to assertions.
- Identify control deficiencies and propose practical remediation.
- Evaluate evidence reliability and sufficiency.
- Determine report modifications and emphasis-of-matter implications.
- Write audit conclusions that follow from the work performed.

**Practice deliverables**
- Risk assessment memo for a fictional client.
- Control walkthrough with deficiency ranking and remediation plan.
- Evidence map linking assertions, procedures, results, and conclusions.
- Reporting decision tree for clean, qualified, adverse, disclaimer, and special circumstances.

## Chapter 4: REG Core - Taxation and Regulation
**Goal:** Build practical command of routine federal tax compliance, professional responsibility, and business law.

**Key concepts**
- Tax ethics, Circular 230-style responsibilities, preparer penalties, statute of limitations, and federal tax procedures.
- Agency, contracts, debtor-creditor relationships, secured transactions, business structures, and liability.
- Individual taxation: gross income, adjustments, deductions, credits, filing status, dependents, and property transactions.
- Entity taxation: C corporations, S corporations, partnerships, basis, distributions, and routine compliance.
- Property basis, cost recovery, gains, losses, character, and common book-tax differences.

**Applied skills**
- Classify income, deductions, credits, and filing positions.
- Compute basis before and after contributions, distributions, sales, and exchanges.
- Identify when a legal rule changes the accounting or tax answer.
- Prepare compact workpapers that support a tax return position.
- Spot when a fact pattern belongs in TCP-level planning rather than REG routine compliance.

**Practice deliverables**
- Individual return issue checklist with AGI, taxable income, and credit computations.
- Entity basis workbook for partnership, S corporation, and C corporation scenarios.
- Business law issue-spotting chart.
- Tax research note with authority, conclusion, and documentation retained.

## Chapter 5: Discipline Choice Studio - BAR, ISC, or TCP
**Goal:** Choose the Discipline section that fits your strengths, career goals, and study sequence.

**BAR: Business Analysis and Reporting**
- Best fit for candidates who like advanced reporting, analytics, financial statement analysis, and managerial decision support.
- Core skills: analyze performance, evaluate investment alternatives, work with complex accounting topics, and handle state and local government reporting.
- Practice deliverable: management analysis memo explaining trends, ratios, assumptions, and reporting risks.

**ISC: Information Systems and Controls**
- Best fit for candidates drawn to audit technology, data management, cybersecurity, privacy, and SOC engagements.
- Core skills: identify IT risks, evaluate change management and access controls, understand security/confidentiality/privacy expectations, and interpret SOC engagement considerations.
- Practice deliverable: systems-control assessment with data flow, access risks, control activities, and testing approach.

**TCP: Tax Compliance and Planning**
- Best fit for candidates who like tax depth, entity planning, individual planning, and property transaction strategy.
- Core skills: plan around entity structures, dispositions, basis, owner-level effects, and individual tax decisions.
- Practice deliverable: tax planning memo comparing two alternatives with assumptions, calculations, and client-ready recommendation.

**Decision deliverable**
- Discipline memo: preferred section, why it fits, risks, prerequisite Core section, and first three study actions.

## Chapter 6: Simulations, Research, and Workpaper Craft
**Goal:** Turn knowledge into exam-ready execution.

**Key concepts**
- TBS anatomy: exhibits, tabs, source documents, answer formats, and hidden distractors.
- Authoritative support habits: read the prompt first, then the exhibits, then calculate.
- Workpaper discipline: label assumptions, show computations, and keep units straight.
- Partial-credit strategy and knowing when to move on.

**Applied skills**
- Extract only relevant facts from multi-exhibit simulations.
- Build clean calculations under time pressure.
- Tie audit and tax conclusions to support.
- Review answers for sign errors, period errors, and overconfident nonsense.

**Practice deliverables**
- Weekly mixed TBS set with annotated debrief.
- Two-page "simulation traps" catalog.
- Calculator and spreadsheet scratchpad standards.
- Final review packet of high-frequency formulas, procedures, thresholds, and decision rules.

## Chapter 7: Exam Strategy and Candidate Stamina
**Goal:** Pass the exam without letting the exam eat your entire personality.

**Key concepts**
- Section order options: foundation-first (often FAR early), confidence-first, and Discipline-paired sequencing.
- Pairing logic: FAR before BAR, AUD before ISC, REG before TCP when possible.
- Study-hour budgeting and honest weak-area allocation.
- MCQ pacing, TBS time protection, break strategy, and retake recovery.
- Burnout prevention, sleep, exercise, and the sacred art of closing the laptop.

**Applied skills**
- Build a section-specific pacing plan.
- Use practice scores as diagnostic data instead of a referendum on your soul.
- Decide when to reschedule, when to sit, and when to stop adding new material.
- Conduct a post-exam brain dump that helps the next section.

**Practice deliverables**
- Exam-day timing card for each section.
- Two full simulated exams before each scheduled section.
- 72-hour final review plan.
- Retake protocol: score report review, weak-area sprint, and new test date.

## Capstone: The CPA Candidate Binder
Assemble a concise binder that includes:
- Blueprint checklist for all required sections and your selected Discipline.
- FAR, AUD, REG, and Discipline one-page summaries.
- Error log highlights and corrected rules.
- Simulation debriefs and personal trap list.
- Exam calendar, pacing cards, Prometric checklist, and rest plan.
- A short note to Future You explaining why this work matters. Future You is tired, but quite reasonable.

## Assessment Ideas
- Section-specific MCQ sets with error logs tagged by blueprint area, skill level, and trap type.
- Weekly task-based simulation practice using exhibits, calculations, and partial-credit strategy.
- FAR/AUD/REG one-page rule sheets that candidates rebuild from memory.
- Discipline decision memo comparing BAR, ISC, and TCP against strengths, career goals, and prerequisite Core knowledge.
- Full simulated section days with pacing review, break strategy, and retake protocol if needed.
