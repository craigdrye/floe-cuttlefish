# Series 86 Exam Prep
**ID**: `series86` · **Discipline**: Finance · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare equity-research candidates for Series 86-style analysis: financial statements, valuation, economics, industry dynamics, modeling logic, and disciplined question triage.

## Course Design Notes
FINRA's Series 86/87 Research Analyst Exams assess the knowledge needed to prepare written or electronic communications analyzing equity securities, companies, and sectors. This syllabus focuses on Series 86 analytical competence while preserving the local Floe practice import from the failed-question review file as active recall material.

## Chapter 1: Exam Scope and Research Analyst Workflow
- **Key concepts**: Series 86 versus Series 87, SIE co-requisite, research analyst role, written communications, exam timing.
- **Practice questions**: What is being tested: accounting, valuation, economics, or research judgment? Which questions deserve immediate skip-and-return?
- **Work product**: a study tracker organized by FINRA outline area, error type, and confidence.

## Chapter 2: Financial Statements and Accounting Linkages
- **Key concepts**: income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement, accruals, working capital, depreciation, revenue recognition.
- **Practice questions**: How does this transaction affect all three statements? What does cash flow reveal that earnings hides?
- **Work product**: a three-statement linkage drill with journal-style explanation.

## Chapter 3: Ratios, Margins, and Quality of Earnings
- **Key concepts**: gross margin, operating margin, ROE, ROIC, leverage, liquidity, inventory turnover, receivables quality.
- **Practice questions**: Which ratio explains the change? Is the improvement operational or accounting-driven? What red flag appears?
- **Work product**: a ratio dashboard for a fictional company with interpretation notes.

## Chapter 4: Forecasting and Earnings Drivers
- **Key concepts**: revenue drivers, volume, price, mix, cost structure, operating leverage, EPS, dilution, consensus expectations.
- **Practice questions**: Which assumption moves EPS most? What happens if margin compresses? Is the forecast internally consistent?
- **Work product**: a driver-based forecast with sensitivity table.

## Chapter 5: Valuation Methods
- **Key concepts**: comparable companies, precedent transactions, DCF, multiples, WACC, terminal value, free cash flow.
- **Practice questions**: Which multiple is appropriate? What assumption drives the DCF? Does the valuation match the business model?
- **Work product**: a valuation summary triangulating comps, DCF, and sanity checks.

## Chapter 6: Economics, Markets, and Industry Analysis
- **Key concepts**: supply and demand, interest rates, inflation, FX, cyclicality, regulation, industry structure, competitive advantage.
- **Practice questions**: What macro variable matters? Is the company cyclical or defensive? Which industry driver changes the thesis?
- **Work product**: an industry driver map with catalysts and risks.

## Chapter 7: Research Logic and Investment Conclusions
- **Key concepts**: thesis, variant perception, catalysts, risk factors, estimate revisions, target price, recommendation logic.
- **Practice questions**: What must be true for the recommendation? What would disconfirm the thesis? Which risk is material?
- **Work product**: a one-page equity note with thesis, valuation, catalysts, risks, and conclusion.

## Chapter 8: Exam Practice, Error Review, and Internal Floe Resources
- **Key concepts**: calculation triage, distractor detection, formula fluency, failed-question review, active recall, timed sets.
- **Practice questions**: Was the miss conceptual, arithmetic, wording, or timing? Which formula needs repetition? What clue did the question provide?
- **Work product**: a failed-question log using the imported Floe review questions and manual reference.

## Capstone
Complete a timed mixed Series 86 practice set, build a correction log, write a mini equity note from a provided company packet, and identify the next three study drills.

## Assessment Ideas
- Accounting drills graded on statement linkage and precision.
- Valuation exercises graded on method selection and assumption sensitivity.
- Capstone graded on timed accuracy, error diagnosis, and investment logic.
