# Quant Interview Core
**ID**: `quant` · **Discipline**: Finance · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare candidates for first-round quant trading, quant research, and quant developer interviews by building fluency in probability, expected value, counting, mental math, puzzles, market intuition, and clear explanation under pressure.

## Course Design Notes
University career guidance and quant prep resources consistently point to probability, statistics, logic puzzles, mental math, coding, and finance intuition as core screening areas. This syllabus keeps the technical foundation compact but rigorous, with repeated practice in explaining assumptions and checking answers aloud.

## Chapter 1: Interview Map and Problem-Solving Protocol
- **Key concepts**: role types, interviewer signals, clarifying assumptions, problem representation, verbal reasoning, answer checks.
- **Practice questions**: What is being tested? What assumptions are safe? What simple case can verify the approach?
- **Work product**: a personal interview protocol for clarifying, solving, checking, and explaining.

## Chapter 2: Probability Foundations
- **Key concepts**: sample spaces, independence, conditional probability, Bayes' rule, complements, expectation.
- **Practice questions**: Are the events independent? What is easier to count: success or failure? What changes after new information?
- **Work product**: a probability drill set with setup, calculation, and sanity check.

## Chapter 3: Counting and Combinatorics
- **Key concepts**: permutations, combinations, inclusion-exclusion, symmetry, overcounting, recursive counting.
- **Practice questions**: What is being counted exactly once? Which arrangements are indistinguishable? Where is the hidden constraint?
- **Work product**: a counting-pattern notebook grouped by technique.

## Chapter 4: Expected Value and Games
- **Key concepts**: fair games, linearity of expectation, stopping rules, conditional expectation, payoff tables, risk-neutral thinking.
- **Practice questions**: What is the expected payoff? Does optional stopping change the value? Which strategy dominates?
- **Work product**: a fair-game analysis with payoff model and decision rule.

## Chapter 5: Brainteasers and Invariants
- **Key concepts**: parity, invariants, contradiction, extremal arguments, information puzzles, state compression.
- **Practice questions**: What quantity never changes? What would contradiction prove? Which small case reveals the pattern?
- **Work product**: five puzzle explanations that name the reusable reasoning move.

## Chapter 6: Mental Math, Estimation, and Market Numeracy
- **Key concepts**: fast arithmetic, approximations, orders of magnitude, rates, compounding, bid-ask intuition, risk/reward.
- **Practice questions**: Is the answer plausible? Which approximation preserves the decision? What market friction matters?
- **Work product**: a timed mental-math and estimation log with recurring errors.

## Chapter 7: Coding and Data Screen Basics
- **Key concepts**: arrays, hash maps, sorting, dynamic programming basics, simulation, pseudocode, edge cases, testing.
- **Practice questions**: What data structure fits? What is the time complexity? What edge case breaks the first solution?
- **Work product**: five interview solutions with explanation, complexity, and test cases.

## Chapter 8: Mock Interviews and Recovery
- **Key concepts**: timed practice, hint response, mistake recovery, communication, stress control, post-mortem review.
- **Practice questions**: Where did the solution stall? Did the hint reveal a missed pattern? What should be drilled next?
- **Work product**: a mock-interview review sheet with question type, miss type, and next drill.

## Capstone
Complete a 60-minute mixed quant screen: probability, counting, expected value, one puzzle, one market-estimation prompt, and one coding prompt. Submit answers, reasoning notes, and a correction log.

## Assessment Ideas
- Probability and counting sets graded on setup quality and correctness.
- Puzzle explanations graded on transferable reasoning rather than memorized answers.
- Capstone graded on accuracy, pacing, communication, and recovery after hints.
