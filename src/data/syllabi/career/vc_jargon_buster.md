# VC Jargon Buster
**ID**: `vcJargon` - **Discipline**: Finance - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Teach learners to translate venture-capital shorthand into the real diligence task underneath it. The course is for people who hear phrases about ARR quality, runway, moat, cohorts, allocation, strategic interest, or term sheets and need to know what analysis is actually being requested.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test startup diligence language, founder updates, partner comments, investment memo shorthand, cap-table terms, revenue quality, customer evidence, or venture decision-making. The voice can be witty, but the learning goal is practical: turn vague partner-speak into a next action.

## Chapter 1: Revenue, ARR, and Unit Economics
- **Key concepts**: ARR, MRR, contracted revenue, expansion, churn, CAC payback, gross retention, net retention.
- **Practice questions**: What number needs verification? What makes revenue repeatable? Which metric is vanity?
- **Work output**: a diligence note separating reported revenue from durable recurring revenue.

## Chapter 2: Customers, Cohorts, and Adoption
- **Key concepts**: cohort behavior, renewal intent, implementation friction, active usage, reference checks.
- **Practice questions**: Are customers expanding? Would they renew? Is usage deep or superficial?
- **Work output**: customer-call guide with renewal, usage, budget, and switching-cost questions.

## Chapter 3: Market and Moat
- **Key concepts**: TAM, SAM, SOM, competitive intensity, defensibility, workflow stickiness, network effects.
- **Practice questions**: Is the market estimate grounded in real buyers? Is the moat durable or just a nice feature?
- **Work output**: market-sizing memo with assumptions, buyer segments, and risks.

## Chapter 4: Burn, Runway, and Financing Risk
- **Key concepts**: burn rate, runway, hiring plan, downside case, financing cliff, bridge round.
- **Practice questions**: What happens if growth misses? When does cash run out? What assumptions drive survival?
- **Work output**: base/downside runway model with hiring and revenue sensitivities.

## Chapter 5: Terms, Ownership, and Allocation
- **Key concepts**: pre-money, post-money, liquidation preference, pro rata, board rights, allocation, ownership target.
- **Practice questions**: Does the allocation matter? Are terms standard? Who wins in downside outcomes?
- **Work output**: term-sheet summary explaining economics, control, and common-share impact.

## Capstone
Translate a messy partner request into a one-page diligence workplan: questions to answer, evidence to gather, model tabs to build, and risks to flag.
