# AML / CAMS
**ID**: `amlCompliance` · **Discipline**: Finance · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Train learners to work through anti-money-laundering and financial-crime problems with clear risk logic, careful evidence handling, and defensible escalation notes. The course supports CAMS-style preparation while staying grounded in practical analyst work.

## Course Design Notes
ACAMS CAMS materials, FATF standards, FinCEN guidance, and modern financial-crime programs emphasize typologies, customer due diligence, sanctions, monitoring, investigations, reporting, and governance. This syllabus adds analyst workflow, narrative quality, and quality-control habits because AML work lives or dies in the handoff.

## Chapter 1: Financial Crime Landscape
- **Key concepts**: money laundering stages, terrorist financing, proliferation financing, sanctions evasion, predicate crimes, typologies.
- **Practice questions**: What is the suspected financial-crime risk? Which pattern points to placement, layering, or integration? What facts are still missing?
- **Work product**: a typology map linking red flags to likely risk scenarios and evidence needs.

## Chapter 2: Customer Due Diligence and KYC
- **Key concepts**: customer identification, beneficial ownership, source of funds, source of wealth, expected activity, risk rating.
- **Practice questions**: Does the customer profile support the activity? Which ownership fact changes the risk? What documentation is enough?
- **Work product**: a customer risk memo with profile, expected behavior, gaps, and risk rating rationale.

## Chapter 3: Sanctions, PEPs, and Screening
- **Key concepts**: sanctions lists, politically exposed persons, false positives, fuzzy matching, geography risk, escalation thresholds.
- **Practice questions**: Is this a true match? What evidence distinguishes the customer from a listed person? When should funds be held or escalated?
- **Work product**: a screening disposition note with match logic and supporting evidence.

## Chapter 4: Transaction Monitoring
- **Key concepts**: structuring, velocity, round-dollar activity, wires, cash-intensive businesses, mule networks, trade-based laundering.
- **Practice questions**: Which activity is unusual for this profile? What baseline should be used? What benign explanation is plausible?
- **Work product**: an alert review worksheet that separates observed behavior, corroborating evidence, and conclusion.

## Chapter 5: Investigations and Case Building
- **Key concepts**: case chronology, link analysis, evidence sufficiency, internal records, adverse media, investigative scope.
- **Practice questions**: What is the narrative? Which facts support suspicion? Which facts reduce concern?
- **Work product**: an investigation summary with timeline, parties, activity, red flags, and disposition recommendation.

## Chapter 6: Suspicious Activity Reporting and Documentation
- **Key concepts**: SAR logic, narrative structure, escalation, confidentiality, audit trail, decision defensibility.
- **Practice questions**: What should be reported? What should not be overstated? Can another reviewer reconstruct the decision?
- **Work product**: a mock suspicious-activity narrative with clear who, what, when, where, why, and how.

## Chapter 7: AML Program Governance
- **Key concepts**: risk assessment, policies, controls, training, independent testing, model tuning, quality assurance.
- **Practice questions**: Which control failed? What metric shows monitoring quality? What issue should be remediated first?
- **Work product**: a control gap tracker with owner, risk, evidence, remediation, and due date.

## Chapter 8: Advanced Typologies and Emerging Risk
- **Key concepts**: crypto exposure, trade finance, correspondent banking, shell companies, human trafficking finance, cross-border risk.
- **Practice questions**: What makes this pattern harder to detect? Which data source would clarify the risk? How should risk appetite shape the response?
- **Work product**: a typology briefing for one advanced risk area with red flags and analyst questions.

## Capstone
Complete an AML case file from customer profile through alert review, investigation, escalation decision, SAR-style narrative, and quality-control checklist. Include a short reflection on what evidence would change the decision.

## Assessment Ideas
- KYC and screening exercises graded on evidence and rationale.
- Alert reviews graded on proportionality and documentation quality.
- Capstone graded on narrative clarity, risk logic, and defensible escalation.
