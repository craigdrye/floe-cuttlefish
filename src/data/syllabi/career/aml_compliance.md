# AML / CAMS
**ID**: `amlCompliance` · **Discipline**: Finance

## Course Philosophy
This syllabus trains anti-money-laundering analysts to think clearly about customer risk, transaction patterns, escalation discipline, and defensible documentation.

## Module 1: KYC and Customer Risk
- Customer due diligence, beneficial ownership, and source-of-funds basics
- Sanctions, PEP exposure, geography risk, and industry risk
- When a customer profile supports or contradicts expected activity

## Module 2: Monitoring and Alert Review
- Transaction patterns, velocity, structuring, and unusual behavior
- False positives, corroborating evidence, and alert disposition
- Writing clear escalation notes and preserving an audit trail

## Module 3: Investigations and Reporting
- Case-building, narrative discipline, and red-flag synthesis
- Suspicious activity escalation and internal handoff logic
- Governance, regulator expectations, and quality-control habits

## Stretch Zone
- Trade-based laundering, mule networks, crypto risk, and cross-border typologies
