# SOCRA / ACRP Clinical Research
**ID**: `socraAcrp` | **Discipline**: Healthcare | **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners for SOCRA and ACRP-style clinical research qualification study by building practical fluency in Good Clinical Practice, participant protection, protocol execution, documentation, safety reporting, monitoring, investigational product accountability, and study closeout.

## Course Design Notes
SOCRA CCRP materials, ACRP certification handbooks, ICH E6 Good Clinical Practice, FDA guidance, and clinical research training programs emphasize ethical conduct, investigator responsibilities, informed consent, IRB oversight, protocol compliance, data integrity, safety reporting, investigational product control, monitoring, and essential documents. This syllabus turns those requirements into site-level judgment and inspection-ready habits.

## Chapter 1: GCP Foundations and Research Responsibilities
- **Key concepts**: Good Clinical Practice, sponsor, investigator, IRB, ethics committee, monitor, delegation, protocol, essential documents.
- **Practice questions**: Which party is responsible? Was the task properly delegated? What evidence shows the study follows GCP?
- **Work product**: a responsibility matrix for sponsor, investigator, site staff, monitor, and IRB activities.

## Chapter 2: Informed Consent and Participant Protection
- **Key concepts**: informed consent, voluntariness, comprehension, privacy, vulnerable populations, re-consent, documentation.
- **Practice questions**: Was consent obtained before study procedures? What must be explained again? Which consent note is complete?
- **Work product**: an informed-consent process checklist and source note.

## Chapter 3: IRB Review and Essential Regulatory Documents
- **Key concepts**: IRB approval, continuing review, amendments, investigator brochure, financial disclosure, delegation log, training log.
- **Practice questions**: What requires IRB approval before implementation? Which document belongs in the regulatory file? What is expired or missing?
- **Work product**: a regulatory binder review checklist with missing-item actions.

## Chapter 4: Eligibility, Protocol Visits, and Deviations
- **Key concepts**: inclusion criteria, exclusion criteria, screening, enrollment, visit windows, protocol deviation, violation, waiver.
- **Practice questions**: Is the participant eligible? Which procedure is out of window? Does the issue require sponsor or IRB reporting?
- **Work product**: an eligibility worksheet and protocol deviation assessment.

## Chapter 5: Source Documentation and Data Integrity
- **Key concepts**: source documents, CRF, eCRF, ALCOA-C, corrections, audit trail, query resolution, source-to-data verification.
- **Practice questions**: What is the source of truth? Is the correction attributable and traceable? How should the query be answered?
- **Work product**: a source document review with CRF reconciliation and query responses.

## Chapter 6: Safety Reporting and Medical Oversight
- **Key concepts**: adverse event, serious adverse event, unexpected event, causality, severity, relatedness, follow-up, expedited reporting.
- **Practice questions**: Is the event reportable? What timeline applies? Which facts are needed for medical assessment?
- **Work product**: an adverse-event narrative with reporting decision, missing data, and escalation timeline.

## Chapter 7: Investigational Product Accountability
- **Key concepts**: investigational product, randomization, blinding, temperature excursion, receipt, dispensing, returns, reconciliation.
- **Practice questions**: Does the accountability log reconcile? What threatens the blind? How should a temperature excursion be handled?
- **Work product**: an investigational product accountability reconciliation and issue note.

## Chapter 8: Monitoring, Audit Readiness, and Closeout
- **Key concepts**: monitoring visit, source data verification, findings, CAPA, closeout visit, record retention, archive, inspection readiness.
- **Practice questions**: What will the monitor verify? Which finding needs corrective action? What must be resolved before closeout?
- **Work product**: a monitoring follow-up letter, CAPA tracker, and closeout readiness checklist.

## Capstone
Complete a simulated study file from screening through closeout: confirm consent and eligibility, document source data, classify a protocol deviation, process an adverse event, reconcile investigational product, respond to monitoring findings, and prepare a closeout checklist.

## Assessment Ideas
- Consent and eligibility exercises graded on participant protection and protocol accuracy.
- Documentation exercises graded on ALCOA-C principles, traceability, and query quality.
- Capstone graded on GCP judgment, safety escalation, investigational product control, and inspection readiness.
