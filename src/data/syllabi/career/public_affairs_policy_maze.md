# Public Affairs: Policy Maze
**ID**: `publicAffairs` · **Discipline**: Public Affairs / Government Relations

## Course Aim
Public affairs is not just "knowing politics." It is the disciplined work of finding the real decision point, understanding who can move it, building a lawful and credible case, and helping an organization act before the window closes. This course trains professionals to navigate U.S., U.K., and EU policy systems without confusing access with influence, noise with risk, or lobbying with improvisation.

## Course Design Notes
Keep this course workplace-ready and deliverable-driven. Questions should route here when they ask learners to map authority, identify a decision window, compare U.S./U.K./EU processes, prepare advocacy materials, manage compliance risk, or brief executives without partisan drift.

## Who This Is For
- Public affairs, government relations, policy, communications, legal, compliance, ESG, nonprofit advocacy, trade association, and regulatory affairs professionals
- Career changers who need workplace-ready skills in lobbying strategy, issue monitoring, stakeholder engagement, and political risk
- Managers who brief executives, coalitions, boards, or clients on policy threats and opportunities

## Learning Outcomes
Learners will be able to:
- Map how a policy decision will actually be made across legislative, executive, regulatory, judicial, and subnational venues
- Identify stakeholders by authority, incentives, access, salience, legitimacy, opposition risk, and coalition potential
- Build an advocacy plan with messages, evidence, channels, timing, owners, and compliance controls
- Distinguish lawful advocacy, reportable lobbying, campaign activity, gifts/hospitality risk, procurement risk, and reputational red lines
- Compare U.S., U.K., and EU operating models and adapt engagement tactics to each system
- Produce clear workplace deliverables: a policy brief, stakeholder map, meeting memo, consultation response, risk dashboard, and executive recommendation

## Chapter 1: The Public Affairs Operating System
- **Key Concepts**: public affairs vs. lobbying vs. advocacy vs. government relations; issue lifecycles; venues; windows of influence; policy entrepreneurs; inside and outside strategies
- **Workplace Skills**: translating vague business concern into a policy question; finding the live decision point; building an issue intake note
- **Applied Deliverable**: one-page issue triage memo naming the decision, owner, deadline, affected interests, and recommended next step

## Chapter 2: Policy Process and Institutional Power
- **Key Concepts**: bills, committees, amendments, appropriations, delegated legislation, agency rulemaking, consultations, impact assessments, regulators, courts, procurement, enforcement discretion, implementation bottlenecks
- **Workplace Skills**: reading legislative calendars and regulatory agendas; separating formal authority from practical leverage; spotting when "monitor" must become "act"
- **Applied Deliverable**: process map for one policy file, with decision gates, likely timelines, and escalation triggers

## Chapter 3: Stakeholder, Coalition, and Opposition Mapping
- **Key Concepts**: stakeholder salience, veto players, grasstops and grassroots influence, trade associations, unions, NGOs, think tanks, journalists, professional bodies, civil servants, regulators, member-state authorities, and affected communities
- **Workplace Skills**: interview planning; influence scoring; coalition due diligence; opposition analysis; internal alignment across legal, comms, product, finance, and operations
- **Applied Deliverable**: stakeholder map with priority tiers, relationship owners, proof points, risks, and a first-contact plan

## Chapter 4: Evidence, Messaging, and Policy Argument
- **Key Concepts**: theory of change, problem framing, regulatory burden, distributional effects, impact evidence, case studies, economic claims, public-interest tests, message discipline, narrative risk, and credibility
- **Workplace Skills**: turning technical material into policy-relevant evidence; writing concise asks; preparing Q&A for hostile questions; briefing executives without overclaiming
- **Applied Deliverable**: two-page policy brief with a clear ask, supporting evidence, counterargument response, and source notes

## Chapter 5: Lobbying Campaign Design and Engagement Tactics
- **Key Concepts**: direct lobbying, indirect advocacy, earned media, digital campaigns, coalition letters, testimony, consultation responses, site visits, roundtables, parliamentary questions, committee hearings, regulator meetings, and public comment strategies
- **Workplace Skills**: sequencing tactics against the decision calendar; preparing meeting objectives; assigning spokespeople; creating follow-up discipline; measuring influence without vanity metrics
- **Applied Deliverable**: 90-day campaign plan with audiences, channels, milestones, dependencies, compliance checks, and success measures

## Chapter 6: Ethics, Compliance, and Transparency
- **Key Concepts**: registration and disclosure, gifts and hospitality, campaign finance, PACs, political donations, pay-to-play risk, conflicts of interest, procurement restrictions, FARA risk, revolving-door rules, codes of conduct, public trust, and documentation
- **Workplace Skills**: knowing when to call legal/compliance; maintaining contact logs; pre-clearing events and hospitality; separating lobbying from campaign activity; drafting defensible disclosure notes
- **Applied Deliverable**: compliance checklist for a proposed advocacy campaign, including red flags, approvals, records, and escalation paths

## Chapter 7: U.S. Operating Model
- **Key Concepts**: Congress, committees, caucuses, federal agencies, notice-and-comment rulemaking, OMB review, federalism, state attorneys general, ballot initiatives, campaign finance, the Lobbying Disclosure Act, FARA, and congressional gift/travel limits
- **Workplace Skills**: reading bills and Federal Register notices; preparing comment letters; monitoring state-federal interaction; briefing leadership on election and agency-transition risk
- **Applied Deliverable**: U.S. federal-and-state issue tracker with rulemaking, legislative, enforcement, and litigation lanes

## Chapter 8: U.K. Operating Model
- **Key Concepts**: Westminster, Whitehall, ministers, special advisers, select committees, consultations, statutory instruments, devolved administrations, parliamentary procedure, party conferences, the Office of the Registrar of Consultant Lobbyists, and public affairs codes
- **Workplace Skills**: identifying departmental ownership; preparing ministerial and civil-service engagement; adapting to Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland; managing consultant lobbyist registration questions
- **Applied Deliverable**: U.K. engagement plan for a consultation or bill stage, including devolved implications and transparency obligations

## Chapter 9: EU Operating Model
- **Key Concepts**: European Commission agenda-setting, Parliament committees and rapporteurs, Council and member-state positions, trilogues, delegated and implementing acts, Better Regulation, impact assessments, public consultations, EU Transparency Register, and Brussels coalition work
- **Workplace Skills**: tracking a dossier across institutions; identifying access points before a proposal is locked; building member-state strategy; writing evidence for Commission consultations
- **Applied Deliverable**: EU dossier brief with institutional map, rapporteur/shadow rapporteur positions, Council dynamics, consultation status, and likely compromise zones

## Chapter 10: Regulatory Affairs and Implementation Risk
- **Key Concepts**: regulatory strategy, supervision, guidance, enforcement priorities, compliance dates, standards bodies, audits, judicial review, litigation risk, market access, licensing, and operational readiness
- **Workplace Skills**: translating policy change into business impact; connecting advocacy with compliance operations; preparing implementation scenarios; avoiding "win the amendment, lose the rollout" thinking
- **Applied Deliverable**: regulatory impact memo with operational owners, cost/risk ranges, mitigation options, and decision needs

## Chapter 11: Political Risk, Monitoring, and Executive Briefing
- **Key Concepts**: election scenarios, geopolitical spillover, issue heat, reputational exposure, activist campaigns, media cycles, coalition fracture, regulatory calendars, and board-level risk appetite
- **Workplace Skills**: building signal filters; writing nonpartisan updates; distinguishing probability from impact; escalating uncertainty; presenting options instead of predictions
- **Applied Deliverable**: executive risk dashboard with top issues, movement since last update, confidence level, recommended actions, and watchlist items

## Chapter 12: Policy Maze Simulation
- **Scenario**: a cross-border organization faces a proposed rule, a parliamentary inquiry, NGO pressure, and internal disagreement over how aggressively to engage
- **Team Tasks**: map the decision system, select lawful tactics, build a coalition plan, prepare a meeting memo, draft a public-facing position, and brief executives on risks
- **Final Deliverables**: advocacy strategy deck, compliance log, stakeholder map, consultation response outline, and five-minute executive recommendation

## Capstone
Learners complete the policy maze simulation as an integrated workplace exercise: decision map, stakeholder analysis, lawful engagement plan, compliance log, consultation or public-comment outline, and executive recommendation.

## Assessment Ideas
- **Policy Process Map (15%)**: correctly identifies authority, timing, decision gates, and uncertainty
- **Stakeholder and Coalition Map (15%)**: prioritizes actors with evidence and names relationship risks
- **Policy Brief and Meeting Memo (20%)**: states the ask, evidence, objections, and follow-up discipline clearly
- **Compliance Checklist (15%)**: spots disclosure, gifts, donations, procurement, and conflict risks
- **Regional Comparison Brief (15%)**: adapts strategy across U.S., U.K., and EU systems without flattening differences
- **Capstone Advocacy Plan (20%)**: integrates strategy, ethics, timing, deliverables, and executive judgment
