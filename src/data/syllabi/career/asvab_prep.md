# ASVAB: Armed Services Aptitude Prep
**ID**: `asvabPrep` · **Discipline**: Test Prep

## Course Aim
The ASVAB (Armed Services Vocational Aptitude Battery) is the test that opens the door to a military career, and roughly a million people take it every year. It does two jobs at once. Four of its subtests combine into your AFQT score, the percentile that decides whether you can enlist at all. The other subtests roll up into "line scores" that decide which jobs you actually qualify for, from mechanic to medic to electronics technician. This course treats the ASVAB exactly the way a recruit should: a beatable, learnable exam, not a verdict on how smart you are.

The tone here is warm and plain-spoken. We assume you may not have touched algebra since high school, that "sublimation" and "fulcrum" might be fuzzy memories, and that nobody ever explained what a percentile really is. That is fine. Each chapter rebuilds the underlying idea from the ground up, then shows you the kind of question the test will actually ask. You learn the concept, you see the trap, you practice the move.

By the time you finish, you should be able to walk into the testing center knowing what every subtest measures, how the scoring works, which sections matter most for your goal job, and how to squeeze points out of questions you are not sure about. The goal is not just a passing AFQT. It is a score high enough to qualify for the career you actually want.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test ASVAB content review or test-taking strategy: the four AFQT subtests (Arithmetic Reasoning, Mathematics Knowledge, Word Knowledge, Paragraph Comprehension), the technical subtests (General Science, Electronics Information, Auto & Shop Information, Mechanical Comprehension, Assembling Objects), AFQT and line-score mechanics, or pacing on the CAT versus paper versions. Keep explanations beginner-friendly but never wrong: simplify the language, not the facts. Every wrong answer should be a real misconception a test-taker holds, with a specific reason it fails and a forward hint that nudges toward the right method. Favor concrete, military-flavored scenarios (convoys, barracks, rifles, recruits) so the math and science feel grounded rather than abstract.

## Chapter 1: The ASVAB Game Plan
**Core questions:** What is the AFQT and which four subtests build it? What does a percentile score actually mean? How should I guess and pace on the adaptive version?
**Key concepts:** AFQT vs. line scores; percentile scoring (1-99); no penalty for wrong answers; CAT (computer-adaptive) vs. P&P (paper) formats; branch minimum cutoffs.
**Common traps:** Thinking the AFQT averages all nine subtests; reading a percentile as a percent-correct; leaving questions blank to "avoid a penalty"; assuming you can revisit questions on the CAT.

## Chapter 2: Arithmetic Reasoning
**Core questions:** How do I set up distance/rate/time, percent, ratio, unit-rate, and interest word problems?
**Key concepts:** distance = rate × time; percent of a quantity; proportions and scaling factors; unit rate (cost per unit); simple interest (I = P × r × t); averages.
**Common traps:** Answering the wrong half of the question (who left vs. who remained); subtracting a count when you needed a percent; inverting a rate; confusing simple and compound interest.

## Chapter 3: Mathematics Knowledge
**Core questions:** What is the order of operations? How do I solve a linear equation, find area, use the Pythagorean theorem, and apply exponent rules?
**Key concepts:** PEMDAS; isolating a variable by undoing operations; area vs. perimeter and square units; a² + b² = c² and the 3-4-5 family; adding exponents when multiplying like bases.
**Common traps:** Working strictly left to right; forgetting to divide after moving a constant; confusing area with perimeter; stopping at c² instead of taking the root; multiplying exponents when you should add.

## Chapter 4: Word Knowledge
**Core questions:** What does this word most nearly mean, alone or in context? How do roots and prefixes help when I do not know a word?
**Key concepts:** synonyms vs. antonyms; meaning in context; common roots and prefixes (bene-, vigil-); mental sentence test.
**Common traps:** Picking the planted antonym; choosing a word that matches the tone but not the meaning; letting an unfamiliar word panic you instead of decoding its parts.

## Chapter 5: Paragraph Comprehension
**Core questions:** What is the main idea, the stated detail, the supported inference, and the author's purpose?
**Key concepts:** umbrella main idea vs. supporting detail; literal stated facts; inference tethered to evidence; inform/instruct vs. persuade vs. entertain vs. express.
**Common traps:** Promoting one detail to main idea; twisting timing on stated-detail questions (before vs. after); inferring beyond the evidence; mismatching the author's purpose to the passage form.

## Chapter 6: General Science
**Core questions:** What are the basic roles of cell organelles, phase changes, Earth/space processes, and human body systems?
**Key concepts:** mitochondria/nucleus/ribosome/membrane roles; sublimation and the phase-change map; tides from the Moon's gravity; circulatory vs. respiratory vs. digestive vs. nervous systems.
**Common traps:** Swapping organelle jobs; confusing sublimation with evaporation or melting; blaming tides on wind or earthquakes; assigning oxygen transport to the lungs instead of the bloodstream.

## Chapter 7: Electronics Information
**Core questions:** How does Ohm's law work? What conducts vs. insulates? What is the difference between series and parallel circuits and between AC and DC?
**Key concepts:** V = I × R and the Ohm's-law triangle; metals conduct, nonmetals insulate; series (one path) vs. parallel (separate paths); AC at the wall, DC from batteries; units (volt, ampere, ohm, watt).
**Common traps:** Multiplying instead of dividing in Ohm's law; calling rubber or glass a conductor; expecting a series string to keep glowing after one break; confusing the units for voltage, current, resistance, and power.

## Chapter 8: Auto & Shop Information
**Core questions:** What do engine oil and the transmission do? Which tool or fastener fits the job?
**Key concepts:** oil lubricates (coolant cools); the transmission transfers power through gear ratios; torque wrench vs. pipe wrench vs. pliers; screws vs. nails vs. rivets vs. glue.
**Common traps:** Thinking oil's main job is cooling; confusing transmission fluid with coolant; grabbing the wrong tool for a measured task; treating all fasteners as interchangeable.

## Chapter 9: Mechanical Comprehension
**Core questions:** How do levers, gears, pulleys, inclined planes, and pressure multiply or trade force?
**Key concepts:** lever arms and fulcrum placement; gear ratios trade speed for torque; a single fixed pulley only redirects force; inclined planes trade distance for force; pressure = force ÷ area.
**Common traps:** Putting the fulcrum in the wrong place; thinking a bigger driven gear spins faster; expecting a single fixed pulley to halve the force; believing a ramp reduces total work or makes a load lighter.

## Chapter 10: Assembling Objects
**Core questions:** What does this subtest actually measure, and how do I solve connection and puzzle items?
**Key concepts:** spatial visualization and mental rotation; connection items match labeled points; puzzle items assemble scattered pieces into a target outline.
**Common traps:** Treating it like a math or memory test; judging connection lines by length or position instead of endpoints; choosing by piece count or appearance instead of actual fit.

## Chapter 11: Test-Taking Strategy and Line Scores
**Core questions:** How do I use elimination and guessing? What are line scores and how should they shape my study plan? How does pacing differ across formats?
**Key concepts:** process of elimination and the no-penalty guessing rule; line scores qualify you for specific jobs; targeting study toward a goal job's composite; CAT vs. paper pacing rules; no calculators.
**Common traps:** Leaving blanks; believing "longest answer" or "first familiar answer" myths; studying only the AFQT four while ignoring the technical subtests your job needs; solving correctly but answering the wrong quantity.

## Capstone: Your ASVAB Battle Plan
Pull everything into one concrete plan you could hand a friend who is testing next month:
- A one-page map of all nine subtests, what each measures, and which feed the AFQT vs. line scores.
- Your target branch's AFQT cutoff and the specific line-score composite for the job you want, confirmed with a recruiter.
- A study schedule that secures a strong AFQT first, then doubles down on the technical subtests your goal job weights most.
- A formula and definition sheet you can rebuild from memory: distance/rate/time, PEMDAS, Pythagorean triples, Ohm's law, simple machines, key science roles, and common roots.
- A personal trap list and pacing card: no blanks, read the final question, watch the units, and never multiply when Ohm's law wants you to divide.
