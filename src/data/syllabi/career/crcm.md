# CRCM Banking Compliance
**ID**: `crcm` | **Discipline**: Finance | **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners for CRCM-style banking compliance work: mapping products to rules, handling complaints, monitoring controls, managing regulatory change, reporting to leadership, and applying major consumer, fair-lending, privacy, community, and financial-crime requirements.

## Course Design Notes
CRCM preparation and bank compliance programs emphasize risk assessment, product knowledge, regulatory coverage, testing, issue management, complaint trends, board reporting, and clear escalation. This syllabus follows that practical compliance-manager logic while adding rule-mapping drills and work products that mirror daily bank compliance responsibilities.

## Chapter 1: Compliance Program Foundations
- **Key concepts**: compliance management system, risk assessment, policies, procedures, training, testing, issue management, accountability.
- **Practice questions**: Which control should prevent the problem? What evidence proves the control worked? What issue needs escalation?
- **Work product**: a compliance program map linking risks, controls, owners, evidence, and reporting cadence.

## Chapter 2: Product-Rule Mapping
- **Key concepts**: deposit products, credit products, servicing, channels, disclosures, lifecycle events, rule inventory, change triggers.
- **Practice questions**: Which rules attach to this product? Where in the customer journey does the obligation arise? What system owns the data?
- **Work product**: a product-rule matrix for one bank product with requirements, owners, controls, and testing evidence.

## Chapter 3: Core Consumer Regulations
- **Key concepts**: Regulation Z, Regulation B, Regulation E, HMDA, RESPA, Reg CC, disclosures, timing, error resolution, adverse action.
- **Practice questions**: Which disclosure or notice is required? What timing applies? What data must be captured or reported?
- **Work product**: a consumer-regulation quick reference with trigger events, timing, required content, and control points.

## Chapter 4: Complaints, UDAAP, and Customer Harm
- **Key concepts**: complaint intake, root cause, UDAAP, remediation, marketing claims, fees, scripts, vulnerable customers.
- **Practice questions**: Is there customer harm? Does the practice create unfair, deceptive, or abusive risk? What trend should be escalated?
- **Work product**: a complaint trend memo with root cause, affected population, UDAAP analysis, and recommended action.

## Chapter 5: Fair Lending and Community Obligations
- **Key concepts**: fair lending, disparate treatment, disparate impact, redlining, pricing, underwriting, HMDA, CRA, SCRA, MLA.
- **Practice questions**: Which protected-class risk is present? What data should be reviewed? Does the program meet community or servicemember obligations?
- **Work product**: a fair-lending and community-risk dashboard with metrics, exceptions, and follow-up actions.

## Chapter 6: BSA/AML, Privacy, and Information Protection
- **Key concepts**: BSA/AML program, customer due diligence, suspicious activity, sanctions interface, GLBA, privacy notices, safeguarding.
- **Practice questions**: Where does compliance hand off to financial crimes? What customer information is protected? Which disclosure or safeguard applies?
- **Work product**: a cross-functional control map for BSA/AML, privacy, information security, and customer communications.

## Chapter 7: Monitoring, Testing, and Issue Management
- **Key concepts**: compliance monitoring, independent testing, sampling, control design, control effectiveness, findings, corrective action.
- **Practice questions**: What should be tested? Is the sample adequate? What evidence supports closure of the finding?
- **Work product**: a monitoring plan with scope, population, sample, test steps, exceptions, issue rating, and remediation tracking.

## Chapter 8: Regulatory Change and Board Reporting
- **Key concepts**: regulatory change management, impact assessment, implementation plans, policy updates, metrics, board reporting, examiner readiness.
- **Practice questions**: What changed? Which products and controls are affected? What should senior management or the board know?
- **Work product**: a regulatory-change impact memo and board-reporting page with risk, status, metrics, and decisions needed.

## Capstone
Build a compliance management package for a fictional bank product: product-rule matrix, consumer-regulation reference, complaint trend memo, fair-lending dashboard, BSA/AML and GLBA control map, monitoring plan, regulatory-change memo, and board-reporting page.

## Assessment Ideas
- Rule-mapping exercises graded on coverage, timing, and ownership.
- Complaint and UDAAP cases graded on harm analysis and remediation logic.
- Capstone graded on practical compliance judgment, control evidence, and executive-ready reporting.
