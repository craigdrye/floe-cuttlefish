# Venture Capital Roadmap
**ID**: `ventureCapitalRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Finance - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Train learners to think like early-stage investors: find promising markets, test founder claims, judge traction, understand deal terms, and write clear investment recommendations. The goal is practical venture judgment, not startup mystique.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test venture career skills, sourcing, diligence, market maps, startup metrics, investment memos, fund mechanics, or portfolio support. Keep the tone direct: what evidence would change the decision?

## Chapter 1: Venture Model and Fund Math
- **Key concepts**: power law, fund size, ownership target, reserves, management fees, carry, DPI, TVPI.
- **Practice questions**: How does the fund make money? What return does this deal need to matter? Why does ownership matter?
- **Work output**: fund-math note linking check size, ownership, exit value, and return potential.

## Chapter 2: Sourcing and Market Mapping
- **Key concepts**: thesis, founder network, category map, buyer pain, timing, wedge, competitive landscape.
- **Practice questions**: Why now? Which companies define the category? What makes this lead worth a meeting?
- **Work output**: market map with segments, key startups, incumbents, and open questions.

## Chapter 3: Startup Metrics and Diligence
- **Key concepts**: ARR, growth, retention, churn, CAC payback, burn, runway, cohort quality, usage.
- **Practice questions**: Which metric is real? What would customers confirm? Where could the data be misleading?
- **Work output**: diligence checklist separating reported claims from evidence needed.

## Chapter 4: Founder, Product, and Customer Evidence
- **Key concepts**: founder-market fit, product pull, reference calls, implementation friction, switching cost, roadmap risk.
- **Practice questions**: Is the founder learning fast? Are customers urgent or merely polite? What would make adoption stall?
- **Work output**: customer-call plan and founder assessment memo.

## Chapter 5: Terms, Memo, and Investment Decision
- **Key concepts**: valuation, pre-money, post-money, liquidation preference, pro rata, board rights, allocation, IC memo.
- **Practice questions**: Are terms acceptable? What is the core risk? What must the partnership believe?
- **Work output**: investment memo with thesis, evidence, risks, terms, and recommendation.

## Chapter 6: Portfolio Support and Follow-On Decisions
- **Key concepts**: board updates, KPI tracking, hiring support, bridge round, inside round, markups, reserves.
- **Practice questions**: What help is useful? When should the fund double down? What warning signs need action?
- **Work output**: portfolio update with metrics, asks, risks, and follow-on view.

## Capstone
Build a venture investment pack for a fictional startup: sourcing thesis, market map, diligence checklist, customer-call plan, fund-math note, and one-page investment recommendation.
