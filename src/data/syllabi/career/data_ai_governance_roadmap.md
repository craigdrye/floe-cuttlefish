# Data / AI Governance Roadmap
**ID**: `dataAiGovernanceRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Technology - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Teach learners to understand data and AI governance language in practical terms: lineage, access, model risk, evaluations, guardrails, drift, human oversight, and accountability. The goal is to turn abstract governance into launch and monitoring decisions.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test data stewardship, AI risk management, model lifecycle controls, privacy, lineage, evals, guardrails, impact assessment, incident response, or human-in-the-loop design. Keep the tone current but not buzzword-heavy.

## Chapter 1: Data Foundations
- **Key concepts**: data owner, steward, lineage, classification, quality, retention, access control.
- **Practice questions**: Where did the data come from? Who can use it? Is it fit for purpose?
- **Work output**: dataset governance card.

## Chapter 2: Model Risk and Use-Case Triage
- **Key concepts**: model inventory, intended use, impacted users, risk tier, impact assessment, accountability.
- **Practice questions**: What could go wrong? Who is affected? What review is required?
- **Work output**: AI use-case risk assessment.

## Chapter 3: Evaluation and Guardrails
- **Key concepts**: eval set, hallucination, bias, robustness, prompt injection, retrieval leakage, policy guardrail.
- **Practice questions**: What evidence supports launch? Which failure mode is still untested?
- **Work output**: eval and guardrail plan.

## Chapter 4: Monitoring and Drift
- **Key concepts**: drift, performance monitoring, incident trigger, versioning, retraining, retirement.
- **Practice questions**: Has performance changed? What requires escalation?
- **Work output**: monitoring checklist with thresholds.

## Chapter 5: Transparency and Human Oversight
- **Key concepts**: disclosure, explanation, appeal, human review, challenge process, audit trail.
- **Practice questions**: Can a person understand or contest the decision? Is oversight meaningful?
- **Work output**: user-facing governance note.

## Capstone
Prepare a launch gate packet for an AI feature: data provenance, risk tier, eval results, controls, monitoring, and residual-risk decision.
