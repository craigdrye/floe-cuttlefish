# Procurement and Contracting Roadmap
**ID**: `procurementContractingRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Operations - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners to support procurement and contracting work from requirement definition through solicitation, award, performance, changes, and closeout. The course emphasizes clear requirements, fair competition, source-selection discipline, contract administration, and practical risk management.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when learners need a career path through sourcing, public or private procurement, contract types, solicitations, proposals, evaluations, negotiations, vendor management, and contract files. Keep the tone concrete: every chapter should end in a work product someone could use.

## Chapter 1: Requirements and Acquisition Planning
- **Key concepts**: need statement, scope, market research, stakeholders, acquisition strategy, timeline, budget, approval path.
- **Practice questions**: What problem is being bought? Is the scope clear enough? What market evidence supports the approach?
- **Work output**: acquisition plan with requirement, market findings, schedule, risks, and decision points.

## Chapter 2: Sourcing and Solicitation
- **Key concepts**: competition, RFQ, RFP, statement of work, specifications, evaluation criteria, supplier questions, amendments.
- **Practice questions**: Does the solicitation match the requirement? Are criteria fair and measurable? What needs clarification?
- **Work output**: solicitation checklist with scope, instructions, criteria, due dates, and clarification log.

## Chapter 3: Pricing and Contract Types
- **Key concepts**: fixed price, cost reimbursement, time and materials, price analysis, cost realism, incentives, total cost.
- **Practice questions**: Which contract type fits the risk? Is the price reasonable? What cost driver could surprise the buyer?
- **Work output**: pricing and contract-type memo with rationale, assumptions, and negotiation issues.

## Chapter 4: Evaluation, Negotiation, and Award
- **Key concepts**: responsiveness, responsibility, technical evaluation, tradeoff, best value, negotiation objective, award file.
- **Practice questions**: Which offer best meets the need? What weakness matters? What must be documented before award?
- **Work output**: source-selection summary with evaluation results, tradeoff rationale, and award recommendation.

## Chapter 5: Contract Administration and Closeout
- **Key concepts**: kickoff, deliverables, performance monitoring, invoices, changes, disputes, vendor scorecards, closeout.
- **Practice questions**: Is the vendor performing? Is a change in scope or schedule? What evidence closes the file?
- **Work output**: contract administration tracker with deliverables, issues, changes, payments, and closeout actions.

## Capstone
Build a procurement file for a fictional purchase: acquisition plan, solicitation checklist, pricing and contract-type memo, source-selection summary, and contract administration tracker.
