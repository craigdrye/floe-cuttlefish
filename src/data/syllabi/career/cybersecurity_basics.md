# Cybersecurity Basics
**ID**: `cybersecurity` · **Discipline**: Technology

## Course Philosophy
This syllabus covers practical defensive security: common attack patterns, identity protection, and everyday operational habits that reduce risk.

## Module 1: Core Threats
- Phishing, credential theft, social engineering, and malware basics
- Attack surface thinking for individuals and small teams
- Why "low sophistication" attacks still work

## Module 2: Identity and Access
- Strong passwords, password managers, MFA, and recovery paths
- Least privilege, role-based access, and account hygiene
- Device security and update discipline

## Module 3: Response and Prevention
- Spotting suspicious behavior and reporting early
- Basic incident steps: isolate, preserve, escalate
- Security culture and habit formation

## Stretch Zone
- Network basics, cloud permissions, logging, and secure-by-default workflows
