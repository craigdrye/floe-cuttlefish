# Cybersecurity Basics
**ID**: `cybersecurity` · **Discipline**: Technology · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Teach practical defensive cybersecurity for people joining modern workplaces: common threats, identity protection, secure habits, incident response, and the language needed to work with security teams.

## Course Design Notes
NIST NICE, CISA guidance, and entry-level cybersecurity certificates emphasize risk awareness, identity, networks, threat recognition, incident response, and operational practice. This version keeps the scope beginner-friendly while making the course immediately useful for employees, analysts, founders, and career switchers.

## Chapter 1: Security Thinking and Risk
- **Key concepts**: confidentiality, integrity, availability, threat, vulnerability, likelihood, impact, attack surface.
- **Practice questions**: What are we protecting? Who might attack it? What would the cost of failure be?
- **Work product**: a simple risk register for a personal device, small business, or team workflow.

## Chapter 2: Phishing, Social Engineering, and Credential Theft
- **Key concepts**: phishing, pretexting, business email compromise, credential stuffing, suspicious links, reporting culture.
- **Practice questions**: What makes this message suspicious? Which part tries to create urgency? What should the recipient do next?
- **Work product**: a phishing triage guide with examples, red flags, and escalation steps.

## Chapter 3: Identity and Access
- **Key concepts**: password managers, MFA, passkeys, recovery codes, least privilege, role-based access, joiner-mover-leaver processes.
- **Practice questions**: Which accounts need the strongest protection? Who has access they no longer need? What happens if MFA is lost?
- **Work product**: an access hygiene checklist for a small team.

## Chapter 4: Devices, Networks, and Cloud Basics
- **Key concepts**: updates, endpoint protection, disk encryption, Wi-Fi risk, VPNs, cloud permissions, shared responsibility.
- **Practice questions**: What is managed by the user, employer, or cloud provider? Which device settings reduce the biggest risk?
- **Work product**: a secure setup checklist for a laptop, phone, and cloud workspace.

## Chapter 5: Malware, Ransomware, and Common Attacks
- **Key concepts**: malware, ransomware, drive-by downloads, malicious attachments, lateral movement, backups, blast radius.
- **Practice questions**: How could malware enter? What data would it reach? Which controls slow the spread?
- **Work product**: a prevention map linking attack path, control, owner, and evidence.

## Chapter 6: Logging, Monitoring, and Detection Basics
- **Key concepts**: logs, alerts, indicators of compromise, false positives, anomaly detection, ticket quality.
- **Practice questions**: What event should be logged? Which alert is urgent? What context would help an investigator?
- **Work product**: a sample incident ticket with timeline, affected assets, indicators, and current status.

## Chapter 7: Incident Response
- **Key concepts**: preparation, identification, containment, eradication, recovery, lessons learned, communication.
- **Practice questions**: What should be isolated first? Who needs to know? What evidence should be preserved?
- **Work product**: a one-page incident playbook for suspicious login, lost device, or phishing click.

## Chapter 8: Security Culture and Career Paths
- **Key concepts**: secure defaults, policy usability, risk communication, security awareness, SOC, GRC, cloud security, appsec.
- **Practice questions**: How do you make the safe path easier? Which role fits your strengths? What should a non-security employee know?
- **Work product**: a personal security learning plan with next certifications, labs, or workplace responsibilities.

## Capstone
Audit a small organization or fictional startup. Produce a risk register, identity checklist, phishing guide, incident playbook, and prioritized 30-day security improvement plan.

## Assessment Ideas
- Threat-recognition drills graded on reasoning and recommended action.
- Access and device checklists graded on practicality and risk coverage.
- Capstone graded on prioritization, clarity, and incident readiness.
