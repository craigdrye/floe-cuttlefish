# Medical Billing Jargon Buster
**ID**: `medicalBillingJargon` - **Discipline**: Healthcare - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Help learners decode payer and revenue-cycle language into the next useful action. The course covers denials, clean claims, modifiers, prior authorization, medical necessity, appeals, remittance advice, COB, underpayments, and the politely confusing language of reimbursement.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test billing shorthand, denial language, payer edits, claim status, appeal logic, documentation support, or revenue-cycle escalation. Keep the tone concrete: identify the blocker, find the evidence, choose the next workflow step.

## Chapter 1: Claim Basics and Clean Submission
- **Key concepts**: claim, encounter, CPT, ICD-10-CM, HCPCS, modifier, NPI, place of service, clean claim.
- **Practice questions**: What field is wrong? What documentation supports the service? What must be corrected before submission?
- **Work output**: clean-claim review checklist.

## Chapter 2: Denials and Remittance Language
- **Key concepts**: denial, rejection, CARC, RARC, EOB, ERA, allowed amount, patient responsibility, write-off.
- **Practice questions**: Did the payer deny or reject? Is the problem coding, coverage, authorization, eligibility, or documentation?
- **Work output**: denial triage note with root cause and next step.

## Chapter 3: Authorization and Medical Necessity
- **Key concepts**: prior authorization, referral, medical necessity, LCD/NCD, payer policy, retro authorization.
- **Practice questions**: Was authorization required? Does documentation meet policy? What can be appealed?
- **Work output**: authorization appeal packet outline.

## Chapter 4: Modifiers, Bundling, and Edits
- **Key concepts**: modifier logic, NCCI edits, bundled services, distinct procedural service, units, laterality.
- **Practice questions**: Does the modifier explain a real distinction? Is the edit correct? What documentation is needed?
- **Work output**: modifier-support note for a corrected claim or appeal.

## Chapter 5: Appeals, Underpayments, and Follow-Up
- **Key concepts**: appeal level, timely filing, corrected claim, reconsideration, contract rate, COB, secondary payer.
- **Practice questions**: What deadline applies? What evidence should go with the appeal? Is payment contractually correct?
- **Work output**: payer follow-up script and appeal evidence checklist.

## Capstone
Take a denial workqueue and classify each item by cause, owner, evidence needed, deadline, and next action.
