# Medical Reasoning
**ID**: `medical` · **Discipline**: Healthcare · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Build disciplined clinical reasoning for educational casework: gathering the right facts, forming a prioritized differential, recognizing danger, communicating uncertainty, and reducing diagnostic error. This is a reasoning and patient-safety course, not a treatment manual.

## Course Design Notes
The course draws from clinical reasoning education, AAMC entrustable professional activities, diagnostic-excellence work, and patient-safety frameworks. It is designed for learners who need better medical thinking habits: clinicians in training, healthcare-adjacent professionals, medical writers, health product teams, and advanced learners working through case simulations.

## Chapter 1: Clinical Reasoning Mindset
- **Key concepts**: chief concern, problem representation, illness scripts, working diagnosis, differential diagnosis, safety net.
- **Practice questions**: What is the one-sentence problem representation? Which diagnosis is most likely? Which diagnosis is most dangerous?
- **Work product**: a case summary with problem representation, prioritized differential, and immediate safety concerns.

## Chapter 2: Triage and Red Flags
- **Key concepts**: instability, ABCs, red flags, time-critical diagnoses, abnormal vital signs, escalation thresholds.
- **Practice questions**: Is the patient stable? What cannot wait? Which reassuring clue might be misleading?
- **Work product**: a triage note distinguishing immediate action, urgent evaluation, and routine follow-up.

## Chapter 3: History, Exam, and Data Gathering
- **Key concepts**: focused history, pertinent positives and negatives, symptom chronology, risk factors, physical exam limits.
- **Practice questions**: What question changes the differential? What finding would move a dangerous diagnosis up or down?
- **Work product**: a focused history-and-exam plan for chest pain, abdominal pain, headache, or shortness of breath.

## Chapter 4: Differential Diagnosis and Mechanism
- **Key concepts**: anatomical frameworks, pathophysiology, VINDICATE-style categories, competing hypotheses, base rates.
- **Practice questions**: What mechanism links the findings? Which diagnosis explains the whole pattern? Which is a tempting mismatch?
- **Work product**: a differential table with supporting features, opposing features, and next data needed.

## Chapter 5: Evidence, Tests, and Bayesian Updating
- **Key concepts**: pretest probability, sensitivity, specificity, likelihood ratios, false reassurance, test thresholds.
- **Practice questions**: Will this test change management? What does a negative result fail to exclude? What is the next best data point?
- **Work product**: a test-selection memo for a simulated case, including what result would change the plan.

## Chapter 6: Cognitive Bias and Diagnostic Error
- **Key concepts**: anchoring, premature closure, availability, confirmation bias, diagnostic momentum, handoff distortion.
- **Practice questions**: What assumption is carrying the case? What alternative has not been tested? What would make the current diagnosis wrong?
- **Work product**: a diagnostic pause checklist applied to three ambiguous cases.

## Chapter 7: Communication, Documentation, and Handoffs
- **Key concepts**: assessment and plan, uncertainty language, escalation, closed-loop communication, patient safety, documentation clarity.
- **Practice questions**: What does the next clinician need to know? What uncertainty should be explicit? What follow-up failure could harm the patient?
- **Work product**: a handoff note with problem representation, differential, pending data, contingency plan, and escalation trigger.

## Chapter 8: Case Conferences and Safety-Net Reasoning
- **Key concepts**: morbidity and mortality thinking, near-miss review, follow-up reliability, systems factors, reflective practice.
- **Practice questions**: Where did the reasoning go wrong? Was the error knowledge, process, communication, or system design?
- **Work product**: a diagnostic-error mini-review with contributing factors and safer next-time process.

## Capstone
Analyze three simulated cases: one urgent, one ambiguous outpatient-style case, and one handoff failure. For each, submit a problem representation, prioritized differential, test strategy, communication plan, and safety-net instructions.

## Assessment Ideas
- Differential exercises graded on prioritization, mechanism, and dangerous-miss awareness.
- Testing questions graded on decision relevance and false-reassurance handling.
- Capstone graded on safety, uncertainty communication, and diagnostic discipline.
