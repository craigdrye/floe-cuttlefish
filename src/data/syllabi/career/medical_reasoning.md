# Medical Reasoning
**ID**: `medical` · **Discipline**: Healthcare

## Course Philosophy
This syllabus develops disciplined clinical reasoning: gathering the right facts, weighing differential possibilities, and prioritizing safety when information is incomplete.

## Module 1: Triage and Initial Assessment
- Urgency, red flags, instability, and first-pass prioritization
- Symptoms vs signs, history-taking, and focused information gathering
- Recognizing when immediate escalation is required

## Module 2: Differential Diagnosis and Evidence
- Building and narrowing a differential with probability and mechanism
- Risk factors, test characteristics, and common cognitive biases
- Matching the next question or test to the decision at hand

## Module 3: Safety and Communication
- Handoffs, uncertainty communication, and documentation clarity
- Medication, allergy, and contraindication awareness
- Patient-safety thinking, follow-up logic, and harm prevention

## Stretch Zone
- Bayes in clinical settings, guideline tradeoffs, and ambiguous-case reasoning
