# Defense Budgeting Roadmap
**ID**: `defenseBudgetingRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Government - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Train learners to understand defense budgeting as a decision system connecting strategy, programs, appropriations, execution, contracts, and oversight. The goal is to make funding conversations clearer, legally safer, and more useful to program leaders.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when learners need a career path through PPBE, appropriations, spend plans, execution reviews, unfunded requirements, reprogramming, fiscal law, and budget briefings. Keep the focus on what an analyst must explain, document, and escalate.

## Chapter 1: Defense Money Flow
- **Key concepts**: authorization, appropriation, budget authority, apportionment, allotment, commitment, obligation, outlay.
- **Practice questions**: What stage is the money in? What action is legally available? Which number belongs in the brief?
- **Work output**: funds-flow map from congressional action through obligation and outlay.

## Chapter 2: PPBE and Program Decisions
- **Key concepts**: planning, programming, budgeting, execution, POM, BES, budget exhibits, program objective, tradeoff.
- **Practice questions**: Is the issue strategic, programmatic, budgetary, or execution-focused? Who owns the decision?
- **Work output**: PPBE decision note translating acronyms into decision, options, timing, and owner.

## Chapter 3: Appropriations and Fiscal Law
- **Key concepts**: purpose, time, amount, color of money, bona fide need, continuing resolution, expired funds, Anti-Deficiency Act.
- **Practice questions**: Is the right appropriation being used? Is the need current? What review is needed before action?
- **Work output**: fiscal-law risk checklist for a pressured funding request.

## Chapter 4: Spend Plans and Execution Reviews
- **Key concepts**: phasing, burn rate, obligation plan, variance, under-execution, carryover, invoice lag, corrective action.
- **Practice questions**: Is the variance timing or scope? What driver explains the miss? What action protects the program?
- **Work output**: execution variance brief with status, driver, risk, and recovery plan.

## Chapter 5: Budget Communication and Oversight
- **Key concepts**: unfunded requirements, reprogramming, audit trail, supporting documentation, congressional interest, leadership brief.
- **Practice questions**: What is the funding story? What evidence supports it? What decision or oversight concern must be surfaced?
- **Work output**: budget review packet with funding status, risks, decisions needed, and evidence gaps.

## Capstone
Prepare a defense budgeting analyst pack for a fictional program: funds-flow map, PPBE decision note, fiscal-law checklist, execution variance brief, and leadership budget review packet.
