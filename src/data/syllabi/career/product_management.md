# Product Management
**ID**: `productManagement` · **Discipline**: Career

## Course Philosophy
This syllabus trains product managers to move from vague feature talk to clear problem framing, prioritization, outcome metrics, and cross-functional decision-making.

## Module 1: User Problems and Discovery
- Jobs to be done, pain points, workflows, and unmet needs
- Interviews, lightweight research, and evidence quality
- Separating user requests from underlying problems

## Module 2: Prioritization and Roadmapping
- Impact vs effort, strategic fit, sequencing, and dependency logic
- MVP scoping, risk reduction, and staged rollout thinking
- Tradeoffs between growth, quality, speed, and platform work

## Module 3: Metrics and Decision-Making
- North-star metrics, guardrails, activation, retention, and funnel health
- Writing product hypotheses and success criteria
- Communicating decisions to design, engineering, leadership, and stakeholders

## Stretch Zone
- Pricing, experimentation, platform strategy, and product sense under ambiguity
