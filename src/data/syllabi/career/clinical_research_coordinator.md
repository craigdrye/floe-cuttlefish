# Clinical Research Coordinator
**ID**: `clinicalResearch` · **Discipline**: Healthcare · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare clinical research coordinators to protect participants, run protocol visits, maintain accurate source documentation, communicate with sponsors and monitors, and keep studies inspection-ready without losing sight of patient dignity.

## Course Design Notes
ACRP certification outlines, ICH Good Clinical Practice, FDA clinical-trial guidance, and university CRC training programs emphasize participant protection, protocol compliance, data integrity, safety reporting, investigational product accountability, and regulatory documentation. This syllabus adds day-to-day workflow habits because coordinator work is where the protocol meets real humans and real calendars.

## Chapter 1: The CRC Role and Clinical Trial Ecosystem
- **Key concepts**: sponsor, investigator, IRB/ethics committee, monitor, CRO, site team, protocol, delegation of authority.
- **Practice questions**: Who owns this decision? Which task must be delegated? What should be escalated to the investigator?
- **Work product**: a role map showing responsibilities, handoffs, and escalation paths.

## Chapter 2: Participant Protection and Informed Consent
- **Key concepts**: informed consent, participant rights, vulnerable populations, voluntariness, privacy, comprehension.
- **Practice questions**: Did the participant understand the study? Was consent obtained before procedures? What should be re-consented?
- **Work product**: an informed-consent checklist and documentation note.

## Chapter 3: Protocol, Eligibility, and Visit Flow
- **Key concepts**: inclusion criteria, exclusion criteria, schedule of assessments, windows, deviations, screening logs.
- **Practice questions**: Is this participant eligible? Which visit procedures are due? What deviation risk exists today?
- **Work product**: a visit-prep worksheet for screening, baseline, and follow-up visits.

## Chapter 4: Source Documentation and Data Quality
- **Key concepts**: source documents, CRFs/eCRFs, ALCOA principles, query resolution, audit trail, corrections.
- **Practice questions**: Where is the source of truth? Is the correction traceable? What data point needs clarification?
- **Work product**: a source-to-CRF reconciliation exercise with query responses.

## Chapter 5: Safety, Adverse Events, and Concomitant Medications
- **Key concepts**: adverse event, serious adverse event, causality, severity, relatedness, concomitant medication, follow-up.
- **Practice questions**: Is this reportable? What details are missing? Who must be notified and when?
- **Work product**: an adverse-event capture form and escalation timeline.

## Chapter 6: Study Product, Randomization, and Blinding
- **Key concepts**: investigational product accountability, randomization, blinding, temperature logs, dispensing, returns.
- **Practice questions**: Is product accountability complete? What threatens the blind? What should be documented after a dispensing error?
- **Work product**: an investigational-product accountability log review.

## Chapter 7: Monitoring, Audits, and Regulatory Binder Discipline
- **Key concepts**: essential documents, delegation log, training log, monitoring visit, CAPA, audit readiness.
- **Practice questions**: What will the monitor ask for? Which document is missing? What corrective action prevents recurrence?
- **Work product**: a regulatory binder audit checklist and CAPA draft.

## Chapter 8: Startup, Closeout, and Site Operations
- **Key concepts**: feasibility, startup, activation, recruitment, retention, closeout, archive, site communication.
- **Practice questions**: What blocks activation? Why are visits slipping? What must be resolved before closeout?
- **Work product**: a study operations dashboard with recruitment, visits, queries, deviations, and open actions.

## Capstone
Run a simulated study month. Prepare a participant visit, document consent and eligibility, resolve data queries, process an adverse event, update the regulatory binder, and write a monitor-ready status email.

## Assessment Ideas
- Consent and eligibility exercises graded on participant protection and protocol accuracy.
- Source documentation graded on traceability and query quality.
- Capstone graded on operational judgment, escalation, and inspection readiness.
