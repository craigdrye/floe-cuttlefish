# AIGP AI Governance
**ID**: `aigp` | **Discipline**: Technology | **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners for AI governance work and AIGP-style qualification study: accountable AI program design, risk classification, impact assessment, documentation, vendor oversight, generative AI evaluation, and governance decisions that can survive scrutiny.

## Course Design Notes
IAPP AIGP materials, the NIST AI Risk Management Framework, EU AI Act concepts, and enterprise AI governance programs emphasize accountability, transparency, risk management, human oversight, privacy, security, and lifecycle controls. This syllabus keeps the work practical: learners produce the memos, registers, assessments, and review notes that governance teams use to approve, monitor, or reject AI systems.

## Chapter 1: AI Governance Roles and Accountability
- **Key concepts**: AI governance, accountability, model owner, system owner, risk committee, policy, standards, decision rights.
- **Practice questions**: Who owns the AI system? Which decision needs documented approval? What evidence shows accountability is real?
- **Work product**: an AI governance role map with owners, approvers, escalation paths, and review cadence.

## Chapter 2: AI System Inventory and Risk Triage
- **Key concepts**: AI system, use case, intended purpose, user population, risk tier, prohibited use, high-risk use, material change.
- **Practice questions**: Is this system in scope? What harm could occur? Which facts change the risk tier?
- **Work product**: an AI inventory entry and risk triage worksheet with rationale.

## Chapter 3: NIST AI RMF and Control Design
- **Key concepts**: govern, map, measure, manage, validity, reliability, safety, security, resilience, explainability, privacy.
- **Practice questions**: Which RMF function is weakest? What control reduces the main risk? What metric proves the control is working?
- **Work product**: a NIST AI RMF control matrix for a selected AI use case.

## Chapter 4: EU AI Act and Regulatory Readiness
- **Key concepts**: risk categories, high-risk AI, provider, deployer, conformity assessment, technical documentation, human oversight.
- **Practice questions**: Which actor obligations apply? Is the use case high risk? What documentation would an auditor request?
- **Work product**: an EU AI Act readiness memo with role, risk category, obligations, and gaps.

## Chapter 5: AI Impact Assessments and Documentation
- **Key concepts**: impact assessment, affected stakeholders, foreseeable misuse, bias, fairness, data provenance, limitations, residual risk.
- **Practice questions**: Who could be harmed? Which assumption needs evidence? What mitigation is proportionate?
- **Work product**: an AI impact assessment with harms, controls, residual risk, and approval recommendation.

## Chapter 6: Generative AI Evaluation and Security
- **Key concepts**: foundation model, prompt injection, jailbreak, hallucination, retrieval, evaluation set, red teaming, content safety.
- **Practice questions**: What failure mode is being tested? Can the system leak sensitive data? Which evaluation result blocks release?
- **Work product**: a generative AI evaluation plan covering quality, safety, prompt injection, and escalation thresholds.

## Chapter 7: Transparency, Notices, and Human Oversight
- **Key concepts**: transparency, disclosure, explainability, contestability, human-in-the-loop, human-on-the-loop, user instruction, logging.
- **Practice questions**: What should users be told? Can a human meaningfully intervene? What record supports the final decision?
- **Work product**: a transparency and oversight checklist for an AI-assisted decision workflow.

## Chapter 8: Vendor Controls and Ongoing Monitoring
- **Key concepts**: third-party risk, vendor due diligence, contractual controls, model updates, incident response, monitoring, audit rights.
- **Practice questions**: What must the vendor disclose? Which contract term protects the organization? What change triggers re-review?
- **Work product**: a vendor AI risk review with required evidence, contract controls, monitoring plan, and acceptance decision.

## Capstone
Build an AI governance review packet for a fictional AI system: inventory entry, risk triage, NIST AI RMF control matrix, EU AI Act readiness memo, impact assessment, genAI evaluation plan if applicable, transparency checklist, and vendor oversight recommendation.

## Assessment Ideas
- Risk triage exercises graded on scope, harm analysis, and rationale.
- Impact assessments graded on stakeholder awareness, mitigation quality, and residual-risk judgment.
- Capstone graded on governance completeness, evidence quality, and defensible approval logic.
