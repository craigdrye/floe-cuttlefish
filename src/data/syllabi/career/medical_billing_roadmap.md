# Medical Billing Roadmap
**ID**: `medicalBillingRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Healthcare - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Train learners to move through the medical billing workflow from patient access to final payment. The course builds practical fluency in eligibility, authorizations, claims, denials, appeals, patient responsibility, and revenue-cycle follow-up.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test billing workflow, payer communication, claim status, denial workqueues, authorization gaps, payment posting, underpayments, or patient balance questions. The learner should always identify the blocker, evidence, owner, and next action.

## Chapter 1: Revenue Cycle Overview
- **Key concepts**: patient access, encounter, charge capture, claim submission, adjudication, payment posting, A/R.
- **Practice questions**: Where is the account in the cycle? What is blocking movement? Who owns the next step?
- **Work output**: revenue-cycle map with handoffs, common delays, and control points.

## Chapter 2: Eligibility, Benefits, and Authorization
- **Key concepts**: eligibility, benefit verification, referral, prior authorization, medical necessity, payer policy.
- **Practice questions**: Is coverage active? Was authorization required? What proof belongs in the account?
- **Work output**: pre-service clearance checklist with coverage, authorization, and documentation status.

## Chapter 3: Claims and Clean Submission
- **Key concepts**: claim form, CPT, ICD-10-CM, HCPCS, modifier, NPI, place of service, clean claim.
- **Practice questions**: What field is missing? Does documentation support the charge? What edit should be fixed before submission?
- **Work output**: clean-claim review note for a professional or facility claim.

## Chapter 4: Denials, Appeals, and Follow-Up
- **Key concepts**: rejection, denial, CARC, RARC, timely filing, corrected claim, reconsideration, appeal packet.
- **Practice questions**: Why did the payer stop payment? Can it be corrected or appealed? What deadline applies?
- **Work output**: denial triage log with cause, deadline, evidence, owner, and next action.

## Chapter 5: Payments, Underpayments, and Patient Balances
- **Key concepts**: ERA, EOB, allowed amount, contractual adjustment, COB, secondary payer, patient responsibility, refund.
- **Practice questions**: Did the payer pay correctly? What is owed by whom? What should be posted, appealed, or refunded?
- **Work output**: payment review worksheet with expected payment, variance, and follow-up path.

## Capstone
Work a simulated billing queue from eligibility through payment. Clear authorization issues, correct claim errors, triage denials, review underpayments, and prepare a patient-balance explanation.
