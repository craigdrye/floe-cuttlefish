# Series 86 & 87 Research Analyst
**ID**: `series8687` - **Discipline**: Finance - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare equity research candidates for the paired Series 86 and Series 87 exams by building valuation fluency, reporting discipline, supervisory awareness, and analyst ethics. The course treats research as both an analytical craft and a regulated communication function.

## Course Design Notes
FINRA's Series 86 and Series 87 Research Analyst Exams test the knowledge needed to analyze equity securities and prepare research communications. Series 86 leans into analysis, valuation, accounting, economics, and industry judgment; Series 87 emphasizes rules, disclosures, conflicts, supervision, and permitted conduct. This syllabus keeps both sides together so learners practice turning analysis into reports that are accurate, documented, independent, and compliant.

## Chapter 1: Research Analyst Role and Exam Map
- **Key concepts**: Series 86, Series 87, research analyst registration, research report, public appearance, investment banking conflicts, SIE relationship.
- **Practice questions**: Is the issue analytical, ethical, disclosure-related, or supervisory? Which exam domain does the question belong to? What facts would change the regulatory answer?
- **Work product**: a two-exam study map with topic areas, weak spots, formulas, rule triggers, and review cadence.

## Chapter 2: Financial Statements and Business Drivers
- **Key concepts**: income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement, accruals, working capital, margins, ROIC, leverage, quality of earnings.
- **Practice questions**: Which financial statement shows the first effect? What metric explains the change in performance? Is the result cash-backed or accounting-driven?
- **Work product**: a company diagnostic table linking financial statement changes to operating drivers and research implications.

## Chapter 3: Forecasting, Modeling, and Sensitivity
- **Key concepts**: revenue build, unit economics, cost structure, operating leverage, EPS, dilution, scenario analysis, consensus estimates, estimate revisions.
- **Practice questions**: Which assumption moves valuation most? Are growth, margin, capex, and working capital assumptions internally consistent? What does the downside case reveal?
- **Work product**: a driver-based forecast with base, upside, and downside cases plus a short explanation of the key swing factor.

## Chapter 4: Equity Valuation Toolkit
- **Key concepts**: DCF, comparable companies, precedent transactions, multiples, WACC, terminal value, free cash flow, sum-of-the-parts, target price.
- **Practice questions**: Which method fits the company and industry? What multiple is defensible? Does the target price follow from assumptions rather than preference?
- **Work product**: a valuation bridge that triangulates DCF, trading multiples, and sanity checks into a target price range.

## Chapter 5: Industry, Market, and Economic Context
- **Key concepts**: market structure, competitive advantage, cyclicality, supply and demand, interest rates, inflation, FX, regulation, catalysts, risk factors.
- **Practice questions**: Which external variable matters most? Is the company exposed to cyclical, structural, or company-specific risk? What catalyst could force estimate revision?
- **Work product**: an industry driver map with key metrics, leading indicators, catalysts, and downside risks.

## Chapter 6: Research Report Discipline
- **Key concepts**: thesis, recommendation, rating system, price target, assumptions, support, balanced risk discussion, source handling, update notes.
- **Practice questions**: Is the conclusion supported by the model? Are risks specific enough? Can a reader separate facts, estimates, and opinion?
- **Work product**: a short research report with thesis, valuation, catalysts, risks, disclosures placeholder, and evidence checklist.

## Chapter 7: Analyst Ethics, Conflicts, and Disclosures
- **Key concepts**: independence, conflicts of interest, compensation disclosure, personal trading, quiet periods, selective disclosure, material nonpublic information, fair dealing.
- **Practice questions**: What conflict must be disclosed? What conduct threatens independence? When should the analyst refuse, escalate, or document the issue?
- **Work product**: a conflict and disclosure memo for a proposed research report, including required disclosures and prohibited conduct.

## Chapter 8: Supervision, Communications, and Exam Execution
- **Key concepts**: approval workflow, supervisory review, public appearances, recordkeeping, communications with sales and trading, exam timing, calculation triage, distractor detection.
- **Practice questions**: Who must approve the communication? Is the statement fair and balanced? Was the missed practice question caused by rule recall, calculation error, or wording?
- **Work product**: a final readiness log combining timed practice results, rule triggers, formula review, and a remediation plan.

## Capstone
Build a mini equity research packet for a fictional public company: financial diagnostic, forecast, valuation range, industry driver map, short report, conflict analysis, disclosure memo, and final exam review log.

## Assessment Ideas
- Valuation exercises graded on method selection, assumption quality, and sensitivity.
- Report drafts graded on thesis support, risk balance, and communication discipline.
- Ethics scenarios graded on conflict recognition, disclosure logic, and escalation judgment.
- Capstone graded on analytical coherence, regulated-reporting awareness, and exam readiness.
