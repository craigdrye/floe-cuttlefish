# Healthcare Compliance Roadmap
**ID**: `healthcareComplianceRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Healthcare - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners to recognize healthcare compliance risk and turn it into practical controls, escalation, documentation, and monitoring. The course covers fraud and abuse, privacy, billing integrity, conflicts, investigations, corrective action, and leadership reporting.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test healthcare compliance judgment, Stark and Anti-Kickback risk, HIPAA signals, billing concerns, hotline triage, investigation discipline, or compliance-program evidence. Keep the voice practical: identify the risk, protect the record, and choose the next defensible step.

## Chapter 1: Healthcare Compliance Map
- **Key concepts**: compliance program, code of conduct, policies, risk assessment, OIG guidance, board oversight, escalation path.
- **Practice questions**: What risk is being managed? Who owns the decision? What evidence shows the program works?
- **Work output**: compliance risk map with owners, policies, controls, evidence, and escalation routes.

## Chapter 2: Fraud, Abuse, and Referral Risk
- **Key concepts**: Anti-Kickback Statute, Stark Law, False Claims Act, fair market value, medical necessity, inducement.
- **Practice questions**: Is value being exchanged? Could referrals be influenced? What documentation supports the arrangement?
- **Work output**: referral-arrangement review note with risk flags, missing evidence, and recommended action.

## Chapter 3: Privacy, Security, and Patient Rights
- **Key concepts**: HIPAA, PHI, minimum necessary, authorization, breach, access rights, business associate, sanction policy.
- **Practice questions**: Was PHI used appropriately? Is this a reportable breach? Who must be notified?
- **Work output**: privacy-incident triage note with facts, impact, notification path, and mitigation.

## Chapter 4: Billing Integrity and Documentation
- **Key concepts**: coding accuracy, medical necessity, upcoding, unbundling, modifier support, overpayment, refund obligation.
- **Practice questions**: Does the record support the claim? Is the error isolated or systemic? What must be corrected?
- **Work output**: billing-compliance audit finding with sample issue, root cause, repayment risk, and corrective action.

## Chapter 5: Investigations, CAPA, and Reporting
- **Key concepts**: hotline intake, non-retaliation, investigation scope, evidence preservation, substantiation, CAPA, monitoring.
- **Practice questions**: What allegation is being tested? Which facts are confirmed? What action prevents recurrence?
- **Work output**: investigation closing memo with finding, rationale, remediation, owner, and follow-up metric.

## Capstone
Build a healthcare compliance response pack for a fictional provider: risk map, referral review, privacy triage, billing audit finding, investigation memo, CAPA plan, and leadership update.
