# People Management
**ID**: `peopleManagement` · **Discipline**: Leadership · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Help new and growing managers build a humane, high-accountability team operating system: set expectations, delegate cleanly, coach performance, run useful 1:1s, handle conflict, hire and onboard thoughtfully, and improve the work system instead of becoming the team's heroic bottleneck.

## Course Design Notes
The original syllabus had practical bones: expectations, feedback, coaching, delegation, hiring, onboarding, conflict, and scale. This rewrite adds manager role clarity, decision processes, psychological safety, performance diagnosis, inclusion, operating rhythms, change, and a capstone management operating system.

## Chapter 1: The Manager Job
**Key concepts**: manager role, ownership, team outcomes, trust, expectations, decision rights, context, accountability, psychological contract.

**Practical questions**
- What is the manager responsible for that an individual contributor is not?
- What decisions belong to the manager, the team, or the company?
- Where does helpful support become bottleneck behavior?

**Workplace skill**
- Write a manager operating charter: team purpose, standards, decision rights, meeting norms, escalation rules, and communication expectations.

## Chapter 2: Goals, Priorities, and Operating Rhythm
**Key concepts**: goals, OKRs, KPIs, prioritization, capacity, role clarity, meeting cadence, 1:1s, team meetings, status systems, decision logs.

**Practical questions**
- What matters most this week, month, and quarter?
- Which meetings create clarity, and which hide confusion?
- How will the team know when priorities change?

**Workplace skill**
- Design a team operating rhythm with 1:1s, team meetings, async updates, decision logs, and priority review.

## Chapter 3: Delegation and Ownership
**Key concepts**: delegation, context, outcome, constraints, authority, check-in cadence, coaching zone, empowerment, accountability.

**Practical questions**
- What outcome is delegated, not just what task?
- How much autonomy is appropriate for the person's skill and risk?
- What check-in prevents drift without micromanagement?

**Workplace skill**
- Write a delegation brief with outcome, why it matters, decision authority, constraints, support, timeline, and review points.

## Chapter 4: Feedback, Coaching, and Performance
**Key concepts**: feedback, feedforward, coaching, skill gap, will gap, system gap, behavior, impact, expectation, performance plan, recognition.

**Practical questions**
- Is this a capability issue, motivation issue, clarity issue, or system issue?
- What behavior needs to change, and what support is fair?
- What should be praised before it silently disappears?

**Workplace skill**
- Conduct a feedback conversation using situation, behavior, impact, expectation, and follow-up.

## Chapter 5: Difficult Conversations and Conflict
**Key concepts**: conflict styles, interests, mediation, emotional regulation, repair, escalation, fairness, documentation, psychological safety.

**Practical questions**
- What is the conflict about on the surface, and what is underneath?
- What needs to be said clearly but respectfully?
- When does conflict require HR, legal, or senior escalation?

**Workplace skill**
- Prepare a difficult conversation plan with facts, desired outcome, likely reactions, boundaries, and follow-up.

## Chapter 6: Hiring, Onboarding, and Team Design
**Key concepts**: role design, hiring signal, structured interview, bias, onboarding, ramp plan, team topology, capability gaps, succession.

**Practical questions**
- What problem is this role meant to solve?
- What evidence predicts success, and what is just interview theater?
- How will a new hire become productive and connected?

**Workplace skill**
- Build a role scorecard, structured interview plan, and 30/60/90 onboarding plan.

## Chapter 7: Inclusion, Motivation, and Team Health
**Key concepts**: belonging, fairness, motivation, autonomy, mastery, purpose, burnout, workload, recognition, inclusion, accessibility, trust.

**Practical questions**
- Who is being heard, interrupted, overloaded, or left out?
- What is draining motivation?
- Where does the team need psychological safety, and where does it need clearer accountability?

**Workplace skill**
- Create a team health dashboard with engagement signals, workload risks, inclusion checks, and action plan.

## Chapter 8: Managing Change and Scaling Yourself
**Key concepts**: change management, communication, resistance, learning loops, retrospectives, process improvement, managing managers, succession, delegation layers.

**Practical questions**
- What change are we asking people to make, and why would it be hard?
- What should the manager stop doing as the team grows?
- What process should improve before adding more people?

**Workplace skill**
- Run a retrospective and convert lessons into a small operating-system improvement.

## Capstone: Manager Operating System
Learners assemble:
- manager operating charter
- team rhythm plan
- delegation brief
- feedback conversation plan
- difficult conversation plan
- role scorecard and onboarding plan
- team health dashboard
- retrospective improvement memo

## Assessment Ideas
- Manager charter
- 1:1 and team meeting design
- Delegation simulation
- Feedback roleplay
- Conflict plan
- Hiring/onboarding packet
- Team health review
- Manager Operating System capstone
