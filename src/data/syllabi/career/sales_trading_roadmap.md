# Sales and Trading Roadmap
**ID**: `salesTradingRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Finance - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners to understand market structure, client flow, risk, trade ideas, and desk communication. The course builds practical fluency in how sales and trading desks turn market information into executable decisions.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test markets, products, pricing, client coverage, trade ideas, risk limits, P&L, order handling, or desk etiquette. Keep the tone fast, precise, and risk-aware.

## Chapter 1: Desk Roles and Market Structure
- **Key concepts**: sales, trader, structurer, market maker, agency, principal, liquidity, bid-ask spread, venue.
- **Practice questions**: Who owns the client relationship? Who owns risk? What makes a market liquid or fragile?
- **Work output**: desk map showing roles, products, clients, and risk handoffs.

## Chapter 2: Products, Pricing, and Risk Measures
- **Key concepts**: equities, rates, credit, FX, options, duration, spread, delta, gamma, vega, basis.
- **Practice questions**: What moves the price? Which risk measure matters? How does the desk hedge exposure?
- **Work output**: product primer with price drivers, risk measures, and hedge options.

## Chapter 3: Client Flow and Trade Ideas
- **Key concepts**: inquiry, axes, color, positioning, catalyst, relative value, macro view, suitability.
- **Practice questions**: What does the client need? Is the idea timely? What risk would make the trade fail?
- **Work output**: client trade idea with setup, expression, upside, downside, and exit logic.

## Chapter 4: Execution, P&L, and Limits
- **Key concepts**: order type, slippage, fill, inventory, VaR, stop-loss, limit breach, mark-to-market, P&L explain.
- **Practice questions**: Did execution help or hurt? What explains P&L? Which risk limit is binding?
- **Work output**: trade recap with execution quality, P&L drivers, and risk status.

## Chapter 5: Regulation and Desk Communication
- **Key concepts**: best execution, MNPI, suitability, restricted list, chat discipline, surveillance, controls.
- **Practice questions**: What can be said? What must be documented? When should compliance be involved?
- **Work output**: compliant desk update separating market color, client interest, and restricted information.

## Capstone
Build a sales and trading morning packet: market recap, client flow summary, product risk primer, trade idea, execution plan, and compliance-aware desk note.
