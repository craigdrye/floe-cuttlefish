# Credit and LevFin Roadmap
**ID**: `creditLevfinRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Finance - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Train learners to analyze leveraged borrowers, debt structures, credit risk, and financing recommendations. The course builds practical judgment about repayment capacity, downside protection, and market appetite.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test credit analysis, leveraged finance, debt capacity, ratings, covenants, capital structure, syndication, or restructuring warning signs. Keep the learner anchored in cash flow and recoverability.

## Chapter 1: Borrower and Capital Structure Basics
- **Key concepts**: senior debt, secured debt, unsecured debt, revolver, term loan, bond, priority, enterprise value.
- **Practice questions**: Who gets paid first? What debt is already outstanding? What collateral supports repayment?
- **Work output**: capital-structure snapshot with maturity, priority, pricing, and security.

## Chapter 2: Credit Metrics and Cash Flow
- **Key concepts**: EBITDA, leverage, interest coverage, free cash flow, working capital, capex, liquidity, fixed charges.
- **Practice questions**: Can the borrower service debt? What drains cash? Which adjustment is aggressive?
- **Work output**: credit metrics page with base and downside cases.

## Chapter 3: LevFin Markets and Debt Capacity
- **Key concepts**: sponsor deal, acquisition financing, ratings, spread, OID, yield, market flex, syndication, investor appetite.
- **Practice questions**: How much debt can clear the market? What terms would investors demand? Where could syndication fail?
- **Work output**: financing recommendation with debt mix, pricing, leverage, and market risks.

## Chapter 4: Covenants, Documentation, and Protection
- **Key concepts**: maintenance covenant, incurrence covenant, restricted payments, baskets, EBITDA add-backs, collateral leakage.
- **Practice questions**: What protection does the lender have? Which term weakens recovery? What behavior is permitted?
- **Work output**: covenant summary with lender protections, issuer flexibility, and red flags.

## Chapter 5: Distress Signals and Recovery Thinking
- **Key concepts**: downgrade, liquidity crunch, maturity wall, amendment, exchange offer, recovery value, restructuring.
- **Practice questions**: What is the first sign of stress? Is liquidity enough? What would creditors recover?
- **Work output**: distress watch note with triggers, recovery assumptions, and recommended action.

## Capstone
Prepare a credit and LevFin packet for a fictional leveraged borrower: capital-structure snapshot, credit metrics, financing recommendation, covenant summary, downside case, and distress watch note.
