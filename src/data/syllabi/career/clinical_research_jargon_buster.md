# Clinical Research Jargon Buster
**ID**: `clinicalResearchJargon` - **Discipline**: Healthcare - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Teach learners to translate clinical-trial site language into safe, documented action. The course turns phrases about consent, source, queries, deviations, AEs, SAE reporting, monitoring, and closeout into concrete GCP tasks.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test clinical research shorthand, monitor comments, site operations, documentation discipline, participant safety, protocol adherence, or inspection-readiness language. The tone should be calm and practical because the work often happens under deadline pressure.

## Chapter 1: Consent, Eligibility, and Participant Rights
- **Key concepts**: informed consent, re-consent, eligibility criteria, screening windows, withdrawal, participant understanding.
- **Practice questions**: Is consent complete? Is the participant eligible? What must be escalated before enrollment?
- **Work output**: consent and eligibility review checklist.

## Chapter 2: Source, CRFs, and Data Integrity
- **Key concepts**: source documentation, CRF, eSource, audit trail, ALCOA, query resolution, late entry.
- **Practice questions**: Can the data be verified? What changed? Who documented it and when?
- **Work output**: source-to-CRF traceability note.

## Chapter 3: Safety Language
- **Key concepts**: AE, SAE, causality, expectedness, reporting timelines, investigator assessment, safety follow-up.
- **Practice questions**: Is this urgent? Who must be notified? What facts are known and pending?
- **Work output**: safety-event escalation summary.

## Chapter 4: Deviations and CAPA
- **Key concepts**: protocol deviation, important deviation, root cause, corrective action, preventive action, recurrence.
- **Practice questions**: What happened? How serious is it? What prevents repeat failure?
- **Work output**: deviation note with impact, reporting path, and CAPA.

## Chapter 5: Monitoring, Closeout, and Inspection Readiness
- **Key concepts**: monitoring visit, action item, essential documents, delegation log, IP accountability, closeout, retention.
- **Practice questions**: What will the monitor ask for? What is missing? Can the site reconstruct the study?
- **Work output**: inspection-readiness checklist.

## Capstone
Translate a monitor follow-up email into a prioritized site action list with safety, data, delegation, IP, and closeout items separated.
