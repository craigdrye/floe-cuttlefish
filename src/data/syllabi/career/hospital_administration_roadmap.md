# Hospital Administration Roadmap
**ID**: `hospitalAdministrationRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Healthcare - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners to understand how hospitals run across patient flow, staffing, finance, quality, compliance, operations, and leadership. The goal is practical administrative judgment, not memorizing org charts.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test hospital operations, department handoffs, capacity pressure, patient access, quality metrics, regulatory risk, budget tradeoffs, or executive communication. Keep the focus on how administrators turn messy hospital signals into safe decisions.

## Chapter 1: Hospital System Map
- **Key concepts**: acute care, service lines, inpatient units, ED, perioperative services, ancillary departments, governance.
- **Practice questions**: Who owns the decision? Where is the handoff? Which metric shows strain?
- **Work output**: hospital operating map with departments, handoffs, leaders, and key measures.

## Chapter 2: Patient Flow and Capacity
- **Key concepts**: admission, discharge, transfer, bed management, length of stay, throughput, observation, bottleneck.
- **Practice questions**: What is blocking flow? Which delay harms patients or capacity? What escalation is needed?
- **Work output**: capacity huddle brief with constraints, risks, owners, and next actions.

## Chapter 3: Finance and Resource Stewardship
- **Key concepts**: operating budget, margin, labor cost, supply cost, payer mix, uncompensated care, productivity.
- **Practice questions**: Is the variance volume, rate, staffing, or utilization? What tradeoff is being made?
- **Work output**: budget variance note with driver, risk, and management response.

## Chapter 4: Quality, Safety, and Compliance
- **Key concepts**: quality measures, patient safety event, accreditation, infection prevention, HIPAA, policy control.
- **Practice questions**: What is the harm signal? What evidence is required? What must be reported or fixed?
- **Work output**: quality issue summary with impact, root cause, corrective action, and follow-up measure.

## Chapter 5: People, Service, and Leadership Communication
- **Key concepts**: staffing model, employee engagement, patient experience, change management, escalation, executive brief.
- **Practice questions**: Who is affected? What message reduces confusion? What decision needs leadership?
- **Work output**: leadership brief translating operations data into a decision and communication plan.

## Capstone
Build a hospital administrator action pack for a simulated week: capacity brief, budget variance note, quality issue summary, staffing concern, and executive update.
