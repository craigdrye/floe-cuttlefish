# Bank Compliance and AML Roadmap
**ID**: `bankComplianceAmlRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Finance - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners to move from entry-level bank compliance awareness into practical AML and financial-crime work. The course emphasizes customer risk, transaction behavior, escalation judgment, documentation, and the compliance controls that make analyst decisions defensible.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when learners need a career path through BSA/AML, KYC, sanctions, transaction monitoring, investigations, SAR logic, and bank compliance controls. Keep scenarios grounded in analyst work products and plain-language risk reasoning.

## Chapter 1: Bank Compliance Foundations
- **Key concepts**: compliance management system, BSA/AML program, three lines of defense, policies, procedures, controls, issue management.
- **Practice questions**: What risk is the bank trying to control? Which team owns the decision? What evidence proves the control worked?
- **Work output**: compliance map linking obligation, control, owner, evidence, and escalation path.

## Chapter 2: KYC and Customer Risk
- **Key concepts**: customer identification, beneficial ownership, source of funds, expected activity, risk rating, enhanced due diligence.
- **Practice questions**: Does the customer profile make sense? Which missing fact changes the risk rating? What documentation is enough?
- **Work output**: customer risk memo with profile, gaps, risk drivers, and recommended next step.

## Chapter 3: Sanctions and Screening
- **Key concepts**: list screening, PEPs, false positives, fuzzy matching, geography risk, blocked property, escalation thresholds.
- **Practice questions**: Is the match credible? What facts separate the customer from the listed party? When should activity pause?
- **Work output**: screening disposition note with match logic, evidence, and escalation recommendation.

## Chapter 4: Transaction Monitoring and Alerts
- **Key concepts**: structuring, unusual activity, velocity, wires, cash activity, peer comparison, alert tuning, false positives.
- **Practice questions**: What is unusual for this customer? What benign explanation fits the facts? What evidence is still needed?
- **Work output**: alert review worksheet separating facts, red flags, mitigating evidence, and conclusion.

## Chapter 5: Investigations, SARs, and Quality Control
- **Key concepts**: case chronology, link analysis, SAR narrative, confidentiality, audit trail, QA review, examiner readiness.
- **Practice questions**: Is suspicion supported? What should not be overstated? Can another reviewer reconstruct the decision?
- **Work output**: investigation summary with timeline, parties, activity, disposition, and QA checklist.

## Capstone
Build an AML analyst case file for a fictional bank customer: compliance map, KYC risk memo, sanctions disposition, transaction alert review, investigation summary, SAR-style narrative, and quality-control checklist.
