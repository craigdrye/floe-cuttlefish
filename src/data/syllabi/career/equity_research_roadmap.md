# Equity Research Roadmap
**ID**: `equityResearchRoadmap` - **Discipline**: Finance - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Teach learners to analyze public companies, build evidence-based forecasts, value stocks, and communicate a clear investment view. The course focuses on repeatable research judgment, not hot takes.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test public-company research, earnings, valuation, models, industry drivers, catalysts, ratings, or note writing. The learner should always connect facts to estimate changes and stock implications.

## Chapter 1: Company and Industry Setup
- **Key concepts**: business model, revenue drivers, segments, margins, market share, cycle, regulation, peers.
- **Practice questions**: What drives revenue? Which industry variable matters most? Who is the true peer set?
- **Work output**: company initiation snapshot with drivers, peers, and key debates.

## Chapter 2: Filings, Earnings, and Management Commentary
- **Key concepts**: 10-K, 10-Q, earnings release, guidance, call transcript, non-GAAP adjustments, backlog.
- **Practice questions**: What changed this quarter? Is guidance credible? Which disclosure affects estimates?
- **Work output**: earnings readout with beats, misses, guidance changes, and questions for follow-up.

## Chapter 3: Forecasting and Model Discipline
- **Key concepts**: revenue build, margin bridge, operating leverage, working capital, capex, EPS, free cash flow.
- **Practice questions**: Which assumption drives the model? What is consensus missing? Does the forecast tie to evidence?
- **Work output**: three-statement forecast with driver notes and sensitivity table.

## Chapter 4: Valuation and Rating Logic
- **Key concepts**: P/E, EV/EBITDA, DCF, sum-of-the-parts, target price, upside/downside, risk-reward.
- **Practice questions**: Which multiple is appropriate? What supports the target price? When would the rating change?
- **Work output**: valuation page with target price, scenarios, and rating rationale.

## Chapter 5: Research Note and Investor Debate
- **Key concepts**: thesis, catalyst, variant perception, consensus, channel checks, risk section, compliance.
- **Practice questions**: What is the debate? What evidence is new? Why should an investor act now?
- **Work output**: concise research note with thesis, estimate impact, valuation, risks, and catalyst.

## Capstone
Write an equity research initiation for a fictional public company: industry setup, earnings readout, forecast model, valuation page, rating logic, and one-page investor note.
