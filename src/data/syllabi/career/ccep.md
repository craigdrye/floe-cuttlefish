# CCEP Compliance Program Design
**ID**: `ccep` - **Discipline**: Compliance - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare compliance professionals for CCEP-style judgment by practicing compliance program design, reporting lines, escalation, investigations, controls, training, monitoring, and board-ready reporting. The course emphasizes building programs that work under pressure, not just policies that read well.

## Course Design Notes
Certified Compliance and Ethics Professional preparation centers on the elements of effective compliance programs: standards, governance, education, communication, monitoring, enforcement, response, and prevention. This syllabus translates those elements into workplace deliverables so learners can design, test, and explain a compliance program with clear ownership and defensible escalation.

## Chapter 1: Compliance Program Architecture
- **Key concepts**: code of conduct, policies, procedures, risk assessment, control environment, compliance charter, program maturity, operating model.
- **Practice questions**: What risk is the program meant to manage? Which standard belongs in the code, a policy, a procedure, or training? What evidence shows the program is operating?
- **Work product**: a compliance program blueprint with risk areas, policies, owners, controls, evidence, and review cycle.

## Chapter 2: Governance, Reporting Lines, and Independence
- **Key concepts**: board oversight, senior management accountability, chief compliance officer role, independence, authority, resources, dotted-line reporting, escalation path.
- **Practice questions**: Does compliance have enough access and authority? Who receives sensitive reports? Where could business pressure distort the decision?
- **Work product**: a reporting-line map showing board, management, compliance, legal, HR, audit, and business-unit responsibilities.

## Chapter 3: Risk Assessment and Control Prioritization
- **Key concepts**: inherent risk, residual risk, likelihood, impact, risk appetite, control design, control testing, remediation, issue ownership.
- **Practice questions**: Which risks should be prioritized first? What control reduces the risk rather than just documenting it? What metric would show improvement?
- **Work product**: a compliance risk assessment with ranked risks, controls, gaps, owners, and remediation dates.

## Chapter 4: Policies, Training, and Communications
- **Key concepts**: policy lifecycle, plain-language standards, role-based training, certifications, speak-up messaging, manager toolkits, communication cadence.
- **Practice questions**: Who needs to know this rule to do their job? Is the training behavior-specific? What message would make reporting safer and clearer?
- **Work product**: a policy rollout plan with audience, training objective, communication channel, certification, and evidence of completion.

## Chapter 5: Reporting Channels and Escalation
- **Key concepts**: hotline, open-door reporting, anonymity, non-retaliation, triage, severity levels, urgent escalation, privileged review, documentation hygiene.
- **Practice questions**: Is this a question, concern, allegation, emergency, or control failure? Who must be notified, by when, and with what facts? What should not be speculated?
- **Work product**: an escalation matrix with intake categories, routing, timelines, decision authority, and documentation requirements.

## Chapter 6: Investigations and Case Management
- **Key concepts**: intake review, investigation plan, scope, evidence preservation, interviews, confidentiality, conflicts, substantiation, corrective action, case closure.
- **Practice questions**: What allegation is being tested? What evidence would prove or disprove it? Is the process fair, consistent, and documented?
- **Work product**: an investigation plan and closing memo template covering allegation, scope, evidence, findings, remediation, and lessons learned.

## Chapter 7: Monitoring, Auditing, and Program Effectiveness
- **Key concepts**: monitoring plan, audit coordination, key risk indicators, testing, data analytics, issue validation, root cause, continuous improvement.
- **Practice questions**: What should compliance monitor directly? What belongs with internal audit? Which metric is a signal rather than noise?
- **Work product**: a monitoring dashboard with KRIs, testing schedule, exceptions, root causes, and follow-up actions.

## Chapter 8: Discipline, Remediation, and Reporting
- **Key concepts**: consistent enforcement, incentives, corrective action, root-cause remediation, board reporting, regulator-ready records, annual program review.
- **Practice questions**: Is discipline consistent with prior cases? Did remediation address the root cause? What should the board see versus management detail?
- **Work product**: a board reporting pack with risk summary, significant issues, investigation trends, remediation status, and program effectiveness narrative.

## Capstone
Design a compliance and ethics program for a fictional organization facing growth, regulatory scrutiny, and reporting-line pressure. Deliver the program blueprint, reporting-line map, risk assessment, policy rollout plan, escalation matrix, investigation memo, monitoring dashboard, and board report.

## Assessment Ideas
- Program design exercises graded on coverage, ownership, and evidence.
- Escalation scenarios graded on severity judgment, routing, and documentation quality.
- Investigation work graded on fairness, scope control, and defensible findings.
- Capstone graded on practical governance, reporting clarity, and program effectiveness logic.
