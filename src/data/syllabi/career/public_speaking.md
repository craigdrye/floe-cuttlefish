# Public Speaking
**ID**: `publicSpeaking` · **Discipline**: Communication · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Help learners speak clearly under pressure: structure ideas, adapt to audiences, deliver with control, use visuals wisely, handle questions, and turn nervous energy into useful attention rather than a tiny weather system in the chest.

## Course Design Notes
The original syllabus had the right core: message design, delivery mechanics, audience adaptation, and Q&A. This rewrite adds audience analysis, speech types, evidence, outlines, visual aids, virtual speaking, persuasive speaking, feedback loops, and a capstone speaker portfolio.

## Chapter 1: Speaker Mindset and Audience Analysis
**Key concepts**: purpose, audience, occasion, constraints, credibility, stakes, anxiety, ethical speaking, listener needs.

**Practical questions**
- Who is listening, what do they know, and what do they need to do after?
- What is the speaking situation really asking for?
- How can nerves become preparation cues rather than performance sabotage?

**Workplace skill**
- Create an audience brief with knowledge level, concerns, desired outcome, risks, and adaptation plan.

## Chapter 2: Message Design and Structure
**Key concepts**: central message, thesis, preview, signposting, transitions, story spine, problem-solution, chronological, topical, compare/contrast, call to action.

**Practical questions**
- What is the one sentence the audience should remember?
- What structure makes the idea easiest to follow?
- Where will listeners get lost?

**Workplace skill**
- Build a talk outline with opening, three-part body, transitions, close, and memory line.

## Chapter 3: Evidence, Story, and Clarity
**Key concepts**: examples, statistics, testimony, analogy, story, citation, evidence quality, plain language, technical translation, specificity.

**Practical questions**
- Which evidence earns trust with this audience?
- What story makes the stakes concrete without manipulating?
- Which jargon should be translated, defined, or deleted?

**Workplace skill**
- Convert a dense briefing into a five-minute clear explanation with evidence and examples.

## Chapter 4: Delivery Mechanics
**Key concepts**: pace, pause, volume, emphasis, articulation, gesture, posture, eye contact, movement, vocal variety, presence.

**Practical questions**
- What delivery habit helps comprehension?
- Which habit distracts from the message?
- Where should silence do some work?

**Workplace skill**
- Record, review, and revise a short speech using a delivery checklist and peer feedback.

## Chapter 5: Visuals and Presentation Aids
**Key concepts**: slide purpose, visual hierarchy, chart clarity, image use, accessibility, handouts, demos, speaker notes, slide restraint.

**Practical questions**
- Does this slide help the audience think, or merely prove the speaker owned a template?
- What should be visual, spoken, or removed?
- Can someone understand the chart quickly?

**Workplace skill**
- Redesign a cluttered slide deck into a clear executive or teaching presentation.

## Chapter 6: Informative, Persuasive, and Executive Speaking
**Key concepts**: informative speech, persuasive speech, executive briefing, recommendation, objection, call to action, stakeholder framing.

**Practical questions**
- Am I informing, persuading, recommending, or facilitating a decision?
- What objection should I answer before the audience asks it?
- What decision does this meeting need?

**Workplace skill**
- Deliver one informative talk and one persuasive/recommendation talk with different structures.

## Chapter 7: Q&A, Panels, and Impromptu Speaking
**Key concepts**: question handling, bridge, clarify, answer, admit uncertainty, panel dynamics, impromptu structure, hostile question, follow-up.

**Practical questions**
- What is the real question behind the question?
- When should I answer directly, clarify, redirect, or promise follow-up?
- How do I stay concise without sounding evasive?

**Workplace skill**
- Practice Q&A drills using the answer-bridge-return structure and uncertainty language.

## Chapter 8: Virtual and Hybrid Presentation Skills
**Key concepts**: camera presence, screen share, chat, remote engagement, pacing, accessibility, facilitation, tech check, recording.

**Practical questions**
- What changes when the audience is remote, distracted, or partly invisible?
- How do I invite participation without creating chaos?
- What backup plan protects the meeting?

**Workplace skill**
- Deliver a virtual briefing with chat prompts, visual aids, timekeeping, and backup plan.

## Capstone: Speaker Portfolio
Learners produce:
- audience brief
- informative speech outline and recording
- persuasive or recommendation speech
- redesigned slide deck
- Q&A response log
- peer feedback and revision memo
- virtual presentation plan
- final live or recorded presentation

## Assessment Ideas
- Audience analysis
- Outline and transcript
- Informative speech
- Persuasive/recommendation speech
- Slide redesign
- Q&A drill
- Virtual briefing
- Speaker Portfolio capstone
