# Defense Budgeting Jargon Buster
**ID**: `defenseBudgetingJargon` - **Discipline**: Government - **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Translate defense-budgeting acronyms and funding language into plain operational meaning. Learners practice identifying whether a phrase is about authority, timing, execution, obligation risk, controls, or a budget story that needs evidence.

## Course Design Notes
Route questions here when they test PPBE language, appropriations, obligation anxiety, execution reviews, unfunded requirements, reprogramming, fiscal-law signals, or audit-readiness shorthand. Keep the course practical for newcomers who need to survive acronym-heavy meetings.

## Chapter 1: Money Words That Sound Similar
- **Key concepts**: authorization, appropriation, budget authority, apportionment, allotment, commitment, obligation, outlay.
- **Practice questions**: Which stage is being discussed? Is money legally available? What action is allowed now?
- **Work output**: funds-flow glossary with one example for each term.

## Chapter 2: PPBE Translation
- **Key concepts**: planning, programming, budgeting, execution, POM, BES, PB, budget exhibits, execution review.
- **Practice questions**: Is the request strategic, programmatic, budgetary, or execution-focused?
- **Work output**: meeting-note translation from acronym language into decision language.

## Chapter 3: Fiscal-Law Signals
- **Key concepts**: purpose, time, amount, bona fide need, Anti-Deficiency Act, expired funds, continuing resolution.
- **Practice questions**: What legal constraint is implied? What needs review before action?
- **Work output**: fiscal-risk checklist for a pressured funding request.

## Chapter 4: Execution and Variance Language
- **Key concepts**: burn rate, spend plan, phasing, obligation rate, under-execution, over-execution, carryover.
- **Practice questions**: What story do the numbers tell? Is the variance timing, scope, contract, or planning?
- **Work output**: variance explanation with driver, risk, and corrective action.

## Chapter 5: Controls and Audit Readiness
- **Key concepts**: supporting documentation, certifying official, segregation of duties, audit trail, FIAR, corrective action.
- **Practice questions**: What evidence is missing? What control failed? Is remediation real?
- **Work output**: audit-ready case note for a payment or obligation issue.

## Capstone
Decode a budget review packet into a plain-language brief: funding status, legal constraints, execution risks, decisions needed, and evidence gaps.
