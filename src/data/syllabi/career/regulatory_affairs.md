# Regulatory Affairs
**ID**: `regulatory` · **Discipline**: Healthcare · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners for life-sciences regulatory affairs work: pathway selection, evidence planning, submission quality, labeling discipline, change control, post-market obligations, and inspection-ready decision records.

## Course Design Notes
RAPS RAC materials, FDA and EMA resources, ICH guidelines, and regulatory-affairs training programs emphasize product lifecycle strategy, submissions, quality systems, clinical evidence, labeling, and post-market compliance. This syllabus is deliberately pathway-aware but role-practical: learners practice writing the documents and decision memos that hold the work together.

## Chapter 1: Regulatory Role and Product Lifecycle
- **Key concepts**: regulatory strategy, product lifecycle, drugs, biologics, devices, intended use, claims, risk classification.
- **Practice questions**: What product is being regulated? What claim is being made? Which lifecycle stage drives the next decision?
- **Work product**: a product-regulatory profile with intended use, market, risk, claims, and evidence needs.

## Chapter 2: Pathways and Agency Interactions
- **Key concepts**: IND, NDA, BLA, 510(k), De Novo, PMA, pre-submission, scientific advice, meeting packages.
- **Practice questions**: Which pathway fits the product? What precedent supports it? What question should be taken to the agency?
- **Work product**: a pathway memo comparing two regulatory routes and recommending one.

## Chapter 3: Evidence, Clinical Development, and Good Practice
- **Key concepts**: nonclinical evidence, clinical trials, GCP, GMP, GLP, endpoints, risk-benefit, data integrity.
- **Practice questions**: What evidence supports safety and effectiveness? Which gap blocks submission? What standard governs the work?
- **Work product**: an evidence-gap table with owner, source, risk, and remediation.

## Chapter 4: Submission Planning and Document Quality
- **Key concepts**: submission modules, eCTD, technical documentation, traceability, version control, reviewer usability.
- **Practice questions**: Can a reviewer find the answer? Are claims supported? Which document contradicts another?
- **Work product**: a submission content plan with dependencies and quality checks.

## Chapter 5: Labeling, Advertising, and Claims Discipline
- **Key concepts**: indication, intended use, labeling, promotional review, off-label risk, substantiation, warnings.
- **Practice questions**: Is the claim supported? Does wording expand intended use? What evidence should be cited?
- **Work product**: a claims review table with proposed wording, support, risk, and decision.

## Chapter 6: Quality Systems and Change Control
- **Key concepts**: design controls, CAPA, deviations, complaints, supplier control, document control, change impact.
- **Practice questions**: Does this change require notification or submission? Which documents must be updated? What is the patient risk?
- **Work product**: a change-control assessment with regulatory impact and approval path.

## Chapter 7: Post-Market Surveillance and Vigilance
- **Key concepts**: adverse event reporting, recalls, field actions, periodic reports, complaint trending, signal detection.
- **Practice questions**: Is this reportable? What timeline applies? What trend suggests corrective action?
- **Work product**: a post-market issue triage note with reporting decision and rationale.

## Chapter 8: Global Strategy and Regulatory Judgment
- **Key concepts**: FDA, EMA, MHRA, Health Canada, global harmonization, ICH, regulatory intelligence, ethics.
- **Practice questions**: Where do requirements diverge? What can be harmonized? How should uncertainty be documented?
- **Work product**: a global launch regulatory matrix with country, pathway, evidence, timing, and risk.

## Capstone
Create a regulatory strategy pack for a fictional drug, biologic, or device: product profile, pathway memo, evidence-gap table, submission plan, claims review, change-control assessment, and post-market triage note.

## Assessment Ideas
- Pathway memos graded on evidence, precedent, and risk logic.
- Claims reviews graded on support and restraint.
- Capstone graded on lifecycle thinking, document quality, and regulatory judgment.
