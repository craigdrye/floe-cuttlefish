# Consulting Cases
**ID**: `consultingCases` · **Discipline**: Career Hopper · **Level**: Career

## Course Aim
Prepare learners to handle consulting case interviews with structured thinking, quantitative steadiness, and crisp recommendations. The course treats cases as a practical decision-making sport: clarify the problem, build a useful structure, test it with evidence, and say what should happen next.

## Course Design Notes
Comparable consulting prep from McKinsey, BCG, Bain, and university career offices emphasizes problem solving, business judgment, analytical comfort, and clear synthesis. This version keeps those fundamentals but adds more repetition on interviewer collaboration, ambiguity, and humane pacing so the course feels less like a trick test and more like a decision lab.

## Chapter 1: What a Case Is Really Testing
- **Key concepts**: business problem framing, interviewer collaboration, hypothesis-led thinking, signal versus polish.
- **Practice questions**: What decision is the client trying to make? What would a good answer need to prove? Which facts are essential before building a structure?
- **Deliverable**: a one-page case-start checklist for clarifying objective, constraints, timeline, customer, and success metric.

## Chapter 2: Structuring Without Sounding Robotic
- **Key concepts**: issue trees, MECE logic, profit trees, market entry, growth, cost reduction, operations, pricing, M&A.
- **Practice questions**: Which frame fits the situation? Where could this frame miss the real cause? How do you adapt after the interviewer adds new information?
- **Deliverable**: three flexible structures for profitability, market entry, and operational turnaround cases.

## Chapter 3: Business Math Under Time Pressure
- **Key concepts**: revenue, cost, margin, contribution margin, breakeven, market sizing, weighted averages, payback.
- **Practice questions**: What assumptions drive the answer? Does the result pass a smell test? What unit conversion could quietly break the math?
- **Deliverable**: a timed math drill sheet with setup, calculation, interpretation, and sanity check columns.

## Chapter 4: Charts, Exhibits, and Data Pulls
- **Key concepts**: exhibit reading, trend detection, segmentation, variance, outliers, causal caution, data-to-insight translation.
- **Practice questions**: What does the chart actually say? What is the most important comparison? What would you ask for next?
- **Deliverable**: five exhibit summaries that move from observation to implication to next action.

## Chapter 5: Case Types and Pattern Recognition
- **Key concepts**: profitability, market sizing, growth strategy, pricing, operations, public sector, non-profit, due diligence.
- **Practice questions**: What is the economic engine? Where does value leak? Which stakeholder constraint matters most?
- **Deliverable**: a case-type map linking common prompts to likely drivers, math tools, and risks.

## Chapter 6: Synthesis and Recommendation
- **Key concepts**: top-down communication, evidence hierarchy, tradeoffs, risks, next steps, executive tone.
- **Practice questions**: What is your answer in one sentence? What evidence supports it? What would you do if the client is worried about risk?
- **Deliverable**: ten final recommendations with answer, evidence, risk, and next-step lines.

## Chapter 7: Handling Pushback and New Information
- **Key concepts**: recalibration, acknowledging mistakes, scenario pivots, interviewer prompts, stress recovery.
- **Practice questions**: What changes after this new fact? Which assumption should be updated? How do you recover from a math error gracefully?
- **Deliverable**: a replay log of three cases showing where the structure changed and why.

## Chapter 8: Full Case Practice and Interview Readiness
- **Key concepts**: pacing, whiteboard hygiene, note structure, behavioral fit, debrief quality, deliberate practice.
- **Practice questions**: Where did the case slow down? Which skill needs the next drill? What pattern keeps appearing across cases?
- **Deliverable**: a personal case-practice plan with target drills, peer feedback prompts, and readiness metrics.

## Capstone
Run two full mock cases: one profitability or growth case and one operations, pricing, or public-sector case. Submit the notes, math, final recommendation, and a short self-critique that names the next training priority.

## Assessment Ideas
- Timed structure drills graded for relevance and adaptability.
- Exhibit interpretation drills graded for insight, not just observation.
- Final mock case scored on problem framing, math, synthesis, collaboration, and recovery.
